What Does A Car Mechanic Make

Written by Robby Jul 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever wondered how much a car mechanic makes? If you're considering a career in automotive repair, it's important to know what kind of salary you can expect. In this post, we'll discuss the average salary for a car mechanic and what factors can affect your earning potential.

As an aspiring mechanic, you might be concerned about the cost of education and the amount of debt you'll incur. It's understandable to want to know if the investment is worth it. Additionally, you may have concerns about job security and career advancement opportunities. These are all valid concerns, and we'll address them in this post.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $25,790, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $71,940. Factors that can affect your salary include your level of experience, location, and the type of employer you work for.

In summary, becoming a car mechanic can be a rewarding career choice that offers job security and good earning potential. With the right training and experience, you can earn a competitive salary and advance your career over time.

Factors that Affect a Car Mechanic's Salary

Now that we've discussed the average salary for a car mechanic, let's take a closer look at some of the factors that can affect your earning potential.

Experience is one of the biggest factors that can impact your salary as a car mechanic. If you're just starting out in the industry, you can expect to earn less than someone who has been working in the field for several years. Additionally, your location can also play a role in your salary. Mechanics in urban areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas.

The type of employer you work for can also impact your salary. If you work for a large dealership or automotive repair chain, you may have access to benefits like health insurance and retirement savings plans that can increase your overall compensation package.

My Personal Experience as a Car Mechanic

As someone who has worked as a car mechanic for over a decade, I can tell you that the earning potential in this field is quite good. When I started out, I was earning around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved up in my career, my salary increased significantly. Today, I earn over $60,000 per year and have access to benefits like health insurance and retirement savings plans.

How to Become a Car Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming a car mechanic, there are a few steps you'll need to take. First, you'll need to complete a training program in automotive repair. This can be done at a vocational school or community college. After completing your training, you can begin working as an entry-level mechanic and gaining experience in the field.

Over time, you can work your way up to more advanced positions and increase your earning potential. Additionally, you may want to consider obtaining certification from the National Institute of Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). This can help you demonstrate your skills and expertise to potential employers.

Job Outlook for Car Mechanics

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations. As long as there are cars on the road, there will be a need for skilled mechanics to repair and maintain them.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for a car mechanic?
A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $21.16 as of May 2020.

Q: How long does it take to become a car mechanic?
A: The length of time it takes to become a car mechanic depends on the training program you choose. Some programs can be completed in as little as six months, while others may take up to two years.

Q: Is it necessary to obtain ASE certification?
A: While ASE certification is not required to work as a car mechanic, it can help you demonstrate your skills and expertise to potential employers. Additionally, some employers may require or prefer ASE certification.

Q: Are there opportunities for career advancement as a car mechanic?
A: Yes, there are many opportunities for career advancement as a car mechanic. With the right training and experience, you can move up to more advanced positions like service manager, shop foreman, or master technician. Additionally, you may want to consider opening your own repair shop or working as a consultant.

Conclusion of What Does a Car Mechanic Make

As you can see, becoming a car mechanic can be a lucrative career choice that offers a lot of potential for growth and advancement. While the initial investment in education and training may seem daunting, it can pay off in the long run with a good salary and job security. If you're passionate about cars and enjoy working with your hands, a career in automotive repair might be right for you.
