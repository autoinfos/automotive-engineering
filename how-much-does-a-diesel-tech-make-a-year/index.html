How Much Does A Diesel Tech Make A Year

Written by Eveline Sep 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a diesel technician but are unsure about the potential salary? Perhaps you are already a diesel tech and are curious about how your salary compares to others in the field. Whatever your situation may be, understanding how much a diesel tech makes a year is important information to have.

The average salary for a diesel technician varies based on several factors, such as location, experience, and industry. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $50,200 as of May 2020. This means that half of all diesel technicians made more than this amount, while the other half made less.

In addition to the median annual wage, diesel technicians may also be eligible for bonuses, overtime pay, and other benefits. For example, some employers offer profit-sharing or commission-based pay structures, which can increase a diesel tech's earning potential.

Overall, becoming a diesel technician can be a lucrative career choice for those with the necessary skills and experience. However, it is important to keep in mind that salaries may vary widely based on location, employer, and other factors.

Personal Experience:

When I started my career as a diesel technician, I was making around $40,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased to over $60,000 per year. Additionally, my employer offered regular bonuses and other incentives, which helped to increase my overall earnings.

Salary Comparison:

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the highest-paying industries for diesel technicians are the federal government, natural gas distribution, and support activities for mining. On the other hand, the lowest-paying industries for diesel techs are automotive repair and maintenance and local government.

Location:

Salaries for diesel technicians can also vary widely based on location. For example, diesel techs in Alaska, Hawaii, and the District of Columbia tend to earn the highest salaries, while those in West Virginia, Mississippi, and Arkansas tend to earn the lowest.

Experience and Education:

As with many careers, experience and education can play a significant role in a diesel tech's salary. Diesel technicians with more certifications and years of experience tend to earn higher salaries than those with less experience.

Personal Experience:

I found that investing in additional certifications and training helped to increase my earning potential as a diesel technician. By staying up-to-date with the latest industry trends and technologies, I was able to position myself as a valuable asset to my employer, which ultimately led to higher pay and better benefits.

Question and Answer:

Q: What is the average hourly wage for a diesel technician?

A: As of May 2020, the median hourly wage for diesel technicians was $24.13.

Q: How much does a diesel technician with 5 years of experience typically make?

A: The salary for a diesel technician with 5 years of experience can vary based on several factors, but the average salary is around $58,000 per year.

Q: What is the job outlook for diesel technicians?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of diesel service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 3 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: How do I become a diesel technician?

A: To become a diesel technician, you typically need a high school diploma or equivalent, as well as some formal training in diesel technology. Some employers may also require certification from organizations such as the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE).

Conclusion of How Much Does a Diesel Tech Make a Year

Overall, the salary for a diesel technician can vary widely based on several factors, including location, industry, and experience. However, with the right skills and certifications, becoming a diesel tech can be a lucrative career choice with plenty of opportunities for growth and advancement.
