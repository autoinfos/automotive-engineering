How Much Does An Entry Level Diesel Mechanic Make

Written by Robby Sep 09, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an entry level diesel mechanic? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make in this field. While diesel mechanics are in high demand, the pay scale can vary depending on a number of factors. In this post, we will take a closer look at how much an entry level diesel mechanic can expect to make, as well as other important considerations for those interested in this career path.

One of the biggest pain points for entry level diesel mechanics is the starting salary. While the pay can be quite competitive once you have a few years of experience under your belt, starting out can be challenging. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $48,500 as of May 2020. However, this number can vary widely depending on factors such as location, industry, and level of experience.

As an entry level diesel mechanic, you can expect to make less than the median wage. According to Glassdoor, the average base salary for an entry level diesel mechanic is around $37,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on the employer and the location.

In summary, as an entry level diesel mechanic, you can expect to make around $37,000 per year. However, this number can vary depending on a variety of factors. It is important to do your research and understand the pay scale in your area before pursuing a career in this field.

What Factors Affect Entry Level Diesel Mechanic Salaries?

Location is one of the biggest factors that can impact entry level diesel mechanic salaries. In general, salaries are higher in areas where there is a high demand for diesel mechanics, such as large cities or industrial areas. Employers may also pay more for diesel mechanics who have specialized skills, such as experience working with specific types of vehicles or equipment.

Another factor that can impact salaries is the type of industry in which you work. For example, diesel mechanics who work in the transportation industry may earn more than those who work in construction or agriculture. Additionally, diesel mechanics who work for the government or for large corporations may earn more than those who work for smaller companies.

My Experience as an Entry Level Diesel Mechanic

When I first started out as an entry level diesel mechanic, I was making around $35,000 per year. However, I was lucky enough to live in an area where there was a high demand for diesel mechanics, so I was able to negotiate a higher starting salary. Over time, as I gained more experience and developed specialized skills, my salary increased significantly. Today, I am making well above the median wage for diesel mechanics.

How Can You Increase Your Salary as an Entry Level Diesel Mechanic?

If you want to increase your salary as an entry level diesel mechanic, there are several things you can do. First, consider pursuing additional training or certifications that will make you more valuable to employers. Additionally, look for job opportunities in industries or areas where there is a high demand for diesel mechanics. Finally, consider negotiating your starting salary to ensure that you are being paid fairly for your skills and experience.

The Pros and Cons of Pursuing a Career as an Entry Level Diesel Mechanic

While there are many benefits to pursuing a career as an entry level diesel mechanic, there are also some downsides to consider. On the positive side, diesel mechanics are in high demand, which means that there are often many job opportunities available. Additionally, the pay can be quite competitive once you have a few years of experience under your belt.

However, there are also some downsides to consider. For example, the work can be physically demanding and may require you to work long hours. Additionally, there is often a steep learning curve when starting out, which can be challenging for those who are new to the field.

How to Get Started as an Entry Level Diesel Mechanic

If you are interested in pursuing a career as an entry level diesel mechanic, there are several steps you can take to get started. First, consider enrolling in a diesel mechanics program at a vocational school or community college. These programs will provide you with the training and skills you need to get started in this field.

Additionally, look for job opportunities in your area and consider reaching out to local employers to inquire about entry level positions. Finally, be prepared to work hard and be patient as you learn the ropes of this challenging and rewarding career.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Entry Level Diesel Mechanic Make

As an entry level diesel mechanic, you can expect to make around $37,000 per year. However, this number can vary depending on a variety of factors, including location, industry, and level of experience. If you are interested in pursuing a career in this field, it is important to do your research and understand the pay scale in your area. Additionally, consider pursuing additional training or certifications to increase your value to employers and negotiate your starting salary to ensure that you are being paid fairly for your skills and experience.
