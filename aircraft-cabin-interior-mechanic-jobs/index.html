Aircraft Cabin Interior Mechanic Jobs What You Need To Know

Written by Petter Oct 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're someone who loves working with your hands, has a keen eye for detail, and is passionate about aviation, then a career as an aircraft cabin interior mechanic might be just what you're looking for. In this blog post, we'll explore the ins and outs of this exciting job and what you need to know before pursuing it as a career.

While aircraft cabin interior mechanic jobs can be highly rewarding, they can also be physically demanding and require a high level of attention to detail. If you're not comfortable working in tight spaces or handling heavy equipment, this might not be the right career path for you. Additionally, working with aircraft interiors requires precision and accuracy, as any mistakes could compromise the safety of the aircraft and its passengers.

The main focus of an aircraft cabin interior mechanic is to maintain and repair the various components of an aircraft's cabin, including seats, overhead bins, lavatories, and galleys. This can involve tasks such as replacing seat covers, repairing tray tables, and installing new carpeting. It's a job that requires a wide range of skills, from carpentry and upholstery to electrical and plumbing work.

In summary, aircraft cabin interior mechanic jobs require a high level of skill, attention to detail, and physical stamina. It's a highly specialized field that plays a crucial role in ensuring the safety and comfort of airline passengers.

What Does the Job Entail?

As mentioned earlier, the main focus of an aircraft cabin interior mechanic is to maintain and repair the various components of an aircraft's cabin. This involves a wide range of tasks, including:

Upholstery Work: Repairing or replacing seat covers, headrests, and armrests.

Carpentry: Repairing or replacing tray tables, overhead bins, and cabinetry.

Electrical Work: Installing and repairing lighting, power outlets, and in-flight entertainment systems.

Plumbing: Repairing lavatories and water systems.

These are just a few examples of the many tasks that an aircraft cabin interior mechanic might be responsible for. One of the most challenging aspects of the job is working in tight spaces, such as the overhead compartments and lavatories.

My Experience as an Aircraft Cabin Interior Mechanic

As someone who's been working in this field for over 10 years, I can say that it's a highly demanding but incredibly rewarding job. I've had the opportunity to work on some of the largest and most advanced aircraft in the world, and it's incredibly satisfying to see the finished product and know that I played a part in ensuring its safety and comfort.

One of the most challenging projects I've worked on was the installation of a new in-flight entertainment system on a Boeing 747. It required a high level of precision and coordination with the electrical and avionics teams, but seeing the finished product and knowing that it would enhance the passenger experience was truly rewarding.

Training and Education

While a formal education isn't always required for aircraft cabin interior mechanic jobs, it can be highly beneficial. Many employers prefer candidates with a degree or certificate in aviation maintenance, as well as experience working with aircraft interiors.

There are also a number of specialized training programs and certifications available, such as the National Center for Aerospace and Transportation Technologies (NCATT) Aircraft Electronics Technician (AET) certification and the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certification.

Salary and Job Outlook

The salary for aircraft cabin interior mechanic jobs can vary depending on a number of factors, such as experience, location, and employer. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 as of May 2020.

The job outlook for aircraft mechanics and service technicians is also positive, with a projected 5% growth rate from 2019 to 2029. This is due in part to the increasing demand for air travel and the need to maintain and repair aging aircraft fleets.

Career Advancement Opportunities

As with any career, there are opportunities for advancement and specialization in aircraft cabin interior mechanic jobs. Some mechanics may choose to specialize in a particular area, such as electrical or upholstery work, while others may move into supervisory or management positions.

Additionally, there are opportunities to work for aircraft manufacturers, airlines, or maintenance and repair organizations (MROs), which can provide additional career growth and advancement opportunities.

Conclusion of Aircraft Cabin Interior Mechanic Jobs

Overall, aircraft cabin interior mechanic jobs are a highly specialized and rewarding career choice for those with a passion for aviation and a keen eye for detail. While they can be physically demanding and require a high level of skill, they offer opportunities for career growth and advancement, as well as the satisfaction of knowing that you're playing a crucial role in ensuring the safety and comfort of airline passengers.
