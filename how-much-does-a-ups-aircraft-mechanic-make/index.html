How Much Does A Ups Aircraft Mechanic Make

Written by William Oct 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a UPS aircraft mechanic? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to earn in this field. While there are many factors that can impact your salary as an aircraft mechanic at UPS, there are some general guidelines that can help you get a better understanding of what you can expect.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, aircraft mechanics and service technicians earned an average annual salary of $66,680 as of May 2020. However, this figure can vary widely based on a number of factors, including your level of experience, the location where you work, and the type of aircraft you are working on.

If you are just starting out as an aircraft mechanic at UPS, you can expect to earn an entry-level salary of around $42,000 per year. However, as you gain more experience in the field, your salary can increase significantly, with some experienced aircraft mechanics earning upwards of $100,000 per year.

In summary, the salary you can expect to earn as an aircraft mechanic at UPS can vary greatly depending on a number of factors. However, with experience and expertise in the field, you can expect to earn a competitive salary in this rewarding career.

What is the Average Salary for a UPS Aircraft Mechanic?

When I first started working as an aircraft mechanic at UPS, I was making an entry-level salary of around $42,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and expertise in the field, my salary increased significantly.

What Factors Impact an Aircraft Mechanic's Salary at UPS?

There are a number of factors that can impact an aircraft mechanic's salary at UPS. Some of the most important factors include:

• Level of experience
• Location of work
• Type of aircraft worked on
• Union agreements
• Specializations and certifications held
• Performance and productivity levels

As you gain more experience and expertise in the field, your salary as an aircraft mechanic at UPS is likely to increase. Additionally, specializations and certifications in specific areas of aircraft mechanics can also lead to higher salaries.

Level of Experience

One of the most important factors that can impact an aircraft mechanic's salary at UPS is their level of experience. Entry-level aircraft mechanics can expect to earn a lower salary of around $42,000 per year, while senior aircraft mechanics with years of experience can earn upwards of $100,000 per year.

Location of Work

The location where an aircraft mechanic works can also impact their salary at UPS. For example, aircraft mechanics working in cities with higher costs of living may earn higher salaries to compensate for the increased cost of living. Additionally, aircraft mechanics working in areas with high demand for their services may also earn higher salaries.
