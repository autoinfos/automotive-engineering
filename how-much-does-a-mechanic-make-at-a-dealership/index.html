How Much Does A Mechanic Make At A Dealership

Written by Petter Jun 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanic at a dealership? If so, you may be wondering how much money you can expect to make. The good news is that the earning potential for mechanics at dealerships can be quite lucrative. However, it's important to understand the factors that can impact your salary and to set realistic expectations.

As a mechanic at a dealership, you may face some challenges when it comes to earning a high salary. For example, some dealerships may have a fixed pay scale that doesn't leave much room for negotiation. Additionally, if you're just starting out in your career as a mechanic, you may need to work your way up to higher paying positions over time.

So, how much can you expect to make as a mechanic at a dealership? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for mechanics working in the automotive repair and maintenance industry was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this figure can vary widely based on a number of factors, including your level of experience, your location, and the type of dealership you work for.

In general, mechanics who work for luxury car dealerships or dealerships located in affluent areas may be able to command higher salaries due to the higher-end vehicles they work on and the clientele they serve. Additionally, some dealerships may offer bonuses or commission-based pay structures that can significantly increase your earning potential.

My Experience Working as a Mechanic at a Dealership

When I started my career as a mechanic at a dealership, I was making around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and became more skilled in my trade, my salary gradually increased. After about 5 years on the job, I was making closer to $60,000 per year, thanks in part to a commission-based pay structure that rewarded me for completing jobs quickly and efficiently.

Factors That Can Impact Your Salary as a Mechanic at a Dealership

As mentioned earlier, there are a number of factors that can impact your earning potential as a mechanic at a dealership. Some of these factors include:

  • Your level of experience
  • The type of vehicles you work on
  • The location of the dealership
  • The pay structure offered by the dealership

The Importance of Continuing Education

If you want to increase your earning potential as a mechanic at a dealership, it's important to stay up-to-date with the latest automotive technologies and repair techniques. Consider taking additional training courses or pursuing certifications in areas such as hybrid vehicle repair or advanced engine diagnostics.

Negotiating Your Salary as a Mechanic at a Dealership

If you're looking to negotiate a higher salary as a mechanic at a dealership, it's important to come prepared with research and data to support your case. Look up the average salaries for mechanics in your area or for the specific type of dealership you're interested in working for. Additionally, be prepared to demonstrate your value to the dealership and provide examples of how you have contributed to the success of your current or previous employers.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Mechanic Make at a Dealership

While there is no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how much a mechanic can make at a dealership, it's clear that there is significant earning potential in this field. By staying up-to-date with the latest technologies, pursuing additional training and certifications, and negotiating for fair pay, you can position yourself for a successful and rewarding career as a mechanic at a dealership.

A: Entry-level mechanics at dealerships may make around $30,000 to $35,000 per year, depending on their location and the dealership's pay structure.

A: Yes, some dealerships offer bonuses or commission-based pay structures that can significantly increase a mechanic's earning potential.
