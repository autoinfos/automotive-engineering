How To Get Ase Certified Mechanic A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia May 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming an ASE certified mechanic? If you're passionate about cars and enjoy working with your hands, then this could be the perfect career for you. But, how do you become ASE certified? What are the requirements? And, why should you consider becoming certified in the first place? In this post, we'll answer all of these questions and more.

One of the biggest pain points for people who want to become ASE certified is understanding all of the requirements. There are many different exams that you must pass, and each one has its own set of prerequisites. Additionally, the exams are only offered during certain times of the year, so you need to plan accordingly. It can be overwhelming to navigate all of these requirements on your own.

The first step in becoming an ASE certified mechanic is to gain some hands-on experience in the field. You'll need to have at least two years of work experience in automotive repair before you can even take the exams. This experience can be gained through a vocational school, an apprenticeship, or working in a repair shop. Once you have the required experience, you can start preparing for the exams.

To become ASE certified, you'll need to pass one or more of the exams offered by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). There are over 40 different exams to choose from, covering everything from brakes and steering to engine repair and electrical systems. Each exam consists of 50 to 75 multiple-choice questions, and you'll need to score at least 70% to pass.

Preparing for the Exams

Preparing for the ASE exams can be challenging, but it's essential if you want to pass. One of the best ways to prepare is to take a prep course through a trade school or community college. These courses are designed to help you review the material and get hands-on practice in the areas you'll be tested on. You can also purchase study guides and practice exams online, which can be a great way to supplement your learning.

When I was preparing for my ASE exams, I found it helpful to create flashcards for each of the topics I was studying. I would write the question on one side of the card and the answer on the other. This allowed me to review the material on-the-go and helped me retain the information better. I also made sure to take plenty of practice exams to get comfortable with the test format.

The Benefits of Becoming ASE Certified

So, why should you consider becoming ASE certified? For one, it's a great way to demonstrate your expertise and commitment to the field. Employers are more likely to hire technicians who are certified, as it shows that they have the knowledge and skills needed to perform the job. Additionally, ASE certification can lead to higher pay and better job opportunities, as well as opportunities for advancement.

Renewing Your Certification

Once you've passed your ASE exams and become certified, you'll need to renew your certification every five years. This involves taking a recertification exam or completing a certain number of continuing education credits. Continuing education can be earned through attending training courses, workshops, or conferences related to the automotive industry.

Conclusion

Becoming an ASE certified mechanic is a great way to advance your career and demonstrate your expertise in the field. While the process can be challenging, the benefits are well worth the effort. By gaining the required work experience and preparing for the exams, you can become a certified technician and enjoy all of the perks that come with it.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I take the ASE exams online?

A: No, the ASE exams must be taken in-person at a testing center.

Q: Are there any prerequisites for taking the ASE exams?

A: Yes, you must have at least two years of work experience in automotive repair before you can take the exams.

Q: How many different ASE exams are there?

A: There are over 40 different exams to choose from, covering various areas of automotive repair.

Q: How long does ASE certification last?

A: ASE certification must be renewed every five years.

Conclusion of How to Get ASE Certified Mechanic

Becoming an ASE certified mechanic is a great way to advance your career and demonstrate your expertise in the field. With the right preparation and experience, you can pass the exams and become certified. Not only will this open up new job opportunities and higher pay, but it will also give you a sense of pride and accomplishment in your career.
