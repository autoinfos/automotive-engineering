How Much Do Cad Techs Make

Written by William Sep 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a CAD tech but wondering if the salary is worth it? Well, you've come to the right place. In this post, we'll explore how much CAD techs typically make and what factors can influence their earnings.

One of the main pain points when it comes to pursuing a career as a CAD tech is uncertainty about the salary. After all, you want to make sure that the time and effort you invest in your education and training will pay off in the long run. It's natural to wonder whether you'll be able to earn a comfortable living as a CAD tech.

The good news is that CAD techs can earn a decent salary. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for drafters (which includes CAD techs) was $56,830 in May 2019. Of course, this figure can vary depending on a number of factors, such as your location, industry, and experience.

In summary, CAD techs can make a good living, with a median annual wage of $56,830. However, there are several factors that can affect your earnings.

What Factors Influence CAD Tech Salaries?

Now that we've established a baseline for how much CAD techs typically make, let's take a closer look at the factors that can influence their earnings. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

Location

Where you work can have a big impact on your salary as a CAD tech. For example, CAD techs in California typically earn higher salaries than those in other states. This is partly due to the high cost of living in California, which means that employers have to offer higher salaries to attract talent.

Industry

The industry you work in can also affect your salary as a CAD tech. For instance, CAD techs who work in the aerospace or automotive industries may earn more than those who work in the construction industry. This is because these industries tend to require more complex and specialized CAD work.

Experience

As with most careers, experience plays a role in determining your salary as a CAD tech. Generally speaking, the more experience you have, the more you can expect to earn. This is because experienced CAD techs are often able to take on more complex projects and work more efficiently, which can make them more valuable to employers.

My Personal Experience as a CAD Tech

As a CAD tech with several years of experience, I can confirm that the factors outlined above can have a significant impact on your salary. In my case, I've found that working in the aerospace industry has allowed me to earn a higher salary than I would have in other industries. Additionally, I've been able to negotiate higher salaries as I've gained more experience and taken on more responsibilities.

How Can You Increase Your Salary as a CAD Tech?

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as a CAD tech, there are a few steps you can take:

Get Certified

Obtaining a certification in a specific area of CAD (such as SolidWorks or AutoCAD) can make you more valuable to employers and increase your earning potential.

Specialize

Specializing in a specific area of CAD (such as aerospace or automotive) can also make you more valuable to employers and allow you to command a higher salary.

Stay Up-to-Date

Make sure you stay up-to-date with the latest CAD software and techniques. Employers value CAD techs who are knowledgeable and able to work efficiently.

Question and Answer

Q: Can CAD techs make more than the median annual wage?

A: Yes, CAD techs with specialized skills or experience can earn more than the median annual wage.

Q: What's the job outlook for CAD techs?

A: The job outlook for CAD techs is relatively stable, with a projected growth rate of 4% from 2019 to 2029.

Q: What education is required to become a CAD tech?

A: Most CAD techs have an associate's degree in drafting or a related field.

Q: Is CAD tech a good career choice?

A: Yes, CAD tech is a good career choice for people who enjoy working with technology and have an eye for detail.

Conclusion

In conclusion, CAD techs can make a good living, with a median annual wage of $56,830. However, a number of factors can influence their earnings, including location, industry, and experience. By staying up-to-date with the latest software and techniques, getting certified, and specializing in a specific area of CAD, you can increase your earning potential as a CAD tech.
