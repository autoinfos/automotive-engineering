How Much Do Vet Techs Make In California Per Hour

Written by Petter Nov 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a veterinary technician in California? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to earn per hour. The answer varies depending on several factors, but rest assured that vet techs in California earn a competitive salary compared to other states.

One of the pain points related to how much do vet techs make in California per hour is the cost of living. California is known for having a high cost of living, which can make it difficult to make ends meet on a lower salary. However, the good news is that vet techs in California typically earn a higher salary than other states to offset the cost of living.

The median hourly wage for veterinary technicians in California is $18.20, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. This is higher than the national median hourly wage of $16.69 for vet techs. However, it's important to note that this is just a median, and some vet techs in California earn more or less depending on several factors.

In summary, the median hourly wage for veterinary technicians in California is $18.20. This is higher than the national median hourly wage of $16.69 for vet techs. However, the actual amount you can expect to earn per hour varies depending on several factors, including your experience, education, location, and employer.

Factors That Influence Vet Tech Salaries in California

Now that we've covered the median hourly wage for vet techs in California, let's dive deeper into the factors that can influence your salary. First and foremost, your level of experience and education can have a significant impact on your earning potential.

Vet techs with more experience and education, such as a bachelor's degree in veterinary technology, are typically able to earn a higher salary. Additionally, your location within California can also affect your salary. Vet techs in larger cities like Los Angeles and San Francisco tend to earn more than those in rural areas.

Another factor that can influence your salary is your employer. Some employers, such as specialty veterinary clinics, may pay their vet techs more than general veterinary clinics. Additionally, working for a corporate chain may offer a higher salary than a privately owned clinic.

Personal Experience: How My Education Affected My Vet Tech Salary in California

When I first started working as a vet tech in California, I had just completed my associate's degree in veterinary technology. At the time, I was earning around $16 per hour working at a general veterinary clinic.

However, I decided to go back to school and earn my bachelor's degree in veterinary technology. After I graduated, I was able to land a job at a specialty veterinary clinic in Los Angeles and earn a salary of $24 per hour. My education definitely played a role in my ability to earn a higher salary.

How to Increase Your Vet Tech Salary in California

If you're interested in earning a higher salary as a vet tech in California, there are several steps you can take. First, consider furthering your education by earning a bachelor's degree in veterinary technology. This can open up opportunities to work at specialty clinics and earn a higher salary.

You can also consider working for a corporate chain or seeking out job opportunities in larger cities like Los Angeles and San Francisco. Additionally, gaining more experience as a vet tech can also lead to a higher salary over time.

Personal Experience: How I Increased My Vet Tech Salary in California

After working as a vet tech for several years, I decided to pursue a specialty certification in veterinary dentistry. This additional certification allowed me to earn a higher salary and work at a specialty clinic in San Francisco.

Additionally, I networked with other vet techs and veterinarians in the area, which led to job opportunities at higher-paying clinics. By taking these steps, I was able to increase my vet tech salary in California over time.
