How Much Do Computer Support Technicians Make

Written by William Oct 19, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you considering a career as a computer support technician? One question you might be asking yourself is: how much do computer support technicians make? The answer to that question is not cut-and-dried, as it can vary depending on several factors.

One of the main pain points related to this topic is the fear of entering a field that doesn't offer a competitive salary. It's important to know what kind of earning potential you can expect before investing time and money into a career path.

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for computer support specialists was $54,760 as of May 2020. However, this number can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and industry.

In summary, computer support technicians can expect to make a median annual wage of around $55,000, but this number can fluctuate based on various factors.

Experience and Education

One factor that can greatly impact a computer support technician's salary is their level of experience and education. Entry-level positions may offer lower salaries, while those with more experience and education may have higher earning potential.

For example, a computer support technician with a bachelor's degree in computer science may have a higher salary than someone with just a high school diploma and some technical training. Additionally, those with several years of experience in the field may be able to negotiate a higher salary.

Location

The location of a computer support technician can also play a role in their salary. Those working in metropolitan areas may have higher earning potential than those in rural areas. Cost of living can also impact earning potential, as areas with a higher cost of living may offer higher salaries to compensate.

For example, a computer support technician working in San Francisco, California may have a higher salary than someone working in a smaller city in the Midwest.

Industry

The industry in which a computer support technician works can also impact their salary. For example, those working in the finance or healthcare industries may have higher salaries than those in retail or hospitality.

It's important to research the earning potential in the specific industry you are interested in before pursuing a career as a computer support technician.

Certifications

Obtaining certifications in specific areas of computer support can also increase earning potential. For example, a technician with a CompTIA A+ certification may have a higher salary than someone without the certification.

It's important to stay up-to-date on the latest certifications and technologies in the field in order to increase earning potential.

Conclusion of How Much Do Computer Support Technicians Make

Overall, computer support technicians can expect to make a median annual wage of around $55,000, but this number can vary greatly depending on factors such as experience, location, industry, and certifications. It's important to do your research and stay up-to-date on the latest trends and technologies in the field in order to increase earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for a computer support technician?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for a computer support technician is $26.31 as of May 2020.

Q: How much does location impact a computer support technician's salary?

A: Location can greatly impact a computer support technician's salary. Those in metropolitan areas or areas with a higher cost of living may have higher earning potential.

Q: What kind of certifications can increase a computer support technician's earning potential?

A: Certifications such as CompTIA A+, Network+, and Security+ can increase a computer support technician's earning potential.

Q: Is it possible for a computer support technician to advance to higher-paying positions?

A: Yes, it is possible for a computer support technician to advance to higher-paying positions such as network and computer systems administrators or information security analysts.
