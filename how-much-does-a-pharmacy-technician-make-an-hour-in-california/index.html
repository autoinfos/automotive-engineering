How Much Does A Pharmacy Technician Make An Hour In California

Written by Oliver Aug 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in becoming a pharmacy technician in California? One of the most important things to consider is how much you can expect to earn in this career. In this article, we'll discuss the average hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in California, along with some personal experiences and tips for success in this field.

When it comes to the topic of how much does a pharmacy technician make an hour in California, there are a few pain points to consider. One common concern is the cost of living in California, which is generally higher than the national average. This can make it harder to make ends meet on a lower salary, especially if you're just starting out in your career.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in California is $22.06. This is higher than the national median wage of $16.26 per hour. However, it's important to note that this figure can vary depending on factors like location, experience, and education.

In summary, pharmacy technicians in California can expect to earn a median hourly wage of $22.06. This figure may vary depending on a range of factors, but it's generally higher than the national median wage for this occupation.

What Affects a Pharmacy Technician's Hourly Wage in California?

When it comes to how much does a pharmacy technician make an hour in California, there are several factors that can impact your earnings. One of the biggest factors is your level of experience. As you gain more experience in the field, you may be able to earn a higher hourly wage.

Another factor to consider is your education and training. In California, pharmacy technicians are not required to have a specific degree or certification, but many employers prefer candidates who have completed a pharmacy technician training program. This can help you stand out in a competitive job market and may lead to higher earnings.

Personal Experience: How I Increased My Pharmacy Technician Hourly Wage in California

When I first started working as a pharmacy technician in California, I was earning around $18 per hour. However, after a few years of experience and additional training, I was able to negotiate a higher hourly wage of $25. Here are some tips that helped me increase my earnings:

  • Take advantage of continuing education opportunities to stay up-to-date on the latest trends and technologies in the field.
  • Consider pursuing additional certifications or licenses, such as a Certified Pharmacy Technician (CPhT) credential.
  • Build strong relationships with your colleagues and supervisors to demonstrate your value to the team.
  • Be willing to take on additional responsibilities or tasks to show your dedication and work ethic.

The Role of Location in a Pharmacy Technician's Hourly Wage in California

Another factor to consider when it comes to how much does a pharmacy technician make an hour in California is your location. In general, pharmacy technicians who work in urban areas or larger cities may be able to earn a higher hourly wage than those in smaller towns or rural areas.

However, it's also important to consider the cost of living in your area. Even if you're earning a higher hourly wage, you may need to spend more on housing, transportation, and other expenses to maintain your standard of living.

Education and Training Requirements for Pharmacy Technicians in California

As mentioned earlier, California does not have specific education or training requirements for pharmacy technicians. However, many employers prefer candidates who have completed a pharmacy technician training program or who have earned a certification such as the CPhT credential.

These programs can provide you with the skills and knowledge you need to succeed in this field, and may also help you stand out in a competitive job market. Some common topics covered in pharmacy technician training programs include medication safety, pharmacology, and pharmacy law and ethics.

Tips for Finding a High-Paying Pharmacy Technician Job in California

If you're interested in maximizing your earnings as a pharmacy technician in California, there are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Research potential employers to find companies that offer competitive hourly wages and benefits packages.
  • Network with other pharmacy technicians and healthcare professionals to learn about job openings and career opportunities.
  • Consider working for a larger healthcare organization, such as a hospital or retail pharmacy chain, which may offer more opportunities for advancement.
  • Be willing to relocate to a higher-paying area if necessary, but make sure to consider the cost of living in your new location.
