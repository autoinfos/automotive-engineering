How To Drive An Automatic Car In Uae A Beginner s Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you new to driving an automatic car in UAE? Do you feel a little overwhelmed or intimidated? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people feel the same way when they first start driving an automatic car in UAE. But with a little practice and some helpful tips, you'll be driving smoothly in no time.

Driving an automatic car in UAE can be challenging if you're used to driving a manual car. However, the good news is that automatic cars are much easier to drive. You don't have to worry about shifting gears or using a clutch pedal, which can be a relief for many drivers.

If you're new to driving an automatic car in UAE, the first thing you need to do is familiarize yourself with the car's controls. Unlike manual cars, automatic cars have only two pedals - the accelerator and the brake. To move the car forward, you simply put your foot on the brake pedal and shift the gear from "P" to "D". Then, gently press the accelerator pedal to move forward. To stop the car, simply release the accelerator pedal and press the brake pedal.

In summary, here are some key points to keep in mind when driving an automatic car in UAE:

Understanding the Gear Shift

The gear shift in an automatic car in UAE is located on the center console between the front seats. To shift gears, you simply move the gear shift from "P" to "D" or "R" (reverse). Make sure to come to a complete stop before shifting gears, and always use the parking brake when parked.

Tips for Smooth Driving

When driving an automatic car in UAE, it's important to be smooth and gentle with the accelerator and brake pedals. Avoid sudden or jerky movements, as this can be uncomfortable for your passengers and can also wear down the car's brakes and tires. Remember to keep a safe distance from other cars on the road, and always follow traffic rules and regulations.

How to Handle Hills and Inclines

Driving up hills or inclines can be a challenge for some drivers, especially if they're new to driving an automatic car in UAE. To make it easier, you can shift the gear to "L" or "2" to help the car climb the hill. Conversely, when driving down hills, you can shift to "D" or "4" to help slow the car down.

Maintaining the Car

Finally, it's important to take good care of your automatic car in UAE to ensure that it runs smoothly and safely. This includes regular oil changes, tire rotations, and other maintenance tasks. If you're not comfortable performing these tasks yourself, take your car to a reputable mechanic or auto shop for professional service.

Personal Experience

When I first started driving an automatic car in UAE, I was a little nervous. I had always driven manual cars in the past, and I wasn't sure what to expect. However, after a few minutes of practice, I quickly got the hang of it. I found that driving an automatic car in UAE was much easier than driving a manual car, and I appreciated the convenience of not having to shift gears all the time.

Common Questions About Driving an Automatic Car in UAE

1. Do I need a special license to drive an automatic car in UAE?

No, you don't need a special license to drive an automatic car in UAE. However, you do need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.

2. How do I park an automatic car in UAE?

To park an automatic car in UAE, simply shift the gear to "P" and engage the parking brake. Make sure to park in a safe and legal spot.

3. Can I use my left foot to brake in an automatic car in UAE?

No, it's not recommended to use your left foot to brake in an automatic car in UAE. This can lead to confusion and can also be dangerous, as it can cause the car to accelerate unintentionally.

4. How do I drive an automatic car in UAE in heavy traffic?

When driving an automatic car in UAE in heavy traffic, it's important to remain calm and patient. Avoid sudden movements or aggressive driving, and always follow traffic rules and regulations. Keep a safe distance from other cars on the road, and be prepared to brake or stop if necessary.

Conclusion

Driving an automatic car in UAE may seem daunting at first, but with a little practice and some helpful tips, you'll be driving like a pro in no time. Remember to be patient, stay calm, and follow traffic rules and regulations at all times. Happy driving!
