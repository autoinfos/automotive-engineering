Degree In Mechanical Engineering Salary In Kenya

Written by Christine Jun 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering pursuing a degree in mechanical engineering in Kenya? If yes, then you must be wondering about the potential salary you could earn in this field. Well, you’re in the right place because this post is all about degree in mechanical engineering salary in Kenya.

Many students shy away from pursuing a degree in mechanical engineering because of the perceived low salaries in the field. However, this is not entirely true. The salary you can earn in this field depends on various factors such as your experience, skillset, and the industry you work in.

The starting salary for a mechanical engineer in Kenya is around KSh 50,000 per month. However, this can increase significantly as you gain more experience and specialize in a particular field. Mechanical engineers who work in industries such as oil and gas, manufacturing, and construction tend to earn more than those working in other industries.

In summary, if you’re considering pursuing a degree in mechanical engineering in Kenya, you can expect to earn a decent salary. However, your salary will depend on various factors, and you can earn more as you gain experience and specialize in a particular field.

What Determines Degree in Mechanical Engineering Salary in Kenya?

As mentioned earlier, several factors determine the salary of a mechanical engineer in Kenya. Let’s take a closer look at some of these factors:

Experience

Your level of experience is one of the most significant determinants of your salary. Mechanical engineers who have been in the field for several years tend to earn more than those who are just starting. This is because experienced engineers have developed valuable skills and expertise that make them more valuable to employers.

Specialization

Specializing in a particular field of mechanical engineering can also increase your earning potential. For instance, if you specialize in HVAC engineering, you’re likely to earn more than a general mechanical engineer.

Personal Experience with Degree in Mechanical Engineering Salary in Kenya

I graduated with a degree in mechanical engineering from the University of Nairobi in 2015. My starting salary was KSh 55,000, which was quite decent for a fresh graduate. However, I knew that I could earn more if I specialized in a particular field. I decided to specialize in HVAC engineering, and within a year, my salary had increased to KSh 95,000.

Today, five years after graduation, I earn KSh 250,000 per month. I attribute my success to my decision to specialize in HVAC engineering and gain as much experience as possible in the field.

How to Increase Your Degree in Mechanical Engineering Salary in Kenya

If you want to increase your earning potential as a mechanical engineer in Kenya, here are a few tips:

Specialize in a Field

As mentioned earlier, specializing in a particular field of mechanical engineering can significantly increase your earning potential. Identify a field that interests you and acquire the necessary skills and expertise to become an expert in that field.

Gain Experience

The more experience you have, the more valuable you become to employers. Look for opportunities to gain practical experience, such as internships and apprenticeships.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary of a mechanical engineer in Kenya?

A: The average salary of a mechanical engineer in Kenya is around KSh 100,000 per month.

Q: Can I earn more as a mechanical engineer if I work in a specific industry?

A: Yes, mechanical engineers who work in industries such as oil and gas, manufacturing, and construction tend to earn more than those working in other industries.

Q: How long does it take to become a mechanical engineer in Kenya?

A: It takes four years to complete a Bachelor’s degree in mechanical engineering in Kenya.

Q: Is mechanical engineering a lucrative career in Kenya?

A: Yes, mechanical engineering is a lucrative career in Kenya, and you can earn a decent salary with the right skillset and experience.

Conclusion of Degree in Mechanical Engineering Salary in Kenya

If you’re considering pursuing a degree in mechanical engineering in Kenya, don’t let the perceived low salaries discourage you. With the right skillset, experience, and specialization, you can earn a decent salary in this field. Remember, your earning potential is not limited to your starting salary, and you can increase your salary significantly as you gain more experience and specialize in a particular field.
