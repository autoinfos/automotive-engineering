How To Write A Cover Letter In Resume Tips For Relaxed English Language

Written by Robby Sep 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you struggling with how to write a cover letter in resume that's both professional and easy to understand? Writing a cover letter is a crucial part of any job application, but it can be challenging to strike the right balance between formality and approachability. In this article, we'll give you some tips on how to write a cover letter in resume that showcases your personality and skills while still presenting you as a qualified candidate.

Understanding the Pain Points of Writing a Cover Letter in Resume

One of the biggest pain points of writing a cover letter in resume is figuring out how to convey your qualifications without sounding too formal or stuffy. Many job seekers struggle with finding the right tone for their cover letters, especially if they're not used to writing in a professional context. Additionally, it can be challenging to know how to highlight your skills and accomplishments without simply repeating what's in your resume.

Tips for Writing a Cover Letter in Resume

The key to writing a successful cover letter in resume is to strike a balance between professionalism and approachability. Here are some tips to help you achieve this:

1. Start with a Hook

The first sentence of your cover letter should grab the reader's attention and make them want to keep reading. Consider starting with a personal anecdote or a surprising fact about your industry to pique their interest.

Example:

"As a lifelong lover of animals, I was thrilled to see your opening for a veterinary technician. With over five years of experience in the field, I'm confident that I have the skills and passion to thrive in this role."

2. Use Relaxed Language

While it's important to maintain a professional tone in your cover letter, you don't want to come across as stiff or robotic. Try to use language that's clear and concise, but also conversational and easy to understand. Avoid using overly complex words or industry jargon that might confuse the reader.

Example:

"I've always been passionate about helping others, which is why I pursued a career in social work. Throughout my career, I've had the opportunity to work with a wide range of clients, from children to seniors, and I've learned how to adapt my approach to meet their unique needs."

3. Highlight Your Skills and Accomplishments

Your cover letter is an opportunity to showcase your qualifications and convince the reader that you're the best candidate for the job. Be sure to highlight specific skills and accomplishments that are relevant to the position you're applying for, and explain how they demonstrate your ability to succeed in the role.

Example:

"In my previous role as a marketing coordinator, I was responsible for managing social media campaigns that resulted in a 25% increase in website traffic. I'm confident that my experience in social media marketing, combined with my strong communication skills, make me a great fit for this social media manager position."

4. End with a Call to Action

Finally, be sure to end your cover letter with a clear call to action that encourages the reader to take the next step. Whether it's scheduling an interview or simply following up with you, make it clear what you want them to do next.

Example:

"Thank you for considering my application. I'm excited about the opportunity to join your team and contribute my skills and experience to your organization. Please don't hesitate to contact me if you have any questions or if you'd like to schedule an interview."

Question and Answer

Q: Should I include my salary requirements in my cover letter?

A: It's generally not recommended to include your salary requirements in your cover letter, as this can come across as presumptuous. Instead, focus on highlighting your qualifications and why you're a good fit for the position. If the employer is interested in your application, they'll likely ask about your salary expectations during the interview process.

Q: How long should my cover letter be?

A: Ideally, your cover letter should be no more than one page in length. Be sure to focus on the most important information and keep your writing concise and to the point.

Q: Should I address my cover letter to a specific person?

A: Whenever possible, it's a good idea to address your cover letter to a specific person rather than using a generic greeting like "To Whom It May Concern." This shows that you've done your research and are specifically interested in the company and position you're applying for.

Q: Can I use bullet points in my cover letter?

A: While bullet points can be an effective way to highlight your qualifications, they're generally not recommended for use in a cover letter. This is because cover letters are typically more narrative in nature and are meant to tell a story about your qualifications and experience.

Conclusion of How to Write a Cover Letter in Resume

Writing a cover letter in resume can be challenging, but with these tips, you'll be able to create a document that showcases your skills and personality while still presenting you as a qualified candidate. Remember to focus on striking the right balance between professionalism and approachability, and to always end with a clear call to action that encourages the reader to take the next step.
