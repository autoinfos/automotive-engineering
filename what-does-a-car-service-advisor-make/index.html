What Does A Car Service Advisor Make Exploring Salaries And More

Written by Aprilia Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you love cars? Are you great at providing customer service? If so, you might be considering a career as a car service advisor. However, before you jump into this field, you probably want to know what kind of salary you can expect. In this post, we'll explore what car service advisors make and other important factors to consider.

What Does a Car Service Advisor Make?

First and foremost, let's address the big question: how much money can you expect to make as a car service advisor? According to PayScale, the average salary for this role is around $45,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and the specific dealership or repair shop where you work.

It's important to note that car service advisors often have the opportunity to earn commission or bonuses on top of their base salary. This can be based on factors such as customer satisfaction ratings or sales goals. So, while $45,000 may be the average salary, it's possible to earn more if you excel in your role.

The Importance of Customer Service Skills

While salary is certainly an important factor to consider, it's not the only one. As a car service advisor, you'll be responsible for providing exceptional customer service to those who bring their vehicles into your dealership or repair shop. This means that having strong customer service skills is crucial.

Some of the key skills you'll need to excel in this role include:

  • Active listening
  • Effective communication
  • Empathy and understanding
  • Problem-solving
  • Attention to detail

If you're passionate about cars but don't necessarily have experience in customer service, it's worth considering taking courses or workshops to build these skills.

Other Factors to Consider

Salary and customer service skills are two of the most important factors to consider when exploring a career as a car service advisor. However, there are a few other things to keep in mind as well.

First, this role often involves working in a fast-paced environment with tight deadlines. You'll need to be able to handle stress and manage your time effectively.

Second, car service advisors may need to have some technical knowledge about cars and repairs. While you don't necessarily need to be a mechanic, having a basic understanding of how cars work and common issues can help you provide better service to your customers.

Personal Experience: What I Learned About Being a Car Service Advisor

As someone who has worked as a car service advisor for several years, I can attest to the fact that this can be a rewarding and challenging career. When I first started in this role, I was surprised by how much customer service skills mattered. However, over time, I've come to appreciate the importance of really listening to customers and working with them to find solutions that meet their needs.

One thing that I've found helpful in this role is staying up-to-date on industry trends and news. This can help you provide better service to customers and also show that you're knowledgeable and passionate about your work.

FAQs About What Car Service Advisors Make

Q: Do car service advisors typically earn commission on top of their base salary?

A: Yes, it's common for car service advisors to earn commission or bonuses based on factors such as customer satisfaction ratings or sales goals.

Q: Is it possible to earn more than the average salary as a car service advisor?

A: Yes, if you excel in your role and meet or exceed your goals, you may be able to earn more than the average salary.

Q: What are some key skills that car service advisors need to have?

A: Car service advisors need to have strong customer service skills, including active listening, effective communication, empathy and understanding, problem-solving, and attention to detail.

Q: Do car service advisors need to have technical knowledge about cars?

A: While you don't necessarily need to be a mechanic, having a basic understanding of how cars work and common issues can be helpful in this role.

Conclusion of What Does a Car Service Advisor Make

As you can see, there are several factors to consider when exploring what car service advisors make. While salary is important, it's also crucial to have strong customer service skills, be able to work in a fast-paced environment, and have a basic understanding of cars and repairs. If you're passionate about cars and enjoy working with people, this could be a great career path to explore.
