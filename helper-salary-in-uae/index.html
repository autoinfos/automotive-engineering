Helper Salary In Uae A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you planning to hire a helper in UAE but unsure about the salary rates? Or are you a helper yourself looking for insights about the current rates? Look no further because this article will provide you with a comprehensive guide on helper salary in UAE.

One of the pain points related to helper salary in UAE is the lack of standardization. The rates may vary depending on several factors such as nationality, experience, and job scope. This makes it difficult for both employers and helpers to negotiate and agree on a fair salary.

The target of this article is to provide a clear and updated overview of the current salary rates for helpers in UAE. This will help employers and helpers alike to have a better understanding of the market and make informed decisions when it comes to salary negotiations.

To summarize, this article will cover the following main points:

  • Overview of helper job scope in UAE
  • Factors affecting helper salary rates in UAE
  • Current salary rates for helpers in UAE
  • Tips for negotiating a fair salary

Understanding Helper Salary in UAE

Helper salary in UAE is an important topic to discuss, especially for those who are planning to hire one or work as one. As a former employer of a helper in UAE, I have experienced the challenges of negotiating a fair salary. It is important to note that the salary rates may vary depending on several factors.

One of the factors that affect helper salary rates in UAE is nationality. Helpers from certain countries may have higher or lower rates compared to others. For example, Filipino helpers are known to have higher rates due to their experience and skills.

Experience is another factor that affects helper salary rates. Helpers with more years of experience may command a higher salary compared to those who are just starting out. This is because experienced helpers are expected to have a better understanding of their job scope and can perform their duties more efficiently.

Job scope is also a factor that affects helper salary rates. The more complex the job, the higher the salary may be. For example, a helper who is also a driver may have a higher salary compared to a helper who only performs household chores.

Tips for Negotiating a Fair Salary

Negotiating a fair salary for a helper in UAE can be challenging, but there are tips that can help employers and helpers alike:

  1. Do research on the current market rates to have a better understanding of the salary range.
  2. Consider the helper's experience, nationality, and job scope when negotiating the salary.
  3. Be open to compromise and discuss other benefits such as food and accommodation.
  4. Put everything in writing to avoid misunderstandings in the future.

Current Salary Rates for Helpers in UAE

Based on recent data, the minimum salary for a helper in UAE is AED 1,100 per month. However, the actual rates may vary depending on the helper's nationality, experience, and job scope.

For example, Filipino helpers may have a minimum salary of AED 1,500 per month while Indonesian helpers may have a minimum salary of AED 1,200 per month. Helpers who are also drivers may have a higher salary ranging from AED 1,500 to AED 2,000 per month.

How to Ensure a Fair Salary for Helpers in UAE

Employers can ensure a fair salary for helpers in UAE by following these tips:

  1. Do research on the current market rates and factor in the helper's experience, nationality, and job scope when offering a salary.
  2. Be open to negotiation and consider other benefits such as food and accommodation.
  3. Put everything in writing to avoid misunderstandings and ensure that both parties are in agreement.

Conclusion of Helper Salary in UAE

Helper salary in UAE is an important topic that requires attention from both employers and helpers. By understanding the factors that affect salary rates and following the tips for negotiating a fair salary, both parties can have a successful working relationship. It is also important to ensure that everything is in writing to avoid misunderstandings in the future.

Question and Answer

Q: Can helpers in UAE work for more than one employer?

A: No, it is illegal for helpers in UAE to work for more than one employer. Violators may face fines and deportation.

Q: Are helpers entitled to a day off in UAE?

A: Yes, helpers in UAE are entitled to one day off per week. Employers should ensure that the helper's day off is respected and that they are not required to work on that day.

Q: Can employers deduct money from the helper's salary?

A: Employers are not allowed to deduct money from the helper's salary unless it is for a valid reason such as damages caused by the helper.

Q: Can helpers in UAE change employers?

A: Yes, helpers in UAE can change employers but they need to obtain a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from their current employer. This is to ensure that the helper has completed their contract and that the employer has no objections to the transfer.
