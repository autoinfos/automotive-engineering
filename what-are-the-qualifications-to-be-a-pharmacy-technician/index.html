What Are The Qualifications To Be A Pharmacy Technician

Written by Daniel Jul 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever considered becoming a pharmacy technician? This profession is in high demand and offers stable employment opportunities. However, before embarking on this career path, it is important to understand the qualifications required to become a pharmacy technician.

One of the main pain points related to what are the qualifications to be a pharmacy technician is that it can be confusing to determine what education and training is needed. Additionally, different states may have varying requirements, adding to the complexity of the process.

To become a pharmacy technician, one typically must have a high school diploma or equivalent. Additionally, most states require completion of a pharmacy technician training program, which can be completed at a community college or vocational school. Some states may also require passing a certification exam or obtaining a license.

In summary, to become a pharmacy technician, one needs a high school diploma or equivalent, completion of a pharmacy technician training program, and potentially passing a certification exam or obtaining a license.

Education and Training

The education and training required to become a pharmacy technician can vary depending on the state. Some states may require completion of an accredited program, while others may accept on-the-job training. It is important to research the specific requirements for the state in which you plan to work.

Personally, I completed a pharmacy technician training program at a community college. This program provided a combination of classroom instruction and hands-on experience in a pharmacy setting. It was a great way to learn the necessary skills and gain experience in the field.

Certification and Licensing

In addition to education and training, some states require certification or licensure to work as a pharmacy technician. Certification can be obtained through organizations such as the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB) or the National Healthcareer Association (NHA). Licensing requirements may vary by state, but typically involve passing an exam and completing continuing education requirements.

Personally, I obtained certification through the PTCB after completing my training program. It was a rigorous exam, but it was worth it to have the additional credential and show potential employers that I was knowledgeable and skilled in my field.

Skills and Qualities

In addition to education, training, and certification, there are certain skills and qualities that are important for success as a pharmacy technician. These include:

  • Attention to detail
  • Good communication skills
  • Customer service skills
  • Ability to work well in a team
  • Basic math skills

Personally, I find that attention to detail is one of the most important skills for a pharmacy technician. There is no room for error when it comes to dispensing medications, and it is important to be meticulous in checking prescriptions and ensuring that the correct medication and dosage are provided.

Continuing Education

Finally, it is important to note that continuing education is important for pharmacy technicians. The field of pharmacy is constantly evolving, and it is important to stay up-to-date on new medications and regulations.

Personally, I make a point to attend conferences and workshops to stay current in my field. It is also important to read industry publications and stay informed about any changes in regulations or best practices.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need a college degree to become a pharmacy technician?

A: No, a high school diploma or equivalent is typically all that is required. However, completion of a pharmacy technician training program is usually necessary.

Q: Is certification required to work as a pharmacy technician?

A: Certification requirements vary by state, but it is becoming more common for employers to require certification from organizations such as the PTCB or NHA.

Q: How long does it take to become a pharmacy technician?

A: The length of time it takes to become a pharmacy technician can vary depending on the training program and certification requirements. However, it is typically a relatively short process, ranging from several months to a year or two.

Q: What salary can I expect as a pharmacy technician?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for pharmacy technicians is $35,100. However, this can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and employer.

Conclusion of What Are the Qualifications to Be a Pharmacy Technician

Becoming a pharmacy technician can be a rewarding career path. However, it is important to understand the qualifications required to enter this field. By completing a pharmacy technician training program, obtaining certification or licensure, and developing the necessary skills and qualities, you can embark on a successful career as a pharmacy technician.
