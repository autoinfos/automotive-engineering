How Much Would A Mechanic Make A Year

Written by Luffy Nov 03, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to choosing a career, one of the most important factors to consider is salary. People want to know if they will be able to earn enough money to support themselves and their families. If you are considering becoming a mechanic, you may be wondering how much you could make per year. In this blog post, we will explore the answer to this question and provide some additional information about the earning potential of a mechanic.

Many people assume that mechanics do not make a lot of money, but this is not necessarily true. While some mechanics may not earn as much as others, the salary range for this profession can be quite broad. Additionally, there are many factors that can influence how much a mechanic makes, such as their level of experience, the type of vehicles they work on, and the location of their job.

According to the United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. This means that half of all mechanics in the United States earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. The highest 10 percent of mechanics earned more than $67,430 per year, while the lowest 10 percent earned less than $25,620 per year.

In summary, a mechanic's earning potential can vary widely based on many factors. Some mechanics may earn less than $25,000 per year, while others may earn more than $65,000 per year. However, the median salary for this profession is $44,050 per year.

Factors That Affect a Mechanic's Salary

Experience is one of the biggest factors that can influence how much a mechanic makes. Entry-level mechanics may earn less than those who have been working in the industry for several years. Additionally, mechanics who specialize in working on certain types of vehicles, such as diesel engines or electric cars, may be able to earn more money than those who work on standard gasoline engines.

Location can also have a big impact on a mechanic's salary. Mechanics who live in areas with a higher cost of living may be able to earn more money to offset their expenses. Additionally, mechanics who work for dealerships or other large companies may be able to earn more money than those who work for smaller, independent repair shops.

Education and Certification

While it is possible to become a mechanic without any formal education or certification, having these credentials can help increase a mechanic's earning potential. Many employers prefer to hire mechanics who have completed a training program or who hold certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE).

Specialization

As mentioned earlier, mechanics who specialize in working on certain types of vehicles may be able to earn more money than those who work on standard gasoline engines. This is because they have specialized knowledge and skills that are in high demand. Some popular specializations include working on diesel engines, electric cars, and hybrid vehicles.

Personal Experience

When I first started working as a mechanic, I was earning around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and started specializing in diesel engines, my salary increased to over $60,000 per year. I also moved to a new location where the cost of living was higher, which helped to increase my earning potential.

Future Outlook

According to the United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations. This means that there will continue to be a demand for skilled mechanics in the coming years, which could translate into higher salaries for those who have the necessary skills and experience.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a mechanic make per hour?
A: The median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $21.16 as of May 2020.

Q: What is the highest-paying state for mechanics?
A: The highest-paying state for automotive service technicians and mechanics is Alaska, with a mean annual wage of $56,350 as of May 2020.

Q: Do mechanics get paid overtime?
A: Yes, many mechanics are eligible for overtime pay if they work more than 40 hours per week.

Q: How much does a diesel mechanic make?
A: The median annual salary for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $50,200 as of May 2020.

Conclusion

In conclusion, a mechanic's salary can vary widely based on many factors, including their level of experience, the type of vehicles they work on, and the location of their job. While some mechanics may not earn as much as others, the median salary for this profession is $44,050 per year. Additionally, there are many opportunities for mechanics to increase their earning potential by specializing in certain areas, obtaining certification, and gaining more experience.
