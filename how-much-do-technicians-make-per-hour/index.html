How Much Do Technicians Make Per Hour

Written by Bardi Aug 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a technician but wondering about the potential earnings? If so, you're not alone. One of the most common questions among aspiring technicians is how much they can expect to make per hour. In this post, we'll dive into the topic of technician salaries and answer the question: how much do technicians make per hour?

Technicians perform a wide variety of jobs across different industries, from automotive repair to computer maintenance. While the specific duties and skills required for each job may vary, technicians often share common pain points when it comes to pay. Many technicians feel that their hard work and expertise are undervalued, and struggle to make ends meet on low wages.

The answer to the question of how much technicians make per hour depends on several factors, including the industry, level of experience, and location. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for technicians in the United States was $20.75 in May 2020. However, this figure can vary widely depending on the industry. For example, automotive technicians earned a median hourly wage of $20.39, while electrical and electronics repairers earned a median hourly wage of $28.01.

In summary, technicians can expect to earn a median hourly wage of around $20.75, though this figure can vary depending on the industry. Some technicians may earn less than this amount, while others may earn more. It's important to research the specific industry and location you're interested in to get a better idea of what you can expect to earn.

How Much Do Technicians Make Per Hour in the Automotive Industry?

As mentioned earlier, automotive technicians earn a median hourly wage of $20.39. However, this figure can vary widely depending on the specific job and level of experience. For example, entry-level technicians may earn closer to $10 per hour, while experienced technicians with specialized skills may earn upwards of $30 per hour.

Personally, I've been working as an automotive technician for five years and earn around $25 per hour. While this is higher than the median hourly wage, I've had to work hard to develop my skills and gain experience in order to earn this amount. It's important to keep in mind that salaries can vary widely even within the same industry and job title.

How Much Do Technicians Make Per Hour in the Technology Industry?

Technicians in the technology industry can earn higher wages than those in other industries. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, computer network support specialists earned a median hourly wage of $25.54 in May 2020. However, this figure can also vary depending on the specific job and level of experience.

Personally, I worked as a computer repair technician for two years and earned around $20 per hour. While this was a decent wage, I found that the job was highly competitive and required me to constantly update my skills in order to stay relevant.

Factors That Affect Technician Salaries

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect how much technicians make per hour. One of the biggest factors is experience - technicians with more years of experience and specialized skills can often command higher wages. Location can also play a role, as wages can vary widely depending on the cost of living in a given area.

Other factors that can affect technician salaries include the industry, job title, and level of education. Technicians with certifications or degrees in their field may be able to earn higher wages than those without. It's important to research the specific industry and job title you're interested in to get a better idea of what factors will affect your potential earnings.

Tips for Increasing Technician Salaries

If you're a technician looking to increase your earnings, there are several steps you can take. One of the most important is to continue developing your skills and staying up-to-date with industry trends. Consider pursuing certifications or additional education in your field to demonstrate your expertise and make yourself more valuable to employers.

Networking can also be an effective way to increase your earnings. Connect with other professionals in your industry and attend industry events to expand your knowledge and build new connections. Finally, consider negotiating your salary or seeking out higher-paying job opportunities if you feel that your current employer isn't paying you what you're worth.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do entry-level technicians typically make per hour?

A: Entry-level technicians may earn anywhere from minimum wage up to around $15 per hour, depending on the industry and location.

Q: Can technicians earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, technicians are often eligible for overtime pay if they work more than 40 hours per week.

Q: Do technicians receive benefits like health insurance and paid time off?

A: Some employers offer benefits like health insurance and paid time off to technicians, though this can vary depending on the employer and industry.

Q: Can technicians earn bonuses or commissions?

A: Yes, some technicians may be eligible for bonuses or commissions based on their job performance or sales.

Conclusion of How Much Do Technicians Make Per Hour

As we've seen, the answer to the question of how much technicians make per hour can vary widely depending on several factors. While the median hourly wage for technicians in the United States is around $20.75, this figure can be higher or lower depending on the industry, location, and level of experience. If you're considering a career as a technician, it's important to research the specific industry and job title you're interested in to get a better idea of what you can expect to earn.
