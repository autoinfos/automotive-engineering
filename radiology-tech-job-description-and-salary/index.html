Radiology Tech Job Description And Salary What You Need To Know

Written by Daniel Aug 26, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are interested in a career in healthcare that involves helping others and utilizing technology, becoming a radiologic technologist might be the perfect fit for you. However, before diving into this career path, it's important to understand the job description and salary expectations that come with it.

When it comes to pain points related to radiology tech job description and salary, many individuals may be concerned about the level of education and training required, as well as the potential financial compensation. Additionally, some may be unsure about what exactly the job entails and what types of settings they can work in.

So, what does a radiologic technologist do? In short, they use specialized equipment to take images of a patient's body for diagnostic purposes. This can include X-rays, CT scans, MRI scans, and more. Radiologic technologists must also be knowledgeable in radiation safety and patient care. In terms of salary, the median annual wage for this profession is $62,280, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Overall, a career as a radiologic technologist can offer a fulfilling and financially stable path in healthcare. To further break down the specifics of the job description and salary, let's take a closer look:

Job Description

As mentioned, radiologic technologists use imaging equipment to take pictures of a patient's body for diagnostic purposes. This involves preparing the patient for the procedure, positioning them correctly, and then operating the equipment to capture the necessary images. Radiologic technologists must also be able to recognize the difference between normal and abnormal images, and communicate effectively with other healthcare professionals.

Additionally, radiologic technologists must have a strong understanding of radiation safety and be able to take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their patients from potentially harmful radiation exposure. They must also be able to provide patient care, including answering questions, providing emotional support, and assisting with basic needs such as mobility.

Salary

The median annual wage for radiologic technologists is $62,280, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and type of employer. For example, those working in outpatient care centers may earn a higher wage than those working in hospitals. Additionally, those with advanced certifications or specializations may also earn a higher salary.

Education and Training

In order to become a radiologic technologist, individuals must typically earn an associate's degree in radiologic technology from an accredited program. This typically takes about two years to complete. Additionally, many states require licensure or certification in order to practice in this field. This may involve passing an exam and completing continuing education requirements.

Career Path

There are a variety of career paths available for radiologic technologists. Some may choose to specialize in a specific type of imaging, such as mammography or MRI. Others may choose to advance their education and become a radiologist assistant, which involves working closely with radiologists to provide specialized patient care and assist in procedures. Additionally, some radiologic technologists may choose to pursue a career in education or management.

Question and Answer

Q: Is there a high demand for radiologic technologists?

A: Yes, the Bureau of Labor Statistics predicts that employment of radiologic technologists will grow 7% from 2019 to 2029, which is faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: Can radiologic technologists work in different settings?

A: Yes, radiologic technologists can work in a variety of settings including hospitals, outpatient care centers, and diagnostic imaging centers.

Q: What types of skills are required for a career as a radiologic technologist?

A: Some important skills for this profession include attention to detail, critical thinking, communication, and technical abilities.

Q: Is there room for advancement in this field?

A: Yes, radiologic technologists can advance their careers by specializing in a specific type of imaging, pursuing advanced education, or moving into management or education roles.

Conclusion of Radiology Tech Job Description and Salary

Overall, a career as a radiologic technologist can offer a rewarding and financially stable path in healthcare. By understanding the job description, salary expectations, and potential for advancement, individuals can make an informed decision about whether this career path is right for them.
