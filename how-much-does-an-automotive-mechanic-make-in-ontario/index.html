How Much Does An Automotive Mechanic Make In Ontario

Written by Eveline Jul 01, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering becoming an automotive mechanic but unsure about the salary? Or are you already working as a mechanic and wondering if you are being paid fairly? It's important to understand how much an automotive mechanic makes in Ontario before making any decisions.

The average salary for an automotive mechanic in Ontario is around $50,000 to $70,000 per year. However, the salary can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, type of employer, and type of work.

In general, mechanics who work for dealerships tend to earn more than those who work for independent repair shops. Additionally, those who specialize in a specific area such as diesel engines or transmissions may also earn a higher salary.

Overall, becoming an automotive mechanic in Ontario can be a rewarding career with a decent salary. However, it's important to keep in mind that the job can be physically demanding and may require working long hours or weekends.

Personal Experience with Automotive Mechanic Salary

My husband has been working as an automotive mechanic for over 10 years in Ontario. He started off working for an independent repair shop and earned around $40,000 per year. However, he later switched to working for a dealership and now earns around $60,000 per year with benefits. He enjoys the work and finds it rewarding, but it can be tiring at times.

Factors that Affect Automotive Mechanic Salary

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect the salary of an automotive mechanic. One of the most significant factors is experience. Mechanics with more years of experience tend to earn more than those who are just starting out. The location of the job can also play a role in the salary, as mechanics in larger cities may earn more than those in smaller towns. Additionally, the type of work can also affect the salary, as specialized areas such as electrical systems or air conditioning may pay more.

Training and Education for Automotive Mechanics

In order to become an automotive mechanic in Ontario, it is typically required to have completed a post-secondary program in automotive service technology or a related field. Additionally, many employers prefer to hire those who have obtained certification from the Ontario College of Trades. Continuing education and training is also important for mechanics to stay up to date with the latest technology and techniques.

How to Negotiate a Fair Salary as an Automotive Mechanic

If you are already working as an automotive mechanic and feel that you are not being paid fairly, it may be possible to negotiate a higher salary. Start by doing research on the average salary for your position in your area. Additionally, highlight your skills and experience to your employer and explain why you feel you deserve a higher salary. Be prepared to negotiate and consider other benefits such as vacation time or flexible hours if a higher salary is not possible.

Personal Opinion on Automotive Mechanic Salary

Overall, I believe that automotive mechanics in Ontario are paid a fair salary for their work. It is a skilled trade that requires specialized knowledge and physical labor. However, employers should ensure that their mechanics are being paid fairly and not taking advantage of the high demand for skilled tradespeople.

Question and Answer

Q: Do automotive mechanics in Ontario have good job security?

A: Yes, automotive mechanics in Ontario have good job security as there is a high demand for skilled tradespeople in this field.

Q: How long does it take to become a certified automotive mechanic in Ontario?

A: It typically takes around 2-3 years to complete a post-secondary program in automotive service technology and obtain certification from the Ontario College of Trades.

Q: Can automotive mechanics in Ontario work on different types of vehicles?

A: Yes, automotive mechanics in Ontario are trained to work on a variety of vehicles including cars, trucks, and SUVs.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement in the field of automotive mechanics in Ontario?

A: Yes, there are opportunities for advancement in the field of automotive mechanics in Ontario such as becoming a master mechanic or a service manager.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Automotive Mechanic Make in Ontario

Overall, the salary for an automotive mechanic in Ontario can vary depending on several factors. However, the average salary of $50,000 to $70,000 per year is a decent income for a skilled trade that requires specialized knowledge and physical labor. It's important for employers to ensure that their mechanics are being paid fairly and for mechanics to negotiate for a fair salary if they feel they are not being paid enough.
