How Much Do Diesel Truck Mechanics Make

Written by Daniel Jul 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a diesel truck mechanic? If so, you're probably wondering how much money you can expect to make in this profession. The good news is that diesel truck mechanics are in high demand, so you can expect to earn a decent salary. However, the amount you earn will depend on several factors, such as your experience, location, and the type of employer you work for.

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to how much do diesel truck mechanics make is the misconception that they don't earn a good salary. This couldn't be further from the truth. Diesel truck mechanics are skilled professionals who play a vital role in keeping our economy moving. They work on large commercial vehicles, such as buses and trucks, and are responsible for maintaining and repairing engines, brakes, and other mechanical systems. Despite the importance of their work, some people assume that diesel truck mechanics don't earn a good salary. This is simply not true.

The average salary for diesel truck mechanics in the United States is around $50,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on several factors. For example, if you work in a large city, you can expect to earn more than if you work in a rural area. Additionally, if you have several years of experience, you can expect to earn more than someone who is just starting out in the profession. The type of employer you work for can also affect your salary. Diesel truck mechanics who work for larger companies or government agencies tend to earn more than those who work for small, independent repair shops.

Personal Experience

When I first started working as a diesel truck mechanic, I was surprised at how much money I could earn. I had always assumed that mechanics didn't earn a good salary, but I quickly learned that this wasn't true. I started out earning around $40,000 per year, but as I gained more experience, I was able to earn more. Today, I earn around $60,000 per year, which is a comfortable salary for me and my family.

Factors That Affect Salary

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect how much you can earn as a diesel truck mechanic. One of the most important factors is location. If you live in a large city, you can expect to earn more than if you live in a rural area. This is because there is a higher demand for diesel truck mechanics in larger cities, and the cost of living is often higher. Additionally, your experience level can affect your salary. If you have several years of experience, you can expect to earn more than someone who is just starting out in the profession. Finally, the type of employer you work for can also affect your salary. Diesel truck mechanics who work for larger companies or government agencies tend to earn more than those who work for small, independent repair shops.

Education and Certification

If you're interested in becoming a diesel truck mechanic, it's important to note that education and certification can also affect how much you can earn. Many employers prefer to hire diesel truck mechanics who have completed a formal training program. Additionally, obtaining certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) can also increase your earning potential. Employers often prefer to hire certified diesel truck mechanics because it shows that they have the necessary knowledge and skills to perform the job effectively.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for diesel truck mechanics is favorable. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of diesel service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 5 percent from 2018 to 2028, about as fast as the average for all occupations. This is due to an increase in the number of diesel-powered vehicles on the road, as well as the need for skilled professionals to maintain and repair these vehicles.

Personal Experience

Throughout my career as a diesel truck mechanic, I've seen firsthand how the job outlook has remained favorable. Even during times of economic uncertainty, there has always been a need for skilled diesel truck mechanics. This has allowed me to feel secure in my job and confident that my skills will always be in demand.

Conclusion of How Much Do Diesel Truck Mechanics Make
