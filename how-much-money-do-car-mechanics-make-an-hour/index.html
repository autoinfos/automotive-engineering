How Much Money Do Car Mechanics Make An Hour

Written by William Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a car mechanic but wondering if it offers a good salary? Or maybe you're already a mechanic but not sure if you're being paid what you're worth. Whatever the case may be, understanding how much money car mechanics make an hour is an important consideration.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics is $20.24 per hour as of May 2020. However, this number can vary based on a number of factors such as experience, location, and certification.

In addition to the median wage, many mechanics also receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Some may also have the opportunity to earn bonuses or commissions based on performance or sales. All of these factors can impact the overall salary of a car mechanic.

The Importance of Certification

One factor that can greatly impact the hourly wage of a car mechanic is certification. Mechanics who have completed formal training programs and earned certifications such as the ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) certification can often command higher wages than those who do not have any certifications.

For example, according to the ASE website, ASE-certified technicians can earn up to 36% more than non-certified technicians. This makes certification an important consideration for those looking to increase their earning potential as a car mechanic.

Location Matters

Another factor that can impact how much money car mechanics make an hour is location. Wages for mechanics can vary greatly depending on where they work and the cost of living in that area.

For example, mechanics in cities with higher costs of living such as New York City or San Francisco may be paid more than those in smaller towns or rural areas. It's important to research the average wages for mechanics in your area to get a better understanding of what you can expect to earn.

The Benefits of Experience

Experience is another important factor when it comes to how much money car mechanics make an hour. Mechanic apprentices and entry-level technicians typically earn less than those with several years of experience under their belts.

As a mechanic gains more experience and develops specialized skills, their earning potential may increase. Additionally, experienced mechanics may have the opportunity to move into supervisory or management positions which often come with higher salaries and additional benefits.

Negotiating Your Salary

If you're already working as a car mechanic but feel like you're not being paid what you're worth, it may be time to negotiate your salary. This can be a daunting task but it's important to remember that you have the power to advocate for yourself and your worth.

Start by researching the average wages for mechanics in your area and use that information to make a case for why you deserve a raise. Highlight your skills, experience, and any certifications you may have earned. Be prepared to negotiate and don't be afraid to ask for what you believe you're worth.

Conclusion of How Much Money Do Car Mechanics Make an Hour

Understanding how much money car mechanics make an hour is an important consideration for anyone considering a career in the automotive industry. Factors such as certification, location, and experience can greatly impact a mechanic's earning potential. By doing research, negotiating your salary, and continuing to develop your skills and experience, you can increase your earning potential as a car mechanic.
