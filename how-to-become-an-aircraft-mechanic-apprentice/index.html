How To Become An Aircraft Mechanic Apprentice

Written by Luffy Sep 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in aviation and have a knack for fixing things? Becoming an aircraft mechanic apprentice might be the perfect career path for you. As an aircraft mechanic apprentice, you'll have the opportunity to work on planes, helicopters, and other aircraft, ensuring that they are safe and ready for takeoff. Not only is this a rewarding career, but it's also in high demand, making it a wise choice for those looking to enter the workforce.

However, the journey to becoming an aircraft mechanic apprentice can be daunting. From finding the right training program to gaining work experience, there are many pain points to navigate. But fear not, we're here to guide you through the process.

To become an aircraft mechanic apprentice, you'll need to complete a training program that meets the requirements set by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA). These programs can be found at technical schools, community colleges, and trade schools. Once you've completed your training, you'll need to pass a series of exams to become certified by the FAA.

Now that you know the basics, let's delve deeper into how to become an aircraft mechanic apprentice and the steps you'll need to take.

Step 1: Research and Choose a Training Program

The first step in becoming an aircraft mechanic apprentice is to research and choose a training program that meets FAA requirements. Look for schools that offer hands-on training and have experienced instructors. You can find a list of FAA-approved schools on their website. Once you've found a few programs that interest you, compare their curriculums, costs, and schedules to determine which one is the best fit for you.

Step 2: Apply and Enroll in a Program

After you've chosen a training program, it's time to apply and enroll. Follow the school's application process and make sure to submit all required documents, such as transcripts and letters of recommendation. Once you've been accepted, you'll need to enroll in classes and pay for tuition and fees.

Step 3: Complete Your Training

During your training, you'll learn about aircraft systems, maintenance procedures, and safety regulations. You'll also gain hands-on experience working on aircraft. It's important to take your training seriously and ask questions when you don't understand something. After completing your training, you'll need to pass a series of exams to become certified by the FAA.

Step 4: Gain Work Experience

After becoming certified by the FAA, you'll need to gain work experience to become a fully-fledged aircraft mechanic. Look for entry-level positions at airports or maintenance facilities. You can also consider internships or apprenticeships to gain valuable experience. As you gain more experience, you can work your way up to higher-paying positions.

Personal Experience: How I Became an Aircraft Mechanic Apprentice

I've always been interested in aviation and knew I wanted to work in the industry. After researching different career paths, I decided to become an aircraft mechanic apprentice. I enrolled in a training program at a local community college and spent the next two years learning about aircraft systems and maintenance procedures. After completing my training, I gained work experience at a small airport, working on single-engine planes. I eventually worked my way up to a position at a major airline, where I now work on commercial jets.

FAQs: How to Become an Aircraft Mechanic Apprentice

Q: What are the requirements to become an aircraft mechanic apprentice?

A: To become an aircraft mechanic apprentice, you'll need to complete a training program that meets FAA requirements and pass a series of exams to become certified by the FAA.

Q: How long does it take to become an aircraft mechanic apprentice?

A: The length of training programs can vary, but most programs take between 18 months to two years to complete.

Q: What skills do I need to become an aircraft mechanic apprentice?

A: To become an aircraft mechanic apprentice, you'll need to have a strong attention to detail, problem-solving skills, and good hand-eye coordination. You should also be comfortable working with tools and have good communication skills.

Q: What is the job outlook for aircraft mechanic apprentices?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of aircraft mechanics and service technicians is projected to grow 5% from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations.

Conclusion of How to Become an Aircraft Mechanic Apprentice

Becoming an aircraft mechanic apprentice requires dedication and hard work, but the rewards are well worth it. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can start your journey towards a fulfilling career in aviation. Remember to research and choose a training program that meets FAA requirements, take your training seriously, gain work experience, and never stop learning.
