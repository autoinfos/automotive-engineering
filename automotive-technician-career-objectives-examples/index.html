Automotive Technician Career Objectives Examples

Written by Eveline Aug 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have a passion for cars and fixing them? Are you interested in pursuing a career as an automotive technician? If so, it's important to set clear career objectives to help you achieve your goals. In this blog post, we'll explore some examples of automotive technician career objectives and how you can reach them.

Working as an automotive technician can be a challenging and rewarding career, but it also requires a lot of dedication and hard work. Some common pain points for those in this field include staying up-to-date with the latest technology, dealing with difficult customers, and working long hours.

To overcome these challenges and succeed as an automotive technician, it's important to have clear career objectives. These objectives should outline your long-term goals and the steps you need to take to achieve them.

In this article, we'll explore some common automotive technician career objectives and provide tips on how to reach them. By the end of this post, you'll have a better understanding of what it takes to succeed in this field and how to set yourself up for long-term success.

Objective 1: Become ASE Certified

One common career objective for automotive technicians is to become ASE certified. ASE (National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence) certification is a nationally recognized standard for automotive technicians, and it can open up new job opportunities and help you earn a higher salary.

To become ASE certified, you'll need to pass a series of exams that test your knowledge of automotive repair and maintenance. These exams cover a wide range of topics, from engine repair to electrical systems to suspension and steering.

If you're interested in becoming ASE certified, the first step is to study for the exams. There are many resources available to help you prepare, including study guides, online courses, and practice exams. Once you feel confident in your knowledge, you can register to take the exams at a local testing center.

Objective 2: Specialize in a Specific Area

Another career objective for automotive technicians is to specialize in a specific area of automotive repair. This could be anything from engine repair to electrical systems to brakes and suspension.

Specializing in a specific area can help you become a more valuable employee and increase your job opportunities. It can also help you earn a higher salary and gain more respect within the industry.

To specialize in a specific area, you'll need to gain additional education and training in that area. This could include taking courses at a technical school or community college, attending workshops and seminars, or working under a mentor who specializes in that area.

Objective 3: Advance to Management

For some automotive technicians, the ultimate career objective is to advance to a management position. This could include becoming a service manager, shop foreman, or even owning your own repair shop.

To advance to a management position, you'll need to demonstrate strong leadership skills, excellent communication skills, and a deep knowledge of automotive repair and maintenance. You may also need to earn additional certifications or degrees in business management or related fields.

Objective 4: Start Your Own Business

Finally, another career objective for automotive technicians is to start their own business. This could be a small repair shop or a larger dealership or franchise.

To start your own business, you'll need to have a deep understanding of the automotive industry, strong business skills, and a solid business plan. You may also need to secure funding, hire employees, and navigate the legal and regulatory aspects of starting a business.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to become ASE certified?

A: The length of time it takes to become ASE certified depends on your level of experience and knowledge. Some technicians may be able to pass the exams after just a few months of studying, while others may take several years to prepare.

Q: What is the average salary for automotive technicians?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive technicians was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, salaries can vary widely depending on your level of experience, location, and specialty.

Q: Do I need a degree to become an automotive technician?

A: While a degree is not always required to become an automotive technician, it can be helpful in terms of career advancement and earning potential. Many technicians choose to pursue an associate's degree in automotive technology or a related field.

Q: What are some common challenges that automotive technicians face?

A: Some common challenges for automotive technicians include staying up-to-date with the latest technology, dealing with difficult customers, and working long hours.

Conclusion of Automotive Technician Career Objectives Examples

Setting clear career objectives is essential for anyone looking to succeed as an automotive technician. Whether you're interested in becoming ASE certified, specializing in a specific area, advancing to a management position, or starting your own business, having a clear plan in place can help you achieve your goals and reach your full potential in this exciting and rewarding field.
