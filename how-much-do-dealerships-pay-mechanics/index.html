How Much Do Dealerships Pay Mechanics

Written by Petter Aug 07, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanic in a dealership? Do you wonder how much they get paid? If you're passionate about cars and have a talent for fixing them, then this could be a great career choice. But, before you jump in, it's essential to understand how much do dealerships pay mechanics.

One of the most significant concerns that mechanics have is their salaries. The pay rate varies depending on the dealership, location, and experience level. Many mechanics feel that their pay doesn't reflect the amount of work they do, and they often feel undervalued.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 in May 2020. However, the top 10 percent earned more than $68,880, while the bottom 10 percent earned less than $25,720.

In general, dealerships pay their mechanics more than independent repair shops. However, the pay rate also depends on the brand and type of car. Some luxury car brands may pay their mechanics more than other brands.

Personal Experience with Dealership Pay Rates

As a former mechanic who worked in a dealership, I can say that the pay rate was decent. However, it wasn't easy to negotiate higher pay rates, and there was little room for growth. I felt stuck in my position, and I knew I deserved better pay. Eventually, I decided to leave the dealership and start my repair shop, where I could set my own rates and grow my business.

Factors that Affect Dealership Pay Rates

Several factors can affect how much dealerships pay their mechanics. One of the most significant factors is experience. The more experience you have, the more you can earn. Other factors include certification, location, and type of dealership. Some dealerships may offer bonuses and incentives to motivate their mechanics.

Certification and Education

Dealerships typically prefer mechanics who have certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). ASE certification confirms that the mechanic has the necessary skills and knowledge to perform repairs correctly. Dealerships may also offer training programs to help their mechanics stay up-to-date with the latest technology and repair techniques.

Negotiating Higher Pay Rates

If you're currently working as a dealership mechanic, and you feel that you're not being paid fairly, then it's essential to negotiate for higher pay rates. You can start by researching the average pay rates for mechanics in your area and use that information to support your request. You can also try to negotiate for other benefits, such as bonuses, health insurance, or paid time off.

Conclusion of How Much Do Dealerships Pay Mechanics

Dealerships pay their mechanics decently, but it depends on several factors such as location, experience, certification, and type of dealership. If you're passionate about cars and have the skills and knowledge to repair them, then a career as a mechanic in a dealership could be a great choice. However, it's essential to research the pay rates and negotiate for higher pay rates if you feel that you're not being paid fairly.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do entry-level dealership mechanics make?

A: Entry-level dealership mechanics can expect to earn between $25,000 to $35,000 per year, depending on the location and type of dealership.

Q: Can dealership mechanics earn bonuses?

A: Some dealerships offer bonuses and incentives to their mechanics to motivate them and encourage them to work harder.

Q: Do luxury car dealerships pay their mechanics more?

A: Yes, some luxury car dealerships pay their mechanics more than other brands because of the complexity of the cars and the need for specialized knowledge.

Q: What is the average pay rate for dealership mechanics in the United States?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 in May 2020.
