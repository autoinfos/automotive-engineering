Do Ac Technicians Make Good Money

Written by Luffy Nov 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As the summer heats up, more and more people are relying on their air conditioning units to keep them cool. This means that air conditioning technicians are in high demand. But with the cost of living increasing every year, many people are wondering if AC technicians make good money.

There are a few pain points to consider when it comes to this question. Firstly, the cost of the necessary training and certification can be quite high. Additionally, the work can be physically demanding and may require long hours or being on call during evenings and weekends.

The answer to whether AC technicians make good money is that it depends on a few factors. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for heating, air conditioning and refrigeration mechanics and installers was $50,590 in May 2020. The highest 10 percent earned more than $77,920, while the lowest 10 percent earned less than $30,610.

In summary, AC technicians can make good money, but it can depend on factors such as experience, location, and type of employer.

How to Increase Earning Potential as an AC Technician

One way to increase earning potential as an AC technician is to gain experience and specialize in a certain area such as commercial HVAC systems or specific brands. Additionally, networking with other professionals in the industry can lead to new job opportunities or higher paying positions.

Personally, I started out as an entry-level AC technician but quickly gained experience and moved up the ladder. After a few years, I began specializing in larger commercial systems and was able to negotiate a higher salary.

The Importance of Location

Location is another important factor when it comes to earning potential as an AC technician. In areas with a higher cost of living, such as major cities, technicians can often command higher salaries. Additionally, areas with extreme weather conditions, such as the desert or areas with high humidity, may require more frequent maintenance and repairs, leading to more job opportunities and higher pay.

For example, I currently work in a major city and make a higher salary than I did when I worked in a smaller town with a lower cost of living. However, the cost of living is also higher, so it's important to weigh the pros and cons when considering a job in a certain location.

Continuing Education and Certifications

Continuing education and certifications are also important for increasing earning potential as an AC technician. As technology advances, new techniques and equipment are introduced, making it important to keep up with the latest developments. Additionally, certain certifications, such as those from the North American Technician Excellence (NATE) organization, can lead to higher paying jobs.

Negotiating Salary

When it comes to negotiating salary as an AC technician, it's important to do your research and come prepared with evidence of your experience and skills. Additionally, highlighting any certifications or specialized training can help make a case for a higher salary. It's also important to be open to discussing benefits or other non-salary compensation.

Conclusion of Do AC Technicians Make Good Money

In conclusion, AC technicians can make good money, but it can depend on factors such as location, experience, and type of employer. Gaining experience, specializing in a certain area, and networking with other professionals can all lead to higher earning potential. Additionally, continuing education and certifications can be crucial for staying up-to-date with the latest technology and techniques. When negotiating salary, it's important to come prepared with evidence of your skills and experience and be open to discussing non-salary compensation.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the job outlook for AC technicians?

A: The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects that employment of heating, air conditioning, and refrigeration mechanics and installers will grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: Are there any downsides to being an AC technician?

A: The work can be physically demanding and may require working in uncomfortable conditions, such as in attics or crawl spaces. Additionally, being on call during evenings and weekends can be challenging for some.

Q: What kind of training is required to become an AC technician?

A: Typically, a postsecondary nondegree award or an apprenticeship is required. Some states also require a license.

Q: Can AC technicians work freelance or start their own businesses?

A: Yes, some AC technicians choose to start their own businesses or work as freelancers. However, this can require additional business and marketing skills.
