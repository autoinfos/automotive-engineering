How Much Does Automotive Technician Make An Hour

Written by Aprilia Jul 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever considered becoming an automotive technician but wondered how much money you could make? If so, you're not alone. Many people are curious about the earning potential of this line of work, but the answer isn't always easy to find. In this blog post, we'll explore how much automotive technicians make per hour, factors that can impact their earnings, and what you can expect if you decide to pursue this career.

When it comes to discussing how much automotive technicians make per hour, there are several factors to consider. For starters, a technician's level of experience and training can impact their earnings. Additionally, the geographic location where they work, the type of employer they have, and the specific industry they work in can all play a role in how much they earn per hour. As a result, it's difficult to give a one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how much automotive technicians make per hour.

That said, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $20.24 as of May 2020. This means that half of all workers in this field earned more than $20.24 per hour, while the other half earned less than that amount.

My Personal Experience as an Automotive Technician

As someone who has worked as an automotive technician for several years, I can attest to the fact that earnings can vary widely in this field. When I first started out, I was earning around $15 per hour, but as I gained more experience and certifications, my earnings increased. Today, I earn closer to $30 per hour, which is well above the median hourly wage reported by the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Factors That Can Impact an Automotive Technician's Earnings

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much an automotive technician earns per hour. One major factor is their level of experience and training. Technicians who are just starting out and have limited experience will generally earn less than those who have been in the field for many years and have multiple certifications.

Another factor that can impact earnings is the geographic location where the technician works. In general, technicians who work in urban or suburban areas tend to earn more than those who work in rural locations. This is partly due to the fact that there is more demand for skilled technicians in urban areas and partly due to the higher cost of living in those areas.

Additional Factors That Can Impact Earnings

In addition to experience, training, and geographic location, there are several other factors that can impact how much an automotive technician makes per hour. These include:

  • The type of employer they work for (i.e., dealership, independent repair shop, etc.)
  • The specific industry they work in (i.e., automotive repair, auto body repair, etc.)
  • The type of work they do (i.e., routine maintenance, complex repairs, etc.)
  • The hours they work (i.e., full-time vs. part-time)

How to Increase Your Earnings as an Automotive Technician

If you're interested in becoming an automotive technician and want to maximize your earning potential, there are several steps you can take. First, invest in your education and training. The more certifications and credentials you have, the more valuable you will be to employers.

Second, seek out opportunities to specialize in certain areas of automotive repair. For example, if you become an expert in hybrid or electric vehicle repair, you may be able to command a higher hourly rate than someone who only knows how to work on traditional gas-powered cars.

Third, consider working for a dealership or other high-end repair facility. These employers often pay higher wages than independent repair shops.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for an automotive technician?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $20.24 as of May 2020.

Q: Do automotive technicians get paid well?

A: It depends on several factors, including their level of experience, training, and geographic location. In general, automotive technicians can earn a good living, but earnings can vary widely based on these and other factors.

Q: What can I do to increase my earning potential as an automotive technician?

A: To increase your earning potential as an automotive technician, consider investing in your education and training, specializing in certain areas of automotive repair, and seeking out employment with high-end repair facilities.

Q: How do I know if a career as an automotive technician is right for me?

A: If you enjoy working with your hands, have an aptitude for problem-solving, and are interested in the automotive industry, a career as an automotive technician may be a good fit for you. Consider researching the field further and talking to professionals in the industry to learn more.

Conclusion

As you can see, the answer to the question of how much automotive technicians make per hour is complex and multifaceted. While the median hourly wage reported by the Bureau of Labor Statistics is a good starting point, it's important to remember that earnings can vary widely based on a variety of factors. If you're interested in pursuing a career as an automotive technician, it's important to invest in your education and training and seek out opportunities to specialize in certain areas of automotive repair. By doing so, you can maximize your earning potential and build a rewarding career in this exciting field.
