How To Become A Automotive Mechanic

Written by Petter Jun 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in cars and love to tinker with them? Becoming a mechanic may be the perfect career for you. Not only will you get to work with cars all day, but you'll also have the satisfaction of fixing them and getting them back on the road. But, how do you become a automotive mechanic?

One of the biggest pain points of becoming a automotive mechanic is the education and training required. It can be overwhelming to figure out where to start and what steps to take. However, with the right guidance, becoming a mechanic can be a fulfilling career choice.

The first step to becoming a automotive mechanic is to obtain a high school diploma or GED. After that, you can enroll in a trade school or community college to get the necessary education and training. Look for programs that are accredited by the National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation.

Once you have completed your education, you can start your career as an entry-level mechanic. Gain as much experience as possible by working at a repair shop or dealership. Consider getting certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence to increase your job prospects and earning potential.

Why Become a Automotive Mechanic?

My love for cars started at a young age. I was always fascinated by how they worked and loved to take things apart to see how they functioned. When it came time to decide on a career, becoming a automotive mechanic was the obvious choice. Not only do I get to work with cars all day, but I also get to help people by fixing their vehicles.

Becoming a mechanic also offers job security. Cars will always need maintenance and repairs, so there will always be a demand for mechanics. Plus, the earning potential is great, especially if you specialize in a specific area such as diesel engines or high-performance vehicles.

Education and Training

As mentioned earlier, obtaining a high school diploma or GED is the first step in becoming a automotive mechanic. From there, you can enroll in a trade school or community college to get the necessary education and training. Look for programs that offer hands-on experience in addition to classroom instruction.

During your education, you will learn about the various systems of a car such as the engine, transmission, and electrical system. You'll also learn how to diagnose and repair issues, as well as how to use the necessary tools and equipment.

Certification

Getting certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) is a great way to increase your job prospects and earning potential. ASE certification shows that you have the knowledge and skills required to be a successful mechanic. To become certified, you must pass a series of exams in various areas such as engine repair, brakes, and suspension.

Specialization

Specializing in a specific area such as diesel engines or high-performance vehicles can also increase your earning potential. These areas require specialized knowledge and skills, so mechanics who specialize in them are in high demand.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for automotive mechanics is positive. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 6 percent from 2016 to 2026.

As cars become more advanced, mechanics with specialized knowledge and skills will be in higher demand. Additionally, the aging of vehicles on the road will lead to an increased need for maintenance and repairs.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to become a automotive mechanic?
A: It typically takes about 6 months to 1 year to complete a certificate program, and 2 years to complete an associate's degree program. However, the amount of time it takes can vary depending on the program and whether you attend full-time or part-time.

Q: How much does a automotive mechanic make?
A: The median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $42,090 in May 2019, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Q: What skills do I need to become a automotive mechanic?
A: You'll need good problem-solving skills, mechanical aptitude, attention to detail, and the ability to work with your hands. Strong communication skills are also important for working with customers and explaining issues.

Q: Can I become a automotive mechanic without a degree?
A: While it's possible to become a mechanic without a degree, obtaining education and training from a trade school or community college can increase your job prospects and earning potential.

Conclusion of How to Become a Automotive Mechanic

Becoming a automotive mechanic can be a fulfilling career choice for those who love cars and enjoy working with their hands. With the right education and training, you can start your career as an entry-level mechanic and work your way up to a specialized position. The job outlook for mechanics is positive, and there is always a demand for skilled technicians. So, if you're interested in becoming a mechanic, start researching education programs and take the first step towards a rewarding career.
