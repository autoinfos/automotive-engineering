How Long Does It Take To Get Certified As A Pharmacy Tech

Written by Oliver Jul 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a pharmacy technician, but unsure of how long the certification process takes? You're not alone. Many people are interested in this career path, but are hesitant to commit without knowing what to expect. In this article, we'll break down the timeline for becoming a certified pharmacy tech, so you can make an informed decision.

One of the biggest concerns for those considering the pharmacy tech career is the amount of time it takes to become certified. It's important to note that the timeline can vary depending on individual circumstances, such as the program you choose, your study schedule, and your ability to pass the certification exam. However, the average time frame for becoming certified is typically around one year.

To become certified as a pharmacy tech, you'll need to complete an accredited training program, which can take anywhere from six months to two years. After completing the program, you'll need to pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE), which is administered by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB). The exam is 90 minutes long and consists of 90 multiple-choice questions.

Personal Experience with Certification Process

As someone who has gone through the certification process, I can tell you that it can be challenging, but also rewarding. I chose to enroll in a one-year program, which consisted of both classroom instruction and hands-on training in a pharmacy setting. I found the program to be very comprehensive and felt well-prepared for the certification exam.

When it came time to take the exam, I was nervous, but confident in my abilities. I had studied diligently and felt that I had a good grasp on the material. Fortunately, I passed the exam on my first attempt and was able to start working as a certified pharmacy tech shortly thereafter.

Factors That Can Affect Certification Timeline

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect how long it takes to become certified as a pharmacy tech. One of the biggest factors is the program you choose. Some programs are shorter and more intensive, while others are longer and more comprehensive. It's important to do your research and choose a program that fits your needs and schedule.

Your study schedule can also impact the timeline. If you're able to dedicate more time to studying, you may be able to complete the program and pass the exam more quickly. However, if you have other obligations, such as work or family, you may need to take a more relaxed approach and spread out your studies over a longer period of time.

Additional Requirements for Certification

In addition to completing an accredited training program and passing the PTCE, there are a few other requirements you'll need to meet in order to become certified as a pharmacy tech. These include:

  • Being at least 18 years of age
  • Having a high school diploma or equivalent
  • Passing a criminal background check
  • Being up-to-date on all required vaccinations

Tips for Successfully Completing the Certification Process

If you're considering becoming a pharmacy tech, here are a few tips to help you successfully complete the certification process:

  • Choose an accredited program that fits your needs and schedule
  • Create a study schedule and stick to it
  • Take practice exams to help prepare for the PTCE
  • Stay up-to-date on the latest pharmacy news and regulations

Personal Experience with Job Opportunities

After becoming certified, I was able to find a job as a pharmacy tech relatively quickly. There are many job opportunities in this field, including retail pharmacies, hospitals, and long-term care facilities. I found that my certification gave me a competitive edge when it came to job applications, as many employers prefer to hire candidates who are certified.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to complete an accredited training program?

A: Training programs can range from six months to two years, depending on the program you choose.

Q: What is the passing score for the PTCE?

A: The passing score for the PTCE is 1,400 out of 1,600.

Q: How often do I need to renew my certification?

A: Certification must be renewed every two years, and requires completing 20 hours of continuing education.

Q: Can I become a pharmacy tech without going through a training program?

A: It is possible to become a pharmacy tech without completing a training program, but certification is typically required for employment in this field.

Conclusion of How Long Does It Take to Get Certified as a Pharmacy Tech

Becoming a certified pharmacy tech can be a fulfilling and lucrative career choice. While the certification process can be challenging, it typically takes around one year to complete. By choosing an accredited program that fits your needs, creating a study schedule, and staying up-to-date on the latest pharmacy news and regulations, you can successfully complete the certification process and start working in this exciting field.
