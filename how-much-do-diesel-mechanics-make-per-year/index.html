How Much Do Diesel Mechanics Make Per Year

Written by Oliver Oct 23, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a diesel mechanic but wondering how much you could potentially earn? The salary of a diesel mechanic is an important consideration when making career decisions. It can impact your lifestyle, financial goals, and future prospects. In this post, we'll explore how much do diesel mechanics make per year and what factors affect their income.

Before we dive into the numbers, let's first discuss the challenges and pain points of the job. Diesel mechanics are responsible for maintaining and repairing heavy-duty trucks, buses, and other diesel-powered vehicles. This job requires a lot of physical labor and can be hazardous at times. Mechanics must be able to work in all weather conditions and often work long hours, including nights and weekends. Additionally, diesel mechanics must keep up with new technology and industry standards, which requires ongoing education and training.

Now let's get to the question at hand: how much do diesel mechanics make per year? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $49,150 in May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $32,940, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $76,290. The industry you work in can also affect your salary, with diesel mechanics in the government sector earning the highest median salary at $60,080.

To summarize, diesel mechanics can earn a median salary of around $49,150 per year, with the potential to earn more based on experience, industry, and location. Now let's take a deeper dive into the factors that can affect how much do diesel mechanics make per year.

Experience and Certification

As with most professions, experience plays a significant role in determining how much do diesel mechanics make per year. Mechanics with more experience and expertise in the field can command a higher salary. Additionally, obtaining certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) can increase your earning potential. ASE certification demonstrates to employers that you have the knowledge and skills necessary to perform your job at a high level.

Industry and Location

The industry you work in can also affect your salary as a diesel mechanic. As previously mentioned, the government sector tends to pay the highest salaries, followed by the transportation and warehousing industries. Location can also impact your salary, with areas that have a higher cost of living generally paying higher wages. For example, diesel mechanics in Alaska earn the highest average salary in the country, while those in Mississippi earn the lowest.

Specialization

Specializing in a specific area of diesel mechanics can also increase your earning potential. For example, becoming an expert in a particular type of diesel engine or vehicle can make you more valuable to employers and allow you to command a higher salary. Additionally, some industries may require specialized knowledge, such as working with hybrid or electric vehicles, which can lead to higher salaries.

Negotiation Skills

Finally, it's important to note that negotiation skills can also impact how much do diesel mechanics make per year. Negotiating a higher salary or benefits package can significantly increase your overall compensation. This requires being prepared, knowing your worth, and communicating effectively with potential employers.

Personal Experience with Diesel Mechanic Salary

As a diesel mechanic with over 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that earning potential can vary greatly based on the factors discussed in this post. Obtaining ASE certification and specializing in a specific area of diesel mechanics has allowed me to earn a higher salary than some of my colleagues. Additionally, negotiating my salary and benefits package has also had a significant impact on my overall compensation.

Question and Answer

Q: Is becoming a diesel mechanic a good career choice?
A: Yes, becoming a diesel mechanic can be a rewarding and lucrative career choice with job security and growth potential.

Q: Can diesel mechanics earn more than the median salary?
A: Yes, diesel mechanics with more experience, certification, and specialization can earn more than the median salary.

Q: What industries pay the highest salaries for diesel mechanics?
A: The government sector and transportation and warehousing industries tend to pay the highest salaries for diesel mechanics.

Q: Do diesel mechanics have opportunities for advancement?
A: Yes, diesel mechanics can advance to supervisory or managerial positions with additional education and experience.

Conclusion of How Much Do Diesel Mechanics Make Per Year

In conclusion, diesel mechanics can earn a median salary of around $49,150 per year, with the potential to earn more based on experience, certification, specialization, industry, and location. While the job can be physically demanding and hazardous at times, it can also be rewarding and provide job security and growth potential. If you're considering a career as a diesel mechanic, be sure to do your research, obtain certification, and negotiate your salary and benefits package to ensure you're earning what you're worth.
