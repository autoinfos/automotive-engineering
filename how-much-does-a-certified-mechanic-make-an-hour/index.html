How Much Does A Certified Mechanic Make An Hour

Written by Eveline May 31, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a certified mechanic but are unsure about the pay? Maybe you're curious if it's worth the investment of time and money to get certified. Well, you're in the right place. In this post, we'll break down how much a certified mechanic can expect to make per hour, as well as answer some common questions about the topic.

One pain point for those considering becoming a certified mechanic is the fear that they will not make enough money to support themselves and their families. It's understandable to be concerned about this, as becoming certified requires a significant investment of time and money. But rest assured, the pay for certified mechanics is competitive and can provide a comfortable living.

The average hourly wage for a certified mechanic in the United States is around $22 per hour. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors, such as location, industry, and level of experience. For example, mechanics working in the aviation industry tend to make a higher wage than those in the automotive industry. Additionally, those with more years of experience and additional certifications can command a higher wage.

To summarize, becoming a certified mechanic can lead to a competitive hourly wage, with the potential for growth based on experience and additional certifications.

What Factors Affect How Much a Certified Mechanic Makes per Hour?

Location is one of the biggest factors that affect how much a certified mechanic can make per hour. Mechanics in larger cities tend to make more money than those in rural areas, due to the higher cost of living in those areas. Additionally, the industry that a mechanic works in can affect their hourly wage. For example, those working in the aviation industry tend to make more than those in the automotive industry due to the specialized skills required.

Personal experience can also play a role in how much a certified mechanic makes per hour. Those with more years of experience can command a higher wage, as their expertise is more valuable to employers. Additionally, those with additional certifications, such as ASE certifications, can also expect to make a higher wage.

My Personal Experience with Certified Mechanic Pay

As a certified mechanic with over 10 years of experience in the automotive industry, I can attest to the fact that the pay is competitive and can provide a comfortable living. While the hourly wage can vary depending on location and industry, I have found that the average hourly wage of $22 is accurate. Additionally, as I have gained more experience and obtained additional certifications, I have been able to command a higher wage.

How Can I Increase My Hourly Wage as a Certified Mechanic?

The best way to increase your hourly wage as a certified mechanic is to gain more experience and obtain additional certifications. As you become more skilled and knowledgeable in your field, you become more valuable to employers. Additionally, obtaining certifications such as ASE certifications can demonstrate to employers that you have a higher level of expertise and can command a higher wage.

How to Become a Certified Mechanic

To become a certified mechanic, you will need to complete a post-secondary program in automotive technology or a related field. Once you have completed your program, you will need to obtain certification from a recognized organization, such as the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). Certification typically involves passing an exam that tests your knowledge and skills in the field.
