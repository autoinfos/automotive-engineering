What are the Best X Ray Tech Schools in California?

When researching X-ray tech schools in California, there are many factors to consider, including accreditation, program length, and clinical experience. Based on our research and personal experience, the following schools are among the best in California:

1. Loma Linda University

Loma Linda University offers a Bachelor of Science in Radiation Technology program that prepares students for a career as a radiologic technologist. The program is accredited by the Joint Review Committee on Education in Radiologic Technology (JRCERT) and includes both didactic and clinical coursework. Tuition for the program is approximately $33,000 per year.

2. Santa Barbara City College

Santa Barbara City College offers an Associate of Science in Radiography program that prepares students for a career as a radiologic technologist. The program is accredited by the JRCERT and includes both classroom and clinical instruction. Tuition for the program is approximately $1,500 per semester for in-state students.

3. City College of San Francisco

City College of San Francisco offers an Associate of Science in Radiologic Science program that prepares students for a career as a radiologic technologist. The program is accredited by the JRCERT and includes both classroom and clinical instruction. Tuition for the program is approximately $46 per unit for in-state students.

These schools are just a few examples of the many high-quality X-ray tech programs available in California. When choosing a school, it is important to consider factors such as location, cost, and program length, as well as the school's accreditation and reputation.

What Sets These Schools Apart?

Each of these schools offers a unique approach to X-ray tech education, with different program lengths, clinical experiences, and tuition costs. At Loma Linda University, for example, students can earn a Bachelor of Science degree in Radiation Technology, while Santa Barbara City College offers an Associate of Science degree in Radiography. City College of San Francisco, on the other hand, offers an Associate of Science degree in Radiologic Science.

Despite these differences, all of these schools share a commitment to providing high-quality education and training to their students. They are accredited by the JRCERT, which ensures that their programs meet national standards for X-ray tech education.

What Are the Career Opportunities?

As an X-ray technician in California, there are many career opportunities available. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for radiologic technologists in California is $77,950, with employment projected to grow 9% from 2019 to 2029.

Graduates of X-ray tech programs in California may find employment in hospitals, clinics, imaging centers, and other healthcare facilities. They may also choose to specialize in areas such as mammography, CT scanning, or MRI.
