How Many Diesel Ase Certifications Are There

Written by Bardi Aug 14, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you an aspiring diesel mechanic or someone who wants to take their diesel mechanic career to the next level? If so, you might be wondering how many diesel ASE certifications are there. ASE certification is crucial for diesel mechanics who want to stand out in a competitive job market, increase their earning potential, and demonstrate their expertise to employers and clients. In this blog post, we will explore the different diesel ASE certifications available and what they entail.

As a diesel mechanic, you might face many challenges in your career, such as keeping up with the latest technology, troubleshooting complex issues, and meeting tight deadlines. Having the right certifications can help you overcome some of these pain points and boost your confidence, skills, and performance. However, the process of getting certified can be daunting and confusing, especially if you are not familiar with the different types of certifications, their requirements, and their benefits.

Currently, there are nine diesel ASE certifications that cover various aspects of diesel engine repair and maintenance. These certifications are as follows:

Medium/Heavy Truck

This certification covers diesel engines used in medium and heavy-duty trucks, buses, and other vehicles. It includes topics such as engine repair, electrical systems, drivetrain, brakes, suspension, and steering.

Electronic Diesel Engine Diagnosis Specialist

This certification focuses on diagnosing and repairing electronic systems in diesel engines, such as sensors, modules, and wiring. It covers topics such as scan tool usage, data interpretation, and troubleshooting.

Advanced Level Specialist

This certification is a combination of various diesel specialties, including engine performance, drivability, heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC), and electrical/electronic systems. It requires passing multiple tests and having at least two years of relevant work experience.

Electronic Diesel Engine Controls Technician

This certification is similar to the Electronic Diesel Engine Diagnosis Specialist certification but goes more in-depth into advanced electronic systems and components. It covers topics such as fuel injection, emissions control, and power management.

Transit Bus

This certification focuses on diesel engines used in transit and shuttle buses. It covers topics such as engine repair, electrical systems, suspension, and steering, as well as safety and environmental regulations.

Electronic Drivetrain Technician

This certification covers electronic systems and components related to the drivetrain, such as transmissions, differentials, and transfer cases. It includes topics such as diagnosis, repair, and maintenance.

Undercarriage Specialist

This certification is specific to the undercarriage systems of diesel-powered vehicles, such as brakes, steering, suspension, and drivetrain. It requires passing multiple tests and having at least two years of relevant work experience.

Natural Gas Engines

This certification covers natural gas-powered engines used in various applications, such as buses, trucks, and generators. It includes topics such as safety, fuel systems, ignition, and emissions control.

Light Duty Diesel

This certification covers diesel engines used in light-duty vehicles, such as pickup trucks, SUVs, and vans. It includes topics such as engine repair, electrical systems, and emissions control.

By obtaining one or more of these certifications, you can demonstrate your knowledge and skills in a specific area of diesel engine repair and maintenance. You can also differentiate yourself from non-certified mechanics, attract more job opportunities, and potentially earn a higher salary. However, keep in mind that these certifications require preparation, dedication, and ongoing education to maintain their validity and relevance.

Personal Experience with Diesel ASE Certifications

As a diesel mechanic, I have pursued several ASE certifications over the years to expand my expertise and stay competitive in the job market. I started with the Medium/Heavy Truck certification, which helped me gain a deeper understanding of diesel engines in commercial vehicles and pass more complex repairs. Then, I went for the Electronic Diesel Engine Diagnosis Specialist certification, which allowed me to diagnose and solve electronic issues faster and more accurately. Recently, I obtained the Advanced Level Specialist certification, which challenged me to integrate multiple diesel specialties and take my problem-solving skills to the next level. Overall, I believe that ASE certifications have been instrumental in my career growth and satisfaction.

Benefits of Diesel ASE Certifications

Now that you know how many diesel ASE certifications are there and what they cover, you might wonder what their benefits are. Here are some of the advantages of obtaining diesel ASE certifications:

  • Enhance your credibility and professionalism as a diesel mechanic
  • Gain recognition and respect from employers, clients, and colleagues
  • Improve your job prospects and earning potential
  • Expand your knowledge and skills in a specific area of diesel engine repair and maintenance
  • Stay up-to-date with the latest technology and standards in the diesel industry
  • Join a community of like-minded diesel professionals who share your passion and vision

How to Prepare for Diesel ASE Certifications

If you are interested in obtaining one or more diesel ASE certifications, here are some tips to help you prepare:

  • Read and understand the ASE certification requirements and test format
  • Get hands-on experience and practice in the relevant diesel specialty
  • Take ASE preparation courses or self-study using ASE study guides and practice tests
  • Attend diesel seminars, conferences, and webinars to stay current with industry trends and best practices
  • Network with certified diesel mechanics and ask for their advice and feedback

How to Maintain Diesel ASE Certifications

Once you obtain a diesel ASE certification, it is essential to maintain it by fulfilling the recertification requirements. These requirements vary depending on the certification but usually involve retaking the test every five years, completing relevant training courses or seminars, and keeping a record of your work experience and education. By maintaining your certification, you can ensure its validity and value and continue to benefit from its advantages.

Conclusion of How Many Diesel ASE Certifications Are There

In summary, diesel ASE certifications are valuable credentials that can help you advance your diesel mechanic career, improve your skills and knowledge, and gain recognition and respect from others. There are currently nine diesel ASE certifications that cover different aspects of diesel engine repair and maintenance, from medium/heavy truck to natural gas engines. By preparing and obtaining these certifications, you can set yourself apart from other diesel mechanics and achieve your professional goals.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to prepare for a diesel ASE certification?

A: The preparation time for a diesel ASE certification depends on your experience, knowledge, and skills in the relevant diesel specialty. It can range from a few weeks to several months, depending on how much time and effort you can dedicate to studying and practicing.

Q: How much does it cost to obtain a diesel ASE certification?

A: The cost of obtaining a diesel ASE certification varies depending on the certification and the preparation method. It can range from $50 to $300 per test, plus additional fees for study materials, training courses, and recertification. However, some employers may cover the cost of certification for their employees.

Q: Can I take a diesel ASE certification test online?

A: No, diesel ASE certification tests are only available at authorized testing centers, such as community colleges, vocational schools, and training centers. You need to schedule an appointment and bring your ID and other required documents to take the test.

Q: What happens if I fail a diesel ASE certification test?

A: If you fail a diesel ASE certification test, you can retake it after a waiting period of 30 days. You need to pay the full fee again and study more to improve your chances of passing. However, if you fail the test three times in a row, you need to wait for a year before retaking it.
