Bsc Electrical Engineering Salary In Kenya What You Need To Know

Written by William Sep 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering pursuing a BSc Electrical Engineering degree in Kenya? If so, you may be wondering what your salary prospects are after graduation. The truth is, the salary for BSc Electrical Engineering graduates in Kenya can vary widely depending on a number of factors. In this post, we'll take a closer look at the target of BSc Electrical Engineering salary in Kenya and what you can expect as a graduate.

One of the main pain points for BSc Electrical Engineering graduates in Kenya is the lack of consistency in salary. While some graduates may be able to secure well-paying jobs, others may struggle to find work or earn a low salary. This can be frustrating, especially since Electrical Engineering is a highly technical and in-demand field.

So, what is the target of BSc Electrical Engineering salary in Kenya? According to recent research, the average starting salary for a BSc Electrical Engineering graduate in Kenya is Ksh 60,000 per month. However, this figure can vary widely depending on the industry you work in, the size of the company, and your level of experience.

In summary, BSc Electrical Engineering graduates in Kenya can expect a starting salary of around Ksh 60,000 per month, but this can vary depending on a number of factors. Let's take a closer look at some of these factors below.

Factors Affecting BSc Electrical Engineering Salary in Kenya

Industry: The industry you work in can have a big impact on your salary as a BSc Electrical Engineering graduate. For example, graduates who work in the telecommunications industry tend to earn higher salaries than those who work in manufacturing.

Level of Experience: Your level of experience can also affect your salary as a BSc Electrical Engineering graduate. Those with more experience will generally earn a higher salary than those who are just starting out.

Company Size: The size of the company you work for can also affect your salary. Larger companies tend to pay higher salaries than smaller companies.

The Importance of Negotiating Your Salary

One important thing to keep in mind as a BSc Electrical Engineering graduate in Kenya is the importance of negotiating your salary. While it can be tempting to accept the first offer that comes your way, negotiating can help you earn a higher salary and set yourself up for future success.

Tips for Negotiating Your Salary

Research: Do your research and find out what other BSc Electrical Engineering graduates are earning in your industry and at your level of experience. This can give you a better idea of what you should be asking for.

Be Confident: When negotiating your salary, be confident and assertive. Don't be afraid to ask for what you want.

Highlight Your Skills: Make sure to highlight your skills and experience during the negotiation process. This can help you justify your desired salary.

Personal Experience: BSc Electrical Engineering Salary in Kenya

As a recent BSc Electrical Engineering graduate in Kenya, I can attest to the fact that the salary prospects for graduates can be somewhat unpredictable. While I was fortunate enough to secure a well-paying job right out of university, many of my classmates struggled to find work or earn a decent salary.

One thing that I found helpful during my job search was networking. By reaching out to professionals in my industry and attending networking events, I was able to make valuable connections that ultimately helped me land my current job.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average starting salary for a BSc Electrical Engineering graduate in Kenya?

A: The average starting salary for a BSc Electrical Engineering graduate in Kenya is around Ksh 60,000 per month.

Q: What factors can affect my BSc Electrical Engineering salary in Kenya?

A: Factors that can affect your BSc Electrical Engineering salary in Kenya include industry, level of experience, and company size.

Q: Is it important to negotiate my BSc Electrical Engineering salary in Kenya?

A: Yes, negotiating your BSc Electrical Engineering salary in Kenya can help you earn a higher salary and set yourself up for future success.

Q: What tips do you have for negotiating my BSc Electrical Engineering salary in Kenya?

A: Some tips for negotiating your BSc Electrical Engineering salary in Kenya include doing your research, being confident, and highlighting your skills and experience.

Conclusion of BSc Electrical Engineering Salary in Kenya

While the salary prospects for BSc Electrical Engineering graduates in Kenya can be somewhat unpredictable, there are steps you can take to set yourself up for success. By networking, doing your research, and negotiating your salary, you can increase your chances of earning a higher salary and achieving your career goals.
