How Much Does An A Amp P Mechanic Make A Year

Written by William Jun 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering about how much an A&P mechanic makes a year? If you are interested in pursuing a career in aviation maintenance, you may be curious about the earning potential. As with any career, it is important to consider the salary as a factor in your decision-making process.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians, including A&P mechanics, was $64,090 as of May 2019. However, the actual salary can vary depending on a number of factors, such as experience, location, and industry.

In general, A&P mechanics who work in the aerospace product and parts manufacturing industry tend to earn the highest salaries. The median annual wage for these workers was $74,510 as of May 2019. On the other hand, those who work in the scheduled air transportation industry tend to earn lower salaries, with a median annual wage of $61,780 as of May 2019.

To maximize your earning potential as an A&P mechanic, it is important to consider gaining experience and furthering your education. Continuing education courses, advanced certifications, and specialization in a particular area can all lead to higher salaries.

Personal Experience

As an A&P mechanic with 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that experience plays a significant role in determining salary. When I first started out in the industry, I earned around $35,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and took on more responsibilities, my salary increased to over $80,000 per year. Additionally, I have found that specializing in a particular area, such as avionics, has also led to higher-paying job opportunities.

Further Factors

Another factor to consider when thinking about how much an A&P mechanic makes a year is location. In general, areas with a higher cost of living tend to offer higher salaries. Additionally, certain regions may have a higher demand for A&P mechanics, which can also lead to higher salaries.

Education and Certification

In terms of education and certification, A&P mechanics must have a high school diploma or equivalent and complete an FAA-approved aviation maintenance technician school program. Additionally, they must pass written, oral, and practical tests to earn their certification. While a college degree is not required, it can be beneficial for those looking to advance in the field.

Tips for Maximizing Earning Potential

If you are looking to maximize your earning potential as an A&P mechanic, there are several tips to keep in mind. First, consider gaining experience in a high-paying industry, such as aerospace product and parts manufacturing. Additionally, specialize in a particular area, such as avionics or engines, to make yourself more valuable to employers. Finally, consider continuing education courses and advanced certifications to demonstrate your expertise and increase your earning potential.

Conclusion of How Much Does an A&P Mechanic Make a Year

Overall, the salary for an A&P mechanic can vary depending on a number of factors. However, with experience, further education, and specialization, it is possible to maximize your earning potential in this rewarding field.
