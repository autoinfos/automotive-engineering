When it comes to automotive technician salaries, there are a few pain points to keep in mind. First, the average salary for an automotive technician is relatively low compared to other skilled trades. Additionally, entry-level positions may pay less than more experienced roles, which can make it difficult for new technicians to make ends meet.

The good news is that ASE Master Automotive Technicians typically earn higher salaries than their non-certified counterparts. So, what does it take to become an ASE Master Automotive Technician and unlock those higher salary potential? To achieve Master Technician status, you'll need to pass a series of ASE certification exams in various automotive repair and maintenance specialties. Once you've earned the required certifications, you'll need to accumulate two years of relevant work experience before you can officially become an ASE Master Automotive Technician.
