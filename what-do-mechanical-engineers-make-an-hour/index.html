What Do Mechanical Engineers Make An Hour

Written by Oliver Aug 21, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career in mechanical engineering and wondering about the salary? Or perhaps you're already a mechanical engineer and curious about how your hourly rate compares to others in your field. Whatever the case may be, understanding what mechanical engineers make an hour can help you make informed decisions about your career.

As with any profession, there are several factors that can impact a mechanical engineer's hourly rate. These may include their level of education and experience, the company they work for, and the location of their job.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for mechanical engineers in the United States is $42.45. This means that half of all mechanical engineers make more than this amount, while the other half make less. The highest 10% of mechanical engineers make more than $68.31 per hour, while the lowest 10% make less than $27.19 per hour.

In addition to their hourly rate, mechanical engineers may also receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. The value of these benefits can vary depending on the employer and the individual's employment status (full-time, part-time, contract, etc.).

My Experience as a Mechanical Engineer

As a mechanical engineer with 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that the hourly rate can vary widely depending on the specific job and location. In my first job out of college, I was making around $25 per hour, but as I gained more experience and took on more responsibility, my hourly rate increased significantly.

When I switched companies a few years ago, I was able to negotiate a higher hourly rate and better benefits package. I also moved to a different city, which had a higher cost of living but also higher salaries for mechanical engineers.

Factors That Affect Hourly Rates for Mechanical Engineers

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact a mechanical engineer's hourly rate. Here are a few of the most important ones:

Education and Experience

In general, mechanical engineers with more education and experience will command higher hourly rates. This is because they have more specialized knowledge and skills that are in demand in the industry.

Location

The location of a mechanical engineer's job can also impact their hourly rate. For example, mechanical engineers working in large cities with high costs of living may earn higher salaries than those working in smaller towns with lower costs of living.

Conclusion of What Do Mechanical Engineers Make an Hour

Overall, the hourly rate for mechanical engineers can vary widely depending on a variety of factors. If you're considering a career in this field, it's important to do your research and understand the typical salary range for your level of education and experience. By doing so, you can make informed decisions about your career and negotiate a fair hourly rate when the time comes.
