What Does A Heavy Plant Mechanic Do

Written by Daniel Aug 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever wondered what a heavy plant mechanic does? If you're interested in the mechanics of heavy equipment, then this is the perfect post for you. In this article, we'll explore the ins and outs of what a heavy plant mechanic does, as well as the skills and qualifications needed to become one.

Heavy plant mechanics are responsible for maintaining and repairing large construction and mining equipment. They work on a variety of heavy machinery, including bulldozers, excavators, cranes, and trucks. These professionals ensure that the equipment is in good working order, so that it can be used safely and efficiently on job sites.

Heavy plant mechanics are typically responsible for a range of tasks, including diagnosing equipment faults and malfunctions, repairing or replacing faulty components, and carrying out routine maintenance tasks such as oil changes and filter replacements. They also work closely with other members of the construction team, including site managers and equipment operators, to ensure that equipment is used safely and effectively on job sites.

In summary, heavy plant mechanics play a critical role in the construction and mining industries. They are responsible for keeping large machinery in good working order, so that it can be used safely and efficiently on job sites. If you're interested in becoming a heavy plant mechanic, you'll typically need to complete an apprenticeship or vocational training program in heavy equipment mechanics.

What Skills and Qualifications Do You Need to Become a Heavy Plant Mechanic?

As mentioned earlier, heavy plant mechanics are responsible for maintaining and repairing large construction and mining equipment. As such, they need to have a wide range of technical skills, including knowledge of mechanical systems and engines, electrical systems, and hydraulic systems. They also need to be able to read and interpret technical manuals and schematics, and have strong problem-solving skills.

In terms of qualifications, most heavy plant mechanics complete a vocational training program or apprenticeship in heavy equipment mechanics. These programs typically cover topics such as engine repair, hydraulic systems, electrical systems, and welding. Some employers may also require heavy plant mechanics to have a high school diploma or equivalent qualification.

What Does a Typical Day Look Like for a Heavy Plant Mechanic?

As a heavy plant mechanic, your day-to-day tasks may include diagnosing equipment faults, repairing or replacing faulty components, and carrying out routine maintenance tasks. You may also be responsible for conducting safety checks on equipment, and liaising with other members of the construction team to ensure that equipment is used safely and effectively on job sites.

One of the things that makes being a heavy plant mechanic interesting is the variety of tasks that you'll be responsible for. On any given day, you might be working on a bulldozer one minute, and a crane the next. This variety keeps the job interesting and challenging, and ensures that you're always learning new skills and techniques.

How Do You Become a Heavy Plant Mechanic?

To become a heavy plant mechanic, you'll typically need to complete a vocational training program or apprenticeship in heavy equipment mechanics. These programs typically last between 2-4 years, and cover topics such as engine repair, hydraulic systems, electrical systems, and welding.

During your training, you'll work under the supervision of experienced mechanics, who will teach you the skills and techniques needed to diagnose and repair heavy equipment. You'll also have the opportunity to work on a variety of different types of machinery, which will give you a broad range of experience and help you to develop your problem-solving skills.

How Much Do Heavy Plant Mechanics Earn?

The salary of a heavy plant mechanic can vary depending on a number of factors, including their level of experience, the type of equipment they work on, and the region in which they work. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for heavy vehicle and mobile equipment service technicians was $51,590 as of May 2020.

Conclusion of What Does a Heavy Plant Mechanic Do

Heavy plant mechanics play a critical role in the construction and mining industries, ensuring that large machinery is in good working order and can be used safely and efficiently on job sites. If you're interested in becoming a heavy plant mechanic, you'll typically need to complete a vocational training program or apprenticeship in heavy equipment mechanics, and have a range of technical skills and qualifications.
