Are you thinking about becoming an auto mechanic? If so, you might be wondering how much money you can expect to make in this profession. While there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question, there are a few factors that can give you an idea of what to expect. In this post, we'll explore what auto mechanics make per year and what you can do to maximize your earning potential in this field.

When it comes to auto mechanics, there are several pain points that can impact your earning potential. For example, your level of experience, the type of vehicles you work on, and your location can all play a role in how much money you make. Additionally, some mechanics may work on a commission basis, while others are paid a flat rate for their services.

So, what do auto mechanics make per year? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this can vary widely based on factors such as location, experience, and industry. For example, auto mechanics who work for dealerships or other automotive retailers may earn more than those who work for independent repair shops. Similarly, experienced mechanics with advanced certifications may command higher salaries than those who are just starting out.

In summary, auto mechanics can expect to make around $44,050 per year, although this can vary based on a variety of factors. To maximize your earning potential in this field, consider pursuing advanced certifications, gaining experience in different types of vehicles, and seeking out opportunities to work for high-paying employers.
