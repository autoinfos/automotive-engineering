Exploring Plc Automation Jobs In Germany

Written by Daniel Jun 01, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career in PLC automation jobs in Germany? If so, you're not alone. Germany is a hub for automation technology, and there are many job opportunities in this field. However, finding the right job can be challenging, especially if you're not familiar with the German job market. In this blog post, we'll explore PLC automation jobs in Germany and provide you with some helpful tips to help you succeed in your job search.

One of the biggest pain points for job seekers in this field is the lack of knowledge about the German job market. Many international job seekers struggle to understand the local job market and the necessary qualifications to succeed in this field. Additionally, language can be a barrier for those who don't speak German fluently.

The target of PLC automation jobs in Germany is to find a job in the automation industry that utilizes PLC (Programmable Logic Controllers) technology. This technology is used in various industries, including manufacturing, automotive, and energy. The job responsibilities may include designing, programming, and maintaining PLC systems.

In summary, PLC automation jobs in Germany are in high demand, but finding the right job can be challenging. In the next sections, we'll explore this topic in more detail and provide you with some helpful tips to succeed in your job search.

How to Find PLC Automation Jobs in Germany

If you're interested in pursuing a career in PLC automation jobs in Germany, there are several ways to find job opportunities. One way is to search for job postings on online job boards, such as Indeed or Glassdoor. Another way is to reach out to recruitment agencies that specialize in the automation industry. Additionally, networking with professionals in the industry can be helpful in finding job opportunities.

Personally, I found my job in the automation industry through a recruitment agency. They helped me find job opportunities that matched my qualifications and provided me with helpful tips on how to succeed in the job interview.

The Qualifications Required for PLC Automation Jobs in Germany

To succeed in PLC automation jobs in Germany, you need to have a strong background in electrical engineering or computer science. Additionally, you need to be familiar with PLC programming languages, such as ladder logic, structured text, or function block diagram. Knowledge of German language is also an advantage, although not always required.

In my experience, having a master's degree in electrical engineering and knowledge of German language was helpful in finding my job in the automation industry. However, there are also job opportunities for those with a bachelor's degree and English language proficiency.

Salary Expectations for PLC Automation Jobs in Germany

The salary for PLC automation jobs in Germany varies depending on the company, job responsibilities, and qualifications. However, according to Payscale, the average salary for a PLC programmer in Germany is €49,000 per year. This can increase up to €80,000 per year for senior positions.

How to Succeed in a Job Interview for PLC Automation Jobs in Germany

When preparing for a job interview for PLC automation jobs in Germany, it's important to research the company and the job responsibilities. Additionally, you should be familiar with common interview questions and be prepared to answer them. Some common interview questions for this field include:

  • What experience do you have with PLC programming languages?
  • What experience do you have with HMI (Human Machine Interface) programming?
  • What experience do you have with industrial communication protocols (e.g., Modbus, Profibus)?
  • What experience do you have with PLC hardware?

Conclusion of PLC Automation Jobs in Germany

PLC automation jobs in Germany are in high demand, but finding the right job can be challenging. However, with the right qualifications and job search strategies, you can succeed in this field. Remember to research the job market, network with professionals, and prepare for job interviews. Good luck in your job search!
