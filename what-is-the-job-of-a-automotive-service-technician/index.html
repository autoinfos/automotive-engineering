What Does An Automotive Service Technician Do

Written by Daniel Oct 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever wondered what goes on behind the scenes when you drop off your car at the mechanic? Automotive service technicians are the ones responsible for keeping your car running smoothly and repairing any issues that arise. However, their job involves much more than just changing oil and replacing brake pads.

Car troubles can be a major pain point for many people, especially when you rely on your vehicle to get to work or transport your family. It can be frustrating to experience a problem with your car and not know how to fix it. This is where automotive service technicians come in.

The primary job of an automotive service technician is to diagnose and repair issues with cars and other vehicles. They use a wide range of tools and equipment to identify problems with the engine, transmission, brakes, and other components of the vehicle. Once they have identified the issue, they will carry out the necessary repairs or replacements to get your vehicle back on the road.

In summary, the job of an automotive service technician is to diagnose and repair issues with cars and other vehicles. They play a crucial role in keeping our cars running smoothly and ensuring that we can get to where we need to go without any issues.

What Is the Day-to-Day Life of an Automotive Service Technician Like?

As an automotive service technician, every day is different. You might work on a variety of different cars, trucks, and other vehicles, each with their unique set of problems. One day you might be working on a classic car, and the next you might be fixing a modern hybrid.

One of the most important parts of the job is communication. As a technician, you'll need to be able to explain complex issues to customers in a way that they can understand. You'll also need to work with other technicians and service advisors to ensure that repairs are carried out efficiently and effectively.

What Are the Qualifications of an Automotive Service Technician?

Most automotive service technicians have completed a post-secondary program in automotive service technology. These programs typically take two years to complete and cover a range of topics, including engine repair, brakes, transmissions, and electronics. Some technicians also receive on-the-job training to supplement their formal education.

Additionally, many automotive service technicians are certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). ASE certification is a comprehensive exam that tests a technician's knowledge and skills in various areas of automotive repair. Technicians who pass the exam become certified in one or more areas of specialization, such as brakes, suspension, or engine repair.

What Are the Skills Required for an Automotive Service Technician?

Automotive service technicians need a combination of technical and interpersonal skills to succeed in their job. Some of the skills required include:

  • Diagnostic skills to identify issues with vehicles
  • Mechanical skills to carry out repairs and replacements
  • Attention to detail to ensure that repairs are carried out correctly
  • Communication skills to explain complex issues to customers and other technicians
  • Problem-solving skills to find solutions to difficult issues

How Can You Become an Automotive Service Technician?

If you're interested in becoming an automotive service technician, the first step is to complete a post-secondary program in automotive service technology. You can also seek out on-the-job training opportunities to supplement your education.

Once you have completed your education and training, you can seek out certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). This will help you stand out to potential employers and demonstrate your knowledge and skills in automotive repair.

What Is the Future of Automotive Service Technicians?

As cars become more advanced and incorporate more technology, the job of an automotive service technician is becoming more complex. Technicians will need to keep up with the latest advancements in automotive technology and be able to work with a wide range of systems and components.

However, the demand for automotive service technicians is expected to remain strong. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does an automotive service technician make?

A: The median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $42,090 as of May 2020.

Q: What are some common issues that automotive service technicians deal with?

A: Some common issues include engine problems, transmission issues, brake repairs, and electrical problems.

Q: What kind of work environment do automotive service technicians typically work in?

A: Automotive service technicians typically work in repair shops or dealerships. They may also work outdoors or be required to travel to customer locations to perform repairs.

Q: Is the job of an automotive service technician physically demanding?

A: Yes, the job can be physically demanding. Technicians may need to lift heavy parts and spend long periods standing or kneeling.

Conclusion of What Does an Automotive Service Technician Do?

In conclusion, automotive service technicians play a crucial role in keeping our cars running smoothly and ensuring that we can get to where we need to go without any issues. They use a combination of technical and interpersonal skills to diagnose and repair issues with cars and other vehicles. The job can be physically demanding, but it offers a rewarding career for those who enjoy working with their hands and solving complex problems.
