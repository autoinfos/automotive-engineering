How Much Does A Vehicle Service Advisor Make

Written by Christine Aug 08, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you interested in becoming a vehicle service advisor but wondering how much you can expect to earn in this role? If so, you're not alone. Many people want to know the average salary of a vehicle service advisor to ensure they are compensated fairly for their work.

Working as a vehicle service advisor can be a challenging job that requires a lot of patience, attention to detail, and excellent customer service skills. It involves interacting with customers, assessing their vehicle problems, and recommending appropriate repair services. Despite the demanding nature of the job, many people are attracted to this career path because of the potential for a good salary.

The average salary for a vehicle service advisor in the United States is around $41,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and the size of the dealership or repair shop you work for. Some service advisors may earn as little as $29,000 per year, while others can earn upwards of $60,000 per year.

In summary, the salary of a vehicle service advisor can vary significantly depending on a range of factors. However, the average salary in the United States is around $41,000 per year. If you're interested in pursuing a career in this field, it's important to research the specific job market in your area to get a better idea of what you can expect to earn.

Personal Experience with Vehicle Service Advisor Salary

As a former vehicle service advisor myself, I can attest to the fact that the salary can vary widely depending on where you work. When I started in this role at a smaller dealership, I earned around $32,000 per year. However, when I moved to a larger dealership, my salary increased to $45,000 per year.

Factors that Affect Vehicle Service Advisor Salary

There are several factors that can impact the salary of a vehicle service advisor. One of the most significant factors is experience. Those who have been working in this role for many years and have built up a strong base of knowledge and skills are typically able to command a higher salary.

Location is another important factor that can affect salary. In general, service advisors who work in metropolitan areas or in areas where there is a high demand for automotive services tend to earn more than those who work in rural areas or in areas where there is less demand for these types of services.

Size of Dealership or Repair Shop

The size of the dealership or repair shop where you work can also play a role in determining your salary. Larger dealerships or repair shops that have a high volume of customers and a large staff may be able to pay service advisors more than smaller dealerships or repair shops with fewer employees and customers.

How to Negotiate Your Vehicle Service Advisor Salary

If you're interested in pursuing a career as a vehicle service advisor and want to ensure you're fairly compensated for your work, it's important to negotiate your salary. Before accepting a job offer, do your research on the average salary for service advisors in your area and use this information to negotiate a fair salary. You may also want to consider negotiating for benefits such as health insurance, paid time off, and retirement plans.

Personal Experience with Negotiating Salary as a Vehicle Service Advisor

When I was offered my first job as a service advisor, I was hesitant to negotiate my salary because I was afraid of losing the job offer. However, I eventually mustered the courage to ask for a higher salary and was able to negotiate an increase of $5,000 per year. This experience taught me that it's important to know your worth and ask for what you deserve.

Question and Answer Section

Q: How much does a vehicle service advisor make per hour?

A: The average hourly wage for a vehicle service advisor is around $18 per hour.

Q: What education is required to become a vehicle service advisor?

A: While there is no formal education requirement to become a vehicle service advisor, many employers prefer candidates who have completed some postsecondary education in automotive technology or a related field.

Q: Is there room for advancement in a career as a vehicle service advisor?

A: Yes, there is room for advancement in this field. Service advisors can move up to management positions or pursue additional education and training to become certified technicians.

Q: What skills are important for success as a vehicle service advisor?

A: Important skills for success as a vehicle service advisor include strong communication skills, customer service skills, attention to detail, and knowledge of automotive technology and repair procedures.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the salary of a vehicle service advisor can vary significantly depending on factors such as experience, location, and the size of the dealership or repair shop where you work. However, the average salary in the United States is around $41,000 per year. If you're interested in pursuing a career in this field, it's important to research the job market in your area and negotiate your salary to ensure you're fairly compensated for your work.
