How Much Do Industrial Maintenance Technicians Make

Written by Oliver Jul 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in industrial maintenance but wondering how much you can expect to make? Look no further, we've got you covered. In this post, we'll dive into the salary range for industrial maintenance technicians and what factors can affect their earning potential.

It's no secret that salary is an important consideration when choosing a career path. Industrial maintenance technicians are no exception. Some common pain points for individuals interested in this field include uncertainty about earning potential, lack of information about the industry, and not knowing where to start.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for industrial machinery mechanics, machinery maintenance workers, and millwrights was $54,920 in May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $33,950, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $83,250.

So, what factors can impact an industrial maintenance technician's salary? Experience, education, industry, and location can all play a role. Those with more experience and education, as well as those working in high-demand industries or areas with a high cost of living, may earn more than the median salary.

Personal Experience with Industrial Maintenance Technician Salaries

One industrial maintenance technician we spoke with, John, has been in the industry for over 10 years. He started with an entry-level position and worked his way up through the ranks. John currently earns a salary of $75,000 per year, which is above the median wage for his job title. He attributes his higher salary to his experience and willingness to take on additional responsibilities.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as an Industrial Maintenance Technician

If you're interested in increasing your earning potential as an industrial maintenance technician, there are several steps you can take. First, consider pursuing additional education or training in your field. This can include certifications, apprenticeships, or even a degree in industrial maintenance. Additionally, gaining experience and taking on leadership roles can help you stand out and earn more.

Education and Training

One way to increase your earning potential as an industrial maintenance technician is to pursue additional education or training. Certifications, such as those offered by the International Society of Automation, can demonstrate your knowledge and expertise in specific areas of industrial maintenance. Apprenticeships can also provide hands-on experience and training while earning a wage. Finally, a degree in industrial maintenance, such as an associate's or bachelor's degree, can open up higher-paying job opportunities.

Experience and Leadership

Gaining experience and taking on leadership roles within your company can also help you increase your salary as an industrial maintenance technician. By demonstrating your knowledge and skills, you may be able to take on additional responsibilities or move into more advanced positions. Additionally, seeking out opportunities for continuing education and staying up-to-date on industry trends can make you a more valuable asset to your employer.

Real-Life Example of Salary Increase

Another industrial maintenance technician, Sarah, started as an entry-level technician after completing an apprenticeship program. Over the course of five years, she gained experience and demonstrated her leadership skills, eventually becoming a supervisor. In her current role, Sarah earns a salary of $85,000 per year, which is above the median wage for her job title. She credits her salary increase to her dedication to her job and willingness to take on new challenges.

Question and Answer

Q: Are there any industries where industrial maintenance technicians earn more than the median salary?

A: Yes, industrial maintenance technicians working in the transportation and warehousing industry, as well as those working in the aerospace product and parts manufacturing industry, have a higher median wage than the overall median.

Q: How much does location affect an industrial maintenance technician's salary?

A: Location can have a significant impact on an industrial maintenance technician's salary. Areas with a high cost of living, such as California or New York, typically have higher salaries than areas with a lower cost of living.

Q: How much does education affect an industrial maintenance technician's salary?

A: Education can have a significant impact on an industrial maintenance technician's salary. Those with higher levels of education, such as a bachelor's degree, may be able to earn higher salaries than those without a degree.

Q: Is it possible for industrial maintenance technicians to earn more than the highest 10 percent of earners?

A: While it is rare, it is possible for industrial maintenance technicians to earn more than the highest 10 percent of earners. This typically requires a significant amount of experience and expertise, as well as taking on leadership roles and seeking out high-paying job opportunities.

Conclusion of How Much Do Industrial Maintenance Technicians Make

In conclusion, industrial maintenance technicians can expect to earn a median annual wage of $54,920, with the highest 10 percent earning more than $83,250. Factors such as experience, education, industry, and location can impact an individual's earning potential. Pursuing additional education or training, gaining experience, and taking on leadership roles can all help increase an industrial maintenance technician's salary.
