How Much Do Aviation Maintenance Techs Make

Written by Aprilia Oct 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much aviation maintenance technicians make? It's a common question for those interested in pursuing a career in aviation maintenance or for those who are currently in the field and want to know what they can expect in terms of salary.

While aviation maintenance can be a rewarding and exciting career, it's not without its challenges. One of the biggest pain points for many in the field is the uncertainty around salary and job stability.

So, how much do aviation maintenance techs make? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians was $64,090 in May 2020. However, salaries can vary widely depending on factors such as experience, location, and the specific type of aviation maintenance work being performed.

In summary, aviation maintenance technicians can expect to make a median annual wage of around $64,090, but this can vary widely based on various factors.

Understanding Aviation Maintenance Technician Salaries

When I first started my career in aviation maintenance, I was curious about what I could expect in terms of salary. I quickly learned that there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. Instead, salaries can vary widely based on a variety of factors.

One of the biggest factors that can impact aviation maintenance technician salaries is experience. Those with more experience in the field can often command higher salaries than those who are just starting out. Additionally, location can play a role in salary, with some areas of the country offering higher salaries than others.

Factors That Impact Aviation Maintenance Technician Salaries

Another factor that can impact aviation maintenance technician salaries is the type of work being performed. For example, those who work on larger commercial aircraft may earn more than those who work on smaller private planes. Additionally, those who work in specialized areas such as avionics or composites may also be able to command higher salaries.

Experience

As mentioned, experience is a key factor in aviation maintenance technician salaries. Those who have been in the field for several years can often command higher salaries than those who are just starting out. This is because experience typically translates to a higher level of skill and expertise in the field.

Location

Location is another factor that can impact aviation maintenance technician salaries. Salaries can vary widely based on the specific area of the country in which the technician is working. For example, those working in larger cities or on the coasts may be able to command higher salaries than those working in more rural areas.

My Personal Experience

As someone who has worked in aviation maintenance for several years, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary widely based on a variety of factors. When I first started out, I was making around $40,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved into more specialized areas of aviation maintenance, my salary increased.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the median annual wage for aviation maintenance technicians?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians was $64,090 in May 2020.

Q: What factors can impact aviation maintenance technician salaries?

A: Factors that can impact aviation maintenance technician salaries include experience, location, and the type of work being performed.

Q: Can aviation maintenance technicians earn more by working in specialized areas?

A: Yes, aviation maintenance technicians who work in specialized areas such as avionics or composites may be able to command higher salaries.

Q: Do salaries for aviation maintenance technicians vary by location?

A: Yes, salaries for aviation maintenance technicians can vary widely based on the specific area of the country in which they are working.

Conclusion

While there is no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how much aviation maintenance technicians make, it's clear that salaries can vary widely based on a variety of factors. Those interested in pursuing a career in aviation maintenance should do their research and consider factors such as experience, location, and the type of work being performed when evaluating potential salaries.
