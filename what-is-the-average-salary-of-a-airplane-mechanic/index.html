What Is The Average Salary Of An Airplane Mechanic

Written by Aprilia Oct 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Being an airplane mechanic can be a rewarding career, but it also has its challenges. One of the biggest concerns for those in the field is understanding the average salary of an airplane mechanic. If you're considering this career path, you'll want to have a clear understanding of what you can expect to earn before you make any decisions.

The average salary of an airplane mechanic varies depending on a variety of factors, including experience level, location, and the type of aircraft being worked on. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 as of May 2020. This means that half of all airplane mechanics earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less.

It's important to note that the higher end of the pay scale for airplane mechanics can be quite lucrative. For example, the top 10% of earners in this field make more than $95,000 per year. However, it can take several years of experience and specialized skills to reach this level of pay.

In summary, the average salary of an airplane mechanic is around $64,090 per year, but this can vary greatly based on individual factors. It's important to take into account your location, experience level, and the type of aircraft you'll be working on when considering potential earnings in this field.

What Factors Affect the Average Salary of an Airplane Mechanic?

When it comes to the average salary of an airplane mechanic, there are several factors that can impact how much you can expect to earn. For example, experience level is a major factor in determining pay. Those with more experience in the field will typically earn more than those who are just starting out.

Location can also play a role in determining an airplane mechanic's salary. Cities with a high cost of living may offer higher salaries to offset this expense, while rural areas may offer lower salaries due to a lower cost of living.

The type of aircraft being worked on can also impact pay. Mechanics who work on larger, more complex aircraft may earn more than those who work on smaller, simpler planes.

Experience Level

Experience level is one of the biggest factors in determining an airplane mechanic's salary. Those with more experience in the field can expect to earn higher salaries than those who are just starting out.

For example, entry-level airplane mechanics may earn around $40,000 per year, while those with several years of experience can earn upwards of $75,000 per year. Mechanics with more than 20 years of experience can earn six-figure salaries.

Location

Location is another factor that can impact an airplane mechanic's salary. Cities with a high cost of living may offer higher salaries to offset this expense, while rural areas may offer lower salaries due to a lower cost of living.

For example, airplane mechanics working in New York City can expect to earn an average salary of around $85,000 per year, while those working in rural areas of the Midwest may earn closer to $50,000 per year.

What Are Some Tips for Earning a High Salary as an Airplane Mechanic?

If you're interested in earning a high salary as an airplane mechanic, there are several things you can do to increase your earning potential. One of the most important steps is to gain experience in the field. The more experience you have, the higher your earning potential will be.

Another important step is to specialize in a particular area of aircraft maintenance. For example, becoming an expert in avionics or engine repair can help you earn a higher salary than those who work on general maintenance tasks.

Question and Answer

Q: Can airplane mechanics earn more than $100,000 per year?

A: Yes, it is possible for airplane mechanics with many years of experience and specialized skills to earn six-figure salaries.

Q: What is the job outlook for airplane mechanics?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of aircraft mechanics and service technicians is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: Do airplane mechanics need to have any special certifications or licenses?

A: Yes, airplane mechanics are required to have a Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) certification in order to work on aircraft. There are several different types of certifications available, depending on the specific area of aircraft maintenance being worked on.

Q: Are there any other benefits to working as an airplane mechanic besides earning a good salary?

A: Yes, working as an airplane mechanic can be a rewarding career in many other ways. For example, it can offer the opportunity to work with cutting-edge technology and to travel to different parts of the world.

Conclusion of What Is the Average Salary of an Airplane Mechanic

While the average salary of an airplane mechanic may not be as high as some other technical fields, it can still be a lucrative and rewarding career path for those who are interested in aviation. By gaining experience in the field and specializing in a particular area of aircraft maintenance, you can increase your earning potential and enjoy a fulfilling career in this exciting industry.
