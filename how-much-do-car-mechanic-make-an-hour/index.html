How Much Do Car Mechanics Make An Hour

Written by Bardi Jul 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a car mechanic? Perhaps you're curious about the earning potential in this industry. If you're wondering how much do car mechanics make an hour, you're in the right place. In this post, we'll explore the average hourly wage for car mechanics, as well as factors that can impact their salary.

Car mechanics are skilled professionals who are responsible for repairing and maintaining vehicles. They work with a variety of tools and equipment to diagnose and fix problems with engines, transmissions, brakes, and other systems. While this is an important and rewarding profession, there are some challenges that come along with it.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $20.24 in May 2020. This means that half of all mechanics earned more than this amount, while half earned less. The lowest 10 percent of earners in this profession made less than $12.05 per hour, while the highest 10 percent made more than $32.19 per hour.

So, what factors can impact a car mechanic's hourly wage? One key factor is experience. In general, mechanics with more experience will earn a higher salary than those who are just starting out. Additionally, mechanics who specialize in certain areas, such as diesel engines or hybrid vehicles, may earn more money than those who work on a variety of vehicles.

My Personal Experience

When I was considering a career as a car mechanic, one of my biggest concerns was how much money I could make. After doing some research, I found that the average hourly wage for mechanics was around $20 per hour. However, I also learned that there were many opportunities for advancement in this field, and that I could potentially earn more money as I gained experience and specialized in certain areas.

Factors That Impact a Car Mechanic's Salary

In addition to experience and specialization, there are several other factors that can impact a car mechanic's hourly wage. One of these is location. Mechanics who work in large cities or affluent areas may earn more money than those who work in rural areas or less affluent communities. Additionally, mechanics who work for dealerships or other large companies may earn more money than those who work for smaller, independent shops.

Specialization and Certification

Another factor that can impact a car mechanic's hourly wage is specialization and certification. Mechanics who specialize in certain areas, such as engine repair or transmission repair, may earn more money than those who work on a variety of systems. Additionally, mechanics who are certified by organizations like the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) may earn more money than those who are not certified.

Negotiation Skills

Finally, a car mechanic's salary can also be impacted by their negotiation skills. When applying for jobs or negotiating salaries, mechanics who are able to effectively communicate their skills and experience may be able to earn a higher wage than those who are less skilled in this area.

Conclusion

So, how much do car mechanics make an hour? The answer is that it varies based on several factors, including experience, specialization, location, and negotiation skills. However, the median hourly wage for mechanics is around $20 per hour, with opportunities for advancement and higher earnings over time.

Question and Answer

Q: Do car mechanics make a good living?

A: Yes, car mechanics can make a good living. While the hourly wage varies based on several factors, including experience and specialization, the median hourly wage for mechanics is around $20 per hour.

Q: Is it easy to find a job as a car mechanic?

A: Yes, there are usually plenty of job opportunities available for car mechanics. However, competition for jobs can be high in some areas, so it's important to have the right skills and qualifications.

Q: Do car mechanics need to be certified?

A: While certification is not always required, many employers prefer to hire mechanics who are certified by organizations like the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). Certification can demonstrate a mechanic's knowledge and skills in a particular area.

Q: Can car mechanics earn more money over time?

A: Yes, car mechanics can earn more money over time as they gain experience and specialize in certain areas. Additionally, mechanics who are able to effectively negotiate their salaries may be able to earn more money than those who are less skilled in this area.
