Average Software Engineer Salary In Australia What You Need To Know

Written by Petter Jun 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering pursuing a career in software engineering in Australia? Or perhaps you're already a software engineer and want to know how your salary stacks up against the national average? Either way, it's important to have a clear understanding of the average software engineer salary in Australia and what factors can impact that number.

One of the biggest pain points for software engineers in Australia is the discrepancy between salaries in different regions. While major cities like Sydney and Melbourne tend to offer higher salaries, the cost of living in those areas is also higher. Additionally, the demand for software engineers can vary depending on industry and company size, which can impact salary negotiations.

So, what is the average software engineer salary in Australia? According to data from PayScale, the average salary for a software engineer in Australia is AU$79,000 per year. However, this number can vary widely based on factors such as location, experience level, and industry.

To summarize, the average software engineer salary in Australia is AU$79,000 per year, but this number can vary depending on location, experience level, and industry. Now, let's dive deeper into some of the factors that can impact software engineer salaries in Australia.

Location Matters: Salaries by Region

As mentioned earlier, location can play a big role in determining a software engineer's salary in Australia. According to PayScale, the average software engineer salary in Sydney is AU$90,000 per year, while in Melbourne it's slightly lower at AU$85,000 per year. Meanwhile, in Perth the average salary drops to AU$72,000 per year.

Personally, I have experience working as a software engineer in both Sydney and Perth. While Sydney did offer a higher salary, the cost of living was also much higher, making it difficult to save money. In Perth, salaries may be lower but the cost of living is also lower, allowing me to save more money overall.

Experience and Industry Impact Salary Negotiations

Experience level and industry can also play a big role in determining a software engineer's salary in Australia. According to PayScale, entry-level software engineers in Australia can expect to earn an average salary of AU$63,000 per year, while those with 5-9 years of experience can earn an average of AU$90,000 per year. Meanwhile, software engineers in the finance industry tend to earn higher salaries than those in the retail industry.

Personally, I've found that having experience in a high-demand technology or programming language can also impact salary negotiations. Employers may be willing to offer a higher salary for someone with expertise in a specific area that they need.

Gender and Diversity in Software Engineer Salaries

It's important to note that there is still a gender pay gap in the technology industry, including software engineering. According to data from the Workplace Gender Equality Agency, the gender pay gap for software and applications programmers in Australia is 20.1%, with men earning an average of AU$100,000 per year and women earning an average of AU$80,000 per year.

Additionally, there is a lack of diversity in the technology industry, which can impact salary negotiations and opportunities for certain groups. It's important for companies to prioritize diversity and inclusion efforts to ensure fair and equal treatment for all employees.

Tips for Negotiating Your Software Engineer Salary in Australia

If you're a software engineer in Australia, there are several tips you can use to negotiate a higher salary:

  • Research the average salary for your specific role and location
  • Highlight your experience and expertise in high-demand areas
  • Consider negotiating for additional benefits, such as flexible work arrangements or professional development opportunities
  • Be confident in your skills and the value you bring to the company

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average entry-level software engineer salary in Australia?

A: According to PayScale, the average entry-level software engineer salary in Australia is AU$63,000 per year.

Q: How does location impact software engineer salaries in Australia?

A: Salaries can vary widely depending on location in Australia. For example, the average software engineer salary in Sydney is AU$90,000 per year, while in Perth it's only AU$72,000 per year.

Q: What factors can impact salary negotiations for software engineers in Australia?

A: Experience level, industry, and expertise in high-demand areas can all impact salary negotiations for software engineers in Australia.

Q: Is there a gender pay gap in the software engineering industry in Australia?

A: Yes, there is a gender pay gap in the technology industry, including software engineering. According to data from the Workplace Gender Equality Agency, the gender pay gap for software and applications programmers in Australia is 20.1%.

Conclusion of Average Software Engineer Salary in Australia

While the average software engineer salary in Australia is AU$79,000 per year, it's important to remember that this number can vary widely depending on location, experience level, and industry. By researching average salaries, highlighting your expertise, and negotiating confidently, you can ensure that you're being fairly compensated for your skills and experience.
