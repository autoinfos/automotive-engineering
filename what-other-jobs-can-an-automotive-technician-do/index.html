What Other Jobs Can An Automotive Technician Do

Written by William Aug 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have experience as an automotive technician? Are you looking for a career change or just exploring your options? You might be surprised to learn that there are many other career paths that you can take with your skills and experience as an automotive technician.

If you've been working as an automotive technician for a while, you may have noticed some pain points in your current job. Perhaps you're tired of working long hours in a noisy, dirty garage, or you're looking for a change of pace. Whatever your reasons, it's worth exploring other job opportunities that are available to you.

So, what other jobs can an automotive technician do? The answer is: a lot! Here are just a few examples:

Job Option 1: Automotive Manufacturing Technician

If you're interested in working in automotive manufacturing, you might consider becoming an automotive manufacturing technician. In this role, you'll be responsible for assembling and testing vehicles on the production line. You'll need to have a good understanding of automotive mechanics and be able to work quickly and accurately.

I know someone who made the switch from being an automotive technician to working in automotive manufacturing, and he loves it. He says that the work is fast-paced and challenging, but also very rewarding.

Job Option 2: Automotive Instructor

If you enjoy teaching and sharing your knowledge with others, you might consider becoming an automotive instructor. In this role, you'll be responsible for teaching students about automotive mechanics, including engine repair, transmission systems, and more.

One of my former colleagues became an automotive instructor after many years of working as a technician. He says that he enjoys the opportunity to share his knowledge and help the next generation of technicians learn the skills they need to succeed.

Job Option 3: Automotive Service Manager

If you're looking for a leadership role, you might consider becoming an automotive service manager. In this role, you'll be responsible for managing a team of technicians, scheduling repairs, and ensuring that customers are satisfied with the service they receive.

One of my friends made the switch from being a technician to working as a service manager, and he says that he enjoys the opportunity to use his experience and skills in a new way. He also likes the fact that he gets to interact with customers and build relationships with them.

Job Option 4: Automotive Sales Representative

If you're interested in sales and customer service, you might consider becoming an automotive sales representative. In this role, you'll be responsible for selling vehicles and other automotive products to customers.

I know someone who made the switch from being a technician to working as a sales representative, and he says that he enjoys the opportunity to interact with customers and help them find the right vehicle for their needs.

Question and Answer

Q: What skills do I need to transition to another job as an automotive technician?

A: It depends on the job you're interested in. For example, if you want to become an automotive instructor, you'll need to have good communication skills and be able to explain complex concepts in a way that's easy to understand. If you want to become a service manager, you'll need to have good leadership skills and be able to manage a team effectively.

Q: What kind of training do I need to transition to another job as an automotive technician?

A: Again, it depends on the job you're interested in. Some jobs, such as automotive manufacturing technician, may require specific training or certifications. Other jobs, such as automotive instructor or service manager, may require a degree in a related field or relevant work experience.

Q: Is it difficult to transition to another job as an automotive technician?

A: It can be challenging to transition to a new job, especially if you've been working in the same field for a long time. However, with the right training, experience, and attitude, it's definitely possible to make the switch to a new career path.

Q: Are there any downsides to transitioning to another job as an automotive technician?

A: It's important to be aware that different jobs come with different challenges and responsibilities. For example, if you become an automotive sales representative, you'll need to be comfortable with sales targets and commission-based pay. If you become an automotive instructor, you'll need to be comfortable with public speaking and managing a classroom.

Conclusion of What Other Jobs Can an Automotive Technician Do

If you're an automotive technician looking for a change, there are many different career paths that you can explore. From working in automotive manufacturing to becoming an automotive instructor, the possibilities are endless. By leveraging your skills and experience, you can find a job that's both challenging and rewarding.
