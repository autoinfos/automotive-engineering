How Much Do Dealership Receptionists Really Make

Written by Aprilia Jun 22, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering becoming a dealership receptionist? Or maybe you're just curious about how much these professionals earn. Either way, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we're going to explore the average salary of dealership receptionists and what factors can affect their earnings.

Working as a dealership receptionist can be demanding, especially when dealing with disgruntled customers, endless phone calls, and scheduling appointments. But what about their salary? According to recent statistics, the average salary for dealership receptionists in the United States is around $29,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on the location, experience, and dealership.

So, how much can you make as a dealership receptionist? As we mentioned earlier, the national average is around $29,000 per year. However, those in larger cities or with more experience can earn up to $35,000 per year. Additionally, some dealerships offer bonuses or commission based on performance, which can increase their earnings.

Personal Experience

As someone who has worked as a dealership receptionist for over 5 years, I can attest to the fact that the job can be both challenging and rewarding. While the salary may not be the highest, the opportunity to work in a fast-paced environment with a supportive team can make it worth it.

Factors That Affect Earnings

Several factors can affect a dealership receptionist's salary, including:

Location

As with many jobs, location can play a significant role in determining how much a dealership receptionist can earn. Those working in larger cities, where the cost of living is higher, can expect to earn more than those in smaller towns or rural areas.

Experience

Experience is a significant factor in determining earnings for any job, including dealership receptionists. Those with more experience can command a higher salary, as they have a better understanding of the job and can handle more responsibilities.

Bonuses and Commission

Some dealerships offer bonuses or commission based on a dealership receptionist's performance. This can include meeting sales goals, providing excellent customer service, or increasing the number of appointments scheduled. These bonuses can significantly increase their earnings, making it a more lucrative position.

Opportunities for Advancement

While working as a dealership receptionist, there are opportunities for advancement within the dealership. This can include becoming a salesperson, service advisor, or even moving into management positions. These positions often come with higher salaries and bonuses, making it an appealing career path for those interested in the automotive industry.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for dealership receptionists?

A: The average hourly wage for dealership receptionists is around $14 per hour.

Q: Do dealership receptionists get benefits?

A: Yes, many dealerships offer benefits packages to their employees, which can include health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: Can dealership receptionists work part-time?

A: Yes, some dealerships offer part-time positions for those who cannot work full-time hours. However, this can affect their salary and benefits.

Q: Are there any educational requirements to become a dealership receptionist?

A: While a high school diploma or GED is typically required, there are no specific educational requirements to become a dealership receptionist. However, some employers may prefer candidates with previous customer service experience.

Conclusion of How Much Do Dealership Receptionists Make

While working as a dealership receptionist may not be the highest-paying job, it can be a rewarding career path for those interested in the automotive industry. Factors such as location, experience, bonuses, and opportunities for advancement can significantly affect their earnings. Overall, dealership receptionists can expect to earn an average salary of around $29,000 per year, with the potential for increased earnings based on performance and location.
