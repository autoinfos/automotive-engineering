How Much Do Automotive Technicians Make A Year

Written by William Sep 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an automotive technician? If so, you may be wondering how much money you can expect to make in this field. Well, you've come to the right place! In this blog post, we'll be discussing the average salary for automotive technicians and what factors can affect your earnings.

It's no secret that money is an important factor when choosing a career. As an automotive technician, you may have concerns about earning a livable wage. After all, working with cars can be physically demanding and requires specialized knowledge and skills.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this number can vary depending on a variety of factors, such as your location, level of experience, and certifications.

In addition to your location and level of experience, other factors that can affect your earnings as an automotive technician include the type of employer you work for and the types of vehicles you specialize in. For example, automotive technicians who work for dealerships typically earn more than those who work for independent repair shops.

My Experience as an Automotive Technician

As a former automotive technician myself, I can attest to the fact that earnings can vary widely in this field. When I first started out, I was making around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my earnings increased significantly.

One thing to keep in mind is that automotive technicians have the potential to earn more money by specializing in certain areas. For example, technicians who specialize in high-end luxury vehicles or hybrid/electric vehicles may earn more money than those who work on standard cars and trucks.

Certifications and Training

If you're serious about pursuing a career as an automotive technician and want to earn a higher salary, it's important to invest in certifications and training. Many employers prefer to hire technicians who have been certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE).

In addition to ASE certifications, there are also many training programs available for automotive technicians. These programs can help you gain the knowledge and skills you need to advance in your career and earn a higher salary.

Factors That Can Affect Your Earnings

As mentioned earlier, there are many factors that can affect your earnings as an automotive technician. Some of the most important factors to consider include your location, level of experience, certifications, and types of vehicles you specialize in.

For example, automotive technicians who work in large metropolitan areas may earn more money than those who work in rural areas. Similarly, technicians who have been working in the field for many years and have advanced certifications may earn more money than those who are just starting out.

Tips for Maximizing Your Earnings as an Automotive Technician

If you want to maximize your earnings as an automotive technician, there are several things you can do. First, invest in certifications and training programs to gain specialized knowledge and skills. Second, consider specializing in high-end luxury vehicles or hybrid/electric vehicles to increase your earning potential. Finally, consider working for a dealership or other high-paying employer.

Conclusion

In conclusion, automotive technicians can earn a livable wage, with the median annual salary being $44,050 as of May 2020. However, your earnings can vary depending on a variety of factors, such as your location, level of experience, and certifications. By investing in certifications and training programs and specializing in certain areas, you can increase your earning potential as an automotive technician.
