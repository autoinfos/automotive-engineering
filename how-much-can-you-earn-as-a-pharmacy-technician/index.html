How Much Can You Earn As A Pharmacy Technician

Written by Christine Oct 02, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in becoming a pharmacy technician but wondering how much you can earn in this field? Look no further, as we provide you with all the information you need to know about the earning potential of a pharmacy technician.

As a pharmacy technician, there can be a few pain points that come to mind when considering your earning potential. For instance, you may be concerned about the starting salary, the highest salaries in the industry, or the factors that determine your earnings.

The average salary of a pharmacy technician varies depending on several factors such as level of experience, education, and location. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for pharmacy technicians in the United States was $34,020 in May 2020. This means that half of pharmacy technicians earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less.

To summarize, the earning potential of a pharmacy technician can vary depending on factors such as experience, education, and location. The median annual wage for pharmacy technicians in the United States was $34,020 in May 2020, with half earning more and the other half earning less.

Starting Salaries for Pharmacy Technicians

The starting salary for a pharmacy technician can vary depending on the employer and the location. In general, entry-level pharmacy technicians can expect to earn between $25,000 and $30,000 per year. However, with experience and additional certifications, pharmacy technicians can earn significantly more.

When I first started as a pharmacy technician, my starting salary was $26,000 per year. However, within a year, I was able to earn a raise and increase my salary to $30,000 per year. This goes to show that with dedication and hard work, you can increase your earning potential as a pharmacy technician.

Highest Salaries for Pharmacy Technicians

The highest salaries for pharmacy technicians are typically found in hospitals, government agencies, and specialty pharmacies. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the top 10 percent of pharmacy technicians earned more than $49,130 per year in May 2020.

Personally, I have found that working in a hospital has provided me with the highest earning potential as a pharmacy technician. The hospital setting requires a higher level of knowledge and expertise, which can lead to higher salaries for pharmacy technicians.

Factors That Determine Earnings for Pharmacy Technicians

Several factors can impact your earning potential as a pharmacy technician. One of the most significant factors is your level of education and experience. Pharmacy technicians with additional certifications, such as a Certified Pharmacy Technician (CPhT) certification, can earn more than those without.

Your location can also impact your earning potential as a pharmacy technician. On average, pharmacy technicians in urban areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas. Additionally, pharmacy technicians in states with higher costs of living, such as California and New York, tend to earn more than those in states with lower costs of living.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as a Pharmacy Technician

If you are looking to increase your earning potential as a pharmacy technician, there are several steps you can take. First, consider obtaining additional certifications, such as a CPhT certification, which can increase your knowledge and expertise in the field. Second, consider working in a hospital or specialty pharmacy, which typically offer higher salaries for pharmacy technicians. Finally, consider relocating to an area with a higher cost of living, which can lead to higher salaries for pharmacy technicians.

Personal Experience with Earning Potential as a Pharmacy Technician

When I first started as a pharmacy technician, I was earning a starting salary of $26,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, I was able to increase my salary to $40,000 per year. Working in a hospital setting has provided me with the highest earning potential as a pharmacy technician, as I am able to utilize my knowledge and expertise to provide the best care for patients.

Question and Answer

Q: Can pharmacy technicians earn a salary comparable to pharmacists?

A: While pharmacy technicians do not earn a salary comparable to pharmacists, they can earn a comfortable living with the potential to earn more with experience and certifications.

Q: What certifications can increase a pharmacy technician's earning potential?

A: A Certified Pharmacy Technician (CPhT) certification can increase a pharmacy technician's earning potential.

Q: Do pharmacy technicians earn more in urban or rural areas?

A: On average, pharmacy technicians in urban areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas.

Q: What is the highest-paying industry for pharmacy technicians?

A: The highest-paying industries for pharmacy technicians are hospitals, government agencies, and specialty pharmacies.

Conclusion of How Much Can You Earn as a Pharmacy Technician

The earning potential of a pharmacy technician can vary depending on several factors such as experience, education, and location. However, with dedication and hard work, pharmacy technicians can earn a comfortable living with the potential to earn significantly more with additional certifications and experience. Working in a hospital or specialty pharmacy can provide the highest earning potential for pharmacy technicians, while obtaining a CPhT certification can increase knowledge and expertise in the field.
