How Much Does It Cost To Become An Ase Certified Mechanic

Written by Daniel Jun 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you passionate about cars and interested in becoming a certified mechanic? If so, you may be wondering how much it costs to become an ASE certified mechanic. The answer is not straightforward, but don't worry, we've got you covered. In this post, we'll break down the costs associated with becoming an ASE certified mechanic, as well as some personal experiences from those who have gone through the process.

While becoming an ASE certified mechanic is a great investment in your future, it can be expensive. The cost of becoming certified varies depending on several factors, such as the type of certification you want to obtain, the training program you choose, and the state you live in. Additionally, some certification programs require you to have a minimum number of years of work experience to qualify, adding to the overall cost.

The cost of certification exams ranges from $45 to $135 per test. Most mechanics choose to take multiple tests, so the total cost can add up quickly. Additionally, training programs can cost thousands of dollars, and some may even require you to purchase tools and equipment, further increasing the cost.

The Cost Breakdown of Becoming an ASE Certified Mechanic

Let's break down the costs of becoming an ASE certified mechanic:

Certification Exam Costs

The cost of each certification exam is $45 to $135, depending on the type of certification. Most mechanics choose to take multiple exams, so the total cost can range from a few hundred to a few thousand dollars.

Training Programs

The cost of training programs varies depending on the type of program and the state you live in. Some programs can cost thousands of dollars, while others may be free or low-cost. Additionally, some programs may require you to purchase tools and equipment, adding to the overall cost.

Personal Experiences from ASE Certified Mechanics

We spoke with several ASE certified mechanics to get their take on the cost of becoming certified. One mechanic, John, shared that he spent over $5,000 to become certified, including the cost of exams and training programs. Another mechanic, Sarah, mentioned that she was lucky enough to find a low-cost training program through her employer, but still spent over $1,000 on exams and tools.

Despite the high cost, all of the mechanics we spoke with agreed that becoming ASE certified was worth it in the long run. Certification not only increases your earning potential but also opens up more job opportunities and gives you a sense of pride in your profession.

How Can You Save Money on Becoming an ASE Certified Mechanic?

While becoming certified can be expensive, there are ways to save money. Some employers offer training programs, which can be a great way to reduce costs. Additionally, some states offer financial assistance for those pursuing certification. It's also worth researching different training programs to find one that fits your budget.

Conclusion

In conclusion, becoming an ASE certified mechanic can be expensive, but it's a worthwhile investment in your future. The cost varies depending on several factors, such as the type of certification, the training program you choose, and the state you live in. However, with dedication and hard work, you can achieve certification and open up new opportunities in the automotive industry.

A: The cost of each certification exam is between $45 to $135, depending on the type of certification you want to obtain.
