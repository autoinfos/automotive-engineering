How Much Does A Car Accident Make Your Insurance Go Up

Written by Daniel Jun 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Car accidents can be a traumatic experience for anyone involved. Not only can they cause physical and emotional pain, but they can also affect your finances. One of the biggest financial concerns after a car accident is how much your insurance premium will increase. In this blog post, we will explore the topic of how much does a car accident make your insurance go up and provide some helpful information to guide you through this process.

Getting into a car accident is never a pleasant experience. Apart from the physical and emotional pain, it can also lead to a significant increase in your insurance premiums. Insurance companies view drivers who have been in accidents as higher risk, which means they are more likely to file a claim in the future. This increased risk is reflected in higher insurance premiums, making it more expensive to maintain coverage.

The amount your insurance premium will go up after a car accident varies depending on several factors. These factors include the severity of the accident, the number of claims you have filed in the past, your driving record, and the insurance company you are with. The general rule of thumb is that the more severe the accident, the higher your premium increase will be.

In summary, how much does a car accident make your insurance go up depends on several factors, but the severity of the accident is the most significant. The more severe the accident, the higher your premium increase will be. Other factors like your driving record and the insurance company you are with also play a role.

My Personal Experience with How Much Does a Car Accident Make Your Insurance Go Up

A few years ago, I was involved in a car accident that left me with a few minor injuries and significant damage to my car. I had to file a claim with my insurance company, and after the dust settled, I was shocked to see that my insurance premium had increased by nearly 50%. It was a significant increase that made it difficult for me to continue to afford insurance. I had to shop around for a new insurance company to get a better rate.

After my personal experience, I did some research and found out that the severity of the accident was the most significant factor in determining how much your insurance premium will increase. This was true in my case, as the accident was severe, and the insurance company deemed me to be high risk.

How to Lower Your Insurance Premium After a Car Accident

If you have been involved in a car accident and are concerned about the increase in your insurance premium, there are a few things you can do to try and lower it. Firstly, shop around and compare rates from different insurance companies. Some companies may offer better rates for drivers who have been in accidents.

You can also consider increasing your deductible, which is the amount you pay out of pocket before your insurance kicks in. By increasing your deductible, you may be able to lower your premium slightly. However, keep in mind that you will have to pay more out of pocket if you are involved in another accident.

The Role of Your Driving Record in Your Insurance Premium Increase

Your driving record is another factor that can affect how much your insurance premium increases after a car accident. If you have a clean driving record and have never been in an accident before, your premium increase may be less severe than someone who has a history of accidents or traffic violations.

How to Avoid an Insurance Premium Increase After a Car Accident

The best way to avoid an insurance premium increase after a car accident is to drive safely and avoid accidents altogether. However, accidents can happen even to the safest drivers. In this case, your best bet is to be honest with your insurance company and provide them with all the necessary information about the accident. They will work with you to determine the best course of action and may be able to offer you some ways to lower your premium increase.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Car Accident Make Your Insurance Go Up

Car accidents can be a stressful and traumatic experience, and the last thing you want to worry about is how much your insurance premium will increase. However, it is a reality that you will have to face if you are involved in an accident. The amount your premium will increase depends on several factors, but the severity of the accident is the most significant. By understanding these factors and taking steps to lower your premium, you can ensure that you are not hit with an unmanageable increase in your insurance premium.

Question and Answer

Q: How much will my insurance premium increase after a car accident?

A: The amount your insurance premium will increase after a car accident depends on several factors, including the severity of the accident, your driving record, and the insurance company you are with.

Q: How long will my insurance premium stay high after a car accident?

A: The length of time your insurance premium stays high after a car accident varies depending on the severity of the accident and your driving record. Typically, your premium will stay high for three to five years.

Q: Will my insurance premium increase if the accident was not my fault?

A: Your insurance premium may still increase if the accident was not your fault. This is because insurance companies view drivers who have been in accidents as higher risk, regardless of who was at fault.

Q: Can I negotiate with my insurance company to lower my premium increase after a car accident?

A: Yes, you can negotiate with your insurance company to lower your premium increase after a car accident. Be honest about the situation and provide them with all the information they need to make a decision.
