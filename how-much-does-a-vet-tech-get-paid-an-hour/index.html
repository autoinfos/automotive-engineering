How Much Does A Vet Tech Get Paid An Hour

Written by Luffy Nov 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a veterinary technician, but unsure about the pay? Or are you a current vet tech wondering if you are making a fair wage? Whatever your situation may be, it is important to understand how much a vet tech gets paid an hour and what factors can affect their salary.

One of the pain points when it comes to vet tech salary is the misconception that they make a low wage. While it is true that vet techs do not typically make as much as other healthcare professionals, it is important to remember that they have a unique skill set and require specialized training. It is also important to note that pay can vary depending on location and experience.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for veterinary technicians in the United States is $16.72 per hour. However, this can range from $12.26 per hour to $24.98 per hour depending on location, experience, and employer. For example, a vet tech working in a high-cost-of-living city may make more than a vet tech working in a rural area.

In summary, a vet tech's hourly wage can vary depending on several factors, including location, experience, and employer. However, the median hourly wage for veterinary technicians in the United States is $16.72 per hour.

Factors That Affect Vet Tech Pay

Location and experience are two major factors that can affect a vet tech's hourly wage. For example, vet techs working in urban areas tend to make more than those working in rural areas due to cost of living differences. Experience can also play a role in pay, as more experienced vet techs may be able to negotiate a higher salary.

Personally, I have experienced the impact of location on my pay. When I worked as a vet tech in a major city, I made significantly more than when I worked in a small town. However, I also found that my cost of living was higher in the city, so it is important to consider all factors when evaluating pay.

Employer Differences in Pay

Employer can also play a role in a vet tech's hourly wage. For example, a vet tech working at a specialty animal hospital may make more than one working at a general practice. Additionally, some employers may offer benefits such as health insurance or retirement plans, which can impact overall compensation.

When I worked at a specialty animal hospital, I made more than at a general practice due to the specialized skills required. However, I also had to work longer hours and on weekends, so it is important to consider all aspects of a job when evaluating pay.

Vet Tech Certification and Education

Obtaining certification as a vet tech can also impact hourly wage. In some states, certification is required to work as a vet tech, while in others it is optional. However, even in states where it is not required, certification can lead to higher pay and job opportunities.

Additionally, advanced education such as a bachelor's or associate's degree in veterinary technology can also lead to higher pay and advancement opportunities.

Negotiating Vet Tech Pay

If you are a vet tech looking to negotiate your pay, it is important to do your research and know your worth. Consider factors such as location, experience, and employer when determining what you should be making. It is also important to be confident in your skills and be willing to negotiate for a fair wage.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Vet Tech Get Paid an Hour

Overall, a vet tech can expect to make a median hourly wage of $16.72 per hour in the United States. However, pay can vary depending on several factors, including location, experience, and employer. It is important to consider all aspects of a job when evaluating pay and to be willing to negotiate for a fair wage.
