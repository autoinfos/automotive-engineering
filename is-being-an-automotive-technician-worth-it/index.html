Is Being An Automotive Technician Worth It

Written by Daniel Sep 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you love cars? Are you good at fixing things? Then you may have considered becoming an automotive technician. But is it worth it? Is the time and money spent on education and training worth the potential career opportunities? Let's take a closer look.

One of the biggest pain points of becoming an automotive technician is the cost of education and training. It can be expensive to attend a technical school or community college, and the process can take several years. Additionally, the job can be physically demanding and may require working in less-than-ideal conditions.

However, if you have a passion for cars and enjoy working with your hands, becoming an automotive technician can be a fulfilling career choice. It offers job security, as there will always be a need for mechanics to repair and maintain cars. Additionally, the pay can be competitive, especially for those with specialized skills or certifications.

In summary, being an automotive technician is worth it if you have a passion for cars and enjoy working with your hands. While the cost of education and training can be high, the job offers job security and competitive pay.

Personal Experience with Becoming an Automotive Technician

When I was younger, I loved tinkering with cars and fixing things. After high school, I decided to attend a technical school to become an automotive technician. While the program was challenging and required a lot of hard work, it was worth it in the end. I now have a fulfilling career doing something I love, and I never have to worry about job security.

Specializing as an Automotive Technician

One way to increase your job opportunities and earning potential as an automotive technician is to specialize in a particular area. For example, you could become certified in hybrid car repair or learn how to work on diesel engines. By having a specialized skillset, you may be able to command a higher salary and have more job opportunities available to you.

What to Look for in a Technical School

If you decide to pursue a career as an automotive technician, it's important to choose the right technical school. Look for a school that is accredited and has a good reputation in the industry. Additionally, make sure the program offers hands-on training and prepares you for certification exams.

How to Continue Learning as an Automotive Technician

Even after you complete your education and training, it's important to continue learning and staying up-to-date with the latest technology and industry trends. Attend conferences and workshops, and consider pursuing additional certifications to stay competitive in the job market.

Personal Growth as an Automotive Technician

One of the most rewarding aspects of being an automotive technician is the opportunity for personal growth. As you gain experience and learn new skills, you'll become more confident in your abilities and may even have the opportunity to mentor others.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the job outlook for automotive technicians?
A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 6 percent from 2016 to 2026, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: How much do automotive technicians make?
A: The median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $42,090 in May 2019.

Q: Can you become an automotive technician without going to school?
A: While it's possible to learn the skills needed to become an automotive technician on the job, attending a technical school or community college can provide a more structured education and may lead to better job opportunities.

Q: What certifications are available for automotive technicians?
A: There are several certifications available for automotive technicians, including those offered by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) and the Automotive Service Association (ASA).

Conclusion of Is Being an Automotive Technician Worth It

If you have a passion for cars and enjoy working with your hands, becoming an automotive technician can be a rewarding career choice. While the cost of education and training can be high, the job offers job security and competitive pay. By specializing in a particular area and continuing to learn and grow, you can increase your earning potential and advance your career.
