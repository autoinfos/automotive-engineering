Entry Level Helicopter Mechanic Jobs Everything You Need To Know

Written by Petter Sep 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you always been fascinated by helicopters and want to turn your passion into a career? Becoming an entry level helicopter mechanic could be the perfect job for you! In this blog post, we will dive into everything you need to know about entry level helicopter mechanic jobs, including their target, skills required, and potential career paths.

Working as an entry level helicopter mechanic can be a challenging job, but it can also be extremely rewarding. The job requires attention to detail, strong problem-solving skills, and the ability to work under pressure. Additionally, the job can be physically demanding as it involves working with heavy machinery, often in outdoor environments.

If you are interested in becoming an entry level helicopter mechanic, the target is to gain a basic understanding of how helicopters work, as well as knowledge of the different types of helicopter components and systems. This can be achieved through a combination of on-the-job training and attending technical schools or vocational programs.

In summary, entry level helicopter mechanic jobs require a combination of technical skills and physical agility. The job can be challenging, but it can also be extremely rewarding for those who have a passion for aviation and a desire to work in a hands-on environment.

What Skills Do You Need for Entry Level Helicopter Mechanic Jobs?

Entry level helicopter mechanic jobs require a variety of skills, including:

Attention to Detail: As a helicopter mechanic, you will need to have a keen eye for detail to ensure that all components are installed and working correctly.

Problem-Solving: You will need to be able to troubleshoot problems and identify solutions quickly and efficiently.

Physical Agility: The job can be physically demanding, so you will need to be able to lift heavy objects and work in awkward positions.

Communication Skills: You will need to be able to communicate effectively with other mechanics and pilots to ensure that everyone is on the same page.

Additionally, having a basic understanding of math and physics can be beneficial when working as an entry level helicopter mechanic.

What Are Some Career Paths for Entry Level Helicopter Mechanics?

Working as an entry level helicopter mechanic can open up many career paths in the aviation industry. Some potential career paths include:

Aircraft Mechanic: After gaining experience as an entry level helicopter mechanic, you could potentially transition to working on fixed-wing aircraft.

Avionics Technician: If you have an interest in electronics, you could specialize in avionics and work on the electronic systems of helicopters and other aircraft.

Helicopter Pilot: If you have a desire to fly helicopters, working as a helicopter mechanic can be a great way to gain experience and potentially transition into a career as a pilot.

How to Find Entry Level Helicopter Mechanic Jobs?

There are several ways to find entry level helicopter mechanic jobs, including:

Online Job Boards: Websites like Indeed, Glassdoor, and LinkedIn can be great resources for finding job openings in the aviation industry.

Networking: Attending industry events or joining professional organizations can help you connect with others in the industry and potentially lead to job openings.

Apprenticeships: Some helicopter maintenance companies offer apprenticeship programs that can provide on-the-job training and help you gain experience.

How Much Do Entry Level Helicopter Mechanics Make?

The salary for entry level helicopter mechanics can vary depending on the employer, location, and experience. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 as of May 2020.

Conclusion of Entry Level Helicopter Mechanic Jobs

Working as an entry level helicopter mechanic can be a challenging but rewarding job for those who have a passion for aviation and enjoy working in a hands-on environment. The job requires a combination of technical skills, physical agility, and problem-solving abilities. Additionally, entry level helicopter mechanic jobs can open up many career paths in the aviation industry, including working as an aircraft mechanic or avionics technician, or even becoming a helicopter pilot.
