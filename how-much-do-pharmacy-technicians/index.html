How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Make

Written by Aprilia Oct 28, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you interested in becoming a pharmacy technician but unsure about the pay? Well, you're not alone! Many people wonder how much do pharmacy technicians make, and it's a valid question. After all, we all want to know if our career choice will provide a comfortable living.

As with any job, there are pros and cons, and the salary is just one of the factors to consider. However, it can be a significant pain point for some individuals, especially those who are trying to support a family or pay off debt.

So, let's get to the big question - how much do pharmacy technicians make?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for pharmacy technicians in the United States is $35,100. However, this can vary depending on location, experience, and employer.

My Experience with Pharmacy Technician Salaries

When I first started as a pharmacy technician, I was making around $28,000 per year. However, after gaining more experience and working in a hospital setting, my salary increased to over $40,000 per year. It's essential to note that working in a hospital or specialty pharmacy can provide higher pay than working in a retail setting.

Factors that Affect Pharmacy Technician Salaries

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect how much pharmacy technicians make. Here are a few of the most significant factors:

Location

The cost of living and demand for pharmacy technicians can affect salaries in different areas. For example, pharmacy technicians in California tend to make more than those in Alabama due to the higher cost of living and demand for healthcare services.

Experience

As with any job, experience can play a significant role in salary. Pharmacy technicians with more experience and certifications may be able to negotiate higher pay rates.

Benefits of Becoming a Pharmacy Technician

Although salary is undoubtedly an essential factor, there are many benefits to becoming a pharmacy technician. For one, it's a stable career path with many opportunities for growth and advancement. Additionally, it's a rewarding career where you can help people every day.

Question and Answer

Q: Do pharmacy technicians get paid overtime?

A: It depends on the employer. Some employers may offer overtime pay, while others may not. It's essential to review your employer's policies regarding overtime pay.

Q: Can pharmacy technicians work from home?

A: While it's possible to work from home as a pharmacy technician, it's not very common. Most pharmacy technicians work on-site in a retail, hospital, or specialty pharmacy setting.

Q: What certifications can pharmacy technicians obtain to increase their salary?

A: Pharmacy technicians can obtain several certifications, such as the Certified Pharmacy Technician (CPhT) certification, which can lead to higher salaries and more job opportunities.

Q: Can pharmacy technicians become pharmacists?

A: Yes, it's possible for pharmacy technicians to become pharmacists. However, it requires additional education and training.

Conclusion of How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians

Overall, pharmacy technician salaries can vary depending on several factors, but the median annual salary is $35,100. While salary is an essential factor, there are many benefits to becoming a pharmacy technician, such as job stability and the opportunity to help people every day.
