Average Mechanical Engineer Salary In Uae Everything You Need To Know

Written by Bardi Oct 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanical engineer in the UAE? Do you want to know how much you can expect to earn in this field? If so, you’ve come to the right place. In this blog post, we’ll explore everything you need to know about average mechanical engineer salary in UAE, including pain points, target, personal experiences, and more.

It’s no secret that engineering is a lucrative field, and mechanical engineering is no exception. However, like any profession, there are both pros and cons to consider. For example, while mechanical engineers in the UAE can earn a high salary, they may also face stiff competition for jobs and longer work hours.

So, what is the average mechanical engineer salary in UAE? According to Payscale, the average salary for a mechanical engineer in the UAE is AED 92,000 per year. However, salaries can vary widely depending on factors such as experience, industry, and location.

In this article, we’ve covered the basics of average mechanical engineer salary in UAE, including pain points and target. In summary, mechanical engineers in the UAE can earn a high salary, but may face competition and long work hours. Let’s dive deeper into the topic with some personal experiences and more detailed explanations.

What is the Target of Average Mechanical Engineer Salary in UAE?

As mentioned earlier, the target of average mechanical engineer salary in UAE is around AED 92,000 per year. However, it’s important to note that this is just an average, and salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors.

For example, my friend who works as a mechanical engineer in Dubai earns significantly more than the average salary. He has several years of experience and works for a large engineering firm in the oil and gas industry. On the other hand, another friend who is just starting out in the field is earning less than the average salary.

My Personal Experience with Average Mechanical Engineer Salary in UAE

As a mechanical engineer myself, I’ve experienced both the pros and cons of working in this field in the UAE. On the one hand, I earn a comfortable salary and have opportunities for career growth. On the other hand, the job can be demanding, with long work hours and high stress levels.

That being said, I believe that the benefits outweigh the drawbacks. I enjoy the challenge of my work and the satisfaction of seeing a project through from start to finish. Plus, the high salary is definitely a nice perk!

Factors that Affect Average Mechanical Engineer Salary in UAE

Now, let’s take a closer look at some of the factors that can affect average mechanical engineer salary in UAE:

  • Experience: Generally, the more experience you have, the higher your salary will be.
  • Industry: Mechanical engineers can work in a variety of industries, each with its own salary range. For example, those working in the oil and gas industry may earn more than those working in manufacturing.
  • Location: Salaries can vary widely depending on where you live and work in the UAE. Dubai and Abu Dhabi are generally considered to be the highest-paying cities.

How to Increase Your Average Mechanical Engineer Salary in UAE

If you’re looking to increase your average mechanical engineer salary in UAE, there are a few steps you can take:

  • Gain experience: As mentioned earlier, experience is one of the most important factors in determining salary. Look for opportunities to take on more responsibility and build your skills.
  • Get certified: Earning a certification in a specialized area of mechanical engineering can help you stand out from the competition and earn a higher salary.
  • Network: Attend industry events and conferences to meet other professionals in your field and learn about new job opportunities.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average starting salary for a mechanical engineer in UAE?

A: The average starting salary for a mechanical engineer in UAE is around AED 60,000 per year.

Q: How much can a mechanical engineer with 10 years of experience expect to earn in UAE?

A: A mechanical engineer with 10 years of experience can expect to earn around AED 150,000 per year in UAE.

Q: What industries offer the highest salaries for mechanical engineers in UAE?

A: The oil and gas industry and the aerospace industry are among the highest-paying industries for mechanical engineers in UAE.

Q: What skills are important for a mechanical engineer to have in order to earn a high salary in UAE?

A: In addition to technical skills, soft skills such as communication, problem-solving, and leadership can help a mechanical engineer earn a higher salary in UAE.

Conclusion of Average Mechanical Engineer Salary in UAE

Overall, average mechanical engineer salary in UAE is quite high compared to many other fields. However, it’s important to remember that salaries can vary widely depending on factors such as experience, industry, and location. By gaining experience, earning certifications, and networking with other professionals, you can increase your chances of earning a higher salary in this field.
