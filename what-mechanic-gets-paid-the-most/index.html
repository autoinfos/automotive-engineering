What Mechanic Gets Paid The Most

Written by William Jun 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in becoming a mechanic but wondering what kind of mechanic gets paid the most? Look no further! In this article, we will discuss the different types of mechanics and which ones have the highest earning potential.

When it comes to choosing a career, salary is often a major factor. After all, we all want to make a decent living and provide for ourselves and our families. However, it's important to remember that salary isn't everything. Job satisfaction, work-life balance, and opportunities for growth and advancement should also be considered when making a career choice.

So, what mechanic gets paid the most? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, aircraft mechanics and service technicians have the highest median annual salary among all types of mechanics, earning a median salary of $64,090 as of May 2020. This is followed by heavy vehicle and mobile equipment service technicians, who earn a median salary of $51,590 per year.

To summarize, if you're looking to become a mechanic with the highest earning potential, consider becoming an aircraft mechanic or a heavy vehicle and mobile equipment service technician. However, it's important to also consider other factors such as job satisfaction and work-life balance when making your career choice.

What is an Aircraft Mechanic?

As mentioned earlier, aircraft mechanics have the highest earning potential among all types of mechanics. But what exactly does an aircraft mechanic do? Essentially, aircraft mechanics are responsible for ensuring that airplanes and other aircrafts are in safe and working order. They inspect, maintain, and repair aircraft components such as engines, landing gear, and brakes.

Personally, I have a friend who is an aircraft mechanic and he loves his job. He enjoys working with his hands and loves the feeling of fixing something and seeing it fly off into the sky. He also enjoys the challenging and rewarding nature of his work, as well as the high level of responsibility that comes with ensuring the safety of passengers and crew.

What is a Heavy Vehicle and Mobile Equipment Service Technician?

Heavy vehicle and mobile equipment service technicians, on the other hand, work with large vehicles and equipment such as bulldozers, cranes, and tractors. They inspect, maintain, and repair the engines, transmissions, and other components of these vehicles and equipment.

I have another friend who is a heavy vehicle and mobile equipment service technician. He enjoys his job because he gets to work with his hands and solve problems on a daily basis. He also enjoys the variety of work that comes with working on different types of vehicles and equipment.

Salary Differences Between Types of Mechanics

While aircraft mechanics and heavy vehicle and mobile equipment service technicians have the highest earning potential among all types of mechanics, it's important to note that salaries can vary depending on a variety of factors such as location, experience, and education.

For example, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the highest paying industries for aircraft mechanics and service technicians are the federal executive branch, scheduled air transportation, and aerospace product and parts manufacturing. Similarly, the highest paying industries for heavy vehicle and mobile equipment service technicians are local government, natural gas distribution, and truck transportation.

How to Become a Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming a mechanic, there are a few different paths you can take. Some people choose to attend a vocational or technical school to obtain a certificate or associate's degree in automotive technology or a related field. Others may choose to complete an apprenticeship or on-the-job training program.

Regardless of which path you choose, it's important to have a strong foundation in math, science, and problem-solving skills. You should also be comfortable working with your hands and have good communication skills.

Job Outlook for Mechanics

The job outlook for mechanics is generally positive, with overall employment projected to grow 3% from 2020 to 2030. However, job growth may vary depending on the specific type of mechanic and industry.

For example, employment of aircraft mechanics and service technicians is projected to grow 5% from 2020 to 2030, while employment of heavy vehicle and mobile equipment service technicians is projected to grow 4% over the same time period.
