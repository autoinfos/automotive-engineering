What is Trade School for Auto Body Painting?

Trade school for auto body painting is a type of career-focused education that provides students with the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the industry. Courses typically cover a range of topics, including surface preparation, paint mixing and application, color matching, and more. Students also gain hands-on experience through lab work and internships, which can help them build their portfolios and make connections with professionals in the field.

During my time in trade school for auto body painting, I learned the ins and outs of the industry. FAQs About Trade School for Auto Body Painting

What kind of jobs can I get with a trade school degree in auto body painting?

There are many jobs available in the auto body painting industry, including auto body painter, auto body repair technician, paint technician, and more.

What kind of education is required to attend trade school for auto body painting?

Most trade schools require a high school diploma or GED, as well as a willingness to learn and work hard.

How long does it take to complete a trade school program in auto body painting?

Most trade school programs in auto body painting take between 6 months and 2 years to complete, depending on the program and the student's level of commitment.

How much can I expect to earn after completing a trade school program in auto body painting?

Salaries for auto body painters and other professionals in the industry vary depending on experience, location, and other factors. However, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive body and glass repairers was $44,570 as of May 2020.
