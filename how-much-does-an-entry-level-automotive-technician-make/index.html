How Much Does An Entry Level Automotive Technician Make

Written by Eveline Nov 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an entry level automotive technician? Do you wonder how much you can earn in this field? If so, you are not alone. Many people are interested in this career, but they are not sure if it is financially feasible. In this post, we will explore the earning potential of an entry level automotive technician.

Being an entry level automotive technician can be challenging, and it comes with its own set of pain points. You will be working long hours, sometimes in uncomfortable positions. You will also need to have strong problem-solving skills and attention to detail. Additionally, you may need to invest in your own tools and equipment, which can be expensive.

According to the United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $42,090 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $24,090, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $70,820. Entry level automotive technicians typically earn less than the median annual wage.

In summary, entry level automotive technicians can expect to earn a salary that is lower than the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics. However, the earning potential can increase with experience and expertise. Automotive technicians who specialize in certain areas, such as diesel engines or electronic systems, may also earn higher salaries.

What is the Entry Level Salary for an Automotive Technician?

When I started my career as an entry level automotive technician, I earned around $30,000 per year. This was lower than the median annual wage, but I was able to gain valuable experience and build my skills. As I gained more experience and expertise, my salary increased.

How Much Can an Experienced Automotive Technician Earn?

Experienced automotive technicians can earn a higher salary than entry level technicians. According to the United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, the highest 10 percent of automotive service technicians and mechanics earned more than $70,820 as of May 2020. Technicians who specialize in certain areas, such as diesel engines or electronic systems, may also earn higher salaries.

Factors That Affect an Automotive Technician's Salary

Several factors can affect an automotive technician's salary. These include:

1. Experience: Experienced technicians typically earn a higher salary than entry level technicians.

2. Location: Salaries can vary depending on the location of the job. Technicians who work in urban areas or regions with a high cost of living may earn more than technicians who work in rural areas.

3. Specialization: Technicians who specialize in certain areas, such as diesel engines or electronic systems, may earn higher salaries.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as an Automotive Technician

If you want to increase your earning potential as an automotive technician, there are several steps you can take. These include:

1. Gain experience: The more experience you have, the more valuable you will be to potential employers.

2. Specialize: Consider specializing in a certain area, such as diesel engines or electronic systems.

3. Stay up-to-date: Keep up with the latest technology and advancements in the industry.

4. Consider additional certifications: Obtaining additional certifications can demonstrate your expertise and increase your earning potential.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Entry Level Automotive Technician Make

In conclusion, entry level automotive technicians can expect to earn a salary that is lower than the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics. However, with experience and expertise, the earning potential can increase. Technicians who specialize in certain areas may also earn higher salaries. By gaining experience, specializing, staying up-to-date, and obtaining additional certifications, you can increase your earning potential as an automotive technician.
