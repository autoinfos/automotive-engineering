Electrical Engineer Salary In Germany All You Need To Know

Written by Frank Jul 31, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you an electrical engineer planning to move to Germany for work? Or are you simply curious about the average salary of electrical engineers in Germany? Whatever the reason may be, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we'll be exploring all aspects of electrical engineer salary in Germany.

It's no secret that electrical engineering is a highly demanding and competitive field. Electrical engineers are responsible for designing, developing, and testing electrical equipment and systems, making them an integral part of any industry. However, the high demand for electrical engineers also means that they are highly sought after, and their salaries can vary greatly depending on various factors such as experience, location, and industry.

So, what is the average salary of an electrical engineer in Germany? According to data from Payscale, the average salary of an electrical engineer in Germany is €53,000 per year. However, this can vary greatly depending on the industry you work in, your level of experience, and the location you work in. For example, electrical engineers working in the automotive industry can expect to earn significantly more than those working in the renewable energy industry.

In summary, electrical engineer salary in Germany is highly dependent on various factors such as industry, experience, and location. However, the average salary for an electrical engineer in Germany is €53,000 per year.

Factors Affecting Electrical Engineer Salary in Germany

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect the salary of an electrical engineer in Germany. Let's take a look at some of the most important ones:

Industry

The industry you work in can have a significant impact on your salary as an electrical engineer in Germany. For example, electrical engineers working in the automotive industry can expect to earn significantly more than those working in the renewable energy industry.

Location

The location you work in can also have a significant impact on your salary as an electrical engineer in Germany. For example, electrical engineers working in cities such as Munich and Frankfurt can expect to earn more than those working in smaller cities or rural areas.

My Personal Experience with Electrical Engineer Salary in Germany

As an electrical engineer who recently moved to Germany, I can attest to the fact that the salary can vary greatly depending on various factors. I work in the renewable energy industry and earn approximately €45,000 per year, which is slightly lower than the average salary for an electrical engineer in Germany. However, I have colleagues who work in the automotive industry and earn significantly more than I do.

Tips for Negotiating Electrical Engineer Salary in Germany

If you're an electrical engineer planning to move to Germany for work, here are some tips for negotiating your salary:

Do Your Research

Before negotiating your salary, it's important to do your research and find out what the average salary is for someone in your position and industry. This will give you a better idea of what you can expect to earn and help you negotiate effectively.

Highlight Your Skills and Experience

When negotiating your salary, it's important to highlight your skills and experience and emphasize the value you can bring to the company. This will show your employer that you are a valuable asset and increase your chances of getting a higher salary.
