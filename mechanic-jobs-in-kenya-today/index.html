Job Types for Mechanic Jobs in Kenya Today

There are many different types of mechanic jobs in Kenya today, each of which requires a different set of skills and expertise. Some of the most common types of mechanic jobs in Kenya include:

Automotive Technician: An automotive technician is responsible for diagnosing and repairing mechanical problems in cars and trucks. This may involve working on engines, transmissions, brakes, and other systems.

Heavy Equipment Mechanic: A heavy equipment mechanic is responsible for maintaining and repairing large machines, such as bulldozers, cranes, and excavators. This type of mechanic job requires specialized training and expertise.

Motorcycle Mechanic: A motorcycle mechanic specializes in repairing and maintaining motorcycles and other two-wheeled vehicles. This type of mechanic job requires a unique set of skills and expertise.

Diesel Mechanic: A diesel mechanic is responsible for maintaining and repairing diesel engines in trucks, buses, and other heavy vehicles. This type of mechanic job requires specialized training and expertise.

Q&A about Mechanic Jobs in Kenya Today

A: The average salary for a mechanic in Kenya varies depending on the type of job and the level of experience. Entry-level mechanics can expect to earn around Ksh 20,000-30,000 per month, while more experienced mechanics can earn upwards of Ksh 50,000 per month.
