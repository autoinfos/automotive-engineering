How To Become A Radiology Tech In Georgia

Written by Frank Aug 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in a career in radiology? Becoming a radiology tech in Georgia can be a rewarding career choice with a growing demand for healthcare professionals. In this post, we will discuss the process of becoming a radiology tech in Georgia and provide tips to help you succeed.

Pain Points

Before we dive into the steps to become a radiology tech in Georgia, let's talk about some common pain points that many people face when pursuing this career path. Some of these include:

  • Not knowing where to start
  • Feeling overwhelmed by the requirements
  • Difficulty finding the right program or school
  • Concerns about the cost of education

Answering the Target

The first step to becoming a radiology tech in Georgia is to obtain the proper education and training. Most radiology techs have an associate's degree in radiologic technology, which typically takes two years to complete. You can find programs at community colleges, technical schools, and some universities in Georgia.

Once you have completed your education, you will need to become certified by the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT). This requires passing an exam and meeting certain criteria, such as completing an accredited program and adhering to ethical standards.

Finally, you will need to obtain a license from the Georgia Composite Medical Board to practice as a radiologic technologist in Georgia. This requires submitting an application and paying a fee, as well as passing a background check and meeting other requirements.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, to become a radiology tech in Georgia:

  1. Obtain an associate's degree in radiologic technology
  2. Become certified by the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists
  3. Obtain a license from the Georgia Composite Medical Board

Education and Training

When I decided to pursue a career as a radiology tech, I was unsure where to start. I did some research and found several programs in Georgia that offered an associate's degree in radiologic technology. I ultimately chose a community college program that was affordable and had a good reputation.

The program was challenging, but also rewarding. I learned about anatomy, patient care, and how to operate imaging equipment. I also completed clinical rotations to gain hands-on experience in a variety of healthcare settings.

One thing I found helpful was joining a student organization for radiologic technologists. This provided me with opportunities to network with professionals in the field and attend conferences and workshops.

Certification and Licensing

After completing my education, I applied to take the ARRT exam. This required paying a fee and meeting certain eligibility criteria, such as completing an accredited program and following ethical standards. I studied for the exam using study guides and online resources, and was able to pass on my first attempt.

Once I was certified, I applied for a license from the Georgia Composite Medical Board. This involved submitting an application and paying a fee, as well as passing a background check and meeting other requirements. It was a lengthy process, but ultimately worth it to be able to practice as a radiologic technologist in Georgia.

Continuing Education

After obtaining my license, I knew it was important to continue learning and growing as a healthcare professional. I participate in continuing education courses and attend conferences to stay up-to-date on the latest advancements in radiology.

Continuing education is also required to maintain certification with the ARRT. This involves completing a certain number of continuing education credits every two years, which can be obtained through various sources such as online courses, workshops, and conferences.

Career Opportunities

As a radiologic technologist in Georgia, there are many career opportunities available. Some common settings include hospitals, clinics, and imaging centers. There are also opportunities to specialize in certain areas, such as mammography or computed tomography (CT).

Many radiologic technologists also choose to pursue further education and training to become a radiologist assistant or pursue other healthcare careers.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to become a radiology tech in Georgia?

A: Most radiology techs have an associate's degree in radiologic technology, which typically takes two years to complete.

Q: What is the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists?

A: The American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT) is the primary certification organization for radiologic technologists in the United States.

Q: What is the Georgia Composite Medical Board?

A: The Georgia Composite Medical Board is the regulatory agency responsible for licensing and regulating medical professionals in Georgia, including radiologic technologists.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement in the field of radiology?

A: Yes, many radiologic technologists choose to pursue further education and training to become a radiologist assistant or pursue other healthcare careers.

Conclusion of How to Become a Radiology Tech in Georgia

Becoming a radiology tech in Georgia can be a challenging but rewarding career choice. By obtaining the proper education and training, becoming certified and licensed, and continuing to learn and grow as a healthcare professional, you can succeed in this field. Good luck on your journey!
