Why Pharmacy Technician Is A Good Career Choice

Written by Luffy Jul 23, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you looking for a career where you can help people and make a difference in their lives? Do you want to work in the healthcare field without having to go to medical school? If so, becoming a pharmacy technician might be the perfect career for you. Pharmacy technicians play an essential role in the healthcare industry. They work alongside pharmacists to fill prescriptions, assist patients, and manage inventory. However, many people don't realize that pharmacy technicians have a range of responsibilities and opportunities for growth. If you're considering a career as a pharmacy technician, here are some reasons why it might be the right choice for you.

What is a Pharmacy Technician?

A pharmacy technician is a healthcare professional who works under the supervision of a licensed pharmacist. They help prepare and dispense medications, manage inventory, and assist patients with questions or concerns. Pharmacy technicians work in a variety of settings, including hospitals, retail pharmacies, and long-term care facilities.

The Benefits of Becoming a Pharmacy Technician

One of the biggest advantages of becoming a pharmacy technician is the job security. The demand for pharmacy technicians is expected to grow by 4% over the next ten years, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. This growth is due to an aging population and an increased need for prescription medications. Another benefit of becoming a pharmacy technician is the opportunity for career advancement. With experience and additional training, pharmacy technicians can become certified and take on more responsibilities, such as compounding medications or managing a pharmacy.

My Experience as a Pharmacy Technician

As a pharmacy technician myself, I can attest to the many benefits of this career. Not only do I get to help people every day, but I also have the opportunity to learn about new medications and treatments. Plus, the job is never boring - there's always something new to learn or a new challenge to tackle.

The Training and Education Required to Become a Pharmacy Technician

To become a pharmacy technician, you typically need a high school diploma or equivalent. Most pharmacy technicians receive on-the-job training, but some employers may require completion of a pharmacy technician program. These programs can be found at community colleges, vocational schools, and online.

FAQs About Becoming a Pharmacy Technician

Q: How much does a pharmacy technician make?
A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for pharmacy technicians was $35,100 in May 2019. Q: Is becoming a pharmacy technician a good career choice?
A: Yes! Pharmacy technicians have a range of responsibilities and opportunities for growth, and the demand for this profession is expected to grow in the coming years. Q: Do pharmacy technicians need to be certified?
A: Certification is not required in all states, but many employers prefer or require it. The Pharmacy Technician Certification Board and the National Healthcareer Association both offer certification exams. Q: What skills do pharmacy technicians need?
A: Pharmacy technicians need strong attention to detail, communication skills, and the ability to work in a fast-paced environment.

Conclusion

In conclusion, becoming a pharmacy technician is a great career choice for anyone looking to make a difference in the healthcare industry. With job security, opportunities for growth, and the ability to help patients every day, it's no wonder that pharmacy technician is a popular and rewarding career path.
