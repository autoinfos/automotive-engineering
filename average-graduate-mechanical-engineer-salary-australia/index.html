Average Graduate Mechanical Engineer Salary In Australia

Written by Daniel Jun 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a recent graduate in mechanical engineering wondering about your salary prospects in Australia? You're not alone. Many young engineers are curious about what they can expect to earn in their first job. In this blog post, we'll explore the average graduate mechanical engineer salary in Australia, the factors that affect it, and what you can do to increase your earning potential.

What are the Pain Points Related to Average Graduate Mechanical Engineer Salary Australia?

Graduates often face the challenge of finding a job that pays well and aligns with their career goals. In the field of mechanical engineering, there is a lot of competition for entry-level positions, which can drive down salaries. Additionally, the cost of living in Australia's major cities can be high, making it difficult for new graduates to make ends meet on a starting salary.

What is the Average Graduate Mechanical Engineer Salary in Australia?

According to data from PayScale, the average salary for a graduate mechanical engineer in Australia is AUD 61,000 per year. This figure can vary depending on factors such as location, industry, and level of experience. For example, graduates working in the mining and resources sector can earn significantly more than those in other industries.

Additionally, mechanical engineers with postgraduate qualifications or experience in specialized areas such as robotics or renewable energy can command higher salaries. It's important to note that salaries can also vary depending on the specific employer, with larger companies generally offering higher salaries than small businesses.

My Personal Experience with Average Graduate Mechanical Engineer Salary in Australia

As a recent graduate in mechanical engineering, I was initially offered a starting salary of AUD 55,000 per year. While this was lower than I had hoped for, I knew that gaining experience in my field was more important than earning a high salary straight away. Over time, I was able to negotiate a higher salary and take on more responsibility in my role, which has helped to increase my earning potential.

How Can You Increase Your Average Graduate Mechanical Engineer Salary in Australia?

There are several ways to increase your earning potential as a graduate mechanical engineer in Australia. One of the most effective is to gain experience in your field by taking on internships, work placements, or part-time jobs while you're studying. This can help you stand out from other graduates and demonstrate your skills and knowledge to potential employers.

Another way to increase your salary is to specialize in a particular area of mechanical engineering. This can involve gaining postgraduate qualifications or developing expertise in areas such as automation, renewable energy, or materials science. Employers are often willing to pay higher salaries to engineers with specialized skills and knowledge.

Factors That Affect Average Graduate Mechanical Engineer Salary in Australia

There are several factors that can affect the salary you can expect to earn as a graduate mechanical engineer in Australia. Some of the most important include:

  • Location: Salaries can vary depending on the cost of living in different parts of Australia. For example, graduates working in Sydney or Melbourne can expect to earn more than those in regional areas.
  • Industry: Graduates working in industries such as mining and resources, aerospace, or defense can earn higher salaries than those in other industries.
  • Experience: Graduates with work experience, internships, or research experience can command higher salaries than those with no experience.

How to Negotiate Your Average Graduate Mechanical Engineer Salary in Australia

If you're not happy with the salary you're offered as a graduate mechanical engineer in Australia, it's important to negotiate with your employer. This can involve researching the average salaries for your role in your industry and location, and presenting this information to your employer to show that you're being paid below market rates.

You can also negotiate on other factors such as working hours, leave entitlements, or professional development opportunities. Remember to be polite, professional, and prepared to compromise to reach a mutually beneficial agreement.

Conclusion of Average Graduate Mechanical Engineer Salary in Australia

If you're a recent graduate in mechanical engineering, it's natural to be curious about your salary prospects in Australia. While the average starting salary may not be as high as you'd like, there are several ways to increase your earning potential over time. By gaining experience, specializing in a particular area, and negotiating with your employer, you can build a successful career in mechanical engineering and earn a salary that reflects your skills and expertise.
