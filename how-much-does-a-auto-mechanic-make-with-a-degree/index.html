How Much Does An Auto Mechanic Make With A Degree

Written by Petter Aug 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering becoming an auto mechanic but wondering how much you can make with a degree? The automotive industry is growing, and there is a high demand for skilled professionals. However, the salaries of auto mechanics can vary depending on various factors, such as location, experience, and education level. In this blog post, we will discuss the earning potential of auto mechanics with a degree and what you can expect from this career path.

Auto mechanics are essential in keeping vehicles running smoothly, and they play a crucial role in the transportation industry. However, the low barrier to entry and the relatively high demand for auto mechanics mean that the salaries for this profession can be lower compared to other skilled trades. Additionally, without a degree, the salaries can be even lower. Therefore, if you are considering a career in auto mechanics, getting a degree can increase your earning potential.

The average salary for an auto mechanic with a degree is around $50,000 to $60,000 per year. However, the salaries can range from around $30,000 to over $100,000 per year depending on various factors. For example, auto mechanics working in large cities or metropolitan areas tend to earn more than those working in rural areas. Similarly, auto mechanics with more experience or additional certifications tend to earn higher salaries than those starting in the field.

In summary, the earning potential for auto mechanics with a degree can be quite good, with an average salary of $50,000 to $60,000 per year. However, the salaries can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and education level.

Personal Experience: My Journey as an Auto Mechanic with a Degree

When I graduated from high school, I wasn't sure what career path to take. I always had an interest in cars and mechanics, so I decided to pursue a degree in auto mechanics. After completing my degree, I started working at a local auto repair shop. At first, my salary was relatively low, but as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased. Now, several years later, I am earning a comfortable salary and have a stable career in the automotive industry.

The Benefits of Getting a Degree in Auto Mechanics

Getting a degree in auto mechanics can have several benefits, including increasing your earning potential, gaining specialized skills and knowledge, and standing out in a competitive job market. Additionally, having a degree can open up opportunities for advancement in your career, such as becoming a supervisor or manager at an auto repair shop.

Factors that Affect the Salaries of Auto Mechanics with a Degree

The salaries of auto mechanics with a degree can vary depending on various factors, including:

  • Location: Auto mechanics working in large cities or metropolitan areas tend to earn more than those working in rural areas.
  • Experience: Auto mechanics with more experience tend to earn higher salaries than those starting in the field.
  • Certifications: Having additional certifications in specific areas of auto mechanics, such as engine repair or brake systems, can increase your earning potential.
  • Employer: Working for larger auto repair shops or dealerships can often result in higher salaries compared to smaller independent shops.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as an Auto Mechanic with a Degree

If you want to increase your earning potential as an auto mechanic with a degree, there are several steps you can take, including:

  • Continuing education: Pursuing additional certifications or courses can help you gain specialized skills and knowledge, which can increase your earning potential.
  • Gaining more experience: The more experience you have, the higher your salary can be.
  • Working for larger employers: Larger auto repair shops or dealerships tend to offer higher salaries and more opportunities for advancement.
  • Specializing in a specific area of auto mechanics: Having specialized knowledge in specific areas of auto mechanics, such as diesel engines or hybrid systems, can increase your earning potential.

Personal Experience: Advancing My Career in Auto Mechanics

After working as an auto mechanic for several years, I decided to pursue additional certifications in specific areas of auto mechanics, such as engine repair and diagnostics. These certifications opened up opportunities for me to advance my career and become a supervisor at a larger auto repair shop. Now, I am earning a higher salary and have more opportunities for growth and advancement in my career.
