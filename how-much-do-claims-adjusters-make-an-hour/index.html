How Much Do Claims Adjusters Make An Hour

Written by Bardi Aug 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a claims adjuster? Or are you already a claims adjuster wondering if you’re being paid fairly? It’s important to know how much claims adjusters make an hour, as it can affect your career decisions and financial goals. In this blog post, we’ll explore the average hourly wages of claims adjusters and factors that affect their pay.

As a claims adjuster, you may have experienced pain points such as low pay, lack of career advancement, or long hours. It’s crucial to address these issues, as they can lead to job dissatisfaction and burnout. One of the most common pain points is salary, as many claims adjusters feel they’re not being compensated adequately for their work.

The average hourly wage for claims adjusters in the United States is $29.64, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this can vary based on factors such as experience, location, and industry. For example, claims adjusters in the insurance industry tend to make more than those in other industries.

In summary, claims adjusters make an average of $29.64 per hour, but this can vary based on several factors. It’s important to consider these factors when negotiating your salary or deciding whether to pursue a career as a claims adjuster.

Factors That Affect Claims Adjuster Pay

Experience is one of the biggest factors that affect claims adjuster pay. As you gain more experience in the field, you become more valuable to employers and can negotiate higher wages. Location also plays a role, as claims adjusters in areas with a high cost of living tend to make more than those in areas with a lower cost of living.

The industry you work in can also impact your pay. Claims adjusters in the insurance industry tend to make more than those in other industries, such as healthcare or government. Additionally, the type of claims you handle can affect your pay. For example, handling complex or high-value claims may come with a higher salary.

My Experience as a Claims Adjuster

As a former claims adjuster with five years of experience, I’ve seen firsthand how pay can vary in the industry. When I first started, I made $18 an hour, but as I gained more experience and moved up in the company, my pay increased to $35 an hour. However, I’ve also seen colleagues who were stuck at a low wage due to lack of experience or opportunities for advancement.

How to Negotiate a Higher Salary as a Claims Adjuster

If you’re a claims adjuster looking to negotiate a higher salary, there are a few things you can do. First, research the average salary for your position in your area and industry. This will give you an idea of what to expect and what you can realistically ask for.

Next, highlight your experience and accomplishments in your role. Show your employer how you’ve added value to the company and how you can continue to do so in the future. Finally, be confident in your worth and don’t settle for less than what you deserve.

How to Become a Claims Adjuster

If you’re interested in becoming a claims adjuster, there are a few steps you can take. First, research the requirements in your state, as they can vary. Generally, you’ll need a high school diploma or equivalent, and some states require a license or certification.

Next, gain experience in the industry by working in related roles such as customer service or insurance sales. This will give you a better understanding of the industry and make you more competitive when applying for claims adjuster positions.

Conclusion of How Much Do Claims Adjusters Make an Hour

Claims adjusters make an average of $29.64 per hour, but this can vary based on factors such as experience, location, and industry. It’s important to consider these factors when negotiating your salary or deciding whether to pursue a career as a claims adjuster. With the right experience and negotiation skills, you can earn a competitive wage and advance in your career as a claims adjuster.

A: Yes, most claims adjuster positions only require a high school diploma or equivalent.
