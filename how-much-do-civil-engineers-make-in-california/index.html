How Much Do Civil Engineers Make In California

Written by William Oct 12, 2023 · 3 min read

If you're considering a career in civil engineering in California, one of the first things you'll want to know is how much you can expect to earn. After all, you want to be fairly compensated for the hard work and dedication you'll put into your job.

Many people are curious about how much civil engineers make in California. It's a valid concern, especially considering the high cost of living in the state. The truth is that civil engineers in California earn a good salary, but it can vary widely depending on a number of factors, such as education, experience, and location.

According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average annual salary for civil engineers in California is around $100,000. However, this figure can range from around $70,000 to over $140,000 depending on various factors.

In terms of education, those with a bachelor's degree in civil engineering can expect to earn around $80,000 to $100,000 per year, while those with a master's degree or higher can earn upwards of $120,000 per year. Experience also plays a role, with those in more senior positions earning more than those just starting out in the field.

My Personal Experience as a Civil Engineer in California

As a civil engineer working in California, I can attest to the fact that the salary can be quite lucrative. With a few years of experience under my belt, I currently earn around $110,000 per year. Of course, this is just my personal experience, and others may earn more or less depending on their situation.

Factors That Affect Civil Engineer Salaries in California

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much a civil engineer earns in California. Some of these include:

Location

The location where a civil engineer works can have a big impact on their salary. For example, civil engineers working in larger cities like Los Angeles and San Francisco tend to earn more than those in smaller towns or rural areas.

Experience

As with any profession, experience plays a big role in how much a civil engineer can earn. Those just starting out in the field will likely earn less than those with several years of experience under their belt.

Job Outlook for Civil Engineers in California

Fortunately, the job outlook for civil engineers in California is quite positive. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of civil engineers is projected to grow 6 percent from 2018 to 2028, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations. This means that there will be plenty of job opportunities for those entering the field in the coming years.

Conclusion of How Much Do Civil Engineers Make in California

Overall, civil engineering can be a rewarding and lucrative career choice in California. While salaries can vary depending on a number of factors, those with a good education and plenty of experience can expect to earn a comfortable living.
