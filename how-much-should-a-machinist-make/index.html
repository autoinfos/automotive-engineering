How Much Should A Machinist Make

Written by Daniel Oct 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a machinist wondering how much you should be making? Do you feel like you're not being compensated fairly for your skills and hard work? If so, you're not alone.

Many machinists struggle with low pay and lack of recognition for their valuable contributions to manufacturing and engineering industries. They often have to work long hours, deal with hazardous materials, and operate heavy machinery, all while maintaining a high level of precision and quality in their work.

So, how much should a machinist make?

The answer varies depending on several factors, including location, experience, industry, and education. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for machinists was $45,750 as of May 2020. However, top earners in the field made more than $65,000 per year, while those at the bottom end of the scale earned less than $32,940 per year.

Personal Experience with Machinist Pay

As a machinist myself, I can attest to the challenges of finding fair pay in this field. When I first started out, I was making just above minimum wage, despite having completed a four-year apprenticeship program and having several years of experience under my belt.

After some negotiation and job searching, I was able to secure a position with a company that valued my skills and paid me accordingly. However, I know many machinists who are still struggling to make ends meet and feel undervalued in their work.

Factors Affecting Machinist Pay

As mentioned earlier, several factors can impact how much a machinist should make. Location is one of the most significant factors, as wages can vary greatly from state to state and even from city to city within the same state.

Experience and education also play a role in determining pay. Machinists with more years of experience and additional certifications or degrees are often able to command higher salaries.

The industry in which a machinist works can also impact pay. For example, those working in the aerospace or medical device industries may earn more than those working in a small machine shop.

Skills and Certifications

In addition to experience and education, specific skills and certifications can also impact a machinist's pay. For example, those who are proficient in computer-aided design (CAD) or have experience with certain types of machinery may be able to earn higher wages.

Certifications, such as those offered by the National Institute for Metalworking Skills (NIMS), can also demonstrate a machinist's expertise and lead to higher pay.

Negotiating Pay as a Machinist

If you feel like you're not being fairly compensated as a machinist, it's important to speak up and negotiate for better pay. Research the average salaries in your area and industry, and make a case for why you deserve to be paid more based on your experience, skills, and contributions to the company.

It's also important to be open to job opportunities that may offer better pay and benefits. Don't be afraid to network and explore your options, even if it means leaving your current employer.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for a machinist?

A: As of May 2020, the average hourly wage for a machinist in the United States was $22.01 per hour.

Q: Can a machinist make a six-figure salary?

A: Yes, it is possible for a machinist to make a six-figure salary with the right combination of experience, education, and industry connections.

Q: What are some benefits of becoming a machinist?

A: Machinists often enjoy job stability, opportunities for advancement, and the satisfaction of using their skills to create precision parts and machinery.

Q: How can a machinist increase their earning potential?

A: Machinists can increase their earning potential by gaining more experience, pursuing additional certifications or degrees, and networking within their industry to find better job opportunities.

Conclusion

While there is no one-size-fits-all answer to how much a machinist should make, it is important for these skilled professionals to be compensated fairly for their hard work and expertise. By understanding the factors that impact pay and advocating for themselves, machinists can work towards achieving the pay they deserve.
