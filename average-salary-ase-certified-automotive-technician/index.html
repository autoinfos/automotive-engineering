Average Salary For Ase Certified Automotive Technicians What You Need To Know

Written by William Sep 24, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an ASE certified automotive technician? If so, you're likely wondering about the average salary for this profession. After all, you want to make sure that pursuing this career is worth your time and effort. In this blog post, we'll cover everything you need to know about the average salary for ASE certified automotive technicians.

Being an ASE certified automotive technician can be a rewarding career, but it's not without its challenges. One of the biggest pain points for technicians is often the amount of physical labor involved. Working on cars can be physically demanding, and it's not uncommon for technicians to experience fatigue or even injuries as a result. Additionally, the hours can be long and irregular, which can make it difficult to maintain a work-life balance.

Now, let's get to the heart of the matter: the average salary for ASE certified automotive technicians. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, years of experience, and the specific industry in which you work.

The Importance of Experience

When it comes to earning a higher salary as an ASE certified automotive technician, experience is key. Technicians who have been in the field for several years and have accumulated a wealth of knowledge and skills are often able to command higher salaries. Additionally, those who specialize in certain areas, such as electrical systems or engine repair, may also be able to earn more.

Personally, I've been working as an ASE certified technician for over a decade, and I've seen firsthand how experience can impact your earning potential. When I first started out, my salary was on the lower end of the spectrum, but as I gained more experience and expertise, I was able to negotiate for higher pay.

The Role of Location

Location is another important factor to consider when it comes to the average salary for ASE certified automotive technicians. In general, areas with higher costs of living tend to offer higher salaries to offset those costs. Additionally, certain industries may pay more than others. For example, technicians who work for dealerships or specialty repair shops may earn more than those who work for independent repair shops.

Personally, I've worked in several different locations throughout my career, and I've found that the salaries can vary quite a bit depending on where you are. For example, when I worked in a high-cost-of-living city on the East Coast, my salary was significantly higher than when I worked in a smaller town in the Midwest.

The Benefits of Certification

One way to increase your earning potential as an ASE certified automotive technician is to obtain additional certifications. The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) offers a variety of certifications in areas such as engine repair, brakes, and suspension and steering. By becoming certified in one or more of these areas, you can demonstrate your expertise to employers and potentially earn a higher salary.

Negotiating Your Salary

If you're looking to increase your salary as an ASE certified automotive technician, it's important to know how to negotiate effectively. Start by doing your research and finding out what the average salary is for your specific role in your location. Then, come prepared with a list of your accomplishments and skills that make you a valuable asset to the company. Be confident in your abilities and don't be afraid to ask for what you're worth.

Conclusion: Is Being an ASE Certified Automotive Technician Worth It?

Overall, being an ASE certified automotive technician can be a rewarding and fulfilling career choice. While the physical demands and long hours can be a challenge, the average salary for this profession is competitive and can be increased through experience and certifications. Ultimately, whether or not being an ASE certified automotive technician is worth it for you will depend on your personal goals and priorities.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for ASE certified automotive technicians?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $21.17 as of May 2020.

Q: How can I increase my salary as an ASE certified automotive technician?

A: One way to increase your salary is to gain more experience and expertise in your field. Additionally, obtaining additional certifications from organizations such as ASE can also help you earn more.

Q: What industries pay the highest salaries for ASE certified automotive technicians?

A: Generally, industries with higher costs of living such as dealerships and specialty repair shops tend to offer higher salaries for ASE certified automotive technicians.

Q: Is it common for ASE certified automotive technicians to work overtime?

A: Yes, it's not uncommon for ASE certified automotive technicians to work overtime, especially during busy seasons or when there are urgent repairs that need to be made.

Conclusion of Average Salary for ASE Certified Automotive Technicians

As you can see, the average salary for ASE certified automotive technicians can vary depending on a variety of factors. However, with experience and additional certifications, it's possible to earn a competitive salary in this field. If you're passionate about cars and enjoy working with your hands, becoming an ASE certified automotive technician could be a great career choice for you.
