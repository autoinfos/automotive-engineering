How To Get A Dealers License In Oklahoma A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 10, 2023 · 5 min read

Starting a car dealership business in Oklahoma can be a lucrative venture. However, obtaining a dealers license in Oklahoma can be a daunting task. From filling out the necessary paperwork to meeting the state's requirements, there are several steps involved in getting a dealers license. In this blog post, we'll guide you through the process of how to get a dealers license in Oklahoma in relaxed English language, so you can start your car dealership business with confidence.

If you're wondering how to get a dealers license in Oklahoma, you're not alone. Many aspiring car dealers face challenges in obtaining a license due to the complex nature of the process. The state of Oklahoma has strict regulations in place that must be adhered to before a license can be granted. Failure to meet these requirements can lead to delays or even rejection of your application.

Firstly, you need to meet the eligibility requirements set by the Oklahoma Used Motor Vehicle and Parts Commission. These requirements include a valid driver's license, clean criminal record, and a physical address where your dealership will operate. Once you meet these requirements, you can proceed with the application process, which involves submitting the necessary documents and paying the required fees.

In summary, to get a dealers license in Oklahoma, you need to meet the eligibility requirements, submit the required documents, and pay the necessary fees. Additionally, you must comply with state regulations and meet the standards set by the Oklahoma Used Motor Vehicle and Parts Commission.

Step-by-Step Guide to Obtaining a Dealers License in Oklahoma

Obtaining a dealers license in Oklahoma can be overwhelming, but with the right guidance, it can be a smooth process. We've put together a step-by-step guide to help you navigate the process with ease.

Step 1: Meet the Eligibility Requirements

Before you apply for a dealers license in Oklahoma, you need to meet the eligibility requirements set by the Oklahoma Used Motor Vehicle and Parts Commission. These requirements include:

- A valid driver's license

- A clean criminal record

- A physical address where your dealership will operate

Step 2: Complete the Application Forms

Once you meet the eligibility requirements, you can proceed with the application process. You'll need to complete the following forms:

- Application for License to Sell Used Motor Vehicles and Parts (Form UD-10)

- Application for Dealer Plate (Form UD-11)

- Application for Surety Bond (Form UD-12)

Step 3: Submit the Required Documents

In addition to the completed application forms, you'll need to submit the following documents:

- Proof of liability insurance

- A copy of your driver's license

- A copy of your criminal record

- Proof of ownership of the dealership location

- Surety bond

Step 4: Pay the Required Fees

There are several fees involved in obtaining a dealers license in Oklahoma. These fees include:

- Application fee: $200

- License fee: $700

- Dealer plate fee: $20 per plate

- Surety bond fee: Varies

Step 5: Comply with State Regulations

It's essential to comply with state regulations and meet the standards set by the Oklahoma Used Motor Vehicle and Parts Commission. Failure to comply with these regulations can lead to delays or even rejection of your application. Some of the regulations you must adhere to include:

- Displaying your dealers license prominently

- Keeping accurate records of all transactions

- Complying with all state and federal laws

- Maintaining liability insurance coverage
