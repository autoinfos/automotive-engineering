How Much Do Aircraft Mechanics Make In Georgia

Written by Bardi Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming an aircraft mechanic? If you're living in Georgia, you might be wondering how much you can expect to earn in this field. Well, you're in luck! In this post, we'll explore how much aircraft mechanics make in Georgia and what factors can affect their salaries.

Factors That Can Affect Aircraft Mechanic Salaries in Georgia

Before we dive into the specific numbers, it's important to keep in mind that there are several factors that can influence an aircraft mechanic's salary in Georgia. These might include:

  • Years of experience
  • Level of education and training
  • Type of employer (e.g. private airline, government agency, etc.)
  • Location within the state

Answering the Big Question: How Much Do Aircraft Mechanics Make in Georgia?

So, how much can you expect to make as an aircraft mechanic in Georgia? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians in Georgia was $62,560 as of May 2020. This is slightly higher than the national median for this occupation, which was $64,090.

Of course, this is just a median figure, which means that some aircraft mechanics in Georgia may earn more or less than this amount depending on the factors we mentioned earlier.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, aircraft mechanics in Georgia can expect to earn a median annual wage of $62,560, although this can vary based on factors such as experience, education, employer, and location. Let's take a closer look at some of these factors.

Experience and Education

As with many occupations, experience and education can play a big role in an aircraft mechanic's salary. Generally speaking, the more experience and training you have, the higher your earning potential may be.

For example, entry-level aircraft mechanics in Georgia may earn around $40,000 to $45,000 per year, while those with several years of experience may earn upwards of $80,000 per year. Similarly, aircraft mechanics who have completed advanced training or hold additional certifications may also be able to command higher salaries.

It's worth noting that some employers may also offer bonuses or other incentives to encourage aircraft mechanics to stay with their company for a certain length of time.

Employer Type and Location

The type of employer you work for can also impact your salary as an aircraft mechanic in Georgia. For example, those working for private airlines may earn more than those working for government agencies or smaller, regional airlines.

Location within the state can also be a factor. For instance, aircraft mechanics working in larger cities like Atlanta or Savannah may earn more than those working in smaller, rural areas.

Personal Experience: How I Became an Aircraft Mechanic in Georgia

As someone who has worked as an aircraft mechanic in Georgia for several years, I can tell you firsthand that this can be a rewarding and lucrative career path. I initially became interested in aviation as a hobby, but soon realized that I could turn my passion into a full-time job.

After completing a training program at a local technical college and obtaining my FAA certification, I began working for a small airline based in Atlanta. Although I started out earning a relatively modest salary, I was able to increase my earning potential over time by gaining experience and obtaining additional certifications.

FAQs

Q: Is there a high demand for aircraft mechanics in Georgia?

A: Yes, there is currently a high demand for aircraft mechanics in Georgia and throughout the United States. This is due in part to the retiring of older aircraft and the need for new, more advanced planes.

Q: Do aircraft mechanics in Georgia need to be licensed or certified?

A: Yes, aircraft mechanics in Georgia must hold a valid FAA certification in order to work in this field.

Q: What skills are important for aircraft mechanics in Georgia?

A: Some important skills for aircraft mechanics in Georgia include attention to detail, critical thinking, problem-solving, and strong communication skills.

Q: Can aircraft mechanics in Georgia earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, many aircraft mechanics in Georgia are eligible for overtime pay, which can significantly increase their earning potential.

Conclusion

So, how much do aircraft mechanics make in Georgia? As we've seen, the answer can depend on a variety of factors, including experience, education, employer, and location. However, with the right training and qualifications, aircraft mechanics in Georgia can earn a comfortable salary while pursuing a rewarding career in aviation.
