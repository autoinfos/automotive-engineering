The Truth About Aircraft Mechanic Entry Level Salary

Written by Daniel Sep 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Have you ever considered pursuing a career as an aircraft mechanic? If so, you might be wondering about the entry level salary for this profession. While the idea of working on airplanes might seem exciting, it's important to understand the financial realities of the job. So, what can you expect to earn as an aircraft mechanic just starting out?

One of the biggest pain points related to aircraft mechanic entry level salary is the fact that it can vary widely based on a number of factors. For example, where you work and what type of aircraft you're working on can have a big impact on your pay. Additionally, different companies and organizations may offer different salaries for the same job. All of this can make it difficult to know what to expect in terms of compensation.

With that said, the target for aircraft mechanic entry level salary is typically around $20 per hour. This equates to an annual salary of around $41,000. Of course, this is just a general guideline and your actual salary may be higher or lower depending on the factors mentioned above.

In summary, aircraft mechanic entry level salary can be difficult to pin down due to the many variables involved. However, the target salary is typically around $20 per hour. Keep reading to learn more about what affects aircraft mechanic salaries and how you can increase your earning potential in this field.

What Affects Aircraft Mechanic Entry Level Salary?

As mentioned, there are several factors that can impact how much you earn as an aircraft mechanic. One of the biggest is the type of aircraft you're working on. For example, working on smaller planes or helicopters may pay less than working on larger commercial airliners. Additionally, where you work can also have a big impact. Mechanics who work for private companies or airlines may earn more than those who work for smaller organizations.

Another factor to consider is your level of experience and education. Those who have completed a formal training program or who have years of experience in the field may command higher salaries. Additionally, if you have any specialized skills or certifications, such as in avionics or welding, you may be able to earn more money.

Personally, I've found that networking and building relationships within the industry can also be helpful in increasing your earning potential. By connecting with other mechanics and industry professionals, you may be able to learn about new job opportunities or negotiate for higher pay.

My Experience with Aircraft Mechanic Entry Level Salary

When I first started out as an aircraft mechanic, I was lucky enough to land a job with a major airline. While the starting pay wasn't amazing, I knew that there was room for growth and development within the company. Over time, I was able to work my way up to more senior roles and earn a higher salary.

I also made a point to take advantage of any training opportunities that came my way. By expanding my skillset and knowledge base, I was able to become more valuable to my employer and negotiate for higher pay. Additionally, I made a point to build relationships with other mechanics and industry professionals, which helped me learn about new job opportunities and earn more money in the long run.

How to Increase Your Aircraft Mechanic Entry Level Salary

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as an aircraft mechanic, there are several steps you can take. First and foremost, consider pursuing additional education or certifications in the field. This can make you a more valuable asset to your employer and help you command a higher salary.

Additionally, make a point to network and build relationships within the industry. Attend industry events, connect with other mechanics and industry professionals on social media, and consider joining a professional organization or union. By building a strong network, you may be able to learn about new job opportunities or negotiate for higher pay at your current job.

What Are Some Common Salaries for Aircraft Mechanics?

While the target for aircraft mechanic entry level salary is around $20 per hour, there is a wide range of salaries that you may encounter in this field. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 as of May 2020. However, the lowest 10 percent of workers in this field earned less than $38,410 per year, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $100,960 per year.

How Can I Negotiate for a Higher Aircraft Mechanic Salary?

If you feel that you're not being paid what you're worth as an aircraft mechanic, it may be time to negotiate for a higher salary. Start by doing your research and finding out what other mechanics with similar experience and qualifications are earning in your area. Then, schedule a meeting with your supervisor to discuss your concerns and present your case for a raise.

Be sure to come prepared with specific examples of your contributions to the company and any additional skills or certifications you've acquired. Additionally, be open to compromise and alternative solutions, such as a higher bonus or additional vacation time, if your employer is unable to meet your salary demands.

Conclusion of Aircraft Mechanic Entry Level Salary

While the entry level salary for aircraft mechanics can vary based on a number of factors, the target is typically around $20 per hour. To increase your earning potential in this field, consider pursuing additional education and certifications, building relationships within the industry, and negotiating for higher pay when appropriate.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Is it possible to earn more than the target entry level salary as an aircraft mechanic?

A: Yes, it's definitely possible to earn more than $20 per hour as an aircraft mechanic. Factors such as your level of experience, education, and certifications can all impact your earning potential.

Q: What are some common certifications that can help increase an aircraft mechanic's salary?

A: Some common certifications that can help increase an aircraft mechanic's salary include the Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certification, the avionics certification, and the welding certification.

Q: What are some common benefits that aircraft mechanics receive in addition to their salary?

A: Common benefits for aircraft mechanics may include health insurance, retirement plans, paid vacation and sick time, and travel perks such as discounted flights.

Q: How can I find job opportunities as an aircraft mechanic?

A: Job opportunities for aircraft mechanics can be found through online job boards, industry-specific websites and publications, and by networking with other industry professionals.
