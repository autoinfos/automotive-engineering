What Is The Starting Salary Of An Automotive Engineer

Written by Bardi Aug 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you curious about the starting salary of an automotive engineer? If you're considering a career in the automotive industry, it's important to know what you can expect to earn when you start out. In this blog post, we'll explore the starting salary of an automotive engineer and what factors can impact it.

As with any profession, there are certain pain points associated with starting out in the automotive engineering field. For one, the starting salary may not be as high as you would like. Additionally, competition for entry-level positions can be tough, and it may take some time to find the right fit.

So, what is the starting salary of an automotive engineer? According to Glassdoor, the national average for an entry-level automotive engineer is around $68,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors, such as location, industry, and level of education and experience.

In summary, the starting salary of an automotive engineer is around $68,000 per year, with variation depending on different factors. To give you a better idea of what to expect, let's dive into some more details about this topic.

Factors that Impact Starting Salary

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact the starting salary of an automotive engineer. One of the biggest factors is location. Salaries can vary greatly depending on where you live and work. For example, an automotive engineer working in California can expect to earn more than someone working in a smaller city in the Midwest.

Another factor is industry. Automotive engineers can work in a variety of industries, such as automotive manufacturing, aerospace, and defense. Salaries can also vary depending on the specific company you work for, as well as the size of the company.

Education and experience are also important factors that can impact your starting salary. Generally speaking, the more education and experience you have, the higher your starting salary will be. For example, someone with a master's degree in automotive engineering will likely earn more than someone with just a bachelor's degree.

My Experience Starting Out

When I first started out as an automotive engineer, I was excited to begin my career but nervous about what my starting salary would be. I was fortunate enough to land a job at a large automotive manufacturing company in Michigan, where I started out earning around $70,000 per year.

While this may not seem like a lot compared to other engineering fields, I was happy with my starting salary and felt that it was fair for someone just starting out in the industry. Over time, I was able to earn more as I gained more experience and took on more responsibilities.

Tips for Negotiating Your Starting Salary

If you're worried about your starting salary as an automotive engineer, there are a few things you can do to negotiate for a higher salary. First, do your research and find out what the average salary is for someone in your position and industry.

You should also be prepared to discuss your education and experience, and how they make you a valuable asset to the company. If you have any relevant certifications or awards, be sure to mention them as well.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the starting salary of an automotive engineer is around $68,000 per year, but can vary depending on factors such as location, industry, education, and experience. While the starting salary may not be as high as some other engineering fields, it's important to remember that salaries can increase over time as you gain more experience and take on more responsibilities.
