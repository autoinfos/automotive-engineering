How Much Does An Aircraft Engineer Make A Year

Written by Bardi Oct 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an aircraft engineer? One of the main concerns for potential engineers is the salary. It's important to have a clear understanding of how much you can expect to earn before committing to this career path.

The average salary for an aircraft engineer is around $85,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors such as location, experience, and employer. Some aircraft engineers may earn more than $100,000 per year, while others may earn less than $60,000 per year.

In general, aircraft engineers with more experience and those who work for larger companies tend to earn more. Engineers who specialize in certain areas such as avionics or propulsion systems may also earn higher salaries.

In summary, the salary of an aircraft engineer can vary greatly depending on a number of factors. However, with an average salary of around $85,000 per year, this is a career path that can provide a comfortable living for those who are passionate about aviation and engineering.

What is the Importance of Experience in Determining an Aircraft Engineer's Salary?

Experience is one of the main factors that can impact an aircraft engineer's salary. As engineers gain more experience, they become more knowledgeable and skilled in their field. This can make them more valuable to their employer and can lead to higher salaries.

For example, a new graduate who is just starting out in their career may earn around $60,000 per year. However, after five years of experience, they may be able to earn closer to $90,000 per year. This increase in salary is due to the engineer's increased knowledge, skills, and value to their employer.

What Is the Role of Location in Determining an Aircraft Engineer's Salary?

The location of an aircraft engineer can also impact their salary. In general, engineers who work in cities with a higher cost of living tend to earn more. For example, an aircraft engineer working in New York City may earn more than an engineer working in a smaller city with a lower cost of living.

However, it's important to note that this can vary depending on the employer. Some companies may offer higher salaries to attract top talent regardless of location.

How Does the Size of a Company Affect an Aircraft Engineer's Salary?

The size of a company can also impact an aircraft engineer's salary. In general, engineers who work for larger companies tend to earn more than those who work for smaller companies. This is because larger companies often have more resources and may be able to offer higher salaries to attract top talent.

However, this is not always the case. Some smaller companies may offer higher salaries to attract experienced engineers who may be more difficult to find in the job market.

What Is the Future Outlook for Aircraft Engineer Salaries?

The future outlook for aircraft engineer salaries is positive. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of aerospace engineers is projected to grow 3 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations. This growth is due to increased demand for commercial aircraft and military drones.

As demand for aircraft engineers continues to grow, salaries are likely to follow suit. This makes it an exciting time to pursue a career in this field.

My Personal Experience as an Aircraft Engineer

As an aircraft engineer with over 10 years of experience, I can attest to the importance of experience and location in determining salary. When I first started out in my career, I earned around $70,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved to a larger city, my salary increased to over $100,000 per year.

One piece of advice I would give to those considering a career as an aircraft engineer is to specialize in a certain area such as avionics or propulsion systems. This can make you more valuable to your employer and can lead to higher salaries.
