How Much Do Forklift Operators Earn Find Out Here

Written by Frank Oct 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a forklift operator? Or are you already in the field and wondering if you're being paid fairly? Either way, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we'll explore the topic of how much forklift operators earn and give you all the information you need to make an informed decision.

For many people, one of the biggest concerns when considering a career as a forklift operator is the salary. Will it be enough to support their lifestyle? Will they be able to pay their bills and save for the future? These are valid concerns and ones that we'll be addressing in this post.

How Much Do Forklift Operators Earn?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for forklift operators in the United States is $17.11. This means that half of all forklift operators earn more than $17.11 per hour, while the other half earn less. The lowest 10 percent of earners make less than $11.45 per hour, while the highest 10 percent earn more than $25.60 per hour.

It's important to note that these figures can vary depending on a number of factors, including the industry you work in, your level of experience, and the region of the country you're in. Overall, however, forklift operators can expect to earn a decent wage that allows them to support themselves and their families.

The Benefits of Being a Forklift Operator

In addition to a competitive salary, there are many benefits to working as a forklift operator. For one, it's a job that doesn't require a college degree, which means you can enter the workforce quickly and start earning money right away. It's also a job that's in high demand, which means you're likely to find plenty of job opportunities in your area.

Another benefit of being a forklift operator is that it's a job that offers a lot of variety. Depending on the industry you work in, you may be moving products around a warehouse, loading and unloading trucks, or working in a manufacturing facility. This means that no two days are ever the same, which can make the job more interesting and engaging.

How to Become a Forklift Operator

If you're interested in becoming a forklift operator, the first step is to get certified. While certification requirements can vary depending on the state you're in and the industry you work in, most employers will require you to have some level of certification before you can operate a forklift. You can get certified through a number of different organizations, including the National Safety Council and the Occupational Safety and Health Administration.

The Future of Forklift Operators

As the global economy continues to grow, the demand for forklift operators is expected to increase. In fact, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of forklift operators is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is faster than the average for all occupations.

This means that there will be plenty of job opportunities for those who are interested in working as forklift operators in the years to come. And with a competitive salary and numerous benefits, it's a career path that's definitely worth considering.

Conclusion of How Much Do Forklift Operators Earn

Overall, forklift operators can expect to earn a competitive salary that allows them to support themselves and their families. With plenty of job opportunities and a positive job outlook, it's a career path that's definitely worth considering.
