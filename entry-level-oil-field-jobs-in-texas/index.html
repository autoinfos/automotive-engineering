Exploring Entry Level Oil Field Jobs In Texas

Written by Christine Jun 22, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking for an exciting career path in Texas? Have you considered entry level oil field jobs? These jobs offer a unique opportunity for individuals to earn good wages while working in a challenging and dynamic environment. But, like any job, they also come with their own set of challenges.

One of the biggest challenges in entry level oil field jobs in Texas is the physical demands of the job. Long hours, working in harsh weather conditions, and handling heavy equipment can take a toll on your body. Additionally, the job can be mentally challenging as well, with high-pressure situations that require quick thinking and problem-solving skills.

Despite the challenges, entry level oil field jobs in Texas offer a great opportunity for individuals who are willing to put in the hard work. These jobs are in high demand and often come with good pay and benefits.

To land an entry level oil field job in Texas, you will typically need a high school diploma or equivalent, and some companies may require additional certifications or training. It's also important to have a strong work ethic, good communication skills, and the ability to work well in a team environment.

In summary, entry level oil field jobs in Texas offer a unique opportunity for individuals looking for a challenging and rewarding career path. While the job can be physically and mentally demanding, it also comes with good wages and benefits. To succeed in this field, individuals will need to have a strong work ethic and the ability to work well in a team environment.

Types of Entry Level Oil Field Jobs in Texas

There are a variety of entry level oil field jobs in Texas, each with their own unique challenges and benefits. One common entry level job is a floorhand, which involves working on a drilling rig and assisting with drilling operations. This job requires physical strength and the ability to work in a team environment.

Another entry level job is a leasehand, which involves maintaining and repairing equipment on oil and gas leases. This job requires good mechanical skills and the ability to work independently.

What to Expect in Entry Level Oil Field Jobs in Texas

Working in entry level oil field jobs in Texas can be both physically and mentally challenging. Employees can expect to work long hours, sometimes up to 12 hours a day or more. Additionally, the job requires individuals to work in harsh weather conditions, including extreme heat and cold.

Employees must also be prepared to work in high-pressure situations that require quick thinking and problem-solving skills. Safety is always a top priority in the oil and gas industry, and employees must be vigilant about adhering to safety protocols at all times.

How to Prepare for Entry Level Oil Field Jobs in Texas

To prepare for entry level oil field jobs in Texas, individuals should consider completing additional training or certification programs. This can include programs in safety, equipment operation, and other relevant areas. Additionally, individuals should be prepared to work hard and develop a strong work ethic that will help them succeed in this challenging industry.

Personal Experience

My personal experience working in entry level oil field jobs in Texas has been challenging but rewarding. As a floorhand, I've been responsible for assisting with drilling operations, including setting up and maintaining equipment, and ensuring that safety protocols are followed at all times.

While the job can be physically demanding, I've found it to be a great opportunity to develop my skills and gain valuable experience in the industry. I've also appreciated the good pay and benefits that come with the job.
