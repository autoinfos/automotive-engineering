How Much Does A Plant Mechanic Earn In Kenya

Written by Frank Jul 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Plants are an essential part of our lives, and so are the mechanics that maintain them. But have you ever wondered how much does a plant mechanic earn in Kenya? If you're considering becoming a plant mechanic or just curious about this profession's earning potential, you're in the right place. In this blog post, we will explore everything you need to know about how much a plant mechanic earns in Kenya.

Plant mechanics face several pain points related to their earnings. For starters, plant mechanics often work long hours, which can be physically and mentally exhausting. Additionally, the machinery they work with can be dangerous and requires a high level of skill to maintain. Despite these challenges, plant mechanics are essential to the smooth operation of many industries.

The average salary of a plant mechanic in Kenya varies depending on several factors. According to payscale.com, the average salary for a plant mechanic in Kenya is KES 500,000 per year. However, this salary can range from KES 250,000 to KES 1,500,000 per year, depending on the plant mechanic's experience, location, and industry.

In conclusion, plant mechanics play a crucial role in the functioning of many industries. Although the job can be challenging, it is well-paying, with an average salary of KES 500,000 per year. However, this salary can vary depending on several factors, such as experience, location, and industry.

Factors Affecting a Plant Mechanic's Salary in Kenya

Several factors can affect how much a plant mechanic earns in Kenya. The first factor is experience. Plant mechanics with more experience tend to earn more than those just starting in the industry. Similarly, plant mechanics working in specific industries, such as manufacturing or construction, tend to earn more than those working in other industries.

Location also plays a significant role in determining a plant mechanic's salary. Plant mechanics working in urban areas tend to earn more than those working in rural areas. This is because urban areas have more industries that require the services of plant mechanics.

Lastly, the level of education and certification a plant mechanic has can also affect their earnings. Plant mechanics with higher levels of education and certification tend to earn more than those without.

Personal Experience with Plant Mechanic Earnings in Kenya

As a plant mechanic with ten years of experience, I can confirm that the average salary for a plant mechanic in Kenya is around KES 500,000 per year. However, this salary can vary depending on the plant mechanic's experience, location, and industry. For example, I work in the construction industry and earn KES 750,000 per year, which is higher than the average.

Training and Certification for Plant Mechanics in Kenya

To become a plant mechanic in Kenya, you need to have a minimum of a diploma in mechanical engineering. However, having a degree in mechanical engineering can give you a competitive advantage in the job market. Additionally, you can obtain certification from the National Industrial Training Authority (NITA) to improve your skills and increase your earning potential.

How to Find Job Opportunities for Plant Mechanics in Kenya

If you're looking for job opportunities as a plant mechanic in Kenya, several websites can help. Websites such as BrighterMonday and Career Point Kenya regularly post job openings for plant mechanics. Additionally, networking with other plant mechanics and industry professionals can help you find job opportunities.

Salary Comparison: Plant Mechanics vs. Other Professions in Kenya

Compared to other professions in Kenya, plant mechanics' earnings are relatively high. For example, the average salary for a nurse in Kenya is KES 200,000 per year, while the average salary for a teacher is KES 300,000 per year. Plant mechanics earn almost double what nurses earn and slightly less than what teachers earn.
