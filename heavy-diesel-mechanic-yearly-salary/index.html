Are you considering a career as a heavy diesel mechanic? If so, one of your top concerns is likely how much money you can expect to earn each year. As with any career, heavy diesel mechanic yearly salary can be impacted by a range of factors. So, what can you expect in terms of heavy diesel mechanic yearly salary? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $49,150 as of May 2019. However, the top 10 percent of earners in this field made more than $72,180 per year. Keep in mind that these numbers may vary depending on your specific circumstances.

Certifications and Training Programs

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as a heavy diesel mechanic, pursuing additional certifications and training programs can be a smart move. Some of the most valuable certifications for this field include the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) certification and the Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) Master Technician certification. Additionally, many employers offer on-the-job training programs that can help you build your skills and knowledge.

Other Factors That Impact Heavy Diesel Mechanic Yearly Salary

In addition to certifications and training programs, there are a range of other factors that can impact your earning potential as a heavy diesel mechanic. These may include the location where you work, the size and type of company you work for, and the specific industry you work in. For example, mechanics who work for the government or in the aerospace industry may earn more than those who work in other fields.

How to Negotiate Your Heavy Diesel Mechanic Yearly Salary

If you're looking to earn more money as a heavy diesel mechanic, negotiation can be a key tool in your arsenal.
