What Does Used Car Certified Mean

Written by Robby Jul 23, 2023 · 5 min read

Buying a used car can be a daunting task, especially if it's your first time. With so many options available in the market, it's easy to get overwhelmed and confused. One term that you may come across when looking for a used car is "certified." But what does used car certified mean, and is it worth considering?

When you're in the market for a used car, the last thing you want is to end up with a lemon. A lemon is a car that has significant defects that the manufacturer or dealer cannot fix within a reasonable number of attempts. Such cars often come with a lot of issues that can cost you a lot of money to repair. One way to avoid ending up with a lemon is to consider buying a certified used car.

A certified used car is a pre-owned vehicle that has undergone a rigorous inspection and reconditioning process. Typically, the vehicle has undergone a 100-point inspection to ensure that it meets specific standards set by the manufacturer or dealer. If anything fails during the inspection, it is repaired or replaced to ensure that the car is in top condition. Once the car meets the certification criteria, it is sold with an extended warranty and other benefits.

In summary, a used car certified means that the vehicle has undergone a rigorous inspection and reconditioning process to ensure that it is in top condition. It comes with an extended warranty and other benefits that make it worth considering.

What Are the Benefits of Buying a Certified Used Car?

Buying a certified used car comes with several benefits that make it worth considering. Here are some of the benefits:

Reliability

A certified used car is more reliable than a regular used car. The rigorous inspection and reconditioning process ensure that the car is in top condition and free of any major defects. You can be confident that the car will serve you well and not break down unexpectedly.

Warranty

A certified used car comes with an extended warranty that covers major components of the vehicle. The warranty can last up to several years or a certain number of miles, depending on the certification program. This gives you peace of mind knowing that you're covered in case something goes wrong.

Lower Cost

Buying a certified used car can save you a lot of money compared to buying a brand new car. Certified used cars are often a few years old and have already depreciated in value, making them more affordable. Additionally, you can avoid some of the costs associated with buying a new car, such as destination fees, dealer preparation fees, and other add-ons.

How Do You Certify a Used Car?

Certifying a used car involves a rigorous inspection and reconditioning process. Here are the general steps involved in certifying a used car:

Inspection

The first step is to inspect the vehicle thoroughly to identify any defects or issues that need to be addressed. The inspection can involve up to 100 points, depending on the certification program.

Reconditioning

Once the inspection is complete, the car is reconditioned to ensure that it meets the certification criteria. This can involve repairing or replacing any faulty parts, performing a tune-up, and detailing the car to make it look like new.

Testing

The car is then tested to ensure that it performs well and meets the manufacturer's or dealer's standards. This can involve road testing, computer diagnostics, and any other tests deemed necessary.

Certification

Once the car passes all the tests and meets the certification criteria, it is certified and sold with an extended warranty and other benefits.

What Should You Look for When Buying a Certified Used Car?

When buying a certified used car, there are several things you should look for to ensure that you're getting a good deal. Here are some of the things to consider:

Certification Program

Make sure you understand the certification program and what it covers. Different programs have different standards and benefits, so it's essential to know what you're getting.

Inspection Report

Ask to see the inspection report and go through it carefully. Make sure that all the issues identified during the inspection have been addressed and that there are no outstanding problems.

Warranty

Review the warranty offered and make sure you understand what it covers and for how long. Ask questions if anything is unclear.

Vehicle History Report

Get a vehicle history report to see if the car has been in any accidents, has a salvage title, or has any other issues that may affect its value or safety.

Conclusion of What Does Used Car Certified Mean

Buying a used car can be a smart and economical choice, but it can also be risky if you're not careful. If you're in the market for a used car, consider buying a certified used car. A certified used car has undergone a rigorous inspection and reconditioning process to ensure that it is in top condition. It comes with an extended warranty and other benefits that make it worth considering. Just make sure you do your homework and understand what you're getting before you sign on the dotted line.
