What Does A Medical Assistant Technician Do

Written by Eveline Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in a career in healthcare but don't want to spend years in medical school? A medical assistant technician may be the perfect fit for you. Medical assistant technicians are an essential part of any healthcare team and play a crucial role in ensuring patients receive the care they need.

One of the biggest pain points in healthcare is the long wait times patients often experience. Medical assistant technicians are responsible for keeping the flow of patients moving smoothly. They assist with patient check-ins, vital sign measurements, and medical history documentation. By doing so, they help to reduce wait times and ensure patients are seen in a timely manner.

A medical assistant technician is a trained healthcare professional who works alongside doctors and nurses to provide care to patients. Their job duties can vary depending on the healthcare setting they work in, but typically include taking patient vital signs, administering medications, drawing blood, and assisting with medical procedures. They also may be responsible for scheduling appointments, managing medical records, and communicating with patients about their care.

In summary, a medical assistant technician plays a vital role in the healthcare industry, providing essential support to doctors and nurses and ensuring patients receive the care they need.

What Does a Medical Assistant Technician Do and How to Become One?

When I was first considering a career in healthcare, I wasn't sure what path to take. I knew I wanted to help people, but I didn't want to spend years in medical school. That's when I discovered the role of a medical assistant technician.

To become a medical assistant technician, you typically need to complete a post-secondary program that includes both classroom instruction and hands-on training. These programs can range from a few months to two years, depending on the level of education you choose to pursue. Once you complete your training, you may need to pass a certification exam to become a licensed medical assistant technician.

What Skills Do You Need to Be a Medical Assistant Technician?

As a medical assistant technician, you'll need to be able to work well under pressure and have excellent communication skills. You'll be working with patients every day, so the ability to communicate clearly and compassionately is essential. You'll also need to be able to multitask and prioritize tasks effectively, as you'll often be responsible for managing multiple patients at once.

What Is the Job Outlook for Medical Assistant Technicians?

The job outlook for medical assistant technicians is excellent, with the Bureau of Labor Statistics projecting a 19% growth in employment from 2019 to 2029. This growth is due in part to the aging population, which is increasing demand for healthcare services. Additionally, medical assistant technicians are becoming increasingly important in healthcare settings, as they help to reduce wait times and improve patient outcomes.

How Much Do Medical Assistant Technicians Make?

The median annual wage for medical assistant technicians was $35,850 in May 2020, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, wages can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and the healthcare setting you work in.

What Does a Day in the Life of a Medical Assistant Technician Look Like?

One of my favorite things about being a medical assistant technician is that no two days are the same. Some days, I'm responsible for taking patient vital signs and assisting with medical procedures. Other days, I'm responsible for managing medical records and communicating with patients about their care. I love the variety of tasks I get to perform each day, and I feel like I'm making a real difference in my patients' lives.
