Factors Affecting Automotive Mechanic Salary per Hour

While the median hourly wage provides a general idea of what you can expect to make as a mechanic, there are many factors that can affect your earnings. One of the biggest factors is experience. Entry-level mechanics typically earn less than those with years of experience under their belt.

Location can also play a big role in determining your hourly wage as a mechanic. Mechanics in cities with a higher cost of living may earn more than those in rural areas. Additionally, the type of employer can also impact your salary. Mechanics who work for dealerships or automotive repair chains may earn more than those who work for independent repair shops.
