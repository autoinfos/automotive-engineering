How Much Do Aircraft Maintenance Engineers Make In Canada

Written by Robby Aug 29, 2023 · 5 min read

Aircraft maintenance engineers are professionals responsible for ensuring that airplanes and helicopters are safe and reliable for flight. These skilled technicians perform regular inspections, repairs, and maintenance to ensure that aircraft are in good condition and meet all safety standards. However, one question that often comes up is: how much do aircraft maintenance engineers make in Canada?

For many people, salary is an important consideration when choosing a career. After all, you want to be compensated fairly for your skills and expertise. Unfortunately, the answer to this question is not straightforward. The salary of an aircraft maintenance engineer in Canada can vary depending on a number of factors, including experience, location, and employer.

According to data from the Government of Canada Job Bank, the median salary for aircraft maintenance engineers in Canada is around $32 per hour. This works out to an annual salary of approximately $66,560. However, this is just a median figure, which means that some aircraft maintenance engineers may make more or less than this amount.

In general, aircraft maintenance engineers who work for larger airlines or companies tend to earn more than those who work for smaller operators. Additionally, those who have specialized skills or certifications may be able to command higher salaries. For example, an aircraft maintenance engineer who is certified to work on a particular type of aircraft may be able to earn more than one who is not certified.

Personal Experience with Aircraft Maintenance Engineer Salary in Canada

As a former aircraft maintenance engineer in Canada, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary widely in this field. When I first started out, I was making around $25 per hour, which was on the lower end of the scale. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased to around $40 per hour. Of course, this is just my personal experience, and your mileage may vary.

Factors that Affect Aircraft Maintenance Engineer Salary in Canada

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much an aircraft maintenance engineer makes in Canada. One of the biggest factors is location. Salaries tend to be higher in larger cities where there is more demand for skilled technicians. For example, an aircraft maintenance engineer working in Toronto or Vancouver may earn more than one working in a smaller town.

Another factor that can impact salary is experience. Generally speaking, the more experience an aircraft maintenance engineer has, the higher their salary will be. This is because experienced technicians are often able to perform their duties more efficiently and effectively, which can save their employer time and money.

Certifications and Specializations

One way that aircraft maintenance engineers can increase their earning potential is by obtaining specialized certifications. For example, there are certifications available for working on specific types of aircraft, such as Boeing or Airbus planes. Additionally, there are certifications available for specific tasks, such as avionics or sheet metal work.

By obtaining these certifications, aircraft maintenance engineers demonstrate that they have advanced knowledge and skills in their field. This can make them more valuable to employers, which can in turn lead to higher salaries.

Negotiating Salary as an Aircraft Maintenance Engineer in Canada

If you're considering a career as an aircraft maintenance engineer in Canada, it's important to remember that salaries are not set in stone. In many cases, employers are willing to negotiate salaries with qualified candidates. This is especially true if you have specialized skills or certifications that make you particularly valuable.

If you're in the process of negotiating your salary, it's important to do your research beforehand. Look up salary data for your particular location and industry to get an idea of what you should be making. Additionally, be prepared to make a case for why you should be paid a certain amount. This might include highlighting your experience, certifications, or other relevant skills.

Conclusion of How Much Do Aircraft Maintenance Engineers Make in Canada

Overall, the salary of an aircraft maintenance engineer in Canada can vary widely depending on a number of factors. However, with experience, certifications, and the right negotiation skills, it's possible to earn a competitive salary in this field. If you're considering a career as an aircraft maintenance engineer, be sure to do your research and explore all of your options for increasing your earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for an aircraft maintenance engineer in Canada?

A: According to the Government of Canada Job Bank, the median salary for aircraft maintenance engineers in Canada is around $32 per hour, which works out to an annual salary of approximately $66,560.

Q: What factors can impact an aircraft maintenance engineer's salary in Canada?

A: Salary can be impacted by a number of factors, including location, experience, employer, and certifications.

Q: Do aircraft maintenance engineers in Canada need to be certified?

A: Yes, aircraft maintenance engineers in Canada must be certified by Transport Canada in order to work on Canadian-registered aircraft.

Q: Is it possible to negotiate salary as an aircraft maintenance engineer in Canada?

A: Yes, employers may be willing to negotiate salaries with qualified candidates, especially if they have specialized skills or certifications.
