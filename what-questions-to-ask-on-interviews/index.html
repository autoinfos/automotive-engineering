What Questions To Ask On Interviews A Guide For Interior Designers

Written by Luffy Sep 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As an interior designer, you know that the interview process is crucial for finding the right fit for your team. However, it can be difficult to determine what questions to ask on interviews to get the most out of your candidates. In this post, we will guide you through the process of asking the right questions to ensure that you find the ideal candidate for your team.

One of the biggest pain points for interior designers is finding candidates who not only have the necessary skills and experience but also fit in with the company culture. Asking the right questions can help you identify these qualities in potential candidates.

The target of what questions to ask on interviews is to gain a better understanding of the candidate's skills, experience, personality, and work style. This information can help you determine whether a candidate is a good fit for the team and the project at hand.

In summary, when asking what questions to ask on interviews, it is important to focus on both the candidate's technical abilities and their personality. By doing so, you will be better equipped to make an informed decision about whether a candidate is the right fit for your team.

Questions to Ask on Interviews: Technical Skills

When it comes to technical skills, it is important to ask questions that will help you determine whether the candidate has the necessary knowledge and experience to complete the job. For example:

1. What experience do you have in designing and implementing similar projects?

2. What software and tools are you proficient in using?

3. Can you walk me through your design process from start to finish?

4. How do you stay up to date with the latest trends and technologies in the industry?

By asking these questions, you can gain insight into the candidate's technical abilities and determine whether they have the necessary skills to complete the job.

Questions to Ask on Interviews: Personality and Work Style

It is also important to ask questions that will help you understand the candidate's personality and work style. This will help you determine whether they will fit in with the team and be a good fit for the project at hand. For example:

1. How do you handle conflicting opinions with team members or clients?

2. How do you manage your time and prioritize tasks?

3. Can you tell me about a time when you had to deal with a difficult client?

4. How do you handle stress and pressure in a fast-paced environment?

By asking these questions, you can gain insight into the candidate's personality and work style and determine whether they are a good fit for the team and the project.

How to Evaluate Responses to What Questions to Ask on Interviews

Once you have asked the right questions, it is important to evaluate the candidate's responses. Look for responses that demonstrate a strong understanding of the technical aspects of the job as well as a good fit for the company culture. Additionally, pay attention to the candidate's communication skills and ability to articulate their thoughts and ideas effectively.

How to Follow Up After Asking What Questions to Ask on Interviews

After the interview, it is important to follow up with the candidate to thank them for their time and provide any additional information they may need. Additionally, take the time to review your notes and evaluate the candidate's responses to determine whether they are the right fit for your team.

What Not to Ask on Interviews

When asking what questions to ask on interviews, it is important to avoid questions that could be considered discriminatory or irrelevant to the job. For example, it is illegal to ask candidates about their age, race, religion, or disability status. Additionally, questions about a candidate's personal life or family status are also off-limits.

Conclusion of What Questions to Ask on Interviews

Asking the right questions on interviews is essential for finding the right fit for your team. By focusing on both technical skills and personality, you can gain insight into the candidate's abilities and determine whether they are a good fit for your team and the project at hand. Remember to avoid questions that could be considered discriminatory or irrelevant to the job, and follow up with candidates after the interview to provide any additional information they may need.
