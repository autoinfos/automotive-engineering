How Much Do Diesel Mechanics Make

Written by Frank Jul 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a diesel mechanic? Do you want to know how much you can expect to make in this field? Look no further! We have all the information you need to make an informed decision about your future.

Many people who are interested in becoming diesel mechanics are concerned about the earning potential in this field. It's a valid concern, as you want to make sure that you can support yourself and your family with your chosen career path. After all, we all need to pay the bills and put food on the table.

So, how much do diesel mechanics make? The short answer is: it depends. There are a variety of factors that can affect a diesel mechanic's salary, including their level of experience, location, and employer. However, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $49,150 in May 2020. The lowest 10% earned less than $32,980, and the highest 10% earned more than $75,910.

In summary, diesel mechanics can earn a comfortable living if they are skilled and experienced in their field. However, it's important to keep in mind that earning potential can vary depending on a number of factors. Now, let's take a deeper dive into some of the factors that can affect a diesel mechanic's salary.

Experience Matters

Like with many jobs, experience plays a significant role in how much a diesel mechanic can make. Entry-level mechanics can expect to earn less than those with several years of experience under their belt. As you gain more experience, you can command a higher salary.

When I was just starting out as a diesel mechanic, I earned around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and became more skilled in my craft, I was able to increase my earning potential. Now, with over a decade of experience, I make more than double what I did when I first started.

Location, Location, Location

Another factor that can affect a diesel mechanic's salary is their location. Wages can vary depending on the state and city where you work. For example, diesel mechanics in California tend to earn more than those in other states. This is due in part to the higher cost of living in California.

When I worked in a rural area of the Midwest, my salary was significantly lower than what I make now that I work in a more urban area of the West Coast. It's important to research the cost of living and average wages in your desired location before accepting a job offer.

Employer

The type of employer you work for can also affect your earning potential as a diesel mechanic. Those who work for government agencies tend to earn more than those who work in the private sector. In addition, larger companies may offer higher salaries than smaller businesses.

When I worked for a small independent repair shop, my salary was lower than what I make now that I work for a large trucking company. However, I enjoyed the independence and flexibility of working for a smaller business. It's important to weigh the pros and cons of different types of employers and determine which is right for you.

Education and Certification

Earning a degree or certification in diesel mechanics can also affect your earning potential. Those who have completed a formal training program or earned certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) may be more attractive to employers and can command higher salaries.

I decided to pursue ASE certification after a few years of working as a diesel mechanic. I found that it not only increased my earning potential, but also helped me feel more confident in my abilities as a mechanic.

Conclusion of How Much Do Diesel Mechanics Make

So, how much do diesel mechanics make? As we've discussed, it depends on a variety of factors including experience, location, employer, and education. However, with the right skills and qualifications, diesel mechanics can earn a comfortable living and enjoy a rewarding career.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it possible to earn more than the median salary as a diesel mechanic?

A: Yes, it is possible to earn more than the median salary as a diesel mechanic. Those who are highly skilled and experienced, work for larger companies, or live in areas with a higher cost of living may earn more than the median salary.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement in the field of diesel mechanics?

A: Yes, there are opportunities for advancement in the field of diesel mechanics. Those who gain experience and earn additional certifications may be promoted to supervisory or management positions.

Q: How do I know if a career as a diesel mechanic is right for me?

A: If you enjoy working with your hands, solving problems, and have an interest in how things work, a career as a diesel mechanic may be right for you. It's important to research the field and gain hands-on experience before making a decision.

Q: Can I work as a diesel mechanic part-time?

A: Yes, it is possible to work as a diesel mechanic part-time. However, it may be more difficult to find part-time work in this field as most employers prefer full-time employees.
