How Much Do Millwrights Make A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Jun 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in becoming a millwright but unsure about the earning potential of this career? Do you wonder how much do millwrights make on average? If so, you're not alone. Many people who are considering a career in millwrighting are curious about the salary prospects. In this guide, we'll take a closer look at how much millwrights can expect to make, what factors affect their earnings, and more.

Working as a millwright can be a rewarding career choice, but it's important to understand that it's not all sunshine and rainbows. The job can be physically demanding, and the work environment can be noisy, dirty, and potentially dangerous. Additionally, millwrights may be required to work long hours, nights, weekends, and holidays. However, for those who are willing to put in the hard work, the job can offer competitive pay and benefits.

So, how much do millwrights make? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for millwrights was $53,590 as of May 2020. This means that 50 percent of millwrights earn more than this amount, while the other 50 percent earn less. The lowest 10 percent of millwrights earn less than $33,810 per year, while the highest 10 percent earn more than $80,110 per year.

In addition to experience and location, other factors that can affect a millwright's earnings include the industry they work in, the type of work they do, and their level of education and certification. For example, millwrights who work in the utilities industry tend to earn more than those who work in manufacturing. Similarly, millwrights who specialize in complex machinery may earn more than those who work on simpler equipment. Finally, millwrights who have additional certifications or degrees may be able to command higher salaries.

What Is the Salary Range for Millwrights?

When it comes to determining how much do millwrights make, it's important to understand that there is a wide range of salaries within the profession. As mentioned earlier, the median annual wage for millwrights is $53,590. However, this is just an average, and many millwrights earn more or less than this amount.

For example, if you're just starting out as a millwright, you can expect to earn less than someone who has been in the profession for several years. Similarly, if you're working in a rural area with a low cost of living, you may earn less than someone who is working in a large city with a high cost of living.

What Are the Different Career Paths for Millwrights?

Millwrights can work in a variety of industries, including manufacturing, construction, power generation, and more. Within each of these industries, there may be different career paths available. For example, a millwright who works in manufacturing may have the opportunity to become a supervisor or manager, while a millwright who works in construction may be able to start their own business.

In addition, millwrights can specialize in different types of equipment, such as pumps, turbines, or conveyors. By specializing in a particular area, millwrights can develop a unique set of skills that may be in high demand, leading to higher salaries and more job opportunities.

What Are Some of the Benefits of a Career in Millwrighting?

While the salary potential is certainly an important factor to consider when choosing a career, there are many other benefits to working as a millwright. For one, the job can be very fulfilling, as millwrights get to work with their hands and see the tangible results of their labor. In addition, the job can offer opportunities for travel and a chance to work on interesting and complex machinery. Finally, millwrights may enjoy benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

How Can You Increase Your Earning Potential as a Millwright?

If you're already working as a millwright and want to increase your earning potential, there are several steps you can take. First, consider pursuing additional certifications or degrees, such as a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering or a certification in welding. These credentials can demonstrate your expertise and make you a more attractive candidate for higher-paying positions.

In addition, consider specializing in a particular area of millwrighting, such as hydraulics or pneumatics. By developing a unique skill set, you can set yourself apart from other millwrights and command higher salaries.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to become a millwright?
A: Becoming a millwright typically requires completion of a formal apprenticeship program, which can take anywhere from three to five years. In addition, some millwrights may choose to pursue additional education or certifications, which can take additional time.

Q: What industries are millwrights in high demand?
A: Millwrights are in high demand in a variety of industries, including manufacturing, construction, power generation, and more. However, demand may vary depending on the specific region and industry.

Q: Do millwrights typically work full-time or part-time?
A: Most millwrights work full-time, although part-time positions may be available in some industries.

Q: What skills do you need to be a successful millwright?
A: Successful millwrights typically have strong mechanical skills, problem-solving abilities, and attention to detail. They should also be physically fit and able to work in noisy, dirty, and potentially dangerous environments.

Conclusion of How Much Do Millwrights Make

In summary, millwrights can expect to earn a median annual wage of $53,590, although the actual salary range can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, industry, and specialization. While the job can be physically demanding and potentially dangerous, it can also offer competitive pay and benefits, as well as opportunities for career advancement and personal fulfillment.
