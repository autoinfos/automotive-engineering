How Much Does A Pharmacy Technician Earn In Canada

Written by Bardi Oct 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician in Canada? One of the biggest questions you may have is how much you can expect to earn in this position. It's important to have a clear understanding of your potential salary and what factors can impact it.

The salary of a pharmacy technician can vary depending on a few key factors. These include your level of education and experience, the type of pharmacy you work in, and the location of the pharmacy. Additionally, salaries may differ depending on the province or territory you are working in.

According to the Government of Canada Job Bank, the median salary for a pharmacy technician in Canada is $44,096 per year. This means that half of all pharmacy technicians earn more than this amount and half earn less. The lowest 10 percent earn less than $29,000 per year, while the highest 10 percent earn more than $64,000 per year.

In general, pharmacy technicians who work in hospitals tend to earn more than those who work in retail pharmacies. Additionally, those who have completed a formal education program or have several years of experience may be able to earn a higher salary.

My Experience as a Pharmacy Technician in Canada

As a pharmacy technician with five years of experience, I currently earn an annual salary of $52,000. I work in a hospital pharmacy in Toronto, which I believe has contributed to my higher salary. Additionally, I completed a two-year pharmacy technician program at a community college, which I believe helped me to secure a higher starting salary when I first entered the field.

Finding Opportunities to Increase Your Salary

If you're looking to earn a higher salary as a pharmacy technician, there are a few things you can do to increase your earning potential. Consider completing a formal education program, such as a two-year diploma program at a community college. Additionally, gaining experience in a hospital pharmacy or specializing in a certain area, such as compounding, can help you to earn a higher salary.

Salary Differences Across Canada

It's important to note that salaries for pharmacy technicians can vary depending on the province or territory you are working in. For example, pharmacy technicians in Ontario tend to earn higher salaries compared to those in other provinces. This may be due to a higher cost of living in Ontario, as well as the demand for skilled pharmacy technicians in the region.

Negotiating Your Salary as a Pharmacy Technician

If you're starting a new job as a pharmacy technician, it's important to negotiate your salary to ensure that you are getting paid what you're worth. Research the average salary for pharmacy technicians in your area and bring this information to your employer during salary negotiations. Additionally, highlight your education and experience to show why you deserve a higher salary.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Pharmacy Technician Earn in Canada

Overall, the median salary for a pharmacy technician in Canada is $44,096 per year. However, this can vary based on factors such as education, experience, and location. By completing a formal education program and gaining experience in a hospital pharmacy, you may be able to increase your earning potential. It's also important to negotiate your salary to ensure that you are getting paid what you're worth.

Question and Answer

Q: Are pharmacy technicians in demand in Canada?

A: Yes, pharmacy technicians are in demand in Canada due to an aging population and an increased need for healthcare services.

Q: How long does it take to become a pharmacy technician in Canada?

A: It typically takes two years to complete a pharmacy technician program at a community college in Canada.

Q: What are the job prospects for pharmacy technicians in Canada?

A: Job prospects for pharmacy technicians in Canada are good, with a projected growth rate of 9-13 percent over the next few years.

Q: Can pharmacy technicians earn overtime pay in Canada?

A: Yes, pharmacy technicians in Canada are typically eligible for overtime pay if they work more than their scheduled hours.
