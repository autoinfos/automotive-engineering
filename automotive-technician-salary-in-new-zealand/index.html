How Much Do Automotive Technicians Earn In New Zealand

Written by Oliver Jun 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an Automotive Technician in New Zealand? If so, you might be wondering how much you can expect to earn. While it may not be the most glamorous job in the world, it's an essential role that keeps our cars running smoothly.

Unfortunately, the salary for Automotive Technicians in New Zealand can vary greatly depending on a number of factors. Some of the pain points that contribute to this include experience, location, and the type of employer you work for.

So, what can you expect to earn as an Automotive Technician in New Zealand? The answer isn't straightforward, but we'll do our best to explain.

In general, Automotive Technicians in New Zealand can expect to earn between $20 and $35 per hour. However, this can vary depending on your level of experience, with entry-level technicians earning around $20 per hour, and experienced technicians earning up to $35 per hour or more.

Personal Experience with Automotive Technician Salary in New Zealand

As a former Automotive Technician in New Zealand, I can attest to the fact that the salary can vary greatly depending on where you work. When I started out in the industry, I was earning around $22 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and moved into a more specialized role, my salary increased to $28 per hour.

It's important to note that the type of employer you work for can also impact your salary. For example, working for a dealership may provide more stability and potentially higher pay, while working for a smaller independent garage may offer fewer benefits but a more relaxed work environment.

Factors that Affect Automotive Technician Salary in New Zealand

As mentioned earlier, there are a number of factors that can impact your salary as an Automotive Technician in New Zealand. One of the biggest factors is experience. Entry-level technicians can expect to earn less than experienced technicians, as they are still learning and developing their skills.

Location can also play a role in determining your salary. Technicians in larger cities like Auckland and Wellington may earn more than those in smaller towns, as the cost of living is generally higher in these areas.

The type of employer you work for is also a factor. As mentioned earlier, working for a dealership may offer higher pay and more benefits, while working for an independent garage may offer less pay but a more relaxed work environment.

Training and Certification for Automotive Technicians in New Zealand

If you're interested in becoming an Automotive Technician in New Zealand, you'll need to undergo training and certification. Many employers require a minimum of a National Certificate in Motor Industry (Automotive Electrical and Mechanical Engineering) Level 3, which can be obtained through a number of training providers throughout the country.

In addition to certification, ongoing training and professional development is important in this industry to keep up with the latest technology and advancements in automotive engineering.

How to Increase Your Automotive Technician Salary in New Zealand

If you're already working as an Automotive Technician in New Zealand and want to increase your salary, there are a few things you can do. One is to specialize in a particular area, such as diesel engines or hybrid technology. This can make you a more valuable employee and potentially lead to higher pay.

Another option is to gain more experience and become a senior technician or service manager. These roles typically come with higher pay and more responsibility.

Conclusion of Automotive Technician Salary in New Zealand

While the salary for Automotive Technicians in New Zealand can vary greatly depending on a number of factors, it's generally a stable and reliable career choice. With ongoing training and professional development, there are plenty of opportunities to increase your salary and advance in the industry.

A: Many employers require a minimum of a National Certificate in Motor Industry (Automotive Electrical and Mechanical Engineering) Level 3, which can be obtained through a number of training providers throughout the country.
