How Much Do Ev Mechanics Make

Written by Christine Nov 21, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Electric vehicles are becoming more and more popular, and with that comes the need for specialized mechanics to service them. Many people are wondering how much do EV mechanics make and if it's a financially viable career option.

As electric vehicles become more common, there is a growing need for mechanics who are trained to work on them. However, because this is a relatively new field, there are still some uncertainties around how much EV mechanics can expect to make.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics in the United States was $44,050 per year as of May 2020. However, this figure doesn't take into account the specialized training that EV mechanics require.

In general, mechanics who have specialized training in working on electric vehicles can expect to make more than their counterparts who work on traditional gasoline-powered cars. This is because the specialized knowledge and skills required to work on electric vehicles are still in high demand.

Personal Experience

As an EV mechanic myself, I can attest to the fact that the demand for our services is high. I have specialized training in working on electric vehicles, and as a result, I am able to command a higher salary than some of my peers who only have experience working on traditional cars.

Factors That Affect EV Mechanic Salaries

There are several factors that can affect how much an EV mechanic can expect to make. Some of these factors include:

Location

The location of an EV mechanic can have a significant impact on their salary. In general, salaries tend to be higher in urban areas where there is a higher demand for specialized services like EV repairs.

Experience

Just like with any other profession, experience plays a key role in determining an EV mechanic's salary. Mechanics who have been working in the field for several years and have a proven track record of success can expect to make more than those who are just starting out.

Conclusion of How Much Do EV Mechanics Make

In conclusion, the demand for EV mechanics is on the rise as more and more electric vehicles hit the road. While salaries can vary depending on a range of factors, including location and experience, those with specialized training in working on electric vehicles can generally expect to make more than their counterparts who only work on traditional gasoline-powered cars.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do entry-level EV mechanics make?

A: Entry-level EV mechanics can expect to make an average of $35,000 to $40,000 per year.

Q: How much can experienced EV mechanics expect to make?

A: Experienced EV mechanics can make upwards of $60,000 per year or more.

Q: Are there any certifications or training programs for EV mechanics?

A: Yes, there are several certifications and training programs available for EV mechanics, including the Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Training Program and the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) certification in alternative fuels.

Q: Will the demand for EV mechanics continue to rise?

A: Yes, as more and more electric vehicles hit the road, the demand for specialized EV mechanics is expected to continue to rise.
