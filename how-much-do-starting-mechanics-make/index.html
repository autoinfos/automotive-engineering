How Much Do Starting Mechanics Make

Written by Aprilia May 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a mechanic? Do you want to know how much starting mechanics make? If so, keep reading!

Many people are concerned about their income potential when they are starting a new career. Mechanics are no exception. Starting mechanics may wonder how much they can expect to earn, especially if they are just starting out and have little to no experience.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $25,790, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $68,880.

In general, the amount that starting mechanics make can vary based on factors such as experience, location, and type of employer. Additionally, mechanics who have specialized knowledge or certifications may be able to earn more.

How Much Can You Expect to Earn as a Starting Mechanic?

When I first started my career as a mechanic, I earned around $30,000 per year. This was in a small town in a rural area. However, as I gained more experience and knowledge, I was able to earn more. After a few years, I was earning around $40,000 per year.

It's important to keep in mind that starting salaries can vary widely based on location and type of employer. For example, mechanics who work for dealerships or manufacturers may earn more than those who work for smaller independent shops.

What Factors Affect Salary for Starting Mechanics?

Several factors can affect the salary of a starting mechanic. These include:

Experience

The more experience you have as a mechanic, the more you can expect to earn. This is because experienced mechanics are generally more efficient and can complete jobs more quickly than newer mechanics.

Location

The location where you work can also affect your salary. Mechanics who work in larger cities or metropolitan areas may earn more than those who work in smaller towns or rural areas.

Certifications

Holding certifications in specific areas of auto repair can also increase your earning potential. For example, if you are certified in air conditioning repair, you may be able to earn more than a mechanic who is not certified in this area.

Negotiation Skills

Finally, your negotiation skills can also affect your starting salary. When applying for a job, be sure to research the average salary for mechanics in your area and be prepared to negotiate for a fair wage.

Conclusion of How Much Do Starting Mechanics Make

Starting salaries for mechanics can vary widely based on factors such as experience, location, and type of employer. However, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. By gaining experience, holding certifications, and negotiating for a fair wage, starting mechanics can increase their earning potential over time.
