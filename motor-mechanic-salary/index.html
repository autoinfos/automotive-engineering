How Much Do Motor Mechanics Really Make

Written by Oliver Sep 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Let's face it, one of the biggest concerns when it comes to pursuing a career in motor mechanics is the salary. It's no secret that the auto repair industry is often perceived as a low-paying industry, but is that really the case? Let's take a closer look at the motor mechanic salary and see how much a mechanic can really expect to earn in today's market.

The truth is, the motor mechanic salary can vary greatly depending on a variety of factors. Factors such as experience, location, industry, and certifications all play a role in determining a mechanic's salary. Additionally, the type of work a mechanic specializes in can also impact their earnings. For example, a mechanic who specializes in diesel engine repair may earn more than a general automotive mechanic.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. This means that half of all mechanics earned more than this amount, while half earned less. The lowest 10 percent of earners made less than $25,000, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $68,880.

So, what does this all mean? It means that while the motor mechanic salary may not be the highest in the world, it is certainly not the lowest either. With the right experience, certifications, and industry, a mechanic can earn a comfortable living and even potentially earn a six-figure salary.

How to Increase Your Motor Mechanic Salary

One way to increase your motor mechanic salary is to specialize in a particular area. By becoming an expert in a specific area of repair, you can command higher wages due to your specialized knowledge and skills. Additionally, obtaining certifications in your field can also increase your earning potential.

For example, becoming an ASE certified mechanic can increase your marketability and potentially lead to higher wages. ASE certification is widely recognized as the industry standard for automotive technicians and mechanics. By obtaining certification, you can demonstrate your expertise and dedication to your craft.

My Personal Experience with Motor Mechanic Salary

As a former motor mechanic, I can attest to the fact that the motor mechanic salary can be quite lucrative. While I did not specialize in a particular area of repair, I did have a significant amount of experience and certifications under my belt. This allowed me to earn a comfortable living and support my family.

One thing to keep in mind is that the motor mechanic salary can vary greatly depending on location. In certain areas of the country, mechanics may earn significantly more due to a higher demand for their services. Conversely, in areas with a high concentration of mechanics, the wages may be lower due to increased competition.

Factors that Impact Motor Mechanic Salary

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact the motor mechanic salary. Some of these factors include:

  • Experience
  • Location
  • Industry
  • Certifications
  • Type of repair work

How to Negotiate Your Motor Mechanic Salary

When it comes to negotiating your motor mechanic salary, there are a few things to keep in mind. First and foremost, do your research. Look up the average salaries for mechanics in your area and use this information to inform your negotiations.

Additionally, be prepared to demonstrate your skills and experience. Bring any certifications or training records with you to your negotiation to show your dedication to your craft. Finally, be confident and professional. Approach the negotiation as a business transaction and be respectful of your employer's budget constraints.

A: Yes, by becoming an expert in a specific area of repair, a mechanic can command higher wages due to their specialized knowledge and skills.

Conclusion of Motor Mechanic Salary

The motor mechanic salary may not be the highest in the world, but it is certainly not the lowest either. With the right experience, certifications, and industry, a mechanic can earn a comfortable living and even potentially earn a six-figure salary. By specializing in a particular area of repair and obtaining certifications, a mechanic can increase their earning potential and demonstrate their expertise and dedication to their craft.
