How Much Does A Mechanic Make A Year In Nz

Written by Christine Oct 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a mechanic in New Zealand but unsure about the earning potential? Many people wonder how much does a mechanic make a year in NZ. It's a valid concern because earning a decent wage is important for everyone.

As a mechanic, you may face some common pain points, such as low salaries, lack of job security, and long working hours. However, the good news is that if you are skilled, dedicated, and have the right certification, you can earn a good income in this field.

The average yearly income of a mechanic in NZ is around $50,000 to $60,000. However, the salary may vary depending on your experience, qualifications, and location. A qualified mechanic with several years of experience can earn more than $80,000 per year.

In conclusion, becoming a mechanic in New Zealand can be a rewarding career if you have the right skills and certifications. The earning potential is good, but it may vary depending on various factors such as location, experience, and qualifications.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential?

One way to increase your earning potential as a mechanic is to specialize in a particular field. For example, you can become an expert in diesel engines, hybrid vehicles, or electrical systems. By specializing, you can demand a higher salary and have a better chance of getting hired by top employers.

Another way to increase your earning potential is to get certified. Certifications such as Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) or National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (NIASE) can help you stand out from other mechanics and increase your chances of getting hired or promoted.

My Personal Experience as a Mechanic in NZ

I have been working as a mechanic for the last 5 years in Auckland, NZ. Initially, I started with a low salary, but as I gained more experience and certifications, my income increased. Currently, I earn around $65,000 per year, which is a decent income in NZ. However, I am planning to specialize in hybrid vehicles and get certified to increase my earning potential even more.

Factors Affecting Mechanic Salaries in NZ

Several factors affect mechanic salaries in NZ, such as experience, qualifications, location, and type of employer. For example, mechanics working for dealerships or larger companies may earn more than those working for smaller independent repair shops. Similarly, mechanics working in cities like Auckland or Wellington may earn more than those working in rural areas.

How to Negotiate Your Salary as a Mechanic in NZ

Negotiating your salary as a mechanic in NZ can be challenging, but it's essential to ensure you are getting paid what you are worth. Firstly, research the market rates for your skills and experience. Secondly, highlight your accomplishments, skills, and qualifications during the interview process. Lastly, be confident, but also open to negotiation to reach a fair salary.

FAQs

Q. What is the average hourly rate for a mechanic in NZ?

A. The average hourly rate for a mechanic in NZ is around $25 to $30 per hour.

Q. What qualifications do I need to become a mechanic in NZ?

A. To become a mechanic in NZ, you need to have completed a relevant qualification such as a National Certificate in Motor Industry (Automotive Electrical and Mechanical Engineering) Level 4.

Q. What is the demand for mechanics in NZ?

A. The demand for mechanics in NZ is high, especially for those with specialized skills and certifications.

Q. Is it possible to earn more than $100,000 per year as a mechanic in NZ?

A. Yes, it's possible to earn more than $100,000 per year as a mechanic in NZ, especially if you have specialized skills, experience, and certifications.

Conclusion

In conclusion, being a mechanic in New Zealand can be a lucrative career choice. The average salary is around $50,000 to $60,000 per year, but it can be increased by gaining experience, certifications, and specializing in a particular field. With the right skills and qualifications, you can earn a good income and enjoy a fulfilling career in this field.
