How Much Do Engineers Make In Canada Per Hour

Written by Daniel Jun 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in engineering and wondering how much you can make in Canada? Or maybe you're already an engineer and curious about how your salary compares to others in the field. Whatever your reason for being here, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll answer the question of how much do engineers make in Canada per hour and provide some useful insights on the topic.

Engineering is a highly skilled profession that requires years of education, training, and experience. As such, engineers are often compensated well for their expertise. However, the salary of an engineer can vary widely depending on several factors, including the type of engineering, the location, the level of experience, and the employer. Understanding these factors is essential to getting an accurate picture of how much engineers make in Canada per hour.

The average hourly wage for an engineer in Canada is around $44 per hour. However, this can range from $27 per hour for entry-level positions to $66 per hour for more experienced engineers. The highest-paying engineering fields in Canada are petroleum, chemical, and aerospace engineering, with average hourly wages of $72, $60, and $52, respectively. On the other hand, the lowest-paying fields are agricultural and geological engineering, with average hourly wages of $28 and $32, respectively.

My Experience as an Engineer in Canada

As a civil engineer with five years of experience working in Vancouver, I can attest to the fact that engineers in Canada are compensated fairly well. I started out making around $30 per hour as an entry-level engineer and worked my way up to $45 per hour as a senior engineer. Of course, this is just my personal experience, and salaries can vary widely depending on the field and location.

The Factors that Affect Engineer Salaries in Canada

As we mentioned earlier, several factors can impact how much engineers make in Canada per hour. Firstly, the type of engineering is a significant factor. Fields like petroleum, chemical, and aerospace engineering tend to pay higher salaries than fields like agricultural and geological engineering. Secondly, the location also plays a role, with cities like Toronto and Vancouver offering higher salaries than smaller towns and rural areas. Finally, the level of experience and the employer can also impact salaries, with more experienced engineers and larger companies typically paying more.

The Importance of Education and Experience

Education and experience are crucial factors in determining how much engineers make in Canada per hour. Typically, engineers are required to have at least a bachelor's degree in engineering or a related field, and many employers prefer candidates with a master's degree or higher. Furthermore, having several years of experience in the field can lead to higher salaries and greater job opportunities.

Negotiating Your Salary as an Engineer in Canada

When it comes to negotiating your salary as an engineer in Canada, it's essential to do your research and understand the market. Research the average salaries for your field and location and be prepared to negotiate with your employer. Additionally, consider the benefits package that comes with the job, as this can often be just as important as the salary itself.

FAQs About How Much Do Engineers Make in Canada Per Hour

A: The average hourly wage for an engineer in Canada is around $44 per hour.

A: The highest-paying engineering fields in Canada are petroleum, chemical, and aerospace engineering, with average hourly wages of $72, $60, and $52, respectively.

Q: Do engineers in Canada make more than engineers in other countries?

A: It depends on the country and the field. In general, engineers in Canada tend to make more than their counterparts in developing countries but may make less than engineers in countries like the United States or Germany.

Conclusion of How Much Do Engineers Make in Canada Per Hour

Overall, engineers in Canada are compensated fairly well for their expertise and experience. The average hourly wage for an engineer in Canada is around $44 per hour, but this can vary widely depending on several factors. To maximize your earning potential as an engineer in Canada, it's essential to choose the right field, location, and employer, and to continue to educate yourself and gain experience in the field.
