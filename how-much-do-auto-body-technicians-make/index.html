How Much Do Auto Body Technicians Make

Written by Daniel Aug 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as an auto body technician? One of the most common questions people have is how much they can expect to make in this field. In this blog post, we'll explore the earning potential of auto body technicians and what factors can impact their salaries.

Understanding the Pain Points of Auto Body Technicians

Auto body technicians are skilled professionals who repair and refinish damaged vehicles. While this can be a rewarding career, it's important to understand that it can also be physically demanding and require long hours on the job. Additionally, many auto body technicians work in fast-paced environments and may need to meet strict deadlines. All of these factors can impact their overall job satisfaction and earning potential.

Answering the Question: How Much Do Auto Body Technicians Make?

The salary of an auto body technician can vary based on a variety of factors, including their experience, location, and the type of employer they work for. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for auto body technicians in the United States was $42,730 as of May 2020. However, the top 10% of earners in this field made more than $69,120 per year.

Summarizing the Main Points

In summary, auto body technicians can expect to make a median salary of around $42,730 per year. However, this can vary based on a range of factors, including experience, location, and employer. While this can be a physically demanding job with long hours, it can also be a rewarding career for those with a passion for repairing and restoring vehicles.

Factors that Impact an Auto Body Technician's Salary

One of the biggest factors that can impact an auto body technician's salary is their level of experience. Those who are just starting out in the field may make less than those who have been working in the industry for several years. Additionally, the location of an auto body technician can impact their earning potential. Those who work in cities or areas with a high cost of living may make more than those in rural areas.

Another factor that can impact an auto body technician's salary is the type of employer they work for. Those who work for dealerships or auto repair shops may have a more stable income and benefits package compared to those who work for smaller, independent garages. However, those who work for smaller garages may have more opportunities for growth and advancement within the company.

Education and Certification

Another factor that can impact an auto body technician's earning potential is their level of education and certification. Those who have completed formal training programs or have earned certification from organizations like the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) may be more competitive in the job market and can command higher salaries.

Negotiating Your Salary as an Auto Body Technician

If you're interested in becoming an auto body technician or are already working in the field, it's important to know your worth and be prepared to negotiate your salary. Do your research on the average salaries for your area and level of experience, and be prepared to make a case for why you deserve a higher salary. Additionally, consider negotiating for additional benefits like paid time off, health insurance, or retirement plans.

Personal Experience: Earning Potential as an Auto Body Technician

As someone who has worked as an auto body technician for several years, I can attest to the fact that earning potential in this field can vary greatly. While I started out making around $30,000 per year, I was able to increase my salary over time by gaining more experience and taking on additional responsibilities at work. Additionally, I found that earning certifications from organizations like ASE helped me stand out in the job market and command higher salaries.

Question and Answer:

Q: What is the job outlook for auto body technicians?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of auto body technicians is projected to grow 4% from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations. This growth is due in part to an increase in the number of vehicles on the road and the need for repair and maintenance services.

Q: Can auto body technicians earn commission on their work?

A: Yes, some auto body technicians may be paid on a commission basis, meaning they earn a percentage of the total cost of the repairs they complete. However, this can vary based on the employer and the specific job requirements.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement in this field?

A: Yes, there are opportunities for advancement in the auto body repair field. Those with experience and additional certifications may be able to move into management positions or open their own auto repair businesses. Additionally, some auto body technicians may specialize in certain areas like custom painting or restoration, allowing them to command higher salaries.

Q: What are some of the most important skills for auto body technicians?

A: Some of the most important skills for auto body technicians include strong attention to detail, problem-solving ability, and good communication skills. Additionally, those who are skilled at working with their hands and have an eye for aesthetics may be well-suited for this career.

Conclusion of How Much Do Auto Body Technicians Make

Overall, auto body technicians can expect to make a median salary of around $42,730 per year, though this can vary based on a range of factors. By gaining experience, earning certifications, and negotiating their salaries, auto body technicians can increase their earning potential and build rewarding careers in this field.
