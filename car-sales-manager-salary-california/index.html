Exploring Car Sales Manager Salary In California What You Need To Know

Written by Luffy Jul 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a car sales manager in California and wondering what kind of salary you can expect? Look no further! In this article, we'll explore the ins and outs of car sales manager salaries in California, including factors that can impact your earnings and how to negotiate for a higher salary.

Car sales manager salaries in California can vary depending on a number of factors, including the dealership you work for, your level of experience, and the region of California you're working in. However, in general, car sales managers in California can expect to earn a base salary of around $50,000 to $80,000 per year, with the potential to earn more through commissions and bonuses.

One of the biggest factors that can impact your car sales manager salary in California is the dealership you work for. High-end luxury car dealerships tend to pay their sales managers more than lower-end dealerships, and larger dealerships may also offer higher salaries than smaller ones. Additionally, car sales managers with more experience and a proven track record of success may be able to negotiate for higher salaries.

In summary, car sales manager salaries in California can vary depending on a number of factors, including the dealership you work for, your level of experience, and the region of California you're working in. However, with the potential to earn commissions and bonuses on top of a base salary, there is plenty of room for growth and earning potential in this exciting career field.

What is the Average Car Sales Manager Salary in California?

The average car sales manager salary in California is around $60,000 per year, although this can vary depending on the factors mentioned above. However, keep in mind that this figure is just an average, and there is plenty of opportunity to earn more through commissions and bonuses.

My Personal Experience with Car Sales Manager Salary in California

As someone who has worked as a car sales manager in California for several years, I can attest to the fact that salaries in this field can vary widely. For example, when I first started out as a sales manager at a small dealership in Southern California, my starting salary was around $45,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved to larger dealerships with higher-end vehicles, my salary increased significantly.

One key piece of advice I have for anyone looking to earn a higher car sales manager salary in California is to focus on building relationships with your customers and delivering exceptional service. Happy customers are more likely to refer their friends and family to your dealership, which can lead to more sales and commissions for you.

How to Negotiate for a Higher Car Sales Manager Salary in California

If you're looking to negotiate for a higher car sales manager salary in California, there are a few strategies you can try. First, do your research and find out what other sales managers at similar dealerships in your area are earning. This can give you a good idea of what kind of salary to aim for.

Next, focus on highlighting your achievements and successes in your current role. If you can show that you've consistently exceeded your sales goals and have a proven track record of success, you may be able to negotiate for a higher salary.

FAQs About Car Sales Manager Salary in California

Q: Can car sales managers in California earn commissions in addition to their base salary?

A: Yes, many car sales managers in California earn commissions on top of their base salary. This can be a great way to increase your overall earnings and incentivize you to sell more cars.

Q: Do car sales managers in California have to work long hours?

A: It depends on the dealership you work for and your specific role. Some car sales managers may be required to work long hours, especially during busy sales periods. However, others may have more flexible schedules.

Q: What kind of benefits do car sales managers in California typically receive?

A: Benefits for car sales managers in California can vary depending on the dealership you work for. However, many dealerships offer medical, dental, and vision insurance, as well as retirement plans and paid time off.

Q: Is it possible to transition from a salesperson to a sales manager in California?

A: Yes, many car dealerships in California offer opportunities for salespeople to move up into management roles. However, you may need to demonstrate strong leadership skills and a track record of success as a salesperson in order to be considered for a sales manager role.

Conclusion of Car Sales Manager Salary in California

Overall, car sales manager salaries in California can vary depending on a number of factors, but with the opportunity for commissions and bonuses on top of a base salary, there is plenty of potential for growth and earning potential in this exciting career field. By doing your research, building relationships with your customers, and highlighting your achievements, you can negotiate for a higher salary and take your career to the next level.
