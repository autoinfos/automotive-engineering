How To Become A Surgical Tech In Texas

Written by Oliver Jun 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in a career in healthcare that allows you to work in an operating room setting? If so, becoming a surgical tech in Texas may be the right path for you. However, the process of becoming a surgical tech can be overwhelming and confusing at times. In this post, we'll break down the steps you need to take to become a surgical tech in Texas, as well as provide some personal experiences and insights along the way.

As you begin your journey to becoming a surgical tech in Texas, you may encounter some pain points along the way. For example, you may have questions about the specific education and training requirements, or you may be uncertain about the job outlook for surgical techs in Texas. These concerns are completely normal, and we hope to address them in this post.

The first step to becoming a surgical tech in Texas is to complete an accredited surgical technology program. These programs typically take between 9-24 months to complete and may result in a certificate, diploma, or associate's degree. During your program, you will learn about anatomy and physiology, surgical procedures, medical terminology, and more. You will also have the opportunity to gain hands-on experience through clinical rotations.

After completing your surgical technology program, you will need to become certified. The National Board of Surgical Technology and Surgical Assisting (NBSTSA) offers the Certified Surgical Technologist (CST) credential, which is recognized nationwide. To become certified, you will need to pass an exam that covers topics such as patient care, surgical procedures, and sterilization techniques.

Personal Experience: The Importance of Networking

When I was studying to become a surgical tech in Texas, I quickly realized the importance of networking. By attending industry events and connecting with other professionals in the field, I was able to learn about new job opportunities, gain insights into different specialties, and even find a mentor. I highly recommend that anyone who is interested in becoming a surgical tech in Texas take advantage of networking opportunities.

The Job Outlook for Surgical Techs in Texas

The job outlook for surgical techs in Texas is positive. The Texas Workforce Commission projects a 25% increase in employment for surgical technologists in Texas from 2016 to 2026. This growth is due in part to the aging population and advances in surgical technology.

Educational Requirements for Surgical Techs in Texas

As mentioned earlier, completing an accredited surgical technology program is the first step to becoming a surgical tech in Texas. It's important to note that not all surgical technology programs are created equal. When researching programs, be sure to look for accreditation by the Commission on Accreditation of Allied Health Education Programs (CAAHEP) or the Accrediting Bureau of Health Education Schools (ABHES). These accreditations ensure that the program meets certain standards of quality and rigor.

Certification Requirements for Surgical Techs in Texas

In addition to completing an accredited surgical technology program, you will need to become certified to work as a surgical tech in Texas. The NBSTSA offers the CST credential, which is recognized nationwide. To be eligible for certification, you must have completed an accredited surgical technology program and pass the CST exam. You will also need to maintain your certification through continuing education.

Personal Experience: Finding the Right Workplace Culture

When I first started working as a surgical tech in Texas, I found that the workplace culture was just as important as the actual work itself. By finding a workplace culture that aligned with my values and goals, I was able to thrive and grow in my career. I encourage anyone who is considering becoming a surgical tech in Texas to think about what kind of workplace culture they would thrive in.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to become a surgical tech in Texas?

A: The length of time it takes to become a surgical tech in Texas depends on the specific program you choose. Most programs take between 9-24 months to complete.

Q: What kind of salary can I expect as a surgical tech in Texas?

A: The average salary for surgical techs in Texas is $46,000 per year, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Q: Do I need to become certified to work as a surgical tech in Texas?

A: Yes, you must become certified to work as a surgical tech in Texas. The NBSTSA offers the CST credential, which is recognized nationwide.

Q: What kind of workplace settings can surgical techs work in?

A: Surgical techs can work in a variety of settings, including hospitals, outpatient surgery centers, and physician offices.

Conclusion of How to Become a Surgical Tech in Texas

Becoming a surgical tech in Texas is a rewarding career choice for those interested in healthcare and surgery. By completing an accredited surgical technology program and becoming certified, you can be on your way to a fulfilling career in this growing field. Remember to network, research workplace culture, and stay up-to-date with continuing education to continue to grow in your career as a surgical tech in Texas.
