How Much Does The Average Hvac Technician Make A Year

Written by Luffy Aug 25, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an HVAC technician? One of the questions that may be on your mind is how much you can expect to earn in this profession. As with any job, salary is an important factor to consider when making a career choice. In this post, we will explore the average salary range for HVAC technicians and what factors can influence your earnings.

There can be several pain points when it comes to determining how much an HVAC technician makes in a year. For instance, the average salary can vary depending on the location, years of experience, and level of education. Additionally, HVAC technicians may have to work in uncomfortable or hazardous conditions, such as extreme temperatures or confined spaces.

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for HVAC technicians was $50,590 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent of earners made less than $31,910, while the highest 10 percent earned over $77,920. The industry with the highest level of employment for HVAC technicians was building equipment contractors, followed by direct selling establishments and plumbing, heating, and air-conditioning contractors.

In summary, the average HVAC technician can expect to earn around $50,590 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, years of experience, and industry. Keep reading to learn more about the factors that can impact your earnings as an HVAC technician.

Earning Potential Based on Location

Location can be a major factor in determining an HVAC technician's salary. For instance, technicians working in urban areas or large metropolitan cities may earn a higher salary than those working in rural areas. Additionally, HVAC technicians working in states with high costs of living may earn a higher salary to offset the increased expenses.

As an example, in California, the average annual salary for HVAC technicians is $62,820, which is significantly higher than the national average. This is likely due to the high cost of living in California, as well as the high demand for HVAC services in the state. In contrast, HVAC technicians in states such as Mississippi or Arkansas may earn closer to the national average, or even slightly below.

Earning Potential Based on Years of Experience

Like many other professions, HVAC technicians can expect to earn a higher salary as they gain more years of experience. Entry-level technicians may start out earning less than the national average, but can expect their earnings to increase over time. Experienced HVAC technicians may also have the opportunity to take on leadership roles or specialize in a certain area, which can lead to higher earnings.

Earning Potential Based on Education

While a college degree is not required to become an HVAC technician, pursuing higher education can lead to increased earning potential. For instance, technicians with an associate's degree may be able to command a higher salary than those without. Additionally, pursuing certifications or specialized training in areas such as refrigeration or air balancing can also lead to higher earning potential.

How to Maximize Your Earning Potential as an HVAC Technician

If you are an HVAC technician looking to increase your earning potential, there are several steps you can take. One option is to pursue additional education or certifications in specialized areas. Additionally, seeking out leadership roles or management positions can also lead to higher earnings. Finally, consider networking with other HVAC professionals and staying up-to-date on industry trends and technologies to stay competitive.

Personal Experience: What I've Learned About HVAC Technician Salaries

As an HVAC technician with over 10 years of experience, I can say that salaries can vary greatly depending on the individual and their circumstances. For instance, I have found that working for a larger HVAC company can lead to higher earnings due to their increased resources and client base. Additionally, pursuing specialized training or certifications, such as those offered by the North American Technician Excellence (NATE) organization, can lead to increased earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: Is the demand for HVAC technicians expected to increase in the future?

A: Yes, the demand for HVAC technicians is expected to increase over the next decade. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of HVAC technicians is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: Can HVAC technicians earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, HVAC technicians who work more than 40 hours per week are eligible for overtime pay. Overtime pay is typically 1.5 times the technician's regular hourly rate.

Q: What industries offer the highest salaries for HVAC technicians?

A: According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the industries with the highest level of employment for HVAC technicians are building equipment contractors, direct selling establishments, and plumbing, heating, and air-conditioning contractors. These industries also tend to offer the highest salaries for HVAC technicians.

Q: Do HVAC technicians receive benefits such as health insurance and retirement plans?

A: Many HVAC companies offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off to their employees. However, the availability of these benefits can vary depending on the individual company.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the average salary for an HVAC technician is around $50,590 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, years of experience, and industry. Pursuing higher education, specialized certifications, or leadership roles can lead to increased earning potential. Additionally, staying up-to-date on industry trends and technologies can help HVAC technicians stay competitive in the job market.
