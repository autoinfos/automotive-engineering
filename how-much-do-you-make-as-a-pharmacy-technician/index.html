How Much Do You Make As A Pharmacy Technician

Written by Oliver Jun 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a pharmacy technician, you play a vital role in the healthcare industry. However, many people wonder how much they can expect to make in this career. In this blog post, we'll explore the average salary for pharmacy technicians and what factors can impact your earning potential.

Understanding the Pain Points of Pharmacy Technician Salaries

One of the biggest pain points for pharmacy technicians is the perception that they don't make enough money for the work they do. Additionally, many people are unsure about the salary range for this career, which can make it difficult to decide if it's the right fit for them.

What is the Average Salary for a Pharmacy Technician?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for pharmacy technicians is $33,950 as of May 2020. However, this can vary based on factors such as your level of experience, location, and the type of healthcare facility where you work.

Factors That Affect Your Pharmacy Technician Salary

Experience is one of the biggest factors that can impact your salary as a pharmacy technician. Entry-level technicians may make closer to the lower end of the salary range, while those with more experience and specialized skills may earn more.

Your location can also play a role in your earning potential. In general, pharmacy technicians in urban areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas. Additionally, the type of healthcare facility where you work can impact your salary. For example, technicians who work in hospitals may earn more than those who work in retail pharmacies.

Personal Experience

As a pharmacy technician with five years of experience, I currently earn a salary of $40,000 per year. While this is slightly above the median salary, I worked hard to gain specialized skills in areas such as compounding and medication therapy management, which helped me negotiate a higher salary.

Is Becoming a Pharmacy Technician a Good Career Choice?

If you're considering a career as a pharmacy technician, it's important to weigh the potential earnings against other factors such as job satisfaction and growth opportunities. While the salary may not be as high as some other healthcare professions, pharmacy technicians play a crucial role in helping patients get the medications they need to manage their health.

What Education Do You Need to Become a Pharmacy Technician?

In most cases, you'll need to complete a pharmacy technician training program or earn an associate's degree in pharmacy technology. Additionally, some states require pharmacy technicians to be licensed or certified, which may involve passing an exam.

What Skills Do You Need to Succeed as a Pharmacy Technician?

Some of the most important skills for pharmacy technicians include attention to detail, strong communication skills, and the ability to work well as part of a team. Additionally, you'll need to have a solid understanding of medications, dosage forms, and drug interactions.

Personal Experience

When I first started working as a pharmacy technician, I struggled with time management and keeping up with the fast-paced environment of a retail pharmacy. However, over time, I developed strong organizational skills and learned to work efficiently while still ensuring accuracy and patient safety.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can you make more money as a pharmacy technician if you have a certification?

A: Yes, earning a certification such as Certified Pharmacy Technician (CPhT) can help you demonstrate your knowledge and skills to potential employers and may lead to higher pay.

Q: Is it possible to become a pharmacy technician without formal training?

A: While some employers may hire pharmacy technicians without formal training, most require at least a high school diploma and completion of a pharmacy technician training program or associate's degree in pharmacy technology.

Q: What is the job outlook for pharmacy technicians?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of pharmacy technicians is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: How does the salary for pharmacy technicians compare to other healthcare professions?

A: While the salary for pharmacy technicians may not be as high as some other healthcare professions, such as nurses or physician assistants, it can still provide a stable income and opportunities for growth.

Conclusion of How Much Do You Make as a Pharmacy Technician

Overall, the salary for pharmacy technicians can vary based on factors such as experience, location, and the type of healthcare facility where you work. While it may not be the highest-paying healthcare profession, becoming a pharmacy technician can provide a stable income and opportunities for growth.
