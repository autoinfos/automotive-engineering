What is the Target of Occupational Therapy Assistant Schools in Houston, Texas?

The target of occupational therapy assistant schools in Houston, Texas is to provide students with the knowledge and skills they need to become effective occupational therapy assistants. This includes understanding the principles of occupational therapy, assessing patients' needs, developing treatment plans, and providing hands-on therapy to help patients achieve their goals. Occupational therapy assistants work under the supervision of licensed occupational therapists and may work in a variety of settings, including hospitals, clinics, schools, and nursing homes.

In addition to classroom instruction, occupational therapy assistant programs typically include clinical experiences where students can apply what they've learned in real-world settings. This hands-on experience is critical for students to develop the skills they'll need to succeed in their careers.

To summarize, occupational therapy assistant schools in Houston, Texas aim to provide students with the education and training they need to become effective occupational therapy assistants. This includes classroom instruction and clinical experiences that prepare students for the demands of the job.

Financial Aid Options for Occupational Therapy Assistant Schools in Houston, Texas

One of the major concerns for many students considering occupational therapy assistant schools in Houston, Texas is the cost of tuition. Accreditation of Occupational Therapy Assistant Schools in Houston, Texas

When choosing an occupational therapy assistant school in Houston, Texas, it's important to ensure that the program is accredited by the Accreditation Council for Occupational Therapy Education (ACOTE). Accreditation ensures that the program meets high standards for education and prepares students for successful careers as occupational therapy assistants. Additionally, only graduates of accredited programs are eligible to sit for the national certification exam, which is required for licensure in most states.

This growth is due in part to the aging baby boomer population, who will require occupational therapy services to maintain their health and independence. Additionally, advances in medical technology are enabling more patients to survive traumatic injuries and illnesses, increasing the demand for rehabilitation services. Overall, job prospects for occupational therapy assistants in Houston, Texas are excellent.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between an occupational therapy assistant and an occupational therapist?

A: Occupational therapists are licensed healthcare professionals who evaluate and treat patients with physical, mental, or emotional disabilities to help them achieve their goals. Occupational therapy assistants work under the supervision of licensed occupational therapists to provide hands-on therapy to patients.

Q: How long does it take to complete an occupational therapy assistant program in Houston, Texas?

A: Most occupational therapy assistant programs in Houston, Texas take about two years to complete. Some programs may offer accelerated options that allow students to finish in less time.

Q: What is the average salary for an occupational therapy assistant in Houston, Texas?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for occupational therapy assistants in Houston, Texas was $62,200 as of May 2020.

Q: Is licensure required to practice as an occupational therapy assistant in Houston, Texas?

A: Yes, licensure is required to practice as an occupational therapy assistant in Texas. To become licensed, you must graduate from an accredited occupational therapy assistant program, pass the national certification exam, and apply for licensure through the Texas Board of Occupational Therapy Examiners.
