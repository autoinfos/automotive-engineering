How Much Do Mechanics Make At A Dealership In California

Written by Oliver Jul 25, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as a mechanic at a dealership in California, but wondering how much you can expect to earn? Perhaps you're already working in the industry and looking to switch employers or move up the ranks. Whatever your situation, understanding the average salary for dealership mechanics in California can help you make informed decisions about your career path.

When it comes to the automotive industry, California is one of the largest and most lucrative markets in the United States. However, with a high cost of living and competitive job market, it can be challenging to determine a fair wage for mechanics in this state.

According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for auto mechanics in California is $24.81, which amounts to an annual salary of $51,620. However, this figure can vary widely depending on a number of factors, including your level of experience, the type of dealership you work for, and any additional certifications or training you may have.

In general, mechanics who work at luxury car dealerships or specialize in high-end European imports can expect to earn more than those who work at budget-friendly dealerships or focus on domestic vehicles. Additionally, mechanics who hold certifications from organizations like the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) or have completed manufacturer-specific training programs may be able to command higher salaries.

My Personal Experience

As a former mechanic at a luxury car dealership in California, I can attest to the fact that wages can vary widely in this industry. While I was able to earn a comfortable living and enjoyed the benefits of working with high-end vehicles, I knew colleagues who struggled to make ends meet at less prestigious dealerships.

However, I also found that pursuing additional certifications and training helped me earn more money and advance in my career. By completing ASE exams and attending manufacturer-specific training courses, I was able to demonstrate my expertise to employers and negotiate higher wages.

Factors That Affect Mechanic Salaries at Dealerships in California

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much a mechanic can earn at a dealership in California. Here are some of the most significant:

Experience Level

As with any profession, experience is a major factor that can affect a mechanic's salary. Newer mechanics may earn less than those with decades of experience under their belt, as they are still learning the ropes and building their skill set. However, with time and dedication, junior mechanics can work their way up to more senior roles and higher salaries.

Type of Dealership

The type of dealership you work for can also have a significant impact on your salary. As mentioned earlier, luxury car dealerships and those that specialize in high-end imports tend to pay higher wages than budget-friendly dealerships or those that focus on domestic vehicles. Additionally, dealerships located in affluent areas may pay more than those in less desirable neighborhoods.

Qualifications and Certifications

Another key factor in determining a mechanic's salary is their level of education and certifications. Mechanics who hold degrees or certifications in automotive technology or related fields may be able to command higher wages than those without formal education. Additionally, mechanics who hold certifications from organizations like ASE may be able to earn more than those without any specialized training.

FAQs

Q: Can I earn more as a mechanic if I specialize in a particular type of vehicle?

A: Yes. Mechanics who specialize in luxury or high-end vehicles, particularly European imports, can typically command higher wages than those who work on budget-friendly or domestic vehicles.

Q: Do mechanics who work at dealerships earn more than those who work at independent repair shops?

A: In general, yes. Dealership mechanics tend to earn higher salaries than those who work at independent repair shops, although this can vary depending on several factors.

Q: How do I negotiate a higher salary as a mechanic?

A: The best way to negotiate a higher salary as a mechanic is to demonstrate your value to your employer. This can include pursuing additional certifications or training, highlighting your experience and expertise, and providing examples of how you have helped the dealership save money or improve efficiency.

Q: Is it necessary to have ASE certification to work as a mechanic at a dealership in California?

A: While ASE certification is not always required to work as a mechanic at a dealership in California, it is highly recommended. Holding ASE credentials can help you stand out among other job candidates and demonstrate your expertise to potential employers.

Conclusion

So, how much do mechanics make at a dealership in California? While the average hourly wage for auto mechanics in this state is $24.81, this figure can vary widely depending on several factors, including your level of experience, the type of dealership you work for, and any additional certifications or training you may have. By pursuing additional education and certifications and demonstrating your value to your employer, you can work your way up to higher salaries and more senior roles in the automotive industry.
