How Much Do Electrical Engineers Make In Dubai

Written by Robby Sep 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering how much electrical engineers make in Dubai? This is a common question that many people ask, especially those who are interested in pursuing a career in electrical engineering in Dubai.

Electrical engineers in Dubai have a high earning potential. The average salary for an electrical engineer in Dubai is around AED 120,000 per year. However, this figure can vary depending on a range of factors, including experience, qualifications, and the specific industry that the engineer works in.

Overall, electrical engineers in Dubai can earn a very good salary, making it a lucrative career choice. In this article, we’ll explore how much electrical engineers make in Dubai in more detail.

What is the Average Salary for Electrical Engineers in Dubai?

As mentioned above, the average salary for electrical engineers in Dubai is around AED 120,000 per year. However, this figure can vary depending on a range of factors such as:

Factors Affecting Electrical Engineers' Salaries in Dubai

Experience: Electrical engineers with more experience generally earn higher salaries. This is because experienced engineers have a greater understanding of the industry and are often more skilled in their roles.

Qualifications: Electrical engineers with higher qualifications, such as a Master’s degree or PhD, can often earn higher salaries.

Industry: The industry that an electrical engineer works in can also affect their salary. For example, those working in the oil and gas industry tend to earn higher salaries than those working in other industries.

Location: The location of the engineer’s workplace can also affect their salary. Electrical engineers working in Dubai, where the cost of living is high, tend to earn higher salaries than those working in other parts of the UAE.

Job Opportunities for Electrical Engineers in Dubai

There are many job opportunities for electrical engineers in Dubai. The city is home to a range of industries, including oil and gas, construction, and manufacturing, all of which require the expertise of electrical engineers.

Some of the top employers for electrical engineers in Dubai include engineering firms, construction companies, and oil and gas companies.
