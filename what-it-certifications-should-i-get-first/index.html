What It Certifications Should I Get First

Written by Daniel Jun 14, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career in IT but feel overwhelmed by the number of certifications available? It's no secret that IT certifications can help boost your career and income, but which ones should you prioritize? In this post, we'll explore the best IT certifications to get started with and why.

The IT industry is constantly evolving, and keeping up with the latest technologies and skills can be challenging. This is where certifications come in - they validate your knowledge and skills, making you a more attractive job candidate. However, with so many certifications available, it can be difficult to know where to start.

The first certification you should consider getting is the CompTIA A+. This certification covers the basics of IT, including hardware, software, and networking. It's a great starting point for anyone new to the IT industry, and it's recognized by many employers as a valuable certification.

In addition to the CompTIA A+, other IT certifications that you should consider getting include the CompTIA Network+, CompTIA Security+, and Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA). These certifications cover important topics such as networking, security, and infrastructure, and are highly valued by employers.

Personal Experience with CompTIA A+

When I first started my career in IT, I knew very little about the industry. I decided to get the CompTIA A+ certification, and it was the best decision I could have made. The certification gave me a solid foundation in IT and helped me land my first job in the industry.

Personal Experience with CompTIA Network+

After gaining some experience in the industry, I decided to pursue the CompTIA Network+ certification. This certification helped me gain a deeper understanding of networking concepts and protocols, which has been invaluable in my career.

Why CompTIA Security+ is Important

In today's world, cybersecurity is more important than ever. This is where the CompTIA Security+ certification comes in. This certification covers the fundamentals of cybersecurity, including risk management, cryptography, and network security. It's a must-have certification for anyone looking to work in the cybersecurity field.

How Cisco CCNA Can Boost Your Career

The Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA) certification is another valuable certification to consider. This certification covers Cisco technologies and is highly valued by employers. It can help you stand out in a competitive job market and boost your earning potential.

Personal Experience with CCNA

After gaining experience in networking, I decided to pursue the CCNA certification. This certification helped me gain a deeper understanding of Cisco technologies and has opened up many doors in my career.

Conclusion of What IT Certifications Should I Get First

When it comes to IT certifications, it's important to start with the basics. The CompTIA A+ certification is a great starting point for anyone new to the industry, followed by the CompTIA Network+, CompTIA Security+, and Cisco CCNA certifications. These certifications cover important topics and are highly valued by employers. By obtaining these certifications, you'll be well on your way to a successful career in IT.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I skip the CompTIA A+ certification and start with the CompTIA Network+?

A: While it's possible to skip the CompTIA A+ certification, it's not recommended. The CompTIA A+ certification covers important foundational topics that are necessary for success in the IT industry.

Q: How long does it take to get these certifications?

A: The length of time it takes to get these certifications varies depending on your experience and study habits. On average, it takes about 2-3 months of studying to pass each exam.

Q: Are these certifications recognized internationally?

A: Yes, these certifications are recognized internationally and are highly valued by employers around the world.

Q: Do I need to renew these certifications?

A: Yes, most IT certifications require renewal every few years to ensure that your skills and knowledge are up to date.
