How Much Does A Service Manager Make At A Car Dealership

Written by Luffy Jun 04, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a service manager at a car dealership? Before making any decisions, it's important to understand the earning potential of this position. As with any job, there are both advantages and disadvantages to becoming a service manager. In this post, we'll explore how much a service manager typically makes at a car dealership and what factors can affect their salary.

Many people wonder about the earning potential of a service manager, especially given the demanding nature of the job. Service managers are responsible for overseeing the daily operations of a dealership's service department, which includes managing a team of mechanics, scheduling appointments, and ensuring customer satisfaction. While this can be a rewarding position, it can also be stressful and require long hours. As a result, it's important to understand how much you can expect to earn as a service manager before deciding if this is the right career path for you.

The average salary for a service manager at a car dealership varies depending on a number of factors, including the location of the dealership, the size of the service department, and the amount of experience the manager has. According to Glassdoor.com, the average salary for a service manager in the United States is around $65,000 per year. However, salaries can range from $45,000 to $106,000 per year depending on the factors mentioned above.

In summary, becoming a service manager at a car dealership can be a lucrative career choice, but it's important to understand the factors that can impact your salary. By considering the location of the dealership, the size of the service department, and your level of experience, you can get a better idea of what you can expect to earn in this position.

Factors That Affect a Service Manager's Salary

One of the biggest factors that can affect a service manager's salary is their location. Service managers in large cities or metropolitan areas may earn more than those in rural areas due to the higher cost of living. Another factor that can impact earning potential is the size of the service department. Managers at larger dealerships with more mechanics and service bays may earn more than those at smaller dealerships. Finally, experience plays a major role in determining a service manager's salary. Managers with more years of experience in the industry may earn higher salaries than those who are just starting out.

My Experience as a Service Manager

As a former service manager at a car dealership, I can attest to the demanding nature of the job. However, the earning potential made it worth it for me. I worked at a large dealership in a metropolitan area, which allowed me to earn a higher salary than I would have in a smaller town. Additionally, my years of experience in the industry gave me negotiating power when it came to salary. Overall, I found the job to be rewarding and financially lucrative.

How to Become a Service Manager

If you're interested in becoming a service manager at a car dealership, there are a few steps you can take to increase your chances of success. First, it's important to gain experience in the industry by working as a mechanic or service advisor. This will give you a better understanding of how the service department operates and what skills are required to succeed. Additionally, you may want to consider getting a degree in business or management to improve your chances of being hired as a service manager.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Being a Service Manager

Like any job, there are both advantages and disadvantages to becoming a service manager at a car dealership. Advantages include the potential for high earnings, the opportunity to work with a team of mechanics, and the satisfaction of providing top-notch customer service. However, there are also some disadvantages to this position, including the demanding nature of the job and the long hours that may be required during peak business times.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for a service manager at a car dealership?

A: According to Glassdoor.com, the average salary for a service manager in the United States is around $65,000 per year.

Q: What factors can impact a service manager's salary?

A: Factors that can impact a service manager's salary include their location, the size of the service department, and their level of experience.

Q: What are some advantages of being a service manager at a car dealership?

A: Advantages of being a service manager include the potential for high earnings, the opportunity to work with a team of mechanics, and the satisfaction of providing top-notch customer service.

Q: What are some disadvantages of being a service manager at a car dealership?

A: Disadvantages of being a service manager include the demanding nature of the job and the long hours that may be required during peak business times.

Conclusion

In summary, becoming a service manager at a car dealership can be a financially rewarding career choice for those who are willing to put in the hard work. By understanding the factors that can impact your salary and taking steps to gain experience in the industry, you can increase your chances of success in this position.
