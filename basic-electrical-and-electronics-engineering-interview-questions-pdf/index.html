Question 1: What is Ohm's law? Explain its significance in electrical engineering.

Ohm's law is a fundamental concept in electrical engineering that relates the voltage, current, and resistance of a circuit. It states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points, and inversely proportional to the resistance between them. Understanding Ohm's law is crucial for designing and analyzing circuits.

Question 2: What is a transistor? How is it used in electronic circuits?

A transistor is a semiconductor device that can be used as an amplifier or a switch in electronic circuits. It consists of three layers of semiconducting material, with two heavily doped layers on either side of a lightly doped layer. By applying a voltage to the middle layer, the current flowing through the transistor can be controlled.

Question 3: What is a capacitor? How is it used in electronic circuits?

A capacitor is an electronic component that stores electrical energy in an electric field. It consists of two conductive plates separated by a dielectric material. Capacitors are commonly used in electronic circuits for filtering, timing, and energy storage.

Question 4: What is the difference between AC and DC circuits?

AC (alternating current) circuits use a continuously changing voltage and current, while DC (direct current) circuits have a constant voltage and current. AC circuits are used for power transmission over long distances, while DC circuits are used for electronic devices and batteries.
