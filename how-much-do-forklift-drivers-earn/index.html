How Much Do Forklift Drivers Earn

Written by Bardi Jul 23, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you ever wonder how much forklift drivers earn? Are you considering a career in forklift driving and want to know the earning potential? Or are you already a forklift driver and want to know if you're being paid fairly? Whatever your situation, understanding the earning potential of forklift drivers can be helpful.

Being a forklift driver can be a physically demanding job that requires attention to detail, focus, and skill. Despite this, forklift drivers may not always receive the recognition they deserve. Furthermore, some may not be aware of the earning potential of forklift drivers.

The average hourly wage for forklift drivers is around $16-$18 per hour, according to data from PayScale. However, this can vary greatly depending on factors such as location, experience, industry, and type of forklift being operated. Some forklift drivers may earn as little as $12 per hour, while others may earn upwards of $25 per hour.

In summary, forklift drivers can earn an average hourly wage of around $16-$18 per hour. However, this can vary depending on various factors such as location, experience, industry, and type of forklift being operated.

Factors That Affect Forklift Driver Earnings

Location is one of the biggest factors that can affect forklift driver earnings. For example, forklift drivers in large cities such as New York or San Francisco may earn more due to the higher cost of living. Experience is another important factor that can affect earnings. Forklift drivers with more experience may be able to command a higher hourly wage.

Industry can also play a role in forklift driver earnings. For example, forklift drivers in the construction industry may earn more than those in the retail industry. Finally, the type of forklift being operated can also affect earnings. Forklift drivers who operate specialized forklifts such as those used in the construction industry may earn more than those who operate standard forklifts.

Location and Forklift Driver Earnings

As previously mentioned, location can have a big impact on forklift driver earnings. For example, forklift drivers in California may earn more than those in Texas due to the higher cost of living in California. Furthermore, forklift drivers in larger cities such as Los Angeles or San Francisco may earn more than those in smaller cities such as Fresno or Bakersfield.

Experience and Forklift Driver Earnings

Experience is another important factor that can affect forklift driver earnings. Forklift drivers with more experience may be able to command a higher hourly wage than those with less experience. This is because more experienced forklift drivers are generally viewed as being more skilled and therefore more valuable to employers.

Personal Experience with Forklift Driver Earnings

As a former forklift driver with 5 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that earnings can vary greatly depending on factors such as location and industry. When I first started as a forklift driver in a small town in Texas, I earned around $14 per hour. However, when I moved to a larger city in California, my hourly wage increased to $17 per hour.

Furthermore, I found that working in the construction industry paid more than working in the retail industry. When I worked for a construction company, I earned around $20 per hour, while working in a warehouse for a retail company only paid around $15 per hour.

Questions and Answers about Forklift Driver Earnings

Q: Can forklift drivers earn more than $25 per hour?

A: Yes, some forklift drivers may earn upwards of $25 per hour depending on factors such as experience, location, industry, and type of forklift being operated.

Q: Do forklift drivers in larger cities earn more than those in smaller cities?

A: Yes, forklift drivers in larger cities may earn more than those in smaller cities due to the higher cost of living in larger cities.

Q: How does experience affect forklift driver earnings?

A: Forklift drivers with more experience may be able to command a higher hourly wage than those with less experience.

Q: Can the type of forklift being operated affect earnings?

A: Yes, forklift drivers who operate specialized forklifts such as those used in the construction industry may earn more than those who operate standard forklifts.

Conclusion on How Much Do Forklift Drivers Earn

In conclusion, forklift drivers can earn an average hourly wage of around $16-$18 per hour. However, earnings can vary greatly depending on factors such as location, experience, industry, and type of forklift being operated. Understanding these factors can be helpful when considering a career in forklift driving or evaluating your current earning potential as a forklift driver.
