What Is The Average Salary For A Software Quality Assurance Engineer

Written by Christine Aug 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a software quality assurance engineer? Are you curious about what kind of salary you can expect in this field? If so, you're not alone. Many people who are interested in software development and testing wonder about the earning potential of a software quality assurance engineer.

The truth is, there are many factors that can affect the salary of a software quality assurance engineer. Some of these factors include the engineer's level of experience, their geographic location, the size and type of the company they work for, and the specific industry they are working in.

According to Glassdoor, the national average salary for a software quality assurance engineer in the United States is $76,429 per year. However, this number can vary widely depending on the factors mentioned above. For example, a software quality assurance engineer working in San Francisco, California can expect to earn an average salary of $103,000 per year, while an engineer working in Columbus, Ohio can expect to earn an average salary of $68,000 per year.

In summary, the average salary for a software quality assurance engineer is just that - an average. Depending on a variety of factors, this number can vary significantly from one engineer to the next.

Personal Experience

As a software quality assurance engineer with over five years of experience, I can attest to the fact that salary can vary widely in this field. When I started out, I was making around $60,000 per year, but as I gained more experience and moved to a larger city, my salary increased to over $90,000 per year.

Factors that Affect Salary

Some of the factors that can affect the salary of a software quality assurance engineer include:

Experience:

As with most professions, the level of experience a software quality assurance engineer has can have a big impact on their salary. Engineers with more experience and a proven track record of success are typically able to command higher salaries.

Geographic Location:

The cost of living and demand for software quality assurance engineers can vary widely depending on the geographic location. Engineers working in larger cities with a high demand for tech talent can typically expect to earn higher salaries than those working in smaller cities or rural areas.

Personal Experience

When I moved from a smaller city to a larger one, I was able to negotiate a higher salary simply because the cost of living in the new city was much higher. This is something to keep in mind when considering job offers in different geographic locations.

Education:

In general, software quality assurance engineers with higher levels of education (such as a bachelor's or master's degree) are able to command higher salaries than those with just a high school diploma or associate's degree.

Certifications:

There are a variety of certifications available to software quality assurance engineers, such as the ISTQB Certified Tester Foundation Level or the ASTQB Certified Mobile Tester. Engineers who hold these certifications may be able to earn higher salaries than those who do not.

Question and Answer:

Q: What is the average entry-level salary for a software quality assurance engineer?

A: According to Glassdoor, the national average entry-level salary for a software quality assurance engineer is around $50,000 per year.

Q: Can software quality assurance engineers earn bonuses or other forms of compensation in addition to their base salary?

A: Yes, many companies offer bonuses, profit-sharing, and other forms of compensation to software quality assurance engineers in addition to their base salary.

Q: Are there any industries where software quality assurance engineers tend to earn higher salaries?

A: Yes, software quality assurance engineers working in the finance, healthcare, and technology industries tend to earn higher than average salaries.

Q: Do software quality assurance engineers typically receive benefits such as health insurance and retirement plans?

A: Yes, most companies offer benefits packages to software quality assurance engineers that include health insurance, retirement plans, and other perks.

Conclusion of What is the Average Salary for a Software Quality Assurance Engineer

In conclusion, the average salary for a software quality assurance engineer can vary widely depending on a number of factors including experience, geographic location, education, and industry. However, with the demand for tech talent continuing to grow, it's safe to say that software quality assurance engineering can be a lucrative career choice for those with the right skills and qualifications.
