Exploring Entry Level Field Biologist Jobs

Written by Luffy Sep 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you passionate about the environment and nature? Do you want to apply your skills and knowledge to protect our planet's biodiversity? If so, then you might be interested in exploring entry level field biologist jobs. These positions offer a unique opportunity to work in the great outdoors and make a positive impact on the world around us.

However, as with any job search, there are challenges and uncertainties that come with the territory. For instance, you may be wondering how to gain the necessary experience and qualifications, or what kind of salary and benefits you can expect. These are all valid concerns, but rest assured that there are resources and strategies available to help you navigate the field.

What are Entry Level Field Biologist Jobs?

Entry level field biologist jobs refer to positions that involve conducting research, collecting data, and analyzing findings related to various aspects of the natural world. These could include studying animal behavior, monitoring ecosystems, or assessing the impact of human activities on the environment.

Typically, entry level field biologist jobs require a degree in biology, ecology, or a related field, as well as some hands-on experience with field research techniques. Depending on the specific job and employer, you may also need to obtain certifications or licenses, such as a wildlife handling permit or a scuba diving certification.

My Experience in Entry Level Field Biologist Jobs

When I was first starting out in my career as a field biologist, I found it challenging to break into the field. I had a degree in biology and a passion for conservation, but I didn't have much practical experience beyond my coursework. However, I was determined to find a way to gain the skills and knowledge I needed to succeed.

I started by volunteering with local conservation groups and participating in citizen science projects. This allowed me to gain hands-on experience with data collection and analysis, as well as connect with other professionals in the field. I also took advantage of online resources and workshops to learn about new research techniques and technologies.

Job Outlook for Entry Level Field Biologist Jobs

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of zoologists and wildlife biologists (which includes field biologists) is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations. This growth is expected to be driven by increased demand for environmental protections and sustainability efforts, as well as advances in technology and research methods.

Salary and Benefits for Entry Level Field Biologist Jobs

The salary and benefits for entry level field biologist jobs can vary widely depending on the specific job and employer. According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a wildlife biologist in the United States is around $53,000 per year. However, this can range from around $32,000 to over $80,000 per year depending on factors such as experience, location, and job responsibilities.

How to Land a Job in Entry Level Field Biologist Jobs

If you're interested in pursuing a career in field biology, there are several steps you can take to increase your chances of success. These include:

  • Getting a degree in biology, ecology, or a related field
  • Building your skills and experience through internships, volunteer work, and citizen science projects
  • Obtaining certifications or licenses as needed
  • Networking with professionals in the field and attending conferences and workshops
  • Applying for entry level positions and demonstrating your knowledge and enthusiasm for the work

Common Questions about Entry Level Field Biologist Jobs

Q: What kind of work will I be doing as an entry level field biologist?

A: Entry level field biologist jobs can involve a wide range of tasks, depending on the specific job and employer. Some common activities might include conducting surveys, collecting and analyzing data, monitoring wildlife populations, or assisting with research projects.

Q: What kind of degree do I need to work as a field biologist?

A: Most entry level field biologist jobs require a degree in biology, ecology, or a related field. Some employers may also prefer or require a graduate degree, depending on the specific job responsibilities and requirements.

Q: What kind of salary can I expect as an entry level field biologist?

A: The salary for entry level field biologist jobs can vary widely depending on factors such as location, experience, and job responsibilities. According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a wildlife biologist in the United States is around $53,000 per year.

Q: How can I gain the experience and qualifications I need to succeed in a field biologist job?

A: There are several strategies you can use to gain hands-on experience and build your skills in the field, such as volunteering, participating in citizen science projects, and obtaining certifications or licenses as needed.

Conclusion of Entry Level Field Biologist Jobs

Entry level field biologist jobs offer a unique opportunity to work in a rewarding and challenging field, making a positive impact on the environment and our planet's biodiversity. While there may be challenges and uncertainties along the way, with dedication, hard work, and the right resources, you can build a successful career in this exciting and important field.
