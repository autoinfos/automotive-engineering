How Much Do Electrical Technicians Make

Written by Oliver Aug 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an electrical technician but wondering about the earning potential? It's a valid concern for anyone entering a new job field. The good news is that electrical technicians make a solid salary, and with the right experience and education, there's room for growth.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career as an electrical technician is not knowing what to expect in terms of pay. It's important to have a clear idea of what you can expect to earn and how that salary may change as you gain more experience and skills.

The median annual salary for an electrical technician is around $62,000, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. This number can vary depending on a variety of factors, including location, education, and experience. Entry-level positions may start at around $40,000, while those with more experience and advanced certifications can earn upwards of $80,000 or more.

In summary, electrical technicians can make a solid salary with room for growth. The median annual salary is around $62,000, but this can vary depending on a variety of factors.

What Factors Affect an Electrical Technician's Salary?

One of the biggest factors that can affect an electrical technician's salary is location. Those working in areas with a higher cost of living may earn more than those in areas with a lower cost of living. Education and experience can also play a role in salary. Those with advanced certifications and more years of experience may be able to command a higher salary.

Personally, I started my career as an electrical technician with a starting salary of $45,000. As I gained more experience and advanced certifications, my salary increased to around $75,000 after five years on the job. I've found that taking on additional responsibilities and continuing education has helped me to earn more money over time.

What Are the Job Prospects for Electrical Technicians?

The job outlook for electrical technicians is positive, with a projected job growth of 10% over the next decade, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. This growth is due in part to the increasing demand for clean energy and the need for electrical technicians to install and maintain renewable energy systems.

Personally, I've found that there's always a demand for skilled electrical technicians, particularly those with experience in commercial and industrial settings. I've never had trouble finding work or advancing my career in this field.

What Skills Do Electrical Technicians Need?

In order to be successful as an electrical technician, it's important to have a strong understanding of electrical systems and how they work. This requires a certain level of technical knowledge and problem-solving skills. Communication and teamwork are also important, as electrical technicians often work in teams and need to be able to effectively communicate with others.

Personally, I've found that being detail-oriented and patient has helped me to excel in this field. It's important to take the time to thoroughly understand electrical systems and diagnose problems, rather than rushing through a job and potentially making mistakes.

How Do You Become an Electrical Technician?

In order to become an electrical technician, you'll typically need a high school diploma or equivalent, as well as some post-secondary education or vocational training. Many electrical technicians also complete an apprenticeship program, which can provide hands-on training and experience. Advanced certifications, such as those offered by the National Institute for Certification in Engineering Technologies, can also help to advance your career and increase your earning potential.

Personally, I completed a vocational training program in electrical systems and then completed an apprenticeship program with a local electrical company. This allowed me to gain hands-on experience while also earning a salary.

Conclusion of How Much Do Electrical Technicians Make

If you're considering a career as an electrical technician, it's important to have a clear idea of what to expect in terms of salary and job prospects. With a median annual salary of around $62,000 and positive job growth projected over the next decade, electrical technicians can make a solid living. By gaining experience and advanced certifications, there's also room for growth and increased earning potential.
