Is It Worth Being An Auto Mechanic Exploring The Pros And Cons

Written by Bardi Jun 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an auto mechanic but wondering if it's worth it? It's a valid concern since choosing a career path is a significant decision that can impact your life in many ways. In this post, we'll explore the pros and cons of being an auto mechanic to help you make an informed decision.

Pain Points of Being an Auto Mechanic

Before diving into the pros and cons, let's take a look at some of the pain points that come with being an auto mechanic. One of the main challenges is the physical demands of the job, which can include long hours of standing, bending, and working in cramped spaces. Additionally, the job can be dirty and greasy, which may not be appealing to everyone.

Another pain point is the potential for injury since auto mechanics work with heavy machinery and sharp tools. This can lead to accidents and injuries, which is something to consider before pursuing this career.

Is it Worth Being an Auto Mechanic?

The answer to this question depends on your personal goals and preferences. Here are some pros and cons to help you decide:

Pros of Being an Auto Mechanic:

- Job stability: Since cars will always need repairs, there will always be a demand for auto mechanics.

- Good pay: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for auto mechanics in 2020 was $44,050.

- Opportunity for growth: With experience, you can become a master mechanic or open your own shop.

- Job satisfaction: Fixing cars can be a rewarding experience, especially when you see a customer's smile after repairing their vehicle.

Cons of Being an Auto Mechanic:

- Physical demands: As mentioned earlier, the job can be physically demanding and can take a toll on your body.

- Potential for injury: Working with heavy machinery and sharp tools can be dangerous.

- Dirty work environment: The job can be messy and require working with chemicals and oils.

- High stress: The job can be stressful, especially when working with difficult customers or complicated repairs.

Personal Experience with Being an Auto Mechanic

My personal experience with being an auto mechanic has been mostly positive. I have always enjoyed working with cars and find the work to be satisfying. However, the physical demands can be challenging, and I have experienced some injuries on the job. Overall, I believe it's worth it if you have a passion for cars and enjoy working with your hands.

Job Outlook for Auto Mechanics

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the employment of auto mechanics is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations. As cars become more complex, there will be a higher demand for skilled mechanics who can diagnose and repair these vehicles.

How to Become an Auto Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming an auto mechanic, there are a few steps you can take:

- Earn a high school diploma or GED

- Attend a trade school or community college to earn a certificate or degree in automotive technology

- Gain experience through an apprenticeship or entry-level job

- Obtain certification through the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE)

FAQs About Being an Auto Mechanic

Q: Is being an auto mechanic a good career option?

A: It can be a good career option if you enjoy working with cars and don't mind the physical demands of the job.

Q: How much do auto mechanics make?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for auto mechanics in 2020 was $44,050.

Q: What are the working conditions like for auto mechanics?

A: Auto mechanics work in repair shops, which can be noisy and dirty. The job can also be physically demanding and require working in cramped spaces.

Q: What skills are needed to be a successful auto mechanic?

A: Auto mechanics need to have strong problem-solving skills, attention to detail, and the ability to work with their hands. Good customer service skills are also important since they often interact with customers.

Conclusion

Being an auto mechanic can be a rewarding career for those who have a passion for cars and enjoy working with their hands. However, it's important to weigh the pros and cons before pursuing this career path. By considering the information provided in this post, we hope you can make an informed decision about whether being an auto mechanic is worth it for you.
