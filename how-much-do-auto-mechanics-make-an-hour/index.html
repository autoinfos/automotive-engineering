How Much Do Auto Mechanics Make An Hour A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering becoming an auto mechanic? Or are you already in the field but curious about how much you can make per hour? Look no further! In this article, we'll dive into the topic of how much do auto mechanics make an hour and provide you with all the information you need.

Being an auto mechanic can be a rewarding career, but it can also be physically demanding and mentally challenging. One of the biggest concerns for those in the field is how much they can expect to earn per hour. With rising living costs and increasing competition, it's important to know what you're worth and how you can negotiate your salary.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $20.24 in May 2020. However, the range can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and type of employer. Some auto mechanics earn as little as $12 per hour, while others make upwards of $34 per hour.

In summary, the hourly wage for auto mechanics can vary greatly depending on various factors. It's important to research the industry and your local job market to determine what you can expect to earn.

My Experience

When I first started as an auto mechanic, I was making $15 per hour at a local repair shop. However, as I gained more experience and became certified in certain areas, I was able to negotiate a higher wage of $25 per hour at a dealership. It's important to constantly improve your skills and knowledge in order to increase your earning potential.

How to Negotiate Your Salary

If you're looking to negotiate your salary as an auto mechanic, here are some tips:

1. Research the Industry

Knowing the average hourly wage for auto mechanics in your area can give you a basis for negotiation. Use online resources such as Glassdoor or the Bureau of Labor Statistics to gather information.

2. Highlight Your Skills and Experience

Make sure to showcase your certifications, training, and any additional skills you possess. This can demonstrate your value and set you apart from other candidates.

My Personal Negotiation Experience

When I applied for a job at a luxury car dealership, I was able to negotiate my starting wage from $20 per hour to $25 per hour by highlighting my experience in working with high-end vehicles.

Additional Factors that Affect Hourly Wage

Aside from experience and location, other factors that can affect your hourly wage as an auto mechanic include:

1. Type of Employer

Working for a dealership or a chain repair shop may offer a higher wage than working for a small independent shop.

2. Specialization

Specializing in a certain area such as diesel engines, transmissions, or electronics can lead to a higher hourly wage.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I make a comfortable living as an auto mechanic?

A: Yes, with the right skills and experience, you can make a comfortable living as an auto mechanic. The industry is always in demand and offers room for growth and advancement.

Q: Is it difficult to negotiate a higher hourly wage as an auto mechanic?

A: It can be challenging, but with proper research and preparation, you can successfully negotiate a higher wage. Highlighting your skills and experience is key.

Q: What is the job outlook for auto mechanics?

A: The job outlook for auto mechanics is positive, with a projected growth rate of 4% from 2019 to 2029.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement in the auto mechanic field?

A: Yes, with additional training and certifications, you can advance to positions such as a master mechanic or service manager.

Conclusion of How Much Do Auto Mechanics Make an Hour

As you can see, the hourly wage for auto mechanics can vary greatly depending on various factors. It's important to research the industry, your local job market, and highlight your skills and experience. With dedication and hard work, you can make a comfortable living as an auto mechanic.
