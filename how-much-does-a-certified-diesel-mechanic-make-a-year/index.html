How Much Does A Certified Diesel Mechanic Make A Year

Written by Bardi Jul 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in becoming a diesel mechanic? If so, one of the most important things to consider is how much money you can make. After all, you want to make sure that you're getting paid enough to support yourself and your family. In this blog post, we'll be discussing how much a certified diesel mechanic can make in a year.

As with any job, there are certain pain points that come with being a diesel mechanic. First of all, it can be a physically demanding job. You'll be working with heavy machinery and may need to lift heavy parts or tools. In addition, you may need to work long hours, including nights and weekends.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $48,500 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $32,920, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $76,350. Keep in mind that these figures are for all diesel technicians and mechanics, not just those who are certified.

If you're a certified diesel mechanic, you can expect to earn more than someone who isn't certified. Certification shows that you have the knowledge and skills needed to perform your job at a high level. According to the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence, certified diesel mechanics can earn up to 36% more than their non-certified counterparts.

What Does Certification Mean?

Certification means that you've passed a set of exams that demonstrate your knowledge of diesel engines and related systems. There are several organizations that offer certification for diesel mechanics, including the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) and the Automotive Service Association (ASA).

To become certified, you'll need to pass a series of exams that cover topics such as engine repair, electrical systems, and brakes. You'll also need to have at least two years of work experience in the diesel industry, although this requirement can vary depending on the organization offering the certification.

My Personal Experience

As someone who has worked in the diesel industry for several years, I can say that becoming certified was one of the best decisions I ever made. Not only did it help me earn a higher salary, but it also gave me the confidence to tackle more complex projects. Plus, it's a great feeling to know that you're recognized as an expert in your field.

How to Become a Certified Diesel Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming a certified diesel mechanic, the first step is to get some training. Many community colleges and technical schools offer programs in diesel technology. These programs typically take two years to complete and may include both classroom instruction and hands-on training.

After completing your training, you'll need to get some work experience. Most certification programs require at least two years of work experience in the diesel industry, although some may require more. During this time, you'll be learning on the job and gaining practical experience that will be invaluable when it comes time to take your certification exams.

Continuing Education

Once you're certified, it's important to keep up with the latest developments in the diesel industry. This can include attending workshops and seminars, reading industry publications, and staying up-to-date on new technologies and techniques. By continuing your education, you'll be able to provide the best possible service to your customers and stay ahead of the competition.

Salary Negotiation Tips

When it comes time to negotiate your salary, there are a few things you can do to increase your chances of getting paid what you're worth. First of all, do your research and find out what other certified diesel mechanics in your area are earning. This will give you a good idea of what you should be asking for.

Next, be prepared to demonstrate your value to your employer. Highlight your certifications, work experience, and any special skills or knowledge you have that make you an asset to the company. Finally, be confident and assertive when negotiating your salary. Remember, you deserve to be paid what you're worth!

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to become a certified diesel mechanic?

A: It typically takes two years to complete a diesel technology program, and most certification programs require at least two years of work experience in the diesel industry.

Q: How much more can a certified diesel mechanic make compared to a non-certified one?

A: According to the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence, certified diesel mechanics can earn up to 36% more than their non-certified counterparts.

Q: What is the median annual wage for diesel mechanics?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $48,500 as of May 2020.

Q: What are some of the benefits of becoming a certified diesel mechanic?

A: Becoming certified can lead to higher salaries, increased job opportunities, and the confidence to tackle more complex projects.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Certified Diesel Mechanic Make a Year

If you're considering a career as a diesel mechanic, becoming certified can help you earn a higher salary and advance in your career. By completing a diesel technology program, gaining work experience, and passing a certification exam, you'll be well on your way to becoming a highly skilled and highly paid diesel mechanic.
