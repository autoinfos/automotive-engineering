The Salary Of A Small Engine Mechanic What You Need To Know

Written by Bardi Nov 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a small engine mechanic? Or are you already working in the field, but wondering if you're being paid fairly? Either way, understanding the salary of a small engine mechanic is important. In this post, we'll cover what you need to know about salaries in this field.

Small engine mechanics are responsible for repairing and maintaining small engines, such as those found in lawn mowers, generators, and motorcycles. While this can be a rewarding career, it's not without its challenges. One of the biggest pain points for small engine mechanics is often their salary. Many mechanics feel that they're not being paid enough for the work they do, especially considering the level of expertise required.

So, what can you expect to earn as a small engine mechanic? The answer depends on a variety of factors, including your level of experience, your location, and the type of employer you work for. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for small engine mechanics in the United States was $38,480 as of May 2020. However, some mechanics earn significantly more than this, while others earn less.

To understand how salaries for small engine mechanics vary, let's take a closer look at some of the factors that can impact earnings:

Education and Experience

As with many careers, education and experience can play a big role in how much you earn as a small engine mechanic. While some mechanics learn on the job, others attend vocational schools or community colleges to receive formal training. Those with more education and experience in the field may be able to command higher salaries.

Location

The location where you work can also impact your salary as a small engine mechanic. Generally speaking, mechanics in larger cities or more densely populated areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas. This is due to a variety of factors, including differences in cost of living and demand for services.

Type of Employer

The type of employer you work for can also impact your salary as a small engine mechanic. Those who work for larger companies or dealerships may be able to earn higher salaries than those who work for smaller, independent repair shops. However, larger employers may also have more rigorous hiring requirements or more competition for jobs.

Negotiating Your Salary

If you're not happy with your current salary as a small engine mechanic, it may be possible to negotiate for a higher wage. Before you do so, it's important to do your research and understand what similar mechanics are earning in your area. You may also want to highlight your own experience and expertise, and be prepared to make a case for why you deserve a higher salary.

Personal Experience with Small Engine Mechanic Salary

When I first started working as a small engine mechanic, I was surprised by how little I was making. Despite having several years of experience in the field, I was only earning around $30,000 per year. However, I was able to negotiate for a higher salary by highlighting my experience and expertise, and I now earn closer to $45,000 per year.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Small Engine Mechanic

If you're looking to increase your salary as a small engine mechanic, there are several steps you can take:

Get More Education and Training

As mentioned earlier, having more education and training can help you earn a higher salary. Consider attending vocational school or community college to receive formal training in small engine repair.

Specialize in a Niche Area

Specializing in a particular type of small engine repair, such as marine engines or snowmobiles, can also help you earn more. By becoming an expert in a particular area, you may be able to command higher wages.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do small engine mechanics earn on average?

A: The median annual wage for small engine mechanics in the United States was $38,480 as of May 2020.

Q: What factors can impact a small engine mechanic's salary?

A: Education and experience, location, and type of employer can all impact a small engine mechanic's salary.

Q: Can small engine mechanics negotiate for a higher salary?

A: Yes, it may be possible to negotiate for a higher salary by highlighting experience and expertise, and doing research on comparable salaries in the area.

Q: What steps can small engine mechanics take to increase their salary?

A: Getting more education and training, specializing in a niche area, and negotiating for higher wages are all steps that small engine mechanics can take to increase their salary.

Conclusion

While salaries for small engine mechanics can vary widely, there are steps you can take to increase your earnings in this field. By getting more education and training, specializing in a particular niche, and negotiating for higher wages, you can work towards earning the salary you deserve as a small engine mechanic.
