How Much Do Service Technicians Make Per Year

Written by Christine Aug 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career in service technician but wondering how much you could potentially earn? Do you want to know how much service technicians make per year and what factors could affect their salary? In this article, we'll explore the salary range of service technicians, the factors that affect their pay, and some personal experiences of service technicians in the field.

As with any job, there are certain pain points when it comes to how much service technicians make per year. For instance, the pay may not be as high as other jobs that require a similar level of education and training. Additionally, it may take some time before you can earn a higher salary, as you need to gain experience and prove your skills in the field.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median salary for service technicians was $48,730 per year as of May 2020. The lowest 10% earned less than $32,970 per year, while the highest 10% earned more than $76,470 per year. The exact salary you can expect to earn as a service technician can depend on several factors, such as your level of experience, industry, and location.

In summary, service technicians can earn a decent salary with the potential to increase over time. Factors that can affect their pay include experience, industry, and location. If you are considering a career in service technician, it's important to research the specific job and industry to get a better idea of what you can expect to earn.

Salary Range for Service Technicians

As mentioned earlier, the median salary for service technicians as of May 2020 was $48,730 per year. However, this can vary depending on the industry, location, and level of experience. For example, service technicians working in the aerospace industry may earn a higher salary compared to those working in the automotive industry. Similarly, service technicians working in big cities may earn more than those working in rural areas.

Personally, as a service technician working in the automotive industry for several years, I have seen my salary increase gradually as I gained more experience and proved my skills in the field. It's important to note that the salary you can earn as a service technician may also depend on the specific company you work for.

Factors That Affect Service Technicians' Pay

Aside from industry, location, and experience, other factors that can affect service technicians' pay include education and certifications, job performance, and the company's budget. For instance, service technicians with higher levels of education and certifications may earn more than those without. Similarly, service technicians who consistently perform well and meet their job targets may be eligible for bonuses or pay raises. Lastly, a company's budget may also affect how much they can pay their service technicians.

Education and Certifications

Service technicians who hold a degree or certification in their field may earn more than those without. This is because a higher level of education and certification can demonstrate their expertise and knowledge in the field, making them more valuable to employers. Some certification programs for service technicians include the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) certification, which can be obtained by passing an exam.

Job Performance

Service technicians who consistently perform well and meet their job targets may be eligible for bonuses or pay raises. This is because they are contributing to the success of the company and demonstrating their value as an employee. To improve their job performance, service technicians can focus on continuing their education and training, staying up-to-date with industry trends, and providing excellent customer service.

Personal Experiences of Service Technicians

As mentioned earlier, personal experiences of service technicians in the field can vary depending on several factors such as industry, location, and company. Some service technicians work in the automotive industry, where they may specialize in repairing and maintaining cars, trucks, or motorcycles. Others may work in the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) industry, where they may install, repair, or maintain HVAC systems.

Personally, as a service technician working in the automotive industry, I have found the job to be fulfilling and rewarding. I enjoy working with my hands and solving problems, and I feel a sense of accomplishment when I am able to fix a problem with a customer's vehicle. However, the job can also be physically demanding and require long hours, especially during peak season.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to become a service technician?
A: The education and training required to become a service technician can vary depending on the industry and company. Some service technicians may only need a high school diploma or equivalent, while others may need a degree or certification. Typically, it can take anywhere from several months to several years to become a service technician.

Q: What industries employ service technicians?
A: Service technicians can work in a variety of industries, including automotive, HVAC, electronics, and more. They may specialize in repairing and maintaining specific types of equipment, such as cars, computers, or medical equipment.

Q: Can service technicians work remotely?
A: Depending on the industry and company, some service technicians may be able to work remotely, while others may need to work on-site. For instance, service technicians who work in the HVAC industry may need to travel to a customer's home or business to install or repair HVAC systems.

Q: How can service technicians increase their salary?
A: Service technicians can increase their salary by gaining more experience, continuing their education and training, and consistently performing well on the job. They may also be able to negotiate a higher salary with their employer or seek out job opportunities with higher pay.

Conclusion of How Much Do Service Technicians Make Per Year

Overall, service technicians can earn a decent salary with the potential to increase over time. Factors that can affect their pay include experience, industry, and location. If you are considering a career in service technician, it's important to research the specific job and industry to get a better idea of what you can expect to earn. With the right education, training, and experience, you can succeed as a service technician and earn a competitive salary.
