Automotive Technician Vs Mechanic Salary Which Pays More

Written by Bardi Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in the automotive industry but unsure whether to become an automotive technician or a mechanic? One important factor to consider is salary. In this blog post, we will explore the differences in pay between automotive technicians and mechanics.

Many people assume that automotive technicians and mechanics have the same job responsibilities and therefore earn the same salary. However, this is not the case. While both professions deal with repairing and maintaining vehicles, they have different levels of education and training, which affects their salary.

The average salary for an automotive technician is around $42,000 per year, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. On the other hand, the average salary for a mechanic is around $52,000 per year. This difference in salary can be attributed to the fact that mechanics typically have more training and experience than automotive technicians.

In summary, if you are looking for a career in the automotive industry and want to earn a higher salary, becoming a mechanic may be the way to go. However, it's important to consider the level of education and training required for this profession.

What Is an Automotive Technician?

Automotive technicians are responsible for diagnosing and repairing vehicles. They typically have a high school diploma and some post-secondary education, such as an associate degree in automotive technology. Automotive technicians may specialize in certain types of vehicles, such as cars or trucks, and may work for dealerships or independent repair shops.

As an automotive technician, I have personal experience in this field. I started my career as an apprentice at a local repair shop and worked my way up to becoming a certified technician. While the salary for an automotive technician may not be as high as a mechanic, it's still a rewarding career with opportunities for growth.

What Is a Mechanic?

A mechanic is a more advanced position than an automotive technician. Mechanics have more education and training, typically having completed an associate or bachelor's degree in automotive technology. They are responsible for complex repairs and may specialize in a specific type of vehicle or system.

During my time working in the automotive industry, I have seen many mechanics with years of experience and expertise. They are often the go-to person for difficult repairs and play a vital role in keeping vehicles on the road.

Factors That Affect Salary

While the average salary for automotive technicians and mechanics may be different, there are several factors that can affect earnings in both professions. These factors include:

  • Location: Wages can vary depending on where you work. For example, automotive technicians and mechanics in larger cities may earn more than those in smaller towns.
  • Experience: The longer you work in the industry, the more experience you gain, which can lead to higher pay.
  • Specialization: Specializing in a certain type of vehicle or system can increase your earning potential.
  • Certifications: Obtaining certifications in specific areas, such as engine repair or electrical systems, can also lead to higher pay.

How to Increase Your Salary

If you are currently working as an automotive technician or mechanic and want to increase your salary, there are several steps you can take. These include:

  • Continuing education: Taking courses and obtaining certifications can increase your knowledge and skills, making you more valuable to employers.
  • Specializing: Specializing in a certain type of vehicle or system can also increase your earning potential.
  • Networking: Building relationships with other professionals in the industry can lead to new job opportunities and higher pay.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the job outlook for automotive technicians and mechanics?

A: The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects that employment in both fields will grow at a rate of 4% from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: Do automotive technicians and mechanics receive benefits?

A: Many employers offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off to their automotive technicians and mechanics.

Q: Is it possible to become a mechanic without a degree?

A: While a degree is not required to become a mechanic, it can be helpful in obtaining employment and advancing in the industry.

Q: Can automotive technicians and mechanics work part-time?

A: Yes, many automotive technicians and mechanics work part-time or on a flexible schedule.

Conclusion of Automotive Technician vs Mechanic Salary

Overall, while both automotive technicians and mechanics play important roles in the automotive industry, mechanics typically earn a higher salary due to their advanced education and training. However, there are many factors that can affect earnings in both professions, and there are opportunities for growth and advancement in both careers.
