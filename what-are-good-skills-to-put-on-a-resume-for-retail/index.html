What Are Good Skills To Put On A Resume For Retail

Written by Petter Aug 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you wondering what are the best skills to showcase on your resume for a retail job? Whether you're a seasoned retail professional or just starting out, having the right skills on your resume can make all the difference in landing your dream job. In this blog post, we'll explore the top skills that can impress hiring managers and help you stand out from other applicants.

Retail jobs can be challenging, especially during peak seasons or when dealing with difficult customers. Employers are looking for candidates who can handle the pressure and provide exceptional customer service. They want employees who can work well under stress and still maintain a positive attitude. They also want someone who is detail-oriented, reliable, and has a strong work ethic. These are just a few of the pain points that job seekers face when applying for retail positions.

The top skills to put on a retail resume include:

1. Customer Service

Customer service is the backbone of the retail industry. As a retail employee, you will be interacting with customers daily. You must have excellent communication skills and be able to handle customer complaints and inquiries professionally. This skill can be highlighted on your resume by including examples of how you have resolved customer problems in the past.

2. Sales Skills

Sales skills are essential for anyone working in retail. You must be able to sell products to customers and meet sales targets. This skill can be highlighted on your resume by including examples of your sales achievements, such as exceeding monthly sales goals or winning a sales contest.

3. Time Management

Retail jobs can be fast-paced, and you must be able to manage your time effectively. You will need to multitask, prioritize tasks, and work efficiently to meet deadlines. This skill can be highlighted on your resume by including examples of how you have managed your time effectively in the past.

4. Teamwork

Working in retail often involves working as part of a team. You must be able to collaborate with your colleagues to achieve common goals. This skill can be highlighted on your resume by including examples of how you have worked in a team environment in the past.

5. Attention to Detail

In retail, details matter. You must be able to pay attention to small details, such as pricing, product placement, and store cleanliness. This skill can be highlighted on your resume by including examples of how you have demonstrated attention to detail in the past.

6. Cash Handling

Many retail jobs involve handling cash and processing payments. You must be able to handle money accurately and securely. This skill can be highlighted on your resume by including examples of your experience handling cash and your knowledge of cash-handling procedures.

7. Technology Skills

Retail is becoming increasingly technology-dependent. You must be able to use point-of-sale systems, inventory management software, and other retail technology. This skill can be highlighted on your resume by including examples of your experience using retail technology and your ability to learn new software quickly.

8. Flexibility

Retail jobs often involve working irregular hours, including evenings, weekends, and holidays. You must be able to work a flexible schedule and be willing to adapt to changing circumstances. This skill can be highlighted on your resume by including examples of how you have worked flexible schedules in the past.

9. Language Skills

Depending on the location of the retail store, language skills may be essential. If you are multilingual or have proficiency in a language other than English, this can make you stand out from other applicants. This skill can be highlighted on your resume by including your language proficiency and any relevant language experience.

10. Positive Attitude

Finally, having a positive attitude is crucial for success in the retail industry. You must be able to maintain a friendly and approachable demeanor, even when dealing with difficult customers. This skill can be highlighted on your resume by including examples of how you have maintained a positive attitude in the face of adversity.

My Retail Experience

As someone who has worked in retail for several years, I can attest to the importance of having the right skills on your resume. I have found that customer service, sales, and time management are the most critical skills for success in the retail industry. Being able to communicate effectively with customers and close sales are essential for meeting sales targets and contributing to the success of the business. Additionally, being able to manage time effectively is crucial for getting tasks done efficiently and meeting deadlines.

Answering Common Questions:

Q: How should I format my retail resume?

A: Your retail resume should be formatted in a clear and concise manner. Use bullet points to highlight your skills and accomplishments, and make sure to include relevant experience and education.

Q: What if I don't have much experience in retail?

A: If you don't have much experience in retail, focus on highlighting transferable skills that can be applied to the retail industry. For example, if you have worked in customer service, this can be a valuable skill to highlight on your resume.

Q: What should I include in my retail cover letter?

A: Your retail cover letter should introduce yourself and explain why you are interested in the position. Highlight your relevant skills and experience, and explain how they make you a good fit for the role. Finally, thank the employer for considering your application and express your enthusiasm for the opportunity.

Q: How can I stand out from other retail job applicants?

A: To stand out from other retail job applicants, make sure your resume and cover letter are tailored to the specific job you are applying for. Highlight your relevant skills and experience, and explain how they make you a good fit for the role. Additionally, consider including a portfolio of your work or a list of references to showcase your qualifications.

Conclusion of What Are Good Skills to Put on a Resume for Retail

If you are looking to succeed in retail, having the right skills on your resume is crucial. By highlighting your customer service, sales, time management, and other relevant skills, you can show potential employers that you are the right person for the job. Remember to tailor your resume and cover letter to each specific job you apply for and to showcase your personality and enthusiasm for the industry.
