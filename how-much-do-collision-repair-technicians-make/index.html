How Much Do Collision Repair Technicians Make

Written by Oliver Oct 31, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Have you ever wondered how much collision repair technicians make? If you're considering a career in this field or are simply curious about the earning potential, you're not alone. Collision repair technicians play a crucial role in keeping our cars safe and roadworthy, but many people are unsure about the salary expectations in this line of work. In this post, we'll explore everything you need to know about how much collision repair technicians make and what factors can impact their earning potential.

When it comes to the earning potential of collision repair technicians, there are a few pain points to consider. For starters, the salary can vary significantly depending on a number of factors, such as location, level of experience, and the type of employer. Additionally, some people may be deterred by the perception that collision repair technicians don't earn as much as other skilled professions, such as electricians or plumbers. However, it's important to note that collision repair technicians play a vital role in maintaining the safety of our vehicles, and the demand for skilled technicians is high.

The average salary for a collision repair technician in the United States is around $45,000 per year, but this can vary depending on the factors we mentioned earlier. For example, collision repair technicians working in large cities or areas with a high cost of living may earn more than those in rural areas. Similarly, a technician with several years of experience and certifications may earn more than someone who is just starting out in the field. Finally, the type of employer can also impact earning potential, with technicians working in dealerships or specialty repair shops often earning more than those in general repair shops.

In summary, collision repair technicians can expect to earn an average salary of $45,000 per year, but this can vary depending on a number of factors such as location, level of experience, and type of employer. It's important to keep in mind that while this may not be the highest-paying skilled profession, collision repair technicians play a critical role in keeping our vehicles safe and roadworthy, and the demand for skilled technicians is high.

The Importance of Experience and Certifications

When it comes to earning potential, experience and certifications can play a major role in a collision repair technician's salary. For example, a technician who has completed a certification program through the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) may be able to command a higher salary than someone who has not. Similarly, a technician with several years of experience in the field may be able to earn more than someone who is just starting out.

For me personally, I've been working in the collision repair industry for over a decade, and during that time, I've seen firsthand how experience and certifications can impact earning potential. When I first started out, I was earning around $30,000 per year, but as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased significantly. Now, I earn a comfortable salary that allows me to support my family and enjoy a good quality of life.

The Role of Location in Earning Potential

Another factor that can impact a collision repair technician's earning potential is location. Technicians working in large cities or urban areas with a high cost of living may be able to command higher salaries than those in rural areas or smaller towns. This is because the cost of living in these areas is typically higher, and employers may need to offer higher salaries to attract and retain skilled technicians.

For example, I currently work in a large city on the East Coast, and I earn a salary that is higher than some of my colleagues who work in smaller towns or rural areas. However, it's important to keep in mind that the cost of living in my area is also much higher, so while my salary may be higher, my expenses are also higher.

The Impact of Type of Employer on Earning Potential

The type of employer can also play a role in a collision repair technician's earning potential. Technicians working in dealerships or specialty repair shops may be able to command higher salaries than those in general repair shops. This is because dealerships and specialty shops often work on higher-end vehicles that require more specialized knowledge and skill.

For example, I have colleagues who work in dealerships and specialty shops, and they earn significantly more than I do. However, they also work longer hours and may have more demanding schedules than those of us who work in general repair shops. It's important to weigh the pros and cons of different types of employers and determine which one is the best fit for your lifestyle and career goals.

Advancing Your Career and Earning Potential as a Collision Repair Technician

If you're interested in advancing your career and increasing your earning potential as a collision repair technician, there are a few steps you can take. One option is to pursue additional certifications through organizations like ASE or I-CAR. These certifications can demonstrate your expertise and knowledge in specific areas of collision repair, which can make you a more valuable employee and potentially lead to higher salaries.

Another option is to specialize in a specific area of collision repair, such as paintless dent repair or aluminum repair. These specialized skills are in high demand and can often command higher salaries than general collision repair work.

While the salary expectations for collision repair technicians may not be as high as some other skilled professions, there is still a lot of opportunity for growth and advancement in this field. By gaining experience, pursuing certifications, and specializing in specific areas of collision repair, technicians can increase their earning potential and enjoy a rewarding career in the automotive industry.
