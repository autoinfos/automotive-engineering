What Skills Should A Pharmacy Technician Have

Written by Robby Oct 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a pharmacy technician, it is important to have a range of skills to ensure you can perform your job effectively. From communication to attention to detail, there are certain abilities that are essential for success in this field.

Working in a pharmacy can be a challenging job. You will need to interact with customers, handle medications, and work alongside other healthcare professionals. Without the right skills, you may find it difficult to perform your job effectively and could even put patients at risk.

Communication Skills

One of the most important skills for a pharmacy technician is communication. You will need to be able to interact with customers, healthcare professionals, and other staff members. You should be able to communicate effectively, both verbally and in writing, and be able to explain complex medical information to patients in a way that is easy to understand.

For example, I once had a patient who was confused about the medications they were taking. As a pharmacy technician, I was able to explain the dosage and side effects in a way that put their mind at ease. It is important to have the ability to communicate clearly and effectively to ensure patients feel comfortable and informed.

Attention to Detail

Another important skill for a pharmacy technician is attention to detail. You will be responsible for handling medications and ensuring that they are dispensed correctly. A simple mistake could have serious consequences, so it is important to be meticulous in your work.

For example, I once noticed a discrepancy in a patient's prescription, and after investigating further, I discovered that the doctor had accidentally written the wrong dosage. Catching this error saved the patient from potentially harmful side effects.

Pharmacology Knowledge

Pharmacology knowledge is another vital skill for a pharmacy technician. You should have a solid understanding of different medications, their uses, dosages, and side effects. This knowledge will help you to answer patient questions and ensure that medications are dispensed safely and appropriately.

For example, I once had a patient who was taking multiple medications that had potential interactions. Being knowledgeable in pharmacology, I was able to work with the patient's doctor to adjust their dosage and avoid harmful interactions.

Organizational Skills

Lastly, organizational skills are crucial for a pharmacy technician. You will need to manage inventory, track prescription orders, and keep detailed records. Being organized will help you to perform your job efficiently and ensure that nothing falls through the cracks.

For example, I once had to manage a large order of medications for a nursing home. Without proper organization, it would have been easy to miss a medication or misplace an order, which could have had serious consequences for the patients.

Question and Answer

Q: What Technical Skills Should a Pharmacy Technician Have?

A: A pharmacy technician should have technical skills such as the ability to read and interpret prescriptions, perform mathematical calculations, and use computer software to manage patient records and inventory.

Q: How Important is Customer Service in Pharmacy Technician Jobs?

A: Customer service is extremely important in pharmacy technician jobs. Technicians must be able to communicate effectively with patients, answer their questions, and ensure that they feel comfortable and informed.

Q: Is it Necessary to Have a Degree to Become a Pharmacy Technician?

A: No, a degree is not necessary to become a pharmacy technician. However, most states require pharmacy technicians to be licensed or certified, which typically involves completing a training program and passing an exam.

Q: What Can a Pharmacy Technician Do to Improve Their Skills?

A: Pharmacy technicians can improve their skills by staying up-to-date with industry developments, attending training courses, and seeking feedback from their colleagues and supervisors.

Conclusion of What Skills Should a Pharmacy Technician Have

As a pharmacy technician, you will need a range of skills to perform your job effectively. Communication, attention to detail, pharmacology knowledge, and organizational skills are just a few of the abilities that are essential in this field. By honing these skills and staying up-to-date with industry developments, you can provide excellent service to patients and ensure that medications are dispensed safely and correctly.
