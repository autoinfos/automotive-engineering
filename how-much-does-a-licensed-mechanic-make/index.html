How Much Does A Licensed Mechanic Make

Written by Robby Aug 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as a licensed mechanic but wondering how much you can expect to make? Look no further. In this article, we'll explore the average salary of a licensed mechanic and factors that can impact their earnings.

As with any profession, there are both positive and negative aspects to consider when it comes to a licensed mechanic's salary. On the one hand, mechanics can earn a decent income without the need for a college degree. However, the nature of the job can be physically demanding and require long hours, including nights and weekends. Additionally, the amount a licensed mechanic can earn can vary depending on their level of experience, location, and employer.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics in the United States is $44,050. This equates to an hourly wage of $21.19. However, those just starting out in the field can expect to earn closer to $24,000 per year, while those with more experience can earn upwards of $66,000 per year.

In addition to experience, location and employer can also impact a licensed mechanic's salary. Those working in metropolitan areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas, and those working for larger dealerships or specialty shops may earn more than those working for smaller, independent garages. Additionally, licensure and certification can impact earning potential, as more specialized certifications can lead to increased job opportunities and higher salaries.

Personal Experience with Licensed Mechanic Salary

As someone who has worked in the automotive industry for several years, I can attest to the fact that licensed mechanic salaries can vary greatly. When I first started out as a mechanic, I was earning just over minimum wage. However, as I gained more experience and became certified in specialized areas such as air conditioning repair, my salary increased significantly. I also found that working for larger dealerships and specialty shops tended to come with higher salaries and better benefits.

The Impact of Licensure and Certification

As previously mentioned, licensure and certification can have a significant impact on a licensed mechanic's salary. In addition to the basic certification required to work as a mechanic, there are also specialized certifications available in areas such as brakes, transmissions, and engine repair. These certifications not only increase a mechanic's knowledge and skillset, but can also lead to higher salaries and increased job opportunities.

The Importance of Location and Employer

Another factor that can impact a licensed mechanic's salary is location and employer. As someone who has worked in both rural and metropolitan areas, I can attest to the fact that salaries tend to be higher in cities. Additionally, working for larger dealerships or specialty shops can come with higher salaries and better benefits than working for smaller, independent garages.

Tips for Increasing Your Licensed Mechanic Salary

If you're interested in increasing your licensed mechanic salary, there are several steps you can take. First and foremost, consider pursuing specialized certifications in areas such as brakes, transmissions, and engine repair. Additionally, consider seeking out employment with larger dealerships or specialty shops that tend to offer higher salaries and better benefits. Finally, be open to relocating to metropolitan areas where salaries tend to be higher.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it necessary to have a college degree to become a licensed mechanic?
A: No, a college degree is not necessary to become a licensed mechanic. However, completion of a vocational or technical program in automotive repair may be required.

Q: Can licensure and certification impact a licensed mechanic's salary?
A: Yes, licensure and certification can impact a licensed mechanic's salary. Specialized certifications can lead to increased job opportunities and higher salaries.

Q: Do licensed mechanics tend to work long hours?
A: Yes, licensed mechanics may be required to work long hours, including nights and weekends.

Q: Are salaries for licensed mechanics higher in metropolitan areas?
A: Yes, salaries for licensed mechanics tend to be higher in metropolitan areas than in rural areas.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Licensed Mechanic Make

Overall, while the salary of a licensed mechanic can vary based on experience, location, and employer, it is possible to earn a decent income in this field without a college degree. By pursuing specialized certifications, seeking out employment with larger dealerships or specialty shops, and being open to relocation, licensed mechanics can increase their earning potential and build a successful career in the automotive industry.
