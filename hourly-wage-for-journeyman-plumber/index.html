Hourly Wage For Journeyman Plumber The Ultimate Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a journeyman plumber? Do you want to know how much you can expect to earn per hour? Look no further because this guide will provide you with all the information you need to know about hourly wage for journeyman plumber.

As a journeyman plumber, you may encounter some pain points related to hourly wage. This can include uncertainty about how much you will earn, or feeling like your pay does not accurately reflect your skills and experience.

The target hourly wage for journeyman plumber can vary depending on factors such as location, employer, and experience. However, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for plumbers, pipefitters, and steamfitters was $27.22 as of May 2020.

In summary, hourly wage for journeyman plumber is an important factor to consider when pursuing a career in plumbing. While the exact wage can vary based on several factors, the median hourly wage for plumbers, pipefitters, and steamfitters is $27.22.

What is the Average Hourly Wage for Journeyman Plumber?

When I first started my journey as a journeyman plumber, I was curious about how much I could expect to earn per hour. After doing some research, I found that the average hourly wage for journeyman plumber was around $25 to $30 per hour.

However, it's important to note that this can vary based on several factors. For example, plumbers in high-demand areas may earn more than those in less populated areas. Additionally, plumbers with more experience and skills may earn a higher wage than those who are just starting out.

How Can You Negotiate a Higher Hourly Wage?

In my experience, negotiating a higher hourly wage as a journeyman plumber can be challenging but not impossible. When negotiating your wage, it's important to highlight your skills, experience, and certifications. Additionally, research the average wage in your area and use that as a benchmark for negotiations.

It's also important to consider the benefits and perks that come with the job, such as health insurance, retirement plans, and vacation time. These can also be negotiated to improve your overall compensation package.

How Can You Increase Your Hourly Wage as a Journeyman Plumber?

If you're looking to increase your hourly wage as a journeyman plumber, there are several steps you can take. First, consider pursuing additional certifications and training to increase your skills and expertise. This can make you more valuable to employers and increase your earning potential.

You can also consider taking on more responsibility within your current job or seeking out higher-paying job opportunities. Networking with other plumbers and industry professionals can also lead to new opportunities and higher pay.

How Can You Determine if Hourly Wage for Journeyman Plumber is Right for You?

Deciding whether hourly wage for journeyman plumber is right for you ultimately depends on your personal goals and priorities. If you enjoy working with your hands, are mechanically inclined, and want to pursue a career with job security and growth potential, plumbing can be a great option.

However, it's important to consider the physical demands of the job, such as working in cramped spaces or carrying heavy equipment. Additionally, the job can be physically demanding and may require working long hours or in inclement weather conditions.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to become a journeyman plumber?

A: Becoming a journeyman plumber typically requires completing an apprenticeship program, which can take anywhere from 4 to 5 years.

Q: Are there any additional certifications or licenses required to become a journeyman plumber?

A: Yes, in most states, journeyman plumbers are required to have a state-issued license. Additionally, some plumbers may pursue additional certifications in specialized areas such as medical gas installation or backflow prevention.

Q: What are the job prospects for journeyman plumbers?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of plumbers, pipefitters, and steamfitters is projected to grow 8 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is much faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: How much experience do you need to become a journeyman plumber?

A: The amount of experience required to become a journeyman plumber varies based on state requirements and the apprenticeship program. In general, however, journeyman plumbers must complete a certain number of hours of on-the-job training and classroom instruction before becoming certified.

Conclusion of Hourly Wage for Journeyman Plumber

Hourly wage for journeyman plumber can vary based on several factors, including location, experience, and employer. However, the median hourly wage for plumbers, pipefitters, and steamfitters is $27.22. By pursuing additional certifications, negotiating your wage, and seeking out higher-paying job opportunities, you can increase your earning potential as a journeyman plumber.
