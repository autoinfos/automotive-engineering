How Much Do Motorcycle Mechanics Make An Hour

Written by Daniel Aug 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you love the roar of an engine and the thrill of the open road? Are you thinking about pursuing a career as a motorcycle mechanic? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make per hour. Let's explore this topic further.

Motorcycle mechanics are responsible for repairing and maintaining motorcycles, scooters, and other small engine vehicles. While this career can be rewarding, it can also be physically demanding and require long hours. Additionally, the pay for motorcycle mechanics can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and industry.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for motorcycle mechanics in the United States is $19.78. However, the top 10% of motorcycle mechanics make more than $31.86 per hour, while the bottom 10% earn less than $11.79 per hour.

In summary, the amount of money you can make as a motorcycle mechanic depends on various factors. However, with experience and dedication, you can earn a decent wage while doing something you love.

What Affects How Much Motorcycle Mechanics Make?

As previously mentioned, several factors can influence how much money a motorcycle mechanic makes per hour. One of the most significant factors is experience. Generally, the more experience a mechanic has, the higher their hourly wage will be. Additionally, mechanics who work in larger cities or in specialized industries such as racing or custom bike building may earn a higher wage than those who work in smaller towns or in general repair shops.

My Personal Experience

As someone who has worked as a motorcycle mechanic for several years, I can attest to the fact that experience plays a big role in determining pay. When I started as a mechanic, I earned around $15 an hour. However, as I gained more experience and developed specialized skills, my hourly wage increased to around $25. Additionally, I found that working for a specialized shop that focused on custom bike builds and repairs provided me with a higher wage than working for a general repair shop.

Factors That Can Influence Motorcycle Mechanic Pay

In addition to experience and location, other factors can impact how much motorcycle mechanics make. For example, mechanics who have completed formal training programs or have obtained specialized certifications may earn a higher wage than those who have not. Additionally, working for a high-end dealership or repair shop can lead to a higher hourly wage than working for a smaller, independent shop.

How to Increase Your Hourly Wage as a Motorcycle Mechanic

If you want to increase your hourly wage as a motorcycle mechanic, there are several steps you can take. First, consider completing a formal training program or obtaining specialized certifications. These credentials can demonstrate your expertise and make you a more valuable employee. Additionally, seek out opportunities to work for high-end dealerships or specialized repair shops. Finally, continue to develop your skills and knowledge by attending seminars and workshops, reading industry publications, and staying up to date with the latest trends and technologies.

Conclusion of How Much Do Motorcycle Mechanics Make an Hour

In conclusion, the amount of money a motorcycle mechanic makes per hour can vary depending on numerous factors. However, with hard work, dedication, and experience, you can earn a decent wage while pursuing a career you love. If you are passionate about motorcycles and enjoy working with your hands, consider pursuing a career as a motorcycle mechanic.

Question and Answer

Q: Can motorcycle mechanics make a good living?

A: Yes, motorcycle mechanics can make a good living with experience and dedication. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for motorcycle mechanics in the United States is $19.78.

Q: What is the highest-paying industry for motorcycle mechanics?

A: The highest-paying industries for motorcycle mechanics are racing and custom bike building.

Q: Is certification necessary to become a motorcycle mechanic?

A: No, certification is not necessary to become a motorcycle mechanic. However, obtaining certifications can demonstrate your expertise and make you a more valuable employee.

Q: How can motorcycle mechanics increase their hourly wage?

A: Motorcycle mechanics can increase their hourly wage by completing formal training programs or obtaining specialized certifications, seeking out opportunities to work for high-end dealerships or specialized repair shops, and continuing to develop their skills and knowledge.
