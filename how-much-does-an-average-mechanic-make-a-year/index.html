How Much Does An Average Mechanic Make A Year

Written by Frank Aug 19, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanic? One of the biggest questions you may have is how much money you can expect to make. It's important to know the average salary for a mechanic so you can make an informed decision about your future career.

The average yearly salary for a mechanic in the United States is around $42,000. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors such as location, experience, and the type of mechanic you are.

In general, mechanics who work for dealerships or specialty shops tend to make more money than those who work at smaller, independent repair shops. Additionally, mechanics who specialize in a certain type of vehicle, such as diesel trucks, may command a higher salary due to their specialized knowledge and skills.

In conclusion, while the average salary for a mechanic is around $42,000 per year, there are a number of factors that can impact your earning potential. By choosing to work for a dealership or specializing in a certain type of vehicle, you may be able to earn more money as a mechanic.

How Does Your Location Affect Your Salary?

When it comes to how much money you can make as a mechanic, one of the biggest factors is your location. In general, mechanics who work in larger cities tend to make more money than those who work in smaller towns or rural areas. This is because there is typically more demand for mechanics in urban areas, and the cost of living is generally higher.

When I was first starting out as a mechanic, I worked at a small independent shop in a rural area. While I loved the work, I quickly realized that I wasn't making as much money as I could be. That's when I decided to move to a larger city and start working for a dealership. Not only did my salary increase, but I was also able to work on more specialized vehicles which helped me build my skills and expertise.

What Types of Vehicles Pay the Most?

As previously mentioned, specializing in a certain type of vehicle can help you earn more money as a mechanic. In general, mechanics who work on diesel trucks tend to make more money than those who work on smaller cars or motorcycles. This is because diesel trucks are more complex and require specialized knowledge and skills to repair.

When I first started working as a mechanic, I didn't have any particular specialization. However, I quickly realized that I enjoyed working on diesel trucks more than anything else. Not only did I find the work challenging and rewarding, but I was also able to command a higher salary due to my specialized knowledge and skills.

How Can You Increase Your Salary as a Mechanic?

If you're looking to increase your salary as a mechanic, there are a few things you can do. First, consider working for a dealership or specialty shop. These types of employers tend to pay more than smaller independent repair shops. Additionally, consider specializing in a certain type of vehicle, such as diesel trucks or hybrid cars. By doing so, you can command a higher salary due to your specialized knowledge and skills.

How Can You Get Started as a Mechanic?

If you're interested in becoming a mechanic, there are a few steps you can take to get started. First, consider enrolling in a vocational or technical school that offers a program in automotive repair. These programs typically take anywhere from six months to two years to complete, depending on the level of education you're seeking.

After completing your education, consider getting certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). This certification demonstrates to potential employers that you have the skills and knowledge necessary to perform high-quality repairs.

What Do Other Mechanics Have to Say?

When I was first starting out as a mechanic, I was curious to know what other mechanics had to say about the job. So, I reached out to a few colleagues to get their thoughts on the matter.

Q: How much money can you expect to make as a mechanic?

A: "It really depends on where you're working and what type of vehicles you're working on. But in general, you can expect to make anywhere from $30,000 to $50,000 per year."

Q: What advice would you give to someone who wants to become a mechanic?

A: "Get as much education and training as you can. The more specialized knowledge and skills you have, the more money you can make."

Q: What do you love most about being a mechanic?

A: "I love the feeling of fixing something that was broken. There's nothing more satisfying than hearing a customer say 'thank you' after you've repaired their vehicle."

Conclusion of How Much Does an Average Mechanic Make a Year

As you can see, there are a number of factors that can impact how much money you can make as a mechanic. By specializing in a certain type of vehicle or working for a dealership or specialty shop, you can increase your earning potential. However, even if you're just starting out as a mechanic, the average salary of around $42,000 per year is still a solid starting point.
