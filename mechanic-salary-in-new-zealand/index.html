The Truth About Mechanic Salary In New Zealand

Written by Luffy Jun 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you want to become a mechanic in New Zealand but wondering if it's worth it financially? Are you looking for information on mechanic salary in New Zealand? Look no further, because we have all the answers you need.

Being a mechanic in New Zealand can be challenging, especially when it comes to financial stability. The cost of living in New Zealand is relatively high, making it essential to earn a decent income to support yourself and your family.

The average salary for a mechanic in New Zealand is around NZ$46,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on your level of experience, location, and type of mechanic work you do.

In this article, we will discuss the ins and outs of mechanic salary in New Zealand, including how much you can expect to earn, the factors that affect your salary, and tips on how to increase your earnings.

How much can you earn as a mechanic in New Zealand?

As mentioned earlier, the average salary for a mechanic in New Zealand is around NZ$46,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on several factors.

If you're just starting as a mechanic, you can expect to earn around NZ$37,000 per year. As you gain more experience, your salary can increase to NZ$58,000 per year or more.

The type of work you do also affects your salary. For example, if you specialize in repairing high-end cars or work in a dealership, you may earn more than someone who works in an independent garage.

Factors that affect your mechanic salary in New Zealand

Several factors can affect your earning potential as a mechanic in New Zealand. These include:

Experience

The more experience you have, the higher your salary is likely to be. Mechanics who have worked for several years and have a proven track record of high-quality work can earn significantly more than those who are just starting.

Location

The location of your workplace can impact your salary. Mechanics who work in cities or larger towns may earn more than those who work in rural areas.

Type of work

The type of work you do also affects your salary. Specializing in certain areas such as diesel engines or high-end cars can increase your earning potential.

How to increase your mechanic salary in New Zealand

If you want to increase your mechanic salary in New Zealand, there are several things you can do. These include:

  • Specialize in a specific area of mechanic work
  • Complete additional training and certifications
  • Gain more experience and a proven track record of high-quality work
  • Consider working in a larger town or city

Personal experience: A mechanic's journey

John is a mechanic who has been working in New Zealand for over ten years. When he started, he earned around NZ$37,000 per year. However, as he gained more experience and specialized in repairing high-end cars, his salary increased to around NZ$75,000 per year.

According to John, the key to earning a higher salary as a mechanic is to specialize in a specific area of work and gain as much experience as possible. He also recommends completing additional training and certifications to enhance your skills and increase your earning potential.

FAQs about mechanic salary in New Zealand

Q: Is being a mechanic in New Zealand a good career choice?

A: Yes, being a mechanic in New Zealand is a good career choice, especially if you're passionate about cars and enjoy working with your hands. However, it's important to note that the salary may not be as high as other professions.

Q: How much does a diesel mechanic make in New Zealand?

A: A diesel mechanic in New Zealand can earn an average salary of around NZ$54,000 per year.

Q: Can I earn more as a mechanic if I specialize in a specific area of work?

A: Yes, specializing in a specific area of work such as high-end car repair or diesel engines can increase your earning potential as a mechanic in New Zealand.

Q: What qualifications do I need to become a mechanic in New Zealand?

A: To become a mechanic in New Zealand, you will need to complete a relevant qualification, such as a National Certificate in Motor Industry (Automotive Electrical and Mechanical Engineering) (Level 4).

Conclusion of Mechanic Salary in New Zealand

In conclusion, being a mechanic in New Zealand can be a rewarding career choice, but it's important to be aware of the average salary and the factors that can affect your earning potential. Specializing in a specific area of work, gaining more experience, and completing additional training can increase your salary as a mechanic in New Zealand.
