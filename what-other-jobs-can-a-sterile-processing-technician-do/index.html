Working as a Surgical Technologist

One option for a sterile processing technician looking to explore other career paths is to become a surgical technologist. As a surgical technologist, you'll be responsible for preparing the operating room and sterile equipment for surgical procedures. You'll also be responsible for assisting surgeons during the procedure by passing them instruments and other equipment.

I made the transition from being a sterile processing technician to a surgical technologist five years ago, and I have never looked back. I found the work to be more challenging and rewarding, and I enjoyed the opportunity to work more closely with surgeons and other healthcare professionals. It was also a great way to expand my skill set and knowledge of the healthcare industry.

Becoming a Medical Equipment Repair Technician

Another career path that a sterile processing technician can take is to become a medical equipment repair technician. In this role, you'll be responsible for maintaining and repairing medical equipment, including surgical instruments, imaging equipment, and other tools used in the healthcare industry.

This is a great option for someone who enjoys working with their hands and has a strong mechanical aptitude. It's also a good way to expand your knowledge of the healthcare industry and gain experience working with a variety of medical equipment.

Working as a Clinical Engineering Technician

A clinical engineering technician is another career path that a sterile processing technician can take. In this role, you'll be responsible for maintaining and repairing medical equipment, but you'll also have more responsibilities related to the management and administration of healthcare technology.

This is a great option for someone who enjoys working with technology and has strong problem-solving skills. It's also a good way to gain more experience working in a leadership or management role within the healthcare industry.

How to Become a Surgical Technologist

If you're interested in becoming a surgical technologist, you'll need to complete a training program and obtain certification. The training program typically takes between 9 and 24 months to complete, and you'll need to pass a certification exam in order to become certified.

There are several accredited training programs available, including community colleges, vocational schools, and technical schools. You can also obtain certification through the National Board of Surgical Technology and Surgical Assisting (NBSTSA).

Conclusion of What Other Jobs Can a Sterile Processing Technician Do

If you're a sterile processing technician and you're interested in exploring other career paths, there are several options available to you.
