Question and Answer

Q: What qualifications do I need to become a diesel mechanic?

A: To become a diesel mechanic, you will typically need to complete a vocational training program or apprenticeship, and earn certification through a professional organization such as ASE. You may also need a high school diploma or equivalent.

Q: What types of industries hire diesel mechanics?

A: Diesel mechanics may work in a variety of industries, including transportation, construction, agriculture, and more. They may also specialize in a particular type of vehicle or equipment, such as trucks, buses, generators, or heavy machinery.

Q: What skills are required to be a successful diesel mechanic?

A: Successful diesel mechanics typically have strong problem-solving skills, attention to detail, and excellent communication skills. They should also be familiar with diesel engine systems and have experience diagnosing and repairing engine issues.

Q: How much can I expect to earn as a diesel mechanic?

A: Salaries for diesel mechanics can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and type of employer. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $48,500 in May 2020.
