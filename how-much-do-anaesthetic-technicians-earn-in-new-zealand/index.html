What to Expect as an Anaesthetic Technician in New Zealand

As an anaesthetic technician in New Zealand, you'll play a critical role in the operating room. You'll be responsible for preparing patients for surgery, administering anaesthesia, monitoring vital signs, and assisting the anaesthesiologist during the procedure.

While the work can be challenging, it can also be very rewarding. You'll have the satisfaction of knowing that you're helping to ensure the safety and comfort of patients during surgery.

Training and Qualifications for Anaesthetic Technicians in New Zealand

To become an anaesthetic technician in New Zealand, you'll need to complete a two-year diploma program in anaesthetic technology. This program is offered by the Auckland University of Technology and the Southern Institute of Technology.

During the program, you'll learn about the anatomy and physiology of the human body, pharmacology, anaesthesia techniques, and patient care. You'll also complete a number of clinical placements to gain hands-on experience.

After completing the program, you'll need to register with the Medical Sciences Council of New Zealand in order to practice as an anaesthetic technician.
