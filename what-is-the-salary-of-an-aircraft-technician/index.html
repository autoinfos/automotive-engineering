What Is The Salary Of An Aircraft Technician

Written by Bardi Oct 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you have a passion for airplanes and an inclination towards technical work? Becoming an aircraft technician may be the perfect career path for you. However, before embarking on this journey, it's important to understand what the salary of an aircraft technician entails.

As with any job, there are both pros and cons to the salary of an aircraft technician. One of the biggest pain points is the physical demand of the position. Aircraft technicians are required to work in harsh environments, including cramped spaces and extreme temperatures. Additionally, the job can be demanding and require long hours, including nights and weekends.

The salary range for an aircraft technician can vary depending on the level of experience and the specific area of expertise. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians in May 2020 was $65,230. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $38,960, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $97,820.

In conclusion, the salary of an aircraft technician can be a lucrative career path for those who have a passion for airplanes and the technical skills to maintain them. While the job can be physically demanding and require long hours, the median annual salary of $65,230 is a great incentive for those interested in pursuing this career path.

What to Expect with the Salary of an Aircraft Technician

When I first started as an aircraft technician, I had no idea what to expect in terms of salary. However, after a few years of experience under my belt, I can confidently say that the salary range can be quite attractive. The median annual salary of $65,230 is a great incentive for those interested in pursuing this career path.

The Importance of Experience with the Salary of an Aircraft Technician

As with any field, experience plays a crucial role in determining the salary of an aircraft technician. When I first started out, I was making around $40,000 a year, but as I gained more experience and expertise in specific areas, my salary increased significantly. It's important to continually educate yourself and gain experience to advance your career and increase your earning potential.

The Impact of Location on the Salary of an Aircraft Technician

Another factor that can impact the salary of an aircraft technician is location. For example, those working in Alaska or Hawaii may earn more due to the remote location and harsh weather conditions. Additionally, those working for the government or the military may also earn a higher salary compared to those working for private companies.

Negotiating Your Salary as an Aircraft Technician

When it comes to negotiating your salary as an aircraft technician, it's important to do your research and come prepared with specific examples of your experience and expertise. Additionally, consider negotiating other benefits such as vacation time, health insurance, and retirement plans.

Personal Experience with the Salary of an Aircraft Technician

As an aircraft technician with over 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that the salary range can be quite attractive. While the physical demands of the job can be challenging, the ability to work on airplanes and ensure their safety is a rewarding experience. Additionally, as I gained more experience and expertise in specific areas, my salary increased significantly.

Question and Answer

Q: What education is required to become an aircraft technician?

A: An aircraft technician typically requires a certificate, diploma, or associate's degree in aviation maintenance technology or a related field.

Q: What are some of the benefits of being an aircraft technician?

A: Benefits of being an aircraft technician include job security, opportunities for advancement, and the ability to travel and work on different types of airplanes.

Q: Are there any certifications or licenses required to become an aircraft technician?

A: Yes, aircraft technicians must be certified by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) and hold a valid Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) license.

Q: What are some of the challenges of being an aircraft technician?

A: The physical demands of the job, including working in harsh environments and long hours, can be challenging for some. Additionally, the job can be stressful as aircraft technicians are responsible for ensuring the safety of passengers and crew.

Conclusion of What is the Salary of an Aircraft Technician

The salary of an aircraft technician can be a lucrative career path for those with a passion for airplanes and the technical skills to maintain them. While the job can be physically demanding and require long hours, the median annual salary of $65,230 is a great incentive for those interested in pursuing this career path.
