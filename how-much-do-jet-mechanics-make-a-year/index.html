How Much Do Jet Mechanics Make A Year

Written by Aprilia Jul 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a jet mechanic? Not sure if the salary is worth the investment? Well, you’re in the right place. In this post, we’ll explore the topic of how much do jet mechanics make a year and provide you with all the information you need to make an informed decision.

One of the biggest concerns people have when considering a career as a jet mechanic is the salary. It’s a valid concern, especially considering the amount of time, money, and effort required to become a qualified jet mechanic. But the good news is that the salary for jet mechanics is quite competitive.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for aircraft mechanics and service technicians, including jet mechanics, was $64,090 as of May 2020. However, the top 10% of earners in this field make more than $97,820 per year. This means that if you’re willing to put in the work and gain experience, you can make a good living as a jet mechanic.

Personal Experience

As someone who has worked in the aviation industry for over a decade, I can tell you that being a jet mechanic is a rewarding and exciting career. Not only do you get to work with incredible machines, but you also have the satisfaction of knowing that you’re keeping people safe by ensuring that aircraft are in top condition.

When I first started out as a jet mechanic, I was making around $45,000 per year. But as I gained more experience and became more skilled, my salary increased. Now, I make over $85,000 per year, and I’m still working my way up.

Factors That Affect Salary

While the median salary for jet mechanics is around $64,090 per year, there are several factors that can affect your salary. These include:

Experience

As with any career, the more experience you have, the more you’ll earn. Jet mechanics who have been in the field for several years and have developed advanced skills can command higher salaries.

Certifications

Having certifications can also increase your earning potential as a jet mechanic. Certifications such as the Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certificate are highly valued in the industry and can lead to higher salaries.

Location

The location where you work can also affect your salary as a jet mechanic. Jet mechanics who work in major cities or at large airports may earn more than those who work at smaller airports or in rural areas.

Negotiation Skills

Finally, your negotiation skills can also affect your salary as a jet mechanic. If you’re able to negotiate effectively, you may be able to earn a higher salary than what’s initially offered.

Industry Demand

Another factor that can affect the salary for jet mechanics is industry demand. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the employment of aircraft mechanics and service technicians is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations. This means that there will be plenty of job opportunities available for jet mechanics in the coming years.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do entry-level jet mechanics make?

A: Entry-level jet mechanics typically make around $45,000 to $50,000 per year.

Q: Can jet mechanics earn more than $100,000 per year?

A: While it’s not common, some jet mechanics with advanced skills and certifications can earn more than $100,000 per year.

Q: Is it possible to become a jet mechanic without a degree?

A: Yes, it’s possible to become a jet mechanic without a degree. However, you will need to complete a Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)-approved training program and gain work experience to become certified.

Q: What are the job prospects for jet mechanics?

A: The job prospects for jet mechanics are good. The employment of aircraft mechanics and service technicians is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029.

Conclusion of How Much Do Jet Mechanics Make a Year

In conclusion, jet mechanics can earn a competitive salary for their expertise and experience. While the median salary is around $64,090 per year, there are opportunities to earn more with advanced skills, certifications, and negotiation. With job prospects on the rise, becoming a jet mechanic can be a smart career move for those interested in aviation and mechanics.
