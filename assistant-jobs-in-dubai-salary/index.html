Assistant Jobs In Dubai Salary Everything You Need To Know

Written by Daniel Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an assistant in Dubai? Do you want to know more about the salary and benefits of this job? Look no further, because in this post, we will give you all the information you need to know about assistant jobs in Dubai salary.

Working as an assistant can be challenging, especially in a fast-paced city like Dubai. Long working hours, high workload, and demanding bosses can take a toll on your mental and physical health. However, the salary and benefits of this job can make up for these pain points, making it an attractive career option for many.

So, what is the average salary of an assistant in Dubai? According to Payscale, the average salary for an executive assistant in Dubai is AED 101,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on your experience, qualifications, and the industry you work in.

To get a better idea of the salary range for assistant jobs in Dubai, here is a breakdown by industry:

  • Finance: AED 80,000 - AED 180,000 per year
  • Real Estate: AED 60,000 - AED 150,000 per year
  • Construction: AED 70,000 - AED 120,000 per year
  • Healthcare: AED 60,000 - AED 100,000 per year

My Experience with Assistant Jobs in Dubai Salary

As someone who has worked as an assistant in Dubai for over five years, I can attest to the fact that the salary and benefits of this job are quite attractive. Not only did I receive a competitive salary, but I also had health insurance, paid vacation days, and other perks.

However, it is important to note that the workload and responsibilities of an assistant can be quite demanding. You may have to work long hours, attend meetings, manage schedules, and perform other tasks that require a high level of attention to detail and organization.

How to Land a High-Paying Assistant Job in Dubai

If you want to land a high-paying assistant job in Dubai, here are some tips to help you:

  • Get the right education: Having a degree in business administration, management, or a related field can give you an advantage in the job market.
  • Get the right experience: Having previous experience as an assistant or in a related field can also increase your chances of landing a high-paying job.
  • Develop the right skills: To be a successful assistant, you need to have excellent communication, organization, and time-management skills.
  • Network: Attend industry events, join professional organizations, and connect with people in your field to expand your network and increase your chances of finding a job.

Assistant Jobs in Dubai Salary: Benefits and Perks

In addition to a competitive salary, assistant jobs in Dubai often come with other benefits and perks. Some of these include:

  • Health insurance
  • Paid vacation days
  • Bonuses
  • Flexible working hours
  • Opportunities for career growth

Question and Answer

Q: How much does an executive assistant make in Dubai?

A: According to Payscale, the average salary for an executive assistant in Dubai is AED 101,000 per year.

Q: What industries pay the highest salary for assistant jobs in Dubai?

A: The finance and real estate industries tend to pay the highest salary for assistant jobs in Dubai.

Q: What qualifications do I need to land a high-paying assistant job in Dubai?

A: Having a degree in business administration, management, or a related field, as well as previous experience as an assistant or in a related field, can increase your chances of landing a high-paying job.

Q: What are some benefits of working as an assistant in Dubai?

A: Some benefits of working as an assistant in Dubai include health insurance, paid vacation days, bonuses, flexible working hours, and opportunities for career growth.

Conclusion of Assistant Jobs in Dubai Salary

Assistant jobs in Dubai offer a competitive salary and other benefits that make it an attractive career option for many. While the job can be demanding, the right education, experience, skills, and networking can help you land a high-paying job in this field. So, if you are considering a career as an assistant in Dubai, go for it!
