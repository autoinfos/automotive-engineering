What Does A Car Dealership Service Advisor Make

Written by Daniel Sep 01, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered about the salary of a car dealership service advisor? If you're considering a career in this field, you may be curious about what kind of pay you can expect. However, there are many factors that can affect a service advisor's salary, and it can be difficult to find reliable information on the topic.

Car dealership service advisors are responsible for communicating with customers about their vehicle needs, coordinating repairs and maintenance, and ensuring customer satisfaction. However, the job can be demanding and stressful, and service advisors may need to work long hours or deal with difficult customers.

According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a car dealership service advisor in the United States is around $42,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and the dealership's size and reputation. Some service advisors may earn more than $50,000 per year, while others may earn closer to $30,000.

In summary, while car dealership service advisors can expect to earn a decent salary, there are many factors that can affect their pay. It's important to consider the demands of the job and the dealership's reputation before pursuing a career in this field.

What Factors Affect a Car Dealership Service Advisor's Salary?

When it comes to determining a car dealership service advisor's salary, there are several factors to consider. Here are a few that can have a significant impact:

Location:

The cost of living and demand for service advisors can vary depending on the region. For example, a service advisor working in a major city may earn more than one working in a rural area.

Experience:

Service advisors with more experience are often able to negotiate higher salaries. However, it can be difficult to gain experience in this field without starting at a lower pay rate.

Dealership Reputation:

Service advisors working at well-known and respected dealerships may earn more than those working at less reputable dealerships.

Education and Certifications:

Service advisors with specialized education or certifications may be able to command higher salaries. For example, a service advisor with a degree in automotive technology may earn more than one without that qualification.

What My Experience Has Taught Me About a Car Dealership Service Advisor's Salary

During my time working as a car dealership service advisor, I've learned that pay rates can vary widely depending on the dealership's policies and location. However, I've also found that the job can be rewarding in other ways, such as the opportunity to help customers and work with a team of skilled professionals.

Is a Career as a Car Dealership Service Advisor Right for You?

If you enjoy working with people, have strong communication skills, and are interested in the automotive industry, a career as a car dealership service advisor could be a good fit for you. However, it's important to do your research and consider the demands of the job before pursuing this career path.
