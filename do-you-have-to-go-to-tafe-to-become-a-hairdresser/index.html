Do You Have To Go To Tafe To Become A Hairdresser

Written by William Sep 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a hairdresser but unsure if you need to go to TAFE? It's a common question that many aspiring hairdressers ask themselves. Going to TAFE can be a significant investment of time and money, and it's essential to know if it's necessary for your career goals.

One of the main pain points related to do you have to go to TAFE to become a hairdresser is the cost and time commitment. TAFE courses can be expensive, and it's a significant investment to make, especially if you're not sure if it's necessary for your career.

The answer to whether you have to go to TAFE to become a hairdresser is no, you don't need to. While it's a formal education route that is highly recommended, it's not the only way to become a hairdresser. You can get hands-on experience by working in a salon, completing an apprenticeship, or attending a private hairdressing school.

In summary, going to TAFE is not the only way to become a hairdresser. However, it's highly recommended as it provides a formal education that can give you an edge in the industry. It's also worth considering the investment of time and money and whether it's necessary for your career goals.

Benefits of Going to TAFE for Hairdressing

I wanted to share my personal experience with going to TAFE for hairdressing. While I knew it wasn't the only way to become a hairdresser, I wanted to have a formal education that would give me the skills and knowledge to succeed in the industry. My TAFE course provided me with hands-on experience, industry connections, and job opportunities.

Going to TAFE for hairdressing can also provide you with:

1. Industry Connections

TAFE courses often have industry connections that can help you get your foot in the door. You'll have the opportunity to network and meet industry professionals who can provide you with job opportunities or advice on how to succeed in the industry.

2. Formal Education

Going to TAFE gives you a formal education that includes theory and practical skills. You'll learn the latest techniques, trends, and technologies in the industry, giving you an edge when it comes to finding a job or starting your own business.

Alternatives to Going to TAFE for Hairdressing

While going to TAFE is highly recommended, it's not the only way to become a hairdresser. Alternatives include:

1. Working in a Salon

You can start working in a salon and gain hands-on experience by starting as a trainee or apprentice. You'll learn on the job and work your way up the ranks, gaining experience and building your skills.

2. Private Hairdressing Schools

Private hairdressing schools offer courses that provide hands-on experience and formal education. These courses can be shorter than TAFE courses and are often more affordable.

Question and Answer

Q: Is going to TAFE the only way to become a hairdresser?

A: No, while it's highly recommended, you can become a hairdresser by working in a salon, completing an apprenticeship, or attending a private hairdressing school.

Q: How long does a TAFE course for hairdressing take?

A: TAFE courses for hairdressing can range from 12 months to 3 years, depending on the course and level of qualification.

Q: Do I need any qualifications to become a hairdresser?

A: While you don't need formal qualifications to become a hairdresser, it's highly recommended to have a formal education or hands-on experience in the industry.

Q: How much does a TAFE course for hairdressing cost?

A: The cost of a TAFE course for hairdressing can vary depending on the course and location. It's essential to research and compare different options to find the best one for your budget.

Conclusion of Do You Have to Go to TAFE to Become a Hairdresser?

In conclusion, while going to TAFE is highly recommended, it's not the only way to become a hairdresser. You can gain hands-on experience by working in a salon or attending a private hairdressing school. However, going to TAFE can provide you with a formal education, industry connections, and job opportunities. It's worth considering the investment of time and money and whether it's necessary for your career goals.
