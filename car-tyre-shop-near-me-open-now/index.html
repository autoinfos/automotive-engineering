Why Regular Tyre Maintenance is Important

It's important to not only find a car tyre shop near me open now when you need new tyres, but also to regularly maintain your tyres. This includes checking the tyre pressure, rotating the tyres, and getting regular tyre alignments.

Regular tyre maintenance can help extend the life of your tyres and improve the overall performance of your car. It can also help prevent any potential safety issues while driving.

How to Check Your Tyre Pressure

Checking your tyre pressure is an important part of regular tyre maintenance. You should aim to check your tyre pressure at least once a month and before any long trips.

To check your tyre pressure, you will need a tyre pressure gauge. Simply remove the valve cap on your tyre and press the gauge onto the valve stem. The gauge will provide a reading of the pressure in your tyre.

If your tyre pressure is too low, you can add air at a gas station or with a portable air compressor. If your tyre pressure is too high, you can release air with the gauge or at a gas station.

How to Rotate Your Tyres

Rotating your tyres is another important part of regular tyre maintenance. This involves moving the front tyres to the back and the back tyres to the front of your car.

By rotating your tyres, you can help ensure that they wear evenly and extend their lifespan. It's recommended to rotate your tyres every 6,000 to 8,000 miles or as recommended in your car's owner's manual.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I replace my tyres?

A: It's recommended to replace your tyres every 6 years or when the tread depth reaches 2/32 of an inch.

Q: Can I replace just one tyre?

A: It's recommended to replace all four tyres at the same time to ensure even wear and performance. However, if you need to replace just one tyre, it should be the same size and type as the other tyres on your car.

Q: What is the difference between summer and winter tyres?

A: Summer tyres are designed for warm weather and provide better handling and traction on dry and wet roads. Winter tyres are designed for cold weather and provide better handling and traction in snow and ice.

Q: Can I drive on a flat tyre?

A: It's not recommended to drive on a flat tyre as it can damage your wheel and potentially cause a dangerous blowout. Instead, pull over to a safe location and change the tyre or call for roadside assistance.
