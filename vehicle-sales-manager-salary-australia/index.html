What Is The Average Vehicle Sales Manager Salary In Australia

Written by William Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a vehicle sales manager in Australia? While the job can be rewarding, it's important to understand the salary expectations before jumping in. In this post, we'll discuss the average vehicle sales manager salary in Australia, pain points related to the job, and what you can do to increase your earning potential.

One of the biggest pain points for vehicle sales managers in Australia is the commission-based pay structure. While this can lead to higher earnings, it also means that your salary can fluctuate greatly based on the number of sales you make. Additionally, the hours can be long and sometimes require working on weekends or holidays.

On average, a vehicle sales manager in Australia can expect to earn around $70,000 to $80,000 per year. However, this can vary greatly depending on factors such as experience, location, and the dealership you work for. Top-performing sales managers can earn upwards of $150,000 per year.

The Importance of Networking

One of the keys to increasing your earning potential as a vehicle sales manager is networking. Building relationships with other professionals in the industry can lead to new job opportunities and higher-paying positions. I personally have seen the benefits of networking firsthand, as I was able to land a job at a top dealership thanks to a referral from a former colleague.

The Impact of Location

Another factor that can greatly affect your salary as a vehicle sales manager is your location. Dealerships in major cities such as Sydney and Melbourne tend to offer higher salaries due to the higher cost of living. However, competition for these positions can be fierce, so it's important to have a strong resume and track record of success.

Increasing Your Earning Potential

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as a vehicle sales manager in Australia, there are a few steps you can take. First, focus on building strong relationships with your customers to increase sales and commissions. Additionally, consider pursuing additional certifications or training to demonstrate your expertise and value to potential employers.

Negotiating Your Salary

When it comes time to negotiate your salary as a vehicle sales manager, it's important to come prepared with data and examples of your past success. Consider bringing in sales reports or testimonials from satisfied customers to demonstrate your value to the dealership. Additionally, be prepared to negotiate not just your base salary, but also commission rates and other benefits such as bonuses and time off.

Conclusion of Vehicle Sales Manager Salary Australia

While the average vehicle sales manager salary in Australia may not be as high as other industries, there is still ample opportunity for growth and earning potential. By focusing on building strong relationships with customers, networking with other professionals in the industry, and pursuing additional training and certifications, you can increase your earning potential and build a successful career in vehicle sales management.
