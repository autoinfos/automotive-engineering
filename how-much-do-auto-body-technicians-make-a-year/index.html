How Much Do Auto Body Technicians Make A Year

Written by Christine Aug 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to pursue a career in auto body repair but unsure of the earning potential? Understanding how much do auto body technicians make a year can help you plan your future and make informed decisions about your career path.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for auto body and related technicians was $43,580 as of May 2020. However, the salary range can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and type of employer.

In general, auto body technicians working in metropolitan areas tend to earn higher salaries compared to those in rural areas. Additionally, those with more years of experience and advanced certifications can command higher salaries. Auto body technicians working for car dealerships may also earn more than those working for independent repair shops.

In summary, the earning potential for auto body technicians can range from around $30,000 to over $70,000 per year. It's important to research the specific job market in your area and consider factors such as experience and certifications when evaluating potential salary ranges.

The Importance of Certification

While experience is certainly valuable in the auto body repair industry, certifications can also significantly impact earning potential. Many employers prefer to hire technicians who have completed training programs and earned certifications from organizations such as the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) or the Inter-Industry Conference on Auto Collision Repair (I-CAR).

Personal experience has taught me the importance of obtaining certifications in the auto body industry. Early on in my career, I struggled to find employment due to a lack of experience and certifications. After completing an ASE certification program, I was able to secure a higher-paying job and advance my career.

Navigating the Job Market

The job market for auto body technicians can be competitive, but there are steps you can take to increase your earning potential. Consider specializing in a specific area such as collision repair or paint work, as this can make you a more valuable asset to potential employers.

Networking is also important in the auto body industry. Attend industry events, join professional organizations, and build relationships with industry contacts to stay up-to-date on job opportunities and industry trends.

Factors That Affect Earning Potential

There are several factors that can impact how much you can earn as an auto body technician:

Location: Salaries can vary depending on the cost of living and demand for auto body technicians in your area.

Experience: Technicians with more years of experience may be able to command higher salaries.

Certifications: Earning certifications from organizations such as ASE or I-CAR can make you a more valuable asset to potential employers.

Type of employer: Auto body technicians working for car dealerships may earn more than those working for independent repair shops.

Negotiating Your Salary

When negotiating your salary as an auto body technician, it's important to do your research and come prepared. Research the job market in your area and evaluate your experience and certifications to determine a fair salary range.

During the negotiation process, highlight your skills and experience, and be prepared to explain why you are worth the salary you are requesting. Don't be afraid to negotiate and ask for a higher salary if you feel you are being undervalued.

Conclusion of How Much Do Auto Body Technicians Make a Year

The earning potential for auto body technicians can vary depending on several factors, including location, experience, and certifications. While the median salary is around $43,580 per year, technicians can earn anywhere from around $30,000 to over $70,000 per year depending on their skills and qualifications. By specializing in a specific area, earning certifications, and building industry connections, you can increase your earning potential in the auto body industry.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do auto body technicians make per hour?

A: The median hourly wage for auto body technicians was $20.95 as of May 2020, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Q: What is the job outlook for auto body technicians?

A: The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects that employment of auto body technicians will grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: Is it possible to earn a higher salary as an auto body technician?

A: Yes, technicians with more years of experience, advanced certifications, and specialized skills can earn higher salaries in the auto body industry.

Q: What are some common benefits offered to auto body technicians?

A: Benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off are commonly offered to auto body technicians by their employers.
