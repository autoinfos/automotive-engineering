The Duties And Responsibilities Of An Automotive Mechanic

Written by Robby Jul 31, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you someone who loves cars and has a knack for fixing them? If yes, then becoming an automotive mechanic might be the perfect career choice for you. However, before diving into this field, it is essential to understand the duties and responsibilities that come with the job. In this blog post, we'll discuss everything you need to know about the duties and responsibilities of an automotive mechanic.

As an automotive mechanic, you'll be responsible for repairing and maintaining vehicles. This can include tasks like performing diagnostic tests, identifying problems, and fixing them. You'll also be responsible for performing routine maintenance tasks like oil changes, engine tune-ups, and tire rotations. Additionally, you'll be responsible for keeping records of all the work you've done on a particular vehicle.

The target of these duties and responsibilities is to ensure that vehicles are running smoothly and safely. It is the job of an automotive mechanic to identify and fix any issues that could compromise the safety of the driver and passengers. They also play a crucial role in ensuring that vehicles meet emission standards and are environmentally friendly.

In conclusion, the duties and responsibilities of an automotive mechanic are vast and varied. From performing diagnostic tests to routine maintenance tasks, an automotive mechanic's job is to ensure that vehicles are running smoothly and safely. Let's take a closer look at some of these duties and responsibilities.

Performing Diagnostic Tests

One of the primary duties of an automotive mechanic is to perform diagnostic tests on vehicles. These tests help identify any issues or problems that could be affecting the performance of the vehicle. As a mechanic, you'll use specialized equipment to diagnose problems such as check engine lights, strange noises, or poor performance.

During my time as an automotive mechanic, I had a customer who came in with a check engine light on. After performing a diagnostic test, I found that the issue was with the oxygen sensor. I replaced the sensor, and the check engine light turned off, and the vehicle was running smoothly.

Identifying Problems and Fixing Them

Once an automotive mechanic has identified an issue with a vehicle, their next responsibility is to fix it. This can include tasks like replacing faulty parts, repairing damaged components, or adjusting systems to ensure proper performance. As a mechanic, you'll need to have a deep understanding of how vehicles work and be able to troubleshoot problems efficiently.

On one occasion, a customer came to me with a car that was making a strange noise from the engine. After inspecting the vehicle, I found that the issue was with the timing belt. I replaced the timing belt, and the noise disappeared, and the vehicle was running smoothly again.

Routine Maintenance Tasks

Another critical responsibility of an automotive mechanic is to perform routine maintenance tasks like oil changes, engine tune-ups, and tire rotations. These tasks are essential to keep vehicles running smoothly and to prevent any major issues from occurring. As a mechanic, you'll need to follow the manufacturer's recommendations for maintenance and keep accurate records of all the work you've done on a particular vehicle.

During my time as an automotive mechanic, I had a customer who brought in their car for an oil change. While performing the oil change, I noticed that the air filter was dirty and needed replacing. I replaced the air filter, and the customer was grateful that I caught the issue before it caused any major problems.

Ensuring Safety and Meeting Emission Standards

Finally, as an automotive mechanic, you'll be responsible for ensuring that vehicles meet safety and emission standards. This can include tasks like checking brakes, steering, and suspension systems to ensure they're functioning correctly. You'll also need to ensure that the vehicle's emissions are within acceptable limits and that the catalytic converter is functioning correctly.

During my time as an automotive mechanic, I had a customer who brought in their car for an inspection. After inspecting the vehicle, I found that the brakes were worn and needed replacing. I also noticed that the catalytic converter was damaged and needed replacing to ensure that the vehicle's emissions were within acceptable limits. After replacing these components, the vehicle passed inspection and was safe to drive.

Question and Answer

Q: What education do I need to become an automotive mechanic?

A: While a high school diploma is the minimum requirement, most employers prefer candidates who have completed a vocational or technical program in automotive technology.

Q: How much can I expect to earn as an automotive mechanic?

A: The average salary for an automotive mechanic is around $42,000 per year, but this can vary depending on location, experience, and employer.

Q: What are some qualities that make a good automotive mechanic?

A: Good automotive mechanics are detail-oriented, have excellent problem-solving skills, and are knowledgeable about vehicles and their components.

Q: What are some common certifications for automotive mechanics?

A: Some common certifications for automotive mechanics include ASE (Automotive Service Excellence), NATEF (National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation), and AMRA (Automotive Maintenance and Repair Association).

Conclusion of Duties and Responsibilities of an Automotive Mechanic

Becoming an automotive mechanic can be a rewarding career choice for those who love cars and have a knack for fixing them. However, it's essential to understand the duties and responsibilities that come with the job. From performing diagnostic tests to routine maintenance tasks, an automotive mechanic plays a crucial role in ensuring that vehicles are running smoothly and safely. By following manufacturer recommendations and keeping accurate records of all work done, an automotive mechanic ensures that vehicles meet safety and emission standards.
