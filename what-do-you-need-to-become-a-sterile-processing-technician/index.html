What Do You Need To Become A Sterile Processing Technician

Written by Bardi May 30, 2023 · 5 min read

Becoming a sterile processing technician can be a fulfilling career, but it requires certain qualifications and skills. If you are interested in pursuing this field, then it is important to know what you need to become a sterile processing technician.

Sterile processing technicians are responsible for ensuring that medical equipment and instruments are clean and free from contamination, so that patients are safe from infections. They work in hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare facilities, and are an essential part of the healthcare team. However, becoming a sterile processing technician can be challenging, as it requires a combination of education, certification, and experience.

The first step to becoming a sterile processing technician is to earn a high school diploma or GED. After that, you will need to complete a sterile processing technician training program, which can be found at vocational schools, community colleges, and online. These programs typically take six months to a year to complete, and cover topics such as anatomy, microbiology, infection control, and medical terminology. Once you have completed the training program, you will need to pass a certification exam to become a certified sterile processing technician.

In addition to education and certification, it is important for sterile processing technicians to have certain skills and qualities. These include attention to detail, good communication skills, the ability to work under pressure, and the ability to work as part of a team. Sterile processing technicians must also be physically able to stand for long periods of time, and be able to lift heavy objects.

In summary, to become a sterile processing technician you need to:

  • Earn a high school diploma or GED
  • Complete a sterile processing technician training program
  • Pass a certification exam
  • Have certain skills and qualities, such as attention to detail and good communication skills

Education and Certification

When I decided to become a sterile processing technician, I knew that I needed to have the right education and certification to be successful in this field. I started by earning my high school diploma, and then enrolled in a sterile processing technician training program at my local community college. The program was challenging, but it gave me the knowledge and skills that I needed to pass the certification exam.

During the training program, I learned about the importance of infection control, how to properly clean and sterilize medical equipment, and the different types of instruments that are used in healthcare. I also had the opportunity to work with other students in a simulated healthcare environment, which helped me develop my teamwork and communication skills.

Skills and Qualities

Being a sterile processing technician requires more than just education and certification. You also need to have certain skills and qualities that will help you succeed in this field. For example, attention to detail is critical, as even a small mistake could lead to a patient getting an infection. Good communication skills are also important, as sterile processing technicians must be able to work with other healthcare professionals to ensure that patients receive the best possible care.

Working as a sterile processing technician can be physically demanding, so it is important to be in good physical shape. You should be able to stand for long periods of time, be able to lift heavy objects, and have good hand-eye coordination.

Continuing Education

Once you have become a sterile processing technician, it is important to continue your education and stay up-to-date with the latest developments in the field. This can include attending conferences, reading industry publications, and participating in professional organizations. By staying current with the latest trends and best practices, you can continue to provide high-quality care to patients.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for sterile processing technicians is positive, as the demand for healthcare services continues to grow. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of sterile processing technicians is projected to grow 8 percent from 2019 to 2029, much faster than the average for all occupations. This means that there will be plenty of job opportunities for qualified professionals in this field.

Personal Experience

When I first started my career as a sterile processing technician, I was nervous about the responsibility that came with the job. However, with the right education and training, I was able to gain the confidence I needed to be successful in this field.

One of the things that I enjoy most about being a sterile processing technician is the opportunity to work as part of a healthcare team. I feel like I am making a difference in the lives of patients by ensuring that their medical equipment is clean and safe to use.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for a sterile processing technician?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for sterile processing technicians was $36,280 in May 2020.

Q: How long does it take to become a certified sterile processing technician?

A: It typically takes six months to a year to complete a sterile processing technician training program and become certified.

Q: What are some of the biggest challenges of working as a sterile processing technician?

A: One of the biggest challenges is ensuring that medical equipment is properly cleaned and sterilized. This requires attention to detail and a thorough understanding of infection control procedures.

Q: What are some of the rewards of working as a sterile processing technician?

A: Sterile processing technicians play an important role in ensuring that patients receive safe and effective healthcare. By providing clean and sterilized medical equipment, they are helping to prevent infections and improve patient outcomes.

Conclusion of What Do You Need to Become a Sterile Processing Technician

Becoming a sterile processing technician can be a challenging but rewarding career. To be successful in this field, you will need to have the right education, certification, and skills. By following the steps outlined in this article and continuing your education, you can build a successful career as a sterile processing technician and make a positive impact on the lives of patients.
