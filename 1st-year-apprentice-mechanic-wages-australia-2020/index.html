1St Year Apprentice Mechanic Wages Australia 2020 What You Need To Know

Written by Eveline May 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an apprentice mechanic in Australia? While the profession can be rewarding, the starting wage for first-year apprentices can be a concern for many. In this post, we'll explore the average wage for first-year apprentice mechanics in Australia in 2020, as well as other important details you should know before starting your career.

As a first-year apprentice mechanic in Australia, it's important to be aware that your starting wage will be lower than that of a fully qualified mechanic. This can be a significant pain point for many, especially when considering the cost of living in some parts of the country. It's also important to note that the wage can vary depending on your location, the type of employer you work for, and the qualifications you already have.

The average starting wage for first-year apprentice mechanics in Australia in 2020 is around $13.50 per hour. This equates to around $500 per week, before tax. However, it's important to remember that this is just a starting point, and your wage will increase as you progress through your apprenticeship and gain more experience.

In summary, the starting wage for first-year apprentice mechanics in Australia in 2020 is around $13.50 per hour. While this may not seem like much, it's important to remember that this is just the beginning of your career, and your wages will increase as you gain more experience and qualifications.

What to Expect as a First Year Apprentice Mechanic

As a first-year apprentice mechanic, you can expect to learn a lot about the industry and gain practical experience. You'll work alongside qualified mechanics, learning the skills and knowledge required to become a fully qualified mechanic yourself. While the starting wage may be low, it's important to remember that you are investing in your future career and gaining valuable experience.

The Importance of Qualifications

While your starting wage as a first-year apprentice mechanic may be low, it's important to remember that qualifications can help you earn a higher wage in the future. By completing your apprenticeship and gaining additional qualifications, you can increase your earning potential and improve your career prospects.

Apprenticeship Incentives

There are also a range of incentives available to apprentices in Australia, which can help to reduce the financial burden of a low starting wage. For example, the Australian government offers incentives to employers who take on apprentices, including wage subsidies and support for training costs. Additionally, some employers may offer additional benefits, such as tools and uniforms, to help offset the cost of starting your apprenticeship.

How to Negotiate Your Wage

If you feel that your starting wage as a first-year apprentice mechanic is too low, you may be able to negotiate with your employer. However, it's important to approach this conversation in a professional manner and be prepared to demonstrate your value to the company. For example, you may be able to negotiate a higher wage if you have prior experience in a related field, or if you have completed relevant training or qualifications.
