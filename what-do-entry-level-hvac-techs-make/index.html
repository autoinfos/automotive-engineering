What Do Entry Level Hvac Techs Make

Written by Oliver Oct 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an HVAC technician? Do you want to know what kind of salary you can expect to earn as an entry-level technician? If so, keep reading to find out!

Many people are drawn to the HVAC industry because of the job stability and potential for career advancement. However, one of the biggest concerns for those considering a career in HVAC is the starting salary. It can be difficult to know what to expect, especially if you don't have any experience in the field.

The starting salary for an entry-level HVAC technician can vary depending on several factors, including location, experience, and education. However, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for HVAC technicians was $48,730 as of May 2020. This breaks down to an hourly wage of $23.43.

So, what does this mean for entry-level technicians? While starting salaries can vary, most entry-level HVAC technicians can expect to earn around $15-20 per hour. This can increase as you gain more experience and advance in your career.

My Personal Experience

When I first started my career as an HVAC technician, I was making around $16 per hour. While this wasn't a huge salary, I was grateful for the opportunity to gain valuable experience in the field. As I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased to around $25 per hour.

Factors That Affect Salary

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect the starting salary for an entry-level HVAC technician. One of the biggest factors is location. HVAC technicians in larger cities or areas with a higher cost of living may earn a higher salary than those in smaller towns or rural areas.

Education and certifications can also play a role in determining salary. Technicians with more education and certifications may be more likely to earn a higher salary than those without. Additionally, experience can be a major factor in determining salary. HVAC technicians with more experience may be able to negotiate a higher starting salary than those with no experience.

Education and Certification Requirements

While not all HVAC technician jobs require formal education, many employers prefer candidates with some level of education or certification. HVAC programs at technical schools or community colleges can provide valuable hands-on training and education in the field.

Additionally, certifications from organizations such as NATE (North American Technician Excellence) can demonstrate to employers that you have the necessary skills and knowledge to perform the job. These certifications can also lead to higher salaries and better job opportunities.

Negotiating Your Salary

If you're offered a job as an entry-level HVAC technician, it's important to remember that you can negotiate your salary. While you may not be able to negotiate a huge increase in salary, it's always worth asking for a higher wage if you feel that your experience or education warrants it.

Conclusion of What Do Entry Level HVAC Techs Make

Overall, the starting salary for an entry-level HVAC technician can vary depending on several factors. While the median annual salary for HVAC technicians is around $48,730, entry-level technicians can expect to earn around $15-20 per hour. Factors such as location, education, and experience can all play a role in determining salary. However, with dedication and hard work, HVAC technicians can earn a good salary and enjoy a stable career in the industry.

A: Experienced HVAC technicians can earn anywhere from $50,000 to $80,000 per year or more, depending on their location, education, and experience.
