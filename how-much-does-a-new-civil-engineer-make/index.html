How Much Does A New Civil Engineer Make

Written by Frank Aug 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in civil engineering but unsure about the salary? You're not alone. Many people want to know how much they can expect to make as a new civil engineer. In this post, we'll answer that question and provide additional information about the topic.

As a new civil engineer, you may have concerns about your earning potential. It's natural to wonder if your salary will be enough to cover your expenses and support your lifestyle. Additionally, you may be curious about the earning potential for civil engineers in different industries and locations.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for civil engineers was $87,060 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $56,540, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $144,560. However, it's important to note that these figures can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and industry.

In summary, a new civil engineer can expect to earn a median annual salary of $87,060. However, this figure can vary depending on various factors. Let's take a closer look at some of these factors.

Location Matters

The location of your job can have a significant impact on your salary. For example, civil engineers in metropolitan areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas. Additionally, some states have higher average salaries for civil engineers than others. For example, California has the highest average salary for civil engineers in the United States, while Indiana has the lowest.

Experience and Industry

Your experience level and the industry you work in can also affect your salary. Civil engineers with more experience tend to earn higher salaries than those who are just starting out. Additionally, civil engineers who work in certain industries, such as oil and gas extraction or architectural and engineering services, tend to earn more than those who work in other industries.

Education Level

Your education level can also play a role in your earning potential as a civil engineer. Generally, civil engineers with advanced degrees, such as a master's degree or a Ph.D., tend to earn higher salaries than those with only a bachelor's degree. However, it's important to note that obtaining an advanced degree can also be expensive, so it's important to weigh the potential benefits against the costs.

Negotiation Skills

Finally, your negotiation skills can also impact your salary as a civil engineer. When negotiating your salary, it's important to do your research and know your worth. Additionally, it's important to be confident and assertive during the negotiation process.

Personal Experience

When I was a new civil engineer, I was unsure about my earning potential. However, I quickly learned that location and industry can have a significant impact on your salary. I also found that negotiating my salary was key to earning what I felt I was worth.

Industry Insights

According to a recent survey conducted by the American Society of Civil Engineers, civil engineers working in the oil and gas extraction industry had the highest median salary, at $126,471. Those working in the waste management industry had the lowest median salary, at $74,000.

Location Insights

As mentioned earlier, location can also impact your salary as a civil engineer. According to the same survey, civil engineers working in California had the highest median salary, at $116,000. Those working in South Dakota had the lowest median salary, at $62,000.

Salary Negotiation Tips

When negotiating your salary as a civil engineer, it's important to do your research and know your worth. Additionally, it's important to be confident and assertive during the negotiation process. Here are some tips:

  • Research the average salary for civil engineers in your location and industry
  • Highlight your experience and education level
  • Be confident and assertive during the negotiation process
  • Consider negotiating other benefits, such as flexible work hours or additional vacation time

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a civil engineer make per hour?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the mean hourly wage for civil engineers as of May 2020 was $41.84.

Q: What skills do I need to be a civil engineer?

A: Some of the key skills for civil engineers include problem-solving, critical thinking, communication, and attention to detail.

Q: Do civil engineers make a lot of money?

A: Civil engineers can earn a good salary, with the median annual salary being $87,060 as of May 2020. However, salaries can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and industry.

Q: How much do entry-level civil engineers make?

A: According to Payscale, the average salary for an entry-level civil engineer is $60,000 per year.

Conclusion of How Much Does a New Civil Engineer Make

In conclusion, a new civil engineer can expect to earn a median annual salary of $87,060. However, this figure can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and industry. By doing your research, negotiating your salary, and developing in-demand skills, you can increase your earning potential as a civil engineer.
