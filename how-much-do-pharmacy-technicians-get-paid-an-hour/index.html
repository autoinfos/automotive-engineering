How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Get Paid An Hour

Written by Luffy Nov 03, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you considering becoming a pharmacy technician but unsure about how much you will get paid per hour? This is a common concern for many individuals looking to join the healthcare industry. Knowing the salary range can help you plan your finances and make informed decisions about your career path.

The average salary for pharmacy technicians varies depending on the state, location, and experience level. Many pharmacy technicians work part-time, which can affect their hourly wage. Additionally, some employers offer benefits such as healthcare and retirement plans that can increase the overall compensation package.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in the United States is $16.26. The lowest 10 percent earn less than $11.68 per hour, while the highest 10 percent earn more than $23.08 per hour. However, this data may not reflect the current market conditions or the specific situation in your area.

In summary, pharmacy technicians can expect to earn an average hourly wage of $16.26 in the United States. This salary can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and employer benefits.

Personal Experience with Pharmacy Technician Salary

As a certified pharmacy technician with three years of experience working at a retail pharmacy, I can attest to the salary range mentioned above. When I first started my job, I earned $12.50 per hour. However, after gaining more experience and taking on additional responsibilities, my hourly wage increased to $16.50 per hour.

Factors That Affect Pharmacy Technician Salary

Several factors can affect a pharmacy technician's salary, including:

Education and Certification

Pharmacy technicians who have completed a formal education or certification program may have an advantage in the job market and may be able to negotiate a higher salary. Additionally, some states require pharmacy technicians to be certified, which can lead to higher pay.

Experience

Pharmacy technicians with more experience may be able to earn a higher salary than those who are just starting out. As you gain more experience, you may also be able to take on additional responsibilities and earn a higher wage.

Benefits of Being a Pharmacy Technician

Aside from the hourly wage, there are several benefits to working as a pharmacy technician. For one, you get to work in the healthcare industry and help people who need medication. You also get to work in a stable and growing field with many job opportunities.

Question and Answer

Q: Can pharmacy technicians earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, pharmacy technicians who work more than 40 hours per week may be eligible for overtime pay.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement as a pharmacy technician?

A: Yes, pharmacy technicians can advance to roles such as lead technician, supervisor, or even pharmacy manager with additional education and experience.

Q: Do pharmacy technicians typically receive healthcare benefits?

A: Some employers offer healthcare benefits to pharmacy technicians, although this can vary depending on the employer and the employee's status (part-time vs. full-time).

Q: Is it possible to work as a pharmacy technician without formal education or certification?

A: Some employers may hire pharmacy technicians without formal education or certification, although this is becoming less common as more states require certification.

Conclusion of How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Get Paid an Hour

Pharmacy technicians can expect to earn an average hourly wage of $16.26 in the United States, although this can vary depending on several factors such as location and experience. Knowing the salary range can help you make informed decisions about your career path and plan your finances accordingly.
