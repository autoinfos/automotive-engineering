How Long Is Aviation Mechanic School

Written by Christine Sep 04, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you considering a career in aviation as a mechanic, but unsure of how long the training process will take? Look no further, as we break down the duration of aviation mechanic school and what you can expect during your time in training.

Many aspiring aviation mechanics are concerned about the time commitment required to complete their education and training. It's true that becoming a certified aviation mechanic does require a significant investment of time, but the rewards of your career in aviation can be well worth it.

The length of aviation mechanic school can vary depending on the program you choose and the type of certification you are seeking. The most common degree programs for aspiring aviation mechanics are associate's degrees, which typically take two years to complete. However, some programs offer accelerated options that can be completed in as little as 18 months.

My Personal Experience with Aviation Mechanic School

When I was first considering a career in aviation, I was concerned about the time commitment required to become a certified mechanic. However, I found that the hands-on training and classroom instruction provided by my program made the time investment well worth it. I completed my associate's degree in aviation mechanics in just under two years, and was able to secure a job as a certified mechanic shortly after graduation.

What to Expect During Aviation Mechanic School

During your time in aviation mechanic school, you can expect to receive a combination of classroom instruction and hands-on training. You'll learn the basics of aircraft mechanics, including how to read technical manuals, diagnose problems, and repair and maintain various aircraft systems.

The Benefits of an Associate's Degree in Aviation Mechanics

While it is possible to become a certified aviation mechanic without an associate's degree, completing a degree program can provide several benefits. In addition to providing a comprehensive education in aviation mechanics, an associate's degree can also open up additional career opportunities and provide a more competitive salary.

How to Choose the Right Aviation Mechanic School

When choosing an aviation mechanic school, it's important to consider factors such as the school's accreditation, program length, and hands-on training opportunities. It's also a good idea to research the job placement rates for graduates of the program.

FAQs About How Long is Aviation Mechanic School

Q: Can I become a certified aviation mechanic without completing an associate's degree program?

A: Yes, it is possible to become a certified aviation mechanic without completing an associate's degree program. However, completing a degree program can provide additional benefits and opportunities in your career.

Q: How long does it take to complete an accelerated aviation mechanic school program?

A: Accelerated aviation mechanic school programs can typically be completed in as little as 18 months.

Q: What kind of jobs can I expect to have as a certified aviation mechanic?

A: Certified aviation mechanics can work in a variety of settings, including airports, maintenance facilities, and manufacturing plants.

Q: What is the average salary for a certified aviation mechanic?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 as of May 2020.

Conclusion of How Long is Aviation Mechanic School

In conclusion, the length of aviation mechanic school can vary depending on the program you choose and the type of certification you are seeking. While becoming a certified aviation mechanic does require a significant investment of time, completing an associate's degree program can provide additional benefits and open up more career opportunities. With the right education and training, a career as an aviation mechanic can be both rewarding and fulfilling.
