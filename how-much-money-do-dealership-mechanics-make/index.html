How Much Money Do Dealership Mechanics Make

Written by Luffy Jun 17, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you thinking about becoming a dealership mechanic but wondering how much money you can make? The reality is that this profession can be financially rewarding for those who are willing to put in the work. However, there are some pain points to consider when it comes to how much money do dealership mechanics make.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics, including those who work at dealerships, was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, the highest 10 percent earned more than $70,000 per year, and the lowest 10 percent earned less than $25,000 per year.

In order to make a good salary as a dealership mechanic, you need to have the right training and experience. Most employers prefer to hire candidates who have completed formal training programs and earned certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). Additionally, having experience with specific makes and models of vehicles can increase your earning potential.

In summary, the amount of money you can make as a dealership mechanic largely depends on your level of training, experience, and certification. It's also important to note that some dealerships offer bonuses and incentives based on performance, which can increase your overall earnings.

How Experience Impacts Your Salary as a Dealership Mechanic

When I first started working as a dealership mechanic, my salary was on the lower end of the spectrum. However, as I gained more experience working on a variety of vehicles, my earning potential increased. Now, after several years in the industry, I'm making well over the median annual salary for this profession.

The reality is that in the world of dealership mechanics, experience is key. The more hands-on experience you have with different types of vehicles and repairs, the more valuable you become to your employer. This can lead to higher salaries, better job security, and more opportunities for advancement.

How Certification Impacts Your Salary as a Dealership Mechanic

Earning certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) is a great way to increase your earning potential as a dealership mechanic. ASE certification demonstrates to employers and customers that you have the knowledge and skills needed to perform high-quality repairs and services.

Most dealerships prefer to hire mechanics who have ASE certification in one or more areas of specialization, such as engine repair, brakes, or electrical systems. Additionally, mechanics who hold multiple ASE certifications are often eligible for higher salaries and more advanced positions within the dealership.

The Role of Bonuses and Incentives in Dealership Mechanic Salaries

In addition to base salaries, many dealerships offer bonuses and incentives to their mechanics based on performance. These can include bonuses for completing repairs quickly and accurately, selling additional services to customers, or achieving high customer satisfaction ratings.

These bonuses and incentives can add up quickly, and can make a significant difference in your overall earnings as a dealership mechanic. However, it's important to note that they are often tied to specific performance metrics, so it's important to be aware of these metrics and work towards meeting them in order to maximize your earning potential.

Negotiating Your Salary as a Dealership Mechanic

If you're looking to maximize your earning potential as a dealership mechanic, it's important to be willing to negotiate your salary. Before beginning a job, do your research and find out what the average salary is for mechanics in your area with similar levels of experience and certification.

Additionally, be prepared to demonstrate your value to potential employers by highlighting your experience, certification, and any other relevant skills or achievements. By negotiating your salary effectively, you can ensure that you're being paid what you're worth as a dealership mechanic.

Question and Answer

A: Experience is a key factor in determining a dealership mechanic's salary. The more hands-on experience a mechanic has with different types of vehicles and repairs, the more valuable they become to their employer, which can lead to higher salaries and more opportunities for advancement.

Q: How does ASE certification impact a dealership mechanic's salary?
A: Earning certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) is a great way to increase a dealership mechanic's earning potential. Mechanics who hold ASE certifications in one or more areas of specialization are often eligible for higher salaries and more advanced positions within the dealership.

Q: Can bonuses and incentives impact a dealership mechanic's salary?
A: Yes, many dealerships offer bonuses and incentives to mechanics based on performance. These can make a significant difference in a mechanic's overall earnings, but are often tied to specific performance metrics.

Q: Is it possible to negotiate your salary as a dealership mechanic?
A: Yes, it's important to be willing to negotiate your salary as a dealership mechanic. Do your research and find out what the average salary is for mechanics in your area with similar levels of experience and certification, and be prepared to demonstrate your value to potential employers.

Conclusion of How Much Money Do Dealership Mechanics Make

While the amount of money you can make as a dealership mechanic can vary based on a number of factors, including experience, certification, and performance, it is possible to earn a good salary in this profession. By investing in your training and gaining hands-on experience with different types of vehicles and repairs, you can increase your earning potential and achieve financial stability in this rewarding career.
