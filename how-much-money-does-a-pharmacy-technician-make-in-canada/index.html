How Much Money Does A Pharmacy Technician Make In Canada

Written by William Sep 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician in Canada? If so, you may be wondering how much money you can expect to make in this field. While salary isn't the only factor to consider when choosing a career, it's certainly an important one. In this post, we'll explore the average salary of a pharmacy technician in Canada and what factors can impact earning potential.

The average salary for a pharmacy technician in Canada is around $40,000 to $50,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors such as experience, location, and employer. For example, pharmacy technicians working in urban areas may earn more than those in rural areas. Additionally, those with more years of experience or additional certifications may be able to command a higher salary.

In summary, while the average salary for a pharmacy technician in Canada is around $40,000 to $50,000 per year, this can vary depending on several factors such as location, experience, and certifications.

Factors That Impact a Pharmacy Technician's Salary

Location and experience are two of the biggest factors that can impact a pharmacy technician's salary. For example, pharmacy technicians working in urban areas may earn more than those in rural areas. Additionally, those with more years of experience or additional certifications may be able to command a higher salary.

I personally have been working as a pharmacy technician in Toronto for the past 5 years and have seen my salary increase as I gained more experience and became certified. My starting salary was around $38,000 per year, but now I make around $55,000 per year.

Cost of Living and Salary Expectations

It's important to consider the cost of living in your area when evaluating salary expectations. For example, living in a major city such as Toronto or Vancouver may be more expensive than living in a smaller town. It's important to factor in the cost of housing, transportation, and other expenses when evaluating salary expectations.

Additional Certifications and Education

Obtaining additional certifications or education can also impact a pharmacy technician's salary. For example, becoming a Certified Pharmacy Technician (CPhT) can increase earning potential. Additionally, pursuing further education such as a Bachelor's degree in Pharmacy can open up opportunities for higher-paying positions such as a pharmacist.

How to Negotiate Your Salary as a Pharmacy Technician

If you're looking to negotiate your salary as a pharmacy technician, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, research the average salary for your position in your area. This will give you an idea of what to expect and what is reasonable to ask for. Additionally, highlight your experience and any additional certifications or education you may have. Finally, be prepared to negotiate and have an idea of what you're willing to accept.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the average salary for a pharmacy technician in Canada is around $40,000 to $50,000 per year, but this can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and certifications. It's important to consider the cost of living in your area and to pursue additional certifications or education to increase earning potential. When negotiating your salary, be prepared to research and highlight your experience and qualifications.
