Are you a project engineer looking to work in Canada or are you already working in Canada and curious about the average salary of a project engineer? If you answered yes, then this article is for you. Keep reading to find out more about the average salary of project engineers in Canada.

As a project engineer, it can be challenging to negotiate your salary, especially if you are new to the industry or the country. Factors such as your level of experience, location, and the industry you work in can all affect your salary. However, it is crucial to know the average salary of project engineers in Canada to ensure that you are not being underpaid and to negotiate a fair salary.

According to the latest data from the Government of Canada, the average salary of a project engineer in Canada is around $85,000 per year. However, this salary can range from $58,000 to $130,000 depending on your experience, industry, and location. It is essential to note that this salary is before taxes, and the cost of living in Canada can be quite high in some areas.

In conclusion, the average salary of project engineers in Canada is around $85,000 per year, depending on your level of experience, industry, and location. It is crucial to know this information to ensure that you negotiate a fair salary and do not get underpaid.

Factors That Affect the Average Salary of a Project Engineer in Canada

Several factors can affect the average salary of a project engineer in Canada. One of the significant factors is your level of experience. Generally, the more experience you have, the higher your salary will be. Additionally, the industry you work in can affect your salary. For example, project engineers in the oil and gas industry tend to have higher salaries than those in the construction industry. Finally, your location can also affect your salary. Salaries tend to be higher in cities such as Toronto and Vancouver compared to smaller cities and towns.
