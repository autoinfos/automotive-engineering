How Much Do Dental Technicians Make An Hour

Written by Frank Jun 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you passionate about dentistry and considering a career as a dental technician? One important factor to consider before embarking on this career path is the salary range. How much do dental technicians make an hour? Let's explore this topic in more detail.

One common pain point for individuals considering a career as a dental technician is the fear of not earning enough money to support themselves or their family. This fear is valid, especially if you live in an expensive city or have high living expenses. It's important to have a clear understanding of the salary range for dental technicians to make an informed decision about your career.

The average hourly wage for dental technicians in the United States is $21.74 per hour, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, the salary range can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and education. Entry-level dental technicians can expect to earn around $13.54 per hour, while experienced technicians can earn up to $36.02 per hour.

In conclusion, dental technicians can earn a decent hourly wage, with the potential for higher earnings with experience and education. It's important to research the salary range in your area and make an informed decision about your career path.

Understanding Dental Technician Salaries

When I first considered a career as a dental technician, I was unsure about the salary range and whether it would be enough to support myself. However, after doing some research, I found that the average hourly wage for dental technicians is around $21.74 per hour. This can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and education. For example, dental technicians in California typically earn a higher hourly wage than those in other states due to the high cost of living.

In addition to location, experience and education can also impact a dental technician's salary range. As you gain more experience and knowledge in the field, you can expect to earn a higher hourly wage. This is why continuing education and professional development are important for dental technicians who want to increase their earning potential.

My Personal Experience As A Dental Technician

As a dental technician with five years of experience, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary greatly depending on location and experience. When I first started my career in a small town, I earned around $15 per hour. However, after moving to a larger city and gaining more experience, I now earn around $25 per hour.

One thing I've learned is that it's important to negotiate your salary and advocate for yourself. When I was offered a job in a new city, I asked for a higher hourly wage based on my experience and the cost of living in the area. This negotiation resulted in a higher starting salary and set the tone for future salary negotiations.

Factors That Impact Dental Technician Salaries

In addition to location, experience, and education, there are other factors that can impact a dental technician's salary range. One of these factors is the type of employer. Dental technicians who work for private practices or dental laboratories may earn a different hourly wage than those who work for government agencies or educational institutions.

Another factor is the demand for dental technicians in your area. If there are few job openings for dental technicians, the hourly wage may be lower due to the lack of competition. On the other hand, if there is a high demand for dental technicians in your area, you may be able to negotiate a higher hourly wage.

Tips For Negotiating Your Dental Technician Salary

If you're considering a career as a dental technician or are already working in the field, it's important to know how to negotiate your salary. Here are some tips:

  • Research the average hourly wage for dental technicians in your area
  • Highlight your experience and education during salary negotiations
  • Consider negotiating for additional benefits such as healthcare or vacation time
  • Be confident and assertive during salary negotiations
