What Does A Technician Do At A Dealership

Written by Frank Oct 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you curious about what happens behind the scenes when you take your car to a dealership for service? Do you wonder what a technician does to keep your vehicle running smoothly? In this blog post, we'll explore the world of dealership technicians and give you a behind-the-scenes look at their day-to-day work.

When you're having car trouble, it can be frustrating and stressful. You want to get your vehicle fixed as quickly and efficiently as possible, but you may not know what's wrong or how to fix it. That's where dealership technicians come in. They're trained professionals who specialize in diagnosing and repairing all types of vehicles, from cars to trucks to SUVs.

A technician at a dealership is responsible for a wide range of tasks related to vehicle maintenance and repair. They perform routine maintenance services like oil changes and tire rotations, diagnose and repair mechanical and electrical issues, and install new parts and accessories. They use specialized tools and equipment to diagnose problems and make repairs, and they work closely with service advisors to communicate with customers about the work that needs to be done.

In summary, a technician at a dealership is a skilled professional who is responsible for diagnosing and repairing all types of vehicles. They perform routine maintenance services, diagnose and repair mechanical and electrical issues, and install new parts and accessories. They work closely with service advisors to communicate with customers about the work that needs to be done.

What Does a Technician Do at a Dealership - Explained

As a former dealership technician, I can tell you firsthand that the job is both challenging and rewarding. Every day is different, and you never know what kind of vehicle you'll be working on next. Some days, you might be changing oil and rotating tires on a series of cars, while other days you might be diagnosing complex electrical issues on a luxury SUV.

One of the most important aspects of the job is diagnosing problems correctly. A good technician needs to be able to identify the root cause of a problem and come up with a plan to fix it. This requires a combination of knowledge, experience, and problem-solving skills. It's not always easy, but it's incredibly satisfying when you're able to figure out what's wrong and get a vehicle back on the road.

What Does a Technician Do at a Dealership - Training and Certifications

To become a dealership technician, you need to have a high school diploma or equivalent, and you'll typically need to complete a formal training program. Many dealerships offer on-the-job training programs for new hires, but you can also attend a vocational school or community college to get certified.

Once you're hired as a technician, you'll need to continue your education and training to keep up with new technologies and advancements in the field. Many manufacturers offer certification programs for their vehicles, and technicians can earn various levels of certification by completing training and passing exams. These certifications can be a valuable asset for technicians, as they demonstrate expertise and knowledge in specific areas.

What Does a Technician Do at a Dealership - Tools and Equipment

To do their job effectively, technicians need access to specialized tools and equipment. Dealerships typically provide technicians with everything they need to perform their work, including diagnostic tools, lifts, air compressors, and more. Technicians also need to be proficient in using computer software programs to diagnose and repair vehicles.

What Does a Technician Do at a Dealership - Common Questions

Q: How long does it take for a technician to diagnose a problem?

A: It depends on the complexity of the problem. Some issues can be diagnosed quickly, while others may take several hours or even days to diagnose.

Q: Can a technician work on any type of vehicle?

A: Dealership technicians are trained to work on specific makes and models of vehicles. They typically specialize in one or a few brands, so they can develop expertise in those vehicles.

Q: How often should I take my vehicle in for maintenance?

A: It depends on the make and model of your vehicle, as well as your driving habits. Your vehicle's owner's manual will have recommended maintenance schedules, and your service advisor can help you determine the best schedule for your vehicle.

Q: How much does it cost to have a vehicle serviced at a dealership?

A: The cost of service varies depending on the type of service and the make and model of your vehicle. Your service advisor can provide you with an estimate before any work is done.

Conclusion of What Does a Technician Do at a Dealership

Dealership technicians play a crucial role in keeping our vehicles running smoothly. They're skilled professionals who are trained to diagnose and repair all types of vehicles, and they use specialized tools and equipment to get the job done right. Whether you need routine maintenance or complex repairs, a dealership technician can help you get back on the road with confidence.
