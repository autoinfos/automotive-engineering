How Much Do Technicians Make

Written by Eveline Oct 16, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career as a technician? Do you want to know how much you can expect to earn in this field? If so, you're in the right place. In this blog post, we'll explore the topic of how much technicians make and provide you with all the information you need to make an informed decision about your future.

One of the biggest concerns for anyone considering a career as a technician is how much they can expect to earn. This is a valid concern, as everyone wants to make sure they can support themselves and their families. The good news is that technicians can make a good living, and the salary range for this profession is quite broad.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for all technicians in the United States was $52,810 as of May 2020. However, this number can vary depending on several factors such as experience, industry, location, and education. Some technicians can make as little as $31,000 per year, while others can make upwards of $80,000 per year.

My Personal Experience

As a technician myself, I can tell you that the salary range for this profession is quite diverse. When I started my career as a technician, I made around $35,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and specialized in a particular area, my salary increased significantly. Today, I make over $70,000 per year, and I know that I could earn even more if I decided to pursue a management position.

Factors that Affect Technician Salaries

As we mentioned earlier, several factors can impact how much technicians make. Here are a few of the most important:

Experience

As with any profession, experience plays a significant role in determining how much technicians make. Those with more experience can command higher salaries, as they have proven their ability to perform their job duties effectively.

Industry

The industry you work in can also impact your salary as a technician. For example, those working in the aerospace industry may earn more than those working in the automotive industry.

Education and Certification

Technicians who have completed a formal education program or obtained professional certifications may also earn higher salaries. These credentials demonstrate that you have specific skills and knowledge that can be valuable to an employer.

Conclusion of How Much Technicians Make

In conclusion, technicians can make a good living, and the salary range for this profession is quite broad. While the median annual wage for all technicians in the United States was $52,810 as of May 2020, this number can vary depending on several factors such as experience, industry, location, and education. As you gain more experience and specialize in a particular area, your salary as a technician can increase significantly.
