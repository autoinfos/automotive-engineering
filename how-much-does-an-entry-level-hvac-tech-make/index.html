How Much Does An Entry Level Hvac Tech Make

Written by Luffy Aug 16, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career as an HVAC technician? One of the most common questions people have is how much they can expect to make as an entry-level technician. If you're curious about how much an entry-level HVAC tech can make, keep reading.

It's no secret that HVAC technicians are in high demand, and that demand is only expected to grow in the coming years. As a result, many people are considering a career in this field. However, before making the decision to pursue this career path, it's important to understand the earning potential.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for HVAC technicians was $48,730 in May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $31,910, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $77,920. However, it's important to note that these figures represent the median for all HVAC technicians, not just entry-level technicians.

My Personal Experience

When I started out as an entry-level HVAC technician, I earned around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and became more skilled, my earnings increased. After a few years in the field, I was earning close to the median annual wage for HVAC technicians.

Factors That Affect Earnings

There are several factors that can affect how much an entry-level HVAC technician can expect to earn. Some of the most significant factors include:

Education and Training

While a formal education is not always required to become an HVAC technician, completing a training program or apprenticeship can help increase earnings potential. Employers may be more likely to hire someone with formal training or certification, and those with specialized skills, such as experience working with solar panels or other renewable energy systems, may command higher salaries.

Location

Like many other professions, earnings for HVAC technicians can vary depending on the location. Technicians working in larger cities or regions with a high cost of living may earn more than those working in smaller towns or rural areas.

Job Outlook

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of HVAC technicians is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations. This growth is expected to be driven by an increasing emphasis on energy efficiency and environmental sustainability, as well as the need to replace older, outdated HVAC systems.

Question and Answer

Q: Can an entry-level HVAC technician earn more than the median annual wage?

A: Yes, it's possible. Factors such as location, education, and experience can all affect earnings potential.

Q: Do HVAC technicians typically work full-time or part-time?

A: Most HVAC technicians work full-time, although some may work evenings or weekends to accommodate customer needs.

Q: Is there room for advancement in the HVAC field?

A: Yes, HVAC technicians can advance to become supervisors, managers, or even business owners. Additional education and training can help facilitate career advancement.

Q: Are there any risks associated with working as an HVAC technician?

A: Yes, HVAC technicians may be exposed to hazards such as electrical shock, burns, and respiratory problems. However, with proper training and safety measures, these risks can be mitigated.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Entry Level HVAC Tech Make

If you're considering a career as an entry-level HVAC technician, it's important to understand the earning potential of this field. While the median annual wage for HVAC technicians is around $48,730, entry-level technicians may earn less than this amount. However, with experience, education, and specialized skills, it's possible to increase earnings potential in this growing field.
