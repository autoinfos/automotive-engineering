How Much Do Medical Assistants Make In Australia

Written by Eveline Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're considering becoming a medical assistant in Australia, one of the first things you'll want to know is how much you can expect to earn. Understanding the salary range for medical assistants can help you make an informed decision about whether this career is right for you.

According to recent statistics, the average salary for a medical assistant in Australia is around $45,000 to $55,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a variety of factors, including your level of experience, the type of medical facility you work in, and your location.

If you're just starting out in your career as a medical assistant, you can expect to earn on the lower end of the scale. As you gain more experience and take on additional responsibilities, your salary may increase. Additionally, medical assistants who work in specialized areas, such as dermatology or pediatrics, may earn higher salaries than those who work in general practices.

In conclusion, while the salary range for medical assistants in Australia may not be as high as some other healthcare professions, it can still provide a stable and fulfilling career path. By taking into account your experience level, location, and area of specialization, you can determine whether this career is right for you.

Factors That Affect Medical Assistant Salaries in Australia

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much a medical assistant earns in Australia. Some of the most significant factors include:

Experience Level

Medical assistants who are just starting out in their careers can expect to earn on the lower end of the salary scale, while those with more experience may earn higher salaries. This is because experienced medical assistants are often able to take on more responsibilities and handle more complex tasks.

Type of Medical Facility

Medical assistants who work in specialized facilities, such as dermatology clinics or pediatric practices, may earn higher salaries than those who work in general practices. This is because specialized facilities often require more specialized skills and knowledge.
