Question and Answer

Q: What should I look for when test driving a car?

A: When test driving a car, it's important to check for any unusual noises or vibrations, test the brakes and acceleration, and assess the overall comfort and handling of the vehicle.

Q: Should I buy a new or used car?

A: This ultimately depends on your personal preferences and budget. New cars generally come with a higher price tag but offer the latest features and technology, while used cars are more affordable but may have higher mileage and wear and tear.

Q: How can I finance a car purchase?

A: Car dealerships often offer financing options, allowing you to make monthly payments on your vehicle. Alternatively, you can secure a car loan from a bank or credit union.

Q: How often should I get my car serviced?

A: It's recommended to get your car serviced every 6 months or 10,000 km, whichever comes first. Regular maintenance can help prevent costly repairs down the line.
