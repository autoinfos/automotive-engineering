How Much Does A Mechanic At A Dealership Make A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Aug 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a mechanic at a car dealership? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make in this career path. While there is no one-size-fits-all answer, there are several factors that can impact a dealership mechanic's salary. In this article, we'll explore the average salary range for dealership mechanics and what factors can affect your earnings.

As with any career, it's important to understand the potential pain points before deciding if it's the right fit for you. For dealership mechanics, these pain points may include long hours, working on weekends, and dealing with irate customers who are unhappy with their car repairs. Additionally, some dealerships may have a high-pressure sales culture that can make the job more stressful than other mechanic positions.

So, how much can you expect to make as a mechanic at a dealership? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, the salary range for dealership mechanics specifically can vary based on factors such as experience level, location, and the dealership's size and reputation. Some dealership mechanics may earn closer to $30,000 per year, while others with more experience may earn upwards of $70,000 or more.

In summary, becoming a mechanic at a dealership can be a lucrative career path with the potential to earn a good salary. However, it's important to understand the potential pain points and factors that can impact your earnings before deciding if it's the right fit for you.

Factors That Can Impact a Dealership Mechanic's Salary

Experience level is one of the biggest factors that can impact a dealership mechanic's salary. Those who are just starting out in the field may earn closer to the lower end of the salary range, while those with several years of experience may earn closer to the higher end of the range. Additionally, location can play a role in salary, as mechanics in more expensive cities may earn higher salaries to account for the higher cost of living.

Personal Experience: My Journey as a Dealership Mechanic

After completing my automotive technology degree, I landed my first job as a mechanic at a car dealership. While the job came with its challenges, such as dealing with unhappy customers and working on weekends, I found it to be a rewarding career path. I started out earning around $35,000 per year, but after several years of experience and moving up in the ranks, I was able to earn closer to $60,000 per year.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as a Dealership Mechanic

One way to increase your earning potential as a dealership mechanic is to specialize in a particular area, such as diesel engines or hybrid vehicles. Additionally, continuing your education and obtaining certifications can show employers that you are committed to staying up-to-date with the latest technology and advancements in the field. Finally, networking and building relationships with other mechanics and industry professionals can lead to job opportunities and higher-paying positions.

The Future of Dealership Mechanics

As cars become more advanced and rely more heavily on technology, the role of a dealership mechanic is likely to become even more important. This means that there will continue to be a demand for skilled mechanics who are able to diagnose and repair complex issues. However, it also means that dealership mechanics may need to continue learning and adapting to new technology in order to stay competitive and relevant in the field.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I earn more as a dealership mechanic than an independent mechanic?

A: It's possible to earn a higher salary as a dealership mechanic, but it can depend on factors such as experience level and location.

Q: Is it necessary to have a degree to become a dealership mechanic?

A: While a formal degree is not always required, many employers prefer to hire mechanics who have completed a formal training program or apprenticeship.

Q: What skills are important for a dealership mechanic to have?

A: Important skills for a dealership mechanic include strong problem-solving skills, attention to detail, and the ability to work well under pressure.

Q: How can I negotiate a higher salary as a dealership mechanic?

A: Negotiating a higher salary may be possible if you have specialized skills or certifications, or if you have a track record of excellent performance and customer satisfaction.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Mechanic at a Dealership Make

While the salary range for dealership mechanics can vary based on several factors, it's possible to earn a good living working in this field. By understanding the potential pain points, factors that can impact your salary, and ways to increase your earning potential, you can make an informed decision about whether becoming a dealership mechanic is the right career path for you.
