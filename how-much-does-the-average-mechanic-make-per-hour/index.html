How Much Does The Average Mechanic Make Per Hour

Written by Daniel Jun 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you are considering a career as a mechanic, or you are currently working in the field, you may be wondering how much you can make per hour. The answer to this question can depend on a variety of factors, including your experience, education, and location. In this post, we will explore the average hourly wage for mechanics and provide insight into what you can expect to earn. As a mechanic, one of the most significant pain points is the struggle to earn a stable income. Many mechanics work in shops that offer commission-based pay, which can be unpredictable and fluctuate based on the number of jobs that come in. Additionally, the industry can be highly competitive, making it challenging to secure steady employment. The average hourly wage for mechanics in the United States is $19.99, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this number can vary significantly based on a variety of factors, including location, experience, and education. In some areas, mechanics can earn as little as $12 per hour, while in others, they can make upwards of $30 per hour. In conclusion, the average mechanic can expect to make around $20 per hour, although this number can vary significantly based on a variety of factors. If you are considering a career as a mechanic, it is essential to research wages in your area and factor in the cost of living and competition. With the right education, experience, and work ethic, you can earn a stable and rewarding income in this field.

What Factors Affect How Much a Mechanic Makes?

As mentioned, several factors can impact how much a mechanic can make per hour. The most significant factors include experience, education, location, and job type. When it comes to experience, mechanics who have been in the field for longer and have more specialized skills can typically command higher wages. Additionally, mechanics with formal education, such as an associate's degree in automotive technology, can often earn more than those who have only completed on-the-job training. Location is also a significant factor in determining how much a mechanic can make. In areas with a higher cost of living, such as major cities, mechanics can expect to earn more than those in less expensive areas. Additionally, some states have higher minimum wage requirements, which can impact how much a mechanic makes. Finally, the type of job a mechanic has can also impact their wages. Mechanics who work for dealerships or specialty repair shops may earn more than those who work at general repair shops.

My Experience as a Mechanic

As a mechanic with over ten years of experience, I can attest to the fact that wages in this field can be unpredictable. When I first started out, I was working in a small repair shop where I earned a commission-based wage. Some weeks, I would earn a substantial amount of money, while other weeks, I would barely make enough to cover my bills. Over time, I gained more experience and started working for a dealership, where I earned a higher hourly wage. While the work was more specialized and challenging, I found that I was earning a more stable income and had room for growth within the company.

How Can Mechanics Increase Their Earnings?

If you are a mechanic looking to increase your earnings, there are several steps you can take. First, consider pursuing formal education in automotive technology, which can help you gain specialized skills and increase your earning potential. Additionally, consider seeking out employment at a dealership or specialty repair shop, where you may be able to earn a higher wage. Finally, consider starting your own business, which can allow you to set your own rates and take on more clients.

How Does the Future Look for Mechanics?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow six percent from 2016 to 2026, about as fast as the average for all occupations. As vehicles continue to become more complex, there will be a continued demand for skilled mechanics who can diagnose and repair these vehicles.

How to Negotiate Your Wage as a Mechanic

If you are a mechanic who feels that they are being underpaid, it is essential to advocate for yourself and negotiate your wage. First, research wages in your area and come prepared with data to support your request for a higher wage. Additionally, emphasize your experience and any specialized skills you have developed. Finally, be open to compromise and be willing to discuss alternative solutions, such as commission-based pay or bonuses for reaching certain milestones.

With the right skills and work ethic, you can enjoy a stable and rewarding career in this field.
