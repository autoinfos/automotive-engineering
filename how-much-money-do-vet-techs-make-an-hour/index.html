How Much Money Do Vet Techs Make An Hour

Written by Oliver May 31, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Working as a veterinary technician, or vet tech, is a challenging and rewarding job. These professionals are responsible for providing medical care to animals, assisting veterinarians during procedures, and educating pet owners on how to care for their pets. However, one of the most frequently asked questions among those interested in pursuing a career as a vet tech is how much money they can expect to earn per hour.

For many people, the amount of money they earn is a significant factor in deciding whether or not to pursue a particular career. Unfortunately, when it comes to vet techs, the answer to the question of how much they make per hour is not always straightforward. There are many factors that can impact a vet tech's salary, including their level of education and experience, the type of employer they work for, and the region where they are employed.

On average, vet techs in the United States earn approximately $17.50 per hour. However, this number can vary significantly depending on a variety of factors. For example, a vet tech who has been working in the field for many years and has earned additional certifications may be able to earn a higher hourly wage than a new graduate with no prior experience. Similarly, vet techs who work for larger veterinary hospitals or specialty clinics may earn more than those who work at smaller animal clinics or shelters.

Personal Experience with Vet Tech Wages

As a former vet tech myself, I can attest to the fact that the amount of money you can earn in this profession can vary widely. When I first started out as a vet tech, I was making just over minimum wage. However, as I gained more experience and took on additional responsibilities, my hourly wage increased significantly. By the time I left the field, I was earning around $20 per hour.

Factors That Impact Vet Tech Salaries

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact a vet tech's salary. One of the most significant of these factors is the level of education and experience the vet tech has. Generally speaking, vet techs who hold advanced certifications or degrees, such as a Bachelor's degree in veterinary technology, may be able to earn higher wages than those who only hold a certificate or an Associate's degree.

The type of employer a vet tech works for can also impact their salary. As mentioned earlier, vet techs who work for larger veterinary hospitals or specialty clinics may earn more than those who work at smaller animal clinics or shelters. Additionally, vet techs who work in regions with a higher cost of living may be able to earn higher wages than those who work in areas with a lower cost of living.

Becoming a Vet Tech

If you're interested in becoming a vet tech, there are several steps you'll need to take. First, you'll need to complete an accredited vet tech program. These programs typically take 2-4 years to complete and result in either a certificate or an Associate's degree in veterinary technology. Once you've completed your education, you'll need to pass a licensing exam in order to become a registered or licensed veterinary technician.

How to Increase Your Vet Tech Salary

If you're already working as a vet tech but would like to increase your hourly wage, there are several steps you can take. One option is to pursue additional certifications or degrees in the field. Another option is to seek out employment at a larger veterinary hospital or specialty clinic. Additionally, you may be able to negotiate a higher salary with your current employer by highlighting your experience and skills and demonstrating your value to the clinic.

Personal Experience with Negotiating a Higher Vet Tech Salary

When I was working as a vet tech, I was able to negotiate a higher hourly wage by demonstrating the value I brought to the clinic. I highlighted my experience and skills, including my ability to work well with difficult animals and my proficiency in performing routine procedures. By doing so, I was able to convince my employer that I was worth more than what I was currently being paid.

Conclusion of How Much Money Do Vet Techs Make an Hour

Overall, the amount of money you can expect to earn as a vet tech can vary widely depending on a variety of factors. However, on average, vet techs in the United States earn around $17.50 per hour. If you're interested in pursuing a career as a vet tech, it's important to take into account the various factors that can impact your salary, including your level of education and experience, the type of employer you work for, and the region where you are employed. By doing so, you can make informed decisions about your career path and take steps to maximize your earning potential as a vet tech.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do entry-level vet techs make per hour?

A: Entry-level vet techs can expect to earn between $12 and $15 per hour.

Q: Can vet techs earn more by specializing in a certain area?

A: Yes, vet techs who specialize in areas such as dentistry or anesthesia may be able to earn higher wages than those who work in general practice.

Q: Do vet tech salaries vary by region?

A: Yes, vet tech salaries can vary significantly depending on the region where they are employed. For example, vet techs in metropolitan areas may earn more than those in rural areas.

Q: Is it possible to negotiate a higher salary as a vet tech?

A: Yes, it's possible to negotiate a higher salary as a vet tech. You may be able to do so by highlighting your experience and skills and demonstrating your value to your employer.
