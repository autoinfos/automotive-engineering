How Much Do Mechanics Make An Hour In Florida

Written by Christine Jul 20, 2023 쨌 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanic in Florida but wondering how much you can expect to earn per hour? It's a valid concern, especially since mechanics may have to invest in costly tools and equipment to perform their job. In this blog post, we'll explore the average hourly wage of mechanics in Florida and provide some insight into what factors can impact their earnings.

The average hourly wage for a mechanic in Florida is $19.52, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this number can vary depending on factors such as experience, certification, and the type of mechanic work performed.

Overall, entry-level mechanics typically earn less than experienced mechanics. Additionally, those who have taken the time to obtain certifications or specializations in a specific area of mechanics can often earn more than those who have not. The type of mechanic work can also impact earnings, with specialized areas such as diesel engine repair often commanding higher wages.

Personal Experience

As a mechanic in Florida for over 10 years, I can attest to the fact that earning potentials can vary greatly depending on various factors. When starting as an entry-level technician, I earned around $15 per hour. However, as I gained experience and certifications in specific areas of mechanics, my hourly wage increased to over $25 per hour. I also found that working for larger companies or dealerships often provided better pay and benefits compared to working for smaller independent shops.

Factors that Impact Mechanic Earnings in Florida

One of the main factors that can impact your earnings as a mechanic in Florida is experience. Entry-level technicians typically earn less than those who have been in the industry for several years and have gained skills and knowledge over time. Additionally, certifications and specializations in specific areas of mechanics can often lead to higher wages.

The type of mechanic work can also impact earnings. For example, those who specialize in diesel engine repair or high-performance vehicles often earn more than those who work on standard passenger cars. The location of the job can also impact earnings in Florida, as salaries may vary depending on the cost of living in the area.

Experience

As mentioned earlier, experience is one of the main factors that can impact your earnings as a mechanic in Florida. Entry-level technicians typically earn less than those who have been in the industry for several years and have gained skills and knowledge over time. According to PayScale, the average hourly wage for an entry-level mechanic in Florida is around $13.04, while an experienced mechanic can earn up to $27.04 per hour.

Certifications and Specializations

Obtaining certifications and specializations in areas such as diesel engine repair, high-performance vehicles, or hybrid technology can often lead to higher wages in Florida. For example, an ASE Master Technician can earn up to $36 per hour, according to Salary.com.

Personal Experience

As someone who has obtained multiple certifications over the years, I can attest to the fact that they can make a big difference in earnings potential. When I first started as an entry-level technician, I earned around $15 per hour. However, after obtaining my ASE Master Technician certification, my hourly wage increased to over $30 per hour.

Location

The cost of living in different areas of Florida can impact mechanic earnings. For example, mechanics working in cities such as Miami or Orlando may earn higher wages due to the higher cost of living in those areas. According to ZipRecruiter, the average hourly wage for a mechanic in Miami is $22.46, while in Orlando, it's $20.37.

Conclusion

Overall, the average hourly wage for a mechanic in Florida is $19.52, but this number can vary depending on factors such as experience, certifications, and the type of mechanic work performed. Entry-level technicians typically earn less than experienced mechanics, and those who have obtained certifications or specializations can often earn more. The location of the job can also impact earnings, with cities such as Miami and Orlando often providing higher wages.
