Are you interested in becoming a pharmacy technician but unsure if you need to be certified? It's a common question among those considering the pharmacy technician career path. In this blog post, we will discuss the importance of certification and the benefits it brings to your career.

Pain Points of Not Being Certified

Without certification, you may face limited job opportunities, lower pay, and fewer benefits. In many states, certification is required by law to work as a pharmacy technician. Even in states where it's not required, employers may prefer certified candidates over non-certified ones.

Answering the Target Question

Yes, you need to be certified to work as a pharmacy technician in most states. Certification shows that you have the necessary skills and knowledge to perform the job safely and effectively. It also demonstrates your commitment to the profession, which can lead to better job opportunities and higher pay.

Certification Requirements

The requirements for certification vary by state and certification organization. However, in general, you will need to complete a pharmacy technician training program, pass a certification exam, and meet any additional requirements set by your state or certification organization. It's essential to do your research and find out the specific requirements for your state and certification organization.
