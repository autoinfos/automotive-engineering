How Much Do Engineering Techs Make

Have you ever wondered how much engineering techs make? If you're considering a career in the field of engineering, it's important to understand the potential salary range for this role. In this blog post, we'll dive into the topic of how much engineering techs make, including the factors that can impact their salary, and what you can expect to earn in this field.

The Pain Points of How Much Do Engineering Techs Make

One of the biggest pain points related to how much engineering techs make is the wide range of potential salaries in this field. The amount you can earn as an engineering tech can vary depending on your experience, education, location, and the specific industry you work in. This can make it difficult to know what to expect in terms of salary when you're just starting out in your career.

Answering the Question: How Much Do Engineering Techs Make?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for engineering technicians was $58,240 as of May 2020. This means that half of all engineering technicians earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. The lowest 10 percent of engineering technicians earned less than $37,690 per year, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $91,620 per year.

It's important to note that these figures can vary depending on factors such as your level of education, experience, and the industry you work in. For example, engineering technicians working in the aerospace industry tend to earn higher salaries than those working in other industries.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, engineering techs can earn a wide range of salaries depending on their experience, education, location, and industry. The median annual wage for engineering technicians was $58,240 as of May 2020, with the lowest 10 percent earning less than $37,690 per year and the highest 10 percent earning more than $91,620 per year.

Factors That Impact Salaries for Engineering Techs

As mentioned, the amount you can earn as an engineering tech can vary depending on a range of factors. Some of the most common factors that impact salaries for engineering techs include:

Education: Generally, engineering techs with more education (such as a bachelor's degree) tend to earn higher salaries than those with less education.

Experience: The more experience you have as an engineering tech, the higher your potential salary may be. Engineers with more than 20 years of experience can earn six-figure salaries.

Industry: The industry you work in can also impact your salary as an engineering tech. For example, engineering techs working in the aerospace industry tend to earn higher salaries than those working in other industries.

Location: Salaries for engineering techs can also vary depending on your location. Generally, engineering techs working in urban areas or in areas with a high cost of living tend to earn higher salaries than those working in rural areas.

The Importance of Negotiating Your Salary

When you're just starting out in your career as an engineering tech, it can be tempting to accept the first salary offer you receive. However, it's important to remember that negotiating your salary can have a significant impact on your earning potential over time.

One study found that failing to negotiate your salary at the beginning of your career could cost you up to $1 million in lost earnings over the course of your working life. So, don't be afraid to negotiate your salary when starting a new job or asking for a raise in your current position.

Question and Answer

Q: What factors can impact salaries for engineering techs?

A: Factors such as education, experience, industry, and location can all impact salaries for engineering techs.

A: Yes, engineering techs working in industries such as aerospace tend to earn higher salaries than those working in other industries.

Q: Is it important to negotiate your salary as an engineering tech?

A: Yes, negotiating your salary can have a significant impact on your earning potential over time, and failing to negotiate could cost you up to $1 million in lost earnings over the course of your career.

Conclusion of How Much Do Engineering Techs Make

Overall, engineering techs can earn a wide range of salaries depending on their experience, education, location, and industry. It's important to understand the potential salary range for this role and to negotiate your salary when starting a new job or asking for a raise in your current position. By doing so, you can maximize your earning potential and achieve greater financial stability over time.
