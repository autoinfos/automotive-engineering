Exploring High Performance Automotive Technician Jobs

Written by Petter Sep 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you fascinated by cars and their engines? Do you enjoy tinkering with vehicles and fixing mechanical issues? If so, a career as a high performance automotive technician may be the perfect fit for you. Not only is this profession exciting and hands-on, but it also offers excellent job security and potential for career growth. Read on to learn more about this exciting career path.

The Pain Points of High Performance Automotive Technician Jobs

While high performance automotive technician jobs can be incredibly rewarding, they are not without their challenges. For one, the work can be physically demanding, requiring long hours on your feet and repetitive motions. Additionally, the job requires a significant amount of technical knowledge and a willingness to continually learn and adapt to new technologies and advancements in the field. Finally, working with high-performance vehicles often requires a great deal of precision and attention to detail, which can be stressful and mentally taxing.

What is a High Performance Automotive Technician?

A high performance automotive technician is a skilled professional who specializes in working on high-performance vehicles, such as sports cars and race cars. These technicians are responsible for diagnosing and repairing mechanical issues, performing routine maintenance, and installing aftermarket upgrades and modifications. They must have a deep understanding of automotive technology and be able to work with complex systems and components.

Benefits of High Performance Automotive Technician Jobs

Despite the challenges, there are many benefits to pursuing a career as a high performance automotive technician. For one, the demand for skilled technicians continues to grow, offering excellent job security and opportunities for advancement. Additionally, this field offers a great deal of variety, with every day bringing new challenges and opportunities to learn and grow. Finally, working with high-performance vehicles can be incredibly exciting and rewarding, especially for those who are passionate about cars and their engines.

The Role of Education and Training

In order to become a high performance automotive technician, it is important to have a solid foundation in automotive technology and repair. Many technicians start by completing a formal training program at a technical school or community college, which typically takes anywhere from six months to two years to complete. Additionally, many employers require certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE), which involves passing a series of exams to demonstrate proficiency in various areas of automotive repair.

Skills and Qualities of Successful High Performance Automotive Technicians

In addition to formal education and training, successful high performance automotive technicians possess a range of skills and qualities that contribute to their success. These include:

  • Strong analytical skills and the ability to diagnose complex mechanical issues
  • Excellent problem-solving skills and the ability to think creatively and innovatively
  • A strong work ethic and a commitment to quality and attention to detail
  • Excellent communication skills and the ability to explain technical concepts to non-technical customers
  • Physical dexterity and the ability to work with tools and equipment in tight spaces

How to Get Started in High Performance Automotive Technician Jobs

If you are interested in pursuing a career as a high performance automotive technician, there are several steps you can take to get started. Begin by researching technical schools and community colleges in your area that offer automotive technology programs. Consider reaching out to local repair shops and dealerships to inquire about apprenticeship or internship opportunities. Finally, consider pursuing certification from the ASE to demonstrate your proficiency and commitment to the field.

Personal Experience with High Performance Automotive Technician Jobs

As a lifelong car enthusiast, I have always been fascinated by the inner workings of vehicles and the science behind their performance. After completing a technical school program in automotive technology, I began working as a high performance automotive technician at a local dealership. While the work can be challenging at times, I find it incredibly rewarding to be able to work on some of the most exciting and advanced vehicles on the market. I love the variety of the work and the opportunity to continually learn and grow in my field.

Question and Answer Section

Q: What is the average salary for a high performance automotive technician?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics, including high performance technicians, was $44,050 as of May 2020.

Q: Is certification necessary to work as a high performance automotive technician?

A: While certification is not strictly necessary, it is highly recommended and often required by employers. Certification from the ASE demonstrates proficiency in various areas of automotive repair and can help technicians stand out in a competitive job market.

Q: What are some common aftermarket upgrades that high performance automotive technicians install?

A: Some common upgrades include performance exhaust systems, cold air intakes, suspension upgrades, and engine tuning and modifications.

Q: What is the job outlook for high performance automotive technicians?

A: Employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. This growth is due in large part to the increasing complexity of automotive technology and the need for skilled technicians to service and repair advanced vehicles.

Conclusion of High Performance Automotive Technician Jobs

High performance automotive technician jobs offer a challenging and rewarding career path for those who are passionate about cars and their engines. While the work can be physically and mentally demanding, it also offers excellent job security and opportunities for advancement. If you are interested in pursuing a career as a high performance automotive technician, be sure to seek out education and training, certification from the ASE, and opportunities to gain hands-on experience in the field.
