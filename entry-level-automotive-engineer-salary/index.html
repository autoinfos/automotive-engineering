Entry Level Automotive Engineer Salary

Written by Bardi Jun 24, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you passionate about automobiles and engineering? Do you dream of designing and creating cars that will change the world? If you're considering a career as an entry level automotive engineer, one thing you may be wondering about is the salary. Let's take a closer look at what you can expect.

As an entry level automotive engineer, it's important to consider the pain points that come with the salary. You may be starting at a lower salary than you would like, especially considering the amount of education and training that goes into becoming an automotive engineer. Additionally, the automotive industry can be competitive, which can affect your starting salary.

The target for an entry level automotive engineer salary varies depending on location, industry, and company. On average, an entry level automotive engineer can expect to make around $68,000 per year, according to Glassdoor. However, this can range from around $50,000 to $90,000 per year.

In summary, an entry level automotive engineer can expect a starting salary of around $68,000 per year, with some variation depending on location, industry, and company. It's important to consider the potential pain points, such as competition and lower starting salaries, but ultimately the salary can be quite lucrative.

What is the Target of Entry Level Automotive Engineer Salary?

The target of entry level automotive engineer salary is to provide a competitive starting salary for those just entering the field. While it may be lower than some other engineering fields, the potential for growth and advancement is high.

Personally, I started my career as an entry level automotive engineer at a small startup company. While my starting salary was lower than I had hoped, I was able to gain valuable experience and quickly move up the ranks. Within a few years, I was able to double my salary and move into a management position.

How Does Location Affect Entry Level Automotive Engineer Salary?

Location can have a significant impact on entry level automotive engineer salary. For example, in areas with a high cost of living, such as California, salaries may be higher to offset the increased expenses. Additionally, certain regions may have a higher demand for automotive engineers, which can drive up salaries.

When I was first starting out in my career, I found that location played a big role in my salary. While I had offers from companies in both high and low cost of living areas, I ultimately chose a job in a higher cost of living area because the salary was more competitive.

What Skills Are Required for an Entry Level Automotive Engineer?

As an entry level automotive engineer, there are several skills that are required to succeed in the field. These include a strong knowledge of engineering principles, as well as a deep understanding of the automotive industry. Additionally, automotive engineers must be skilled in computer-aided design (CAD) software and have experience with manufacturing processes.

When I was in school studying engineering, I made sure to focus on courses that would be directly applicable to the automotive industry. This included classes on CAD software and manufacturing processes. Additionally, I gained experience through internships and co-op programs, which helped me build my skills and make valuable connections in the industry.

How Can You Negotiate for a Higher Entry Level Automotive Engineer Salary?

If you're concerned about starting with a lower salary as an entry level automotive engineer, there are several steps you can take to negotiate for a higher salary. First, research the average salary for your location and industry, and use this information to make a case for a higher salary. Additionally, highlight any relevant experience or skills you have that could make you a valuable asset to the company.

When I was negotiating my starting salary, I made sure to do my research and come prepared with data to back up my request. Additionally, I highlighted my previous internship experience and coursework that I felt made me uniquely qualified for the position.

Conclusion of Entry Level Automotive Engineer Salary

While the starting salary for an entry level automotive engineer may be lower than some other engineering fields, the potential for growth and advancement is high. By focusing on gaining valuable experience and building your skills, you can quickly move up the ranks and increase your salary over time. With a competitive starting salary and the potential for a rewarding career, becoming an entry level automotive engineer can be a great choice for those with a passion for automobiles and engineering.
