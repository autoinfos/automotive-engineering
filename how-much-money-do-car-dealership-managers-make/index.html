How Much Money Do Car Dealership Managers Make

Written by Luffy Aug 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you curious about the earning potential of car dealership managers? If so, you're not alone. Many people wonder how much money these professionals make and whether it's a lucrative career path. In this article, we'll explore the topic of car dealership manager salaries and provide you with some insight into this exciting field.

One of the biggest pain points for aspiring car dealership managers is uncertainty around their earning potential. Without a clear understanding of what the job pays, it can be difficult to decide whether it's worth pursuing. Additionally, there may be concerns about whether the salary is commensurate with the level of responsibility and stress that comes with the job.

The answer to the question of how much money car dealership managers make is not straightforward. According to Payscale, the average salary for a car dealership manager in the United States is around $71,000 per year. However, salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors, including the size of the dealership, its location, and the manager's level of experience.

In summary, car dealership managers can earn a good salary, but the amount can vary significantly depending on a number of factors. With that said, let's take a closer look at some of the factors that can impact a car dealership manager's earning potential.

Experience and Education

One of the biggest factors that can impact a car dealership manager's salary is their level of experience and education. Generally speaking, managers with more experience and education tend to earn more than those who are just starting out. This is because they bring more value to the dealership and are better equipped to handle the challenges of the job.

For example, a manager with a bachelor's degree in business administration and several years of experience in the automotive industry may be able to command a higher salary than someone with only a high school diploma and no experience.

Location

The location of the dealership is another factor that can impact a manager's salary. In general, managers who work in larger cities or more affluent areas tend to earn more than those who work in rural or less affluent areas. This is because the cost of living is higher in cities and affluent areas, which means that salaries need to be commensurate with that higher cost of living.

Size of the Dealership

The size of the dealership is another factor that can impact a manager's salary. Generally speaking, managers who work at larger dealerships tend to earn more than those who work at smaller dealerships. This is because larger dealerships have more revenue and therefore, can afford to pay their employees more.

Commission and Bonuses

Finally, it's worth noting that many car dealership managers are eligible for commission and bonuses in addition to their base salary. This means that their earning potential can be significantly higher than their base salary alone. For example, a manager who is able to consistently meet or exceed their sales targets may be eligible for a significant bonus at the end of the year.

Personal Experience

As someone who has worked in the automotive industry for many years, I can attest to the fact that car dealership managers can earn a good salary. However, it's important to remember that salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors, as we've discussed. In my experience, managers who are able to build strong relationships with their team and consistently meet their sales targets tend to earn more than those who struggle in these areas.

How to Become a Car Dealership Manager

If you're interested in becoming a car dealership manager, there are a few steps you can take to increase your chances of success. First, consider earning a degree in business administration or a related field. This can help you develop the skills and knowledge you need to succeed in the industry.

Next, gain experience in the automotive industry by working your way up from entry-level positions. This can help you develop a deep understanding of the industry and the challenges that come with managing a dealership. Finally, focus on building strong relationships with your team and consistently meeting your sales targets. This can help you demonstrate your value to the dealership and increase your earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it possible for car dealership managers to earn six-figure salaries?

A: Yes, it's possible for some car dealership managers to earn six-figure salaries. However, this is typically only the case for managers who work at large, high-volume dealerships in affluent areas.

Q: Are commission and bonuses a significant portion of a car dealership manager's salary?

A: Yes, commission and bonuses can be a significant portion of a car dealership manager's salary. This is especially true for managers who are able to consistently meet or exceed their sales targets.

Q: How long does it typically take to become a car dealership manager?

A: The amount of time it takes to become a car dealership manager can vary widely depending on a number of factors, including your level of education, experience in the industry, and the size of the dealership. In general, it can take several years to work your way up to a management position.

Q: What skills are important for car dealership managers to have?

A: Some of the key skills that are important for car dealership managers to have include leadership, communication, sales, and financial management skills. Additionally, it's important for managers to be able to build strong relationships with their team and customers.

Conclusion of How Much Money Do Car Dealership Managers Make

Car dealership managers can earn a good salary, but the amount can vary widely depending on a number of factors. In general, managers with more experience and education tend to earn more than those who are just starting out. Additionally, managers who work in larger cities or more affluent areas tend to earn more than those who work in rural or less affluent areas. Finally, many car dealership managers are eligible for commission and bonuses in addition to their base salary, which means that their earning potential can be significantly higher than their base salary alone.
