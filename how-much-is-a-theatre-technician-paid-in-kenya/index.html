How Much Is A Theatre Technician Paid In Kenya

Written by Frank Jul 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have a passion for theatre technology and are wondering how much you can earn in Kenya? Well, you are in the right place. In this article, we will explore the salary range of a theatre technician in Kenya and what factors affect their remuneration.

Theatre technicians play a crucial role in the success of any theatre production. They are responsible for setting up and operating the lighting, sound, and other technical equipment during rehearsals and performances. Despite their importance, theatre technicians are not widely known, and their salary range is often a mystery to many.

The salary range of a theatre technician in Kenya varies depending on various factors. According to Payscale, the average salary of a theatre technician in Kenya is Ksh 40,000 per month. However, this can range from Ksh 20,000 to Ksh 100,000 per month, depending on the level of experience, skills, and qualifications of the technician.

My Experience as a Theatre Technician in Kenya

As a former theatre technician in Kenya, I can attest that the salary range is quite broad. When I started my career, I earned Ksh 20,000 per month, but as I gained more experience, my salary increased to Ksh 50,000 per month. However, this was after several years of hard work and upskilling myself with relevant certifications.

Factors Affecting the Salary Range of a Theatre Technician in Kenya

The salary range of a theatre technician in Kenya is influenced by several factors. These include:

Level of Experience

The more experienced a technician is, the higher their pay. Experienced technicians have honed their skills over time, making them more efficient in their work.

Qualifications and Certifications

Theatre technicians with relevant qualifications and certifications are likely to earn more than those without. Certifications such as those offered by the Society of Television Lighting and Design (STLD) can boost a technician's salary range.

Nature and Scope of the Production

The size and complexity of a theatre production can also affect a technician's salary range. Large productions that require more equipment and personnel may pay more than smaller productions.

The Benefits of Being a Theatre Technician in Kenya

Despite the salary range being broad, being a theatre technician in Kenya has its perks. Theatre technicians get to work in a dynamic and exciting environment, meet new people, and work on different productions. They also get to hone their skills and gain experience in various aspects of theatre production.

Question and Answer

Q. Can a theatre technician earn more than Ksh 100,000 per month in Kenya?

A. Yes, a theatre technician can earn more than Ksh 100,000 per month, especially if they have vast experience, relevant qualifications and are working on large productions.

Q. How long does it take to become a theatre technician in Kenya?

A. Becoming a theatre technician in Kenya can take as little as six months to a year, depending on the level of training and certification required.

Q. What skills are required to become a theatre technician in Kenya?

A. To become a theatre technician in Kenya, one needs to have skills in lighting, sound, rigging, and stage management. Attention to detail, good communication skills, and teamwork are also essential.

Q. Can theatre technicians in Kenya work on international productions?

A. Yes, theatre technicians in Kenya can work on international productions. However, this is likely to be reserved for experienced technicians with relevant certifications and qualifications.

Conclusion of How Much is a Theatre Technician Paid in Kenya

The salary range of a theatre technician in Kenya is broad, ranging from Ksh 20,000 to Ksh 100,000 per month. However, various factors influence how much a technician can earn, such as experience, qualifications, and the nature of the production. Despite the salary range being broad, being a theatre technician in Kenya has its benefits, such as working in an exciting and dynamic environment, meeting new people, and acquiring new skills.
