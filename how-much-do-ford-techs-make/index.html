How Much Do Ford Techs Make

Written by Aprilia Jul 08, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a Ford technician? Do you want to know how much you can expect to make in this field? If so, you're not alone. Many people are interested in becoming Ford techs, but they want to make sure that the salary is worth the effort. In this post, we'll explore the topic of how much do Ford techs make, including the average salary, factors that can affect pay, and more.

If you're considering a career as a Ford technician, it's important to understand the potential pain points of the job. For example, you may have to work long hours, deal with difficult customers, or handle complex repair jobs. However, if you have a passion for cars and enjoy working with your hands, these challenges may be worth it. In addition, many Ford techs enjoy the job security and opportunities for advancement that come with this career.

So, how much do Ford techs make? According to Glassdoor, the national average salary for a Ford technician is $43,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and certifications. For example, a Ford tech in California may make more than one in Georgia due to the higher cost of living. Similarly, a tech with several years of experience and advanced certifications may earn more than someone who is just starting out.

In summary, becoming a Ford technician can be a rewarding career choice with a decent salary. If you're willing to put in the time and effort to learn the necessary skills, you can expect to earn a living wage and have opportunities for growth.

Factors That Affect a Ford Tech's Salary

Location and experience are two of the biggest factors that can affect how much a Ford tech makes. For example, techs in urban areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas due to the higher cost of living. In addition, techs with several years of experience and advanced certifications can earn significantly more than entry-level techs.

Personally, I started out as an entry-level Ford tech making around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased to around $50,000 per year. I also had opportunities for advancement within the dealership where I worked, which allowed me to earn even more.

Benefits of Becoming a Ford Tech

One of the biggest benefits of becoming a Ford tech is job security. As long as there are cars on the road, there will be a need for skilled technicians to repair and maintain them. In addition, many dealerships offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Finally, becoming a Ford tech allows you to work with your hands and solve complex problems, which can be very rewarding.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Ford Tech

If you want to increase your salary as a Ford tech, there are several steps you can take. First, you can gain experience and advanced certifications, which can help you qualify for higher-paying jobs. Second, you can look for job opportunities in areas with a higher cost of living, which tend to pay more. Finally, you can negotiate your salary when applying for a new job or during performance reviews.

Is Becoming a Ford Tech Right for You?

If you enjoy working with your hands, solving complex problems, and have a passion for cars, becoming a Ford technician may be a great career choice. While the job can be challenging at times, it also offers job security, opportunities for advancement, and a decent salary.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a Ford tech make per hour?
A: The average hourly wage for a Ford technician is around $20 per hour, but this can vary depending on factors such as location and experience.

Q: What certifications do I need to become a Ford tech?
A: While there are no specific certifications required to become a Ford tech, many dealerships prefer candidates who have completed a formal training program or have earned certifications such as ASE or Ford-specific certifications.

Q: How long does it take to become a Ford tech?
A: The length of time it takes to become a Ford tech can vary depending on the individual and the training program. However, many formal training programs take around 6-12 months to complete.

Q: Can I become a Ford tech with no experience?
A: While some dealerships may hire entry-level technicians with no experience, it can be helpful to have some basic knowledge of automotive repair and maintenance before applying for a job.

Conclusion of How Much Do Ford Techs Make

In conclusion, becoming a Ford technician can be a great career choice with a decent salary and opportunities for growth. While the job can be challenging at times, it also allows you to work with your hands, solve complex problems, and have job security in a growing industry. By gaining experience and certifications, you can increase your salary and qualify for higher-paying jobs. So, if you're passionate about cars and enjoy working with your hands, becoming a Ford tech may be the right choice for you.
