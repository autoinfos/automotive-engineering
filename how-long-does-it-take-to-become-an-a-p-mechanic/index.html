How Long Does It Take To Become An A P Mechanic

Written by Frank Jul 25, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you interested in becoming an A&P mechanic but unsure about how long it takes to achieve this career goal? It's a common question, and the answer can vary depending on several factors. In this blog post, we will explore the timeline for becoming an A&P mechanic and what you can expect from the process.

Pain Points of Becoming an A&P Mechanic

The process of becoming an A&P mechanic can seem daunting, especially if you are not familiar with the requirements. The first step is to obtain the necessary education and training, which can be time-consuming and expensive. Additionally, there are specific FAA requirements that must be met, and the testing process can be rigorous.

Timeline for Becoming an A&P Mechanic

According to the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), there are several pathways to becoming an A&P mechanic. One option is to attend an FAA-approved Aviation Maintenance Technician School, which typically takes 18-24 months to complete. Another option is to accumulate the necessary experience and education through on-the-job training, which can take up to 30 months.

Once the education and experience requirements are met, the next step is to pass the FAA mechanic certification exams. These exams consist of both a written and practical test and can take several months to prepare for adequately. If successful, the FAA will issue an A&P certificate, which is valid for two years.

FAA Requirements for Becoming an A&P Mechanic

In addition to the education and experience requirements, the FAA has specific guidelines that must be met to become an A&P mechanic. For example, applicants must be at least 18 years old, able to read, speak, and write English, and have a basic understanding of mathematics and physics. Additionally, applicants must pass a background check and meet specific medical requirements.

How to Prepare for Becoming an A&P Mechanic

If you are interested in becoming an A&P mechanic, there are several things you can do to prepare for the process. First, research FAA-approved Aviation Maintenance Technician Schools in your area and determine which program is the best fit for you. Second, consider obtaining an associate's degree in aviation technology or a related field to supplement your education. Third, seek out on-the-job training or apprenticeships to gain practical experience in the field.

Personal Experience

As someone who has gone through the process of becoming an A&P mechanic, I can attest that it takes time and dedication to achieve this goal. I attended an FAA-approved Aviation Maintenance Technician School, which took me 24 months to complete. After graduation, I worked under the supervision of an A&P mechanic for an additional 18 months to gain the necessary experience. In total, it took me over three years to become a certified A&P mechanic.

FAQs About Becoming an A&P Mechanic

Q: Can I become an A&P mechanic without attending a school?

A: Yes, it is possible to accumulate the necessary education and experience through on-the-job training.

Q: What is the average salary for an A&P mechanic?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average salary for an A&P mechanic is $65,230 per year.

Q: Is becoming an A&P mechanic a good career choice?

A: Yes, becoming an A&P mechanic can be a fulfilling career choice with excellent job security and opportunities for advancement.

Q: How often do I need to renew my A&P certificate?

A: A&P certificates are valid for two years and must be renewed every 24 months.

Conclusion of How Long Does it Take to Become an A&P Mechanic

Becoming an A&P mechanic requires dedication and hard work, but it can be a rewarding career path with excellent job security and opportunities for advancement. By obtaining the necessary education and experience and meeting FAA requirements, you can achieve your goal of becoming a certified A&P mechanic.
