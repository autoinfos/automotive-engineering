How Much Do Radiology Technicians Make In California

Written by Aprilia Sep 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a radiology technician in California but unsure about the earning potential? Many people are hesitant to pursue a career in healthcare due to the high cost of education and the uncertainty of financial stability. However, with the increasing demand for healthcare professionals, radiology technicians can enjoy a rewarding career with a competitive salary.

As with any career, there are potential challenges and uncertainties, but with the right education and experience, radiology technicians can earn a comfortable living while providing valuable services to patients.

A radiology technician's salary in California can vary depending on several factors, such as education level, years of experience, location, and type of employer. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average salary for radiology technicians in California is $71,110 per year, which is higher than the national average of $62,280 per year.

In addition to a competitive salary, radiology technicians in California can enjoy job security and growth opportunities. As the healthcare industry continues to expand, the demand for radiology technicians is expected to increase by 9% in the next decade.

Personal Experience

Education and Training

If you are interested in pursuing a career as a radiology technician in California, you will need to complete a formal education program and obtain certification. Most employers require a degree from an accredited program, such as an Associate's or Bachelor's degree in Radiologic Technology.

After completing an accredited program, you will need to obtain certification from the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT) to become a licensed radiology technician in California. Continuing education is also required to maintain certification and stay up-to-date with the latest technology and practices in the field.

Salary Factors

As previously mentioned, several factors can affect a radiology technician's salary in California. Location is a significant factor, as urban areas tend to have higher salaries compared to rural areas. The type of employer can also affect salary, as hospitals and outpatient care centers tend to pay higher salaries compared to physician offices or diagnostic imaging centers.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for radiology technicians in California is positive, with a projected 9% increase in demand over the next decade. This demand is due to the aging population and advances in medical technology, which require more imaging services.
