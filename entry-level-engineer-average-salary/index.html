Entry Level Engineer Average Salary What You Need To Know

Written by Bardi May 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a recent graduate in engineering or are you considering a career in engineering? One of the most important factors to consider is the entry level engineer average salary. Understanding this figure can help you make informed decisions about your career path and financial future.

Many entry level engineers face a number of pain points related to salary. For example, they may struggle to negotiate a fair starting salary or may feel frustrated with the low salaries offered by some employers. Additionally, some engineers may not fully understand the factors that impact their starting salary, which can lead to confusion and uncertainty about their earning potential.

So, what is the average salary for entry level engineers? According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for entry level engineers in the United States is $73,000. However, this figure can vary widely depending on a number of factors, including your location, industry, and level of education and experience.

In summary, understanding the entry level engineer average salary is crucial for anyone considering a career in this field. It can help you negotiate fair starting salaries, set realistic expectations for your earning potential, and make informed decisions about your career path.

Factors that Impact Entry Level Engineer Average Salary

As mentioned above, entry level engineer average salary can vary widely depending on a number of factors. Here are some of the most important factors to consider:

Location

Location is one of the biggest factors that can impact your starting salary as an entry level engineer. For example, engineers in cities with a high cost of living may be paid more than those in areas with a lower cost of living. Additionally, different regions may have different demand for engineers, which can impact salary levels.

Industry

The industry you work in can also have a big impact on your starting salary. For example, engineers working in the tech industry may earn more than those in the manufacturing industry. Additionally, some industries may be more competitive than others, which can drive up salaries.

My Personal Experience with Entry Level Engineer Average Salary

When I graduated with my degree in engineering, I was excited to start my career but also a bit nervous about negotiating my starting salary. I did my research and found that the entry level engineer average salary in my region was around $70,000. However, I also knew that my specific industry tended to pay higher salaries, so I aimed for a starting salary of $75,000.

When I received my first job offer, I was pleased to see that they were willing to meet my salary expectations. However, I also knew that I could have negotiated for more if I had been willing to push back and advocate for my skills and experience. Overall, I learned that it's important to do your research, know your worth, and be willing to negotiate for fair compensation.

How to Negotiate a Fair Starting Salary as an Entry Level Engineer

If you're an entry level engineer looking to negotiate your starting salary, here are some tips to keep in mind:

Do Your Research

Before you start negotiating, it's important to research the entry level engineer average salary in your region and industry. This can give you a better understanding of what to expect and can help you make a more informed case for fair compensation.

Advocate for Your Skills and Experience

Don't be afraid to speak up and advocate for your skills and experience. If you have a strong education or relevant experience, be sure to highlight this during your negotiations.

Conclusion of Entry Level Engineer Average Salary

In conclusion, understanding the entry level engineer average salary is an important part of planning your career in engineering. By doing your research, advocating for fair compensation, and negotiating effectively, you can set yourself up for a successful and financially rewarding career in this field.
