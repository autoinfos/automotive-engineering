How Much Does A Pharmacy Technician Make In Canada

Written by Bardi Oct 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you enjoy working in a pharmacy and want to pursue a career as a pharmacy technician in Canada? Are you curious about the salary that comes with this profession? If so, you're not alone!

Many people considering becoming a pharmacy technician in Canada wonder about the potential salary they could earn. This is a common pain point for those interested in the profession, as salary plays a major role in career decisions.

According to the Government of Canada Job Bank, the median salary for pharmacy technicians in Canada is $44,096 per year. However, this can vary based on location, experience, and employer. Entry-level pharmacy technicians can expect to earn around $30,000 to $35,000 per year, while those with more experience can earn up to $60,000 per year.

In summary, the salary of a pharmacy technician in Canada can vary significantly. The median salary is $44,096 per year, but this can be influenced by a variety of factors.

What is the Job of a Pharmacy Technician?

Before we dive into the salary aspect, let's take a closer look at what a pharmacy technician does. Pharmacy technicians work alongside pharmacists to help ensure the safe and efficient distribution of prescription medication. They perform a variety of tasks, such as:

- Filling prescriptions

- Preparing medication

- Counting pills

- Managing inventory

- Interacting with patients

As you can see, pharmacy technicians play a crucial role in the healthcare system by making sure patients receive the medication they need.

Factors That Affect a Pharmacy Technician's Salary

As previously mentioned, the salary of a pharmacy technician can vary based on a number of factors. Here are a few things that can influence salary:

- Location: Salaries can differ depending on the province or city you work in.

- Experience: Those with more experience may earn higher salaries.

- Employer: Salaries can vary depending on the type of employer you work for, such as a hospital or retail pharmacy.

Location

Location is a major factor that can influence a pharmacy technician's salary. For example, pharmacy technicians in urban areas may earn a higher salary than those in rural areas due to cost of living differences. According to the Government of Canada Job Bank, the provinces with the highest median salaries for pharmacy technicians are Alberta ($51,376 per year) and British Columbia ($50,336 per year).

Experience

Experience is another factor that can impact a pharmacy technician's salary. Those with more experience may earn a higher salary than those who are just starting out. This is because experienced pharmacy technicians have a greater knowledge base and skill set, which can be valuable to employers. Additionally, experienced pharmacy technicians may be able to take on more responsibilities or leadership roles, which can come with a higher salary.

Personal Experience

As a former pharmacy technician myself, I can attest to the fact that the salary can vary greatly. When I first started as a pharmacy technician in a retail pharmacy, my starting salary was around $32,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved to a different pharmacy, my salary increased to around $45,000 per year.

FAQs

Q: Can pharmacy technicians earn bonuses or other incentives?

A: Yes, some pharmacy technicians may receive bonuses or other incentives for meeting certain performance goals or targets.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement as a pharmacy technician?

A: Yes, some pharmacy technicians may be able to advance to higher-level positions, such as a pharmacy manager or supervisor.

Q: Do pharmacy technicians receive benefits?

A: Yes, many pharmacy technicians receive benefits such as health insurance, paid time off, and retirement plans.

Q: Is there a high demand for pharmacy technicians in Canada?

A: Yes, there is a high demand for pharmacy technicians in Canada. According to the Government of Canada Job Bank, the employment outlook for pharmacy technicians is expected to be good in the coming years.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the salary of a pharmacy technician in Canada can vary based on a number of factors, such as location and experience. The median salary is $44,096 per year, but this can vary from $30,000 to $60,000 per year. Despite the variable salary, pharmacy technicians play a crucial role in the healthcare system by ensuring patients receive the medication they need.
