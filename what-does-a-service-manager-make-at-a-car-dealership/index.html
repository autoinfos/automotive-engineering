What Does A Service Manager Make At A Car Dealership

Written by Oliver Aug 12, 2023 · 5 min read

Working in the automotive industry can be exciting, but it can also be challenging. One of the most vital positions in a car dealership is the service manager. If you're interested in this position, you might be wondering what does a service manager make at a car dealership. In this article, we will explore the answer to that question and more.

As a service manager, you have several responsibilities that can be stressful. For example, you need to ensure that customers are satisfied with the services provided, manage a team of technicians, and oversee the financial performance of the department. All of these challenges can make the job of a service manager a tough one.

A service manager at a car dealership can make on average around $70,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and the size of the dealership. Some service managers can make over $100,000 per year if they work at a large dealership with a high volume of customers.

In summary, a service manager is an essential position in a car dealership. They are responsible for managing and overseeing the service department's performance, ensuring customer satisfaction, and managing a team of technicians. Service managers can make a decent salary, but it can vary depending on several factors.

What Are the Responsibilities of a Service Manager?

As a former service manager at a car dealership, I can tell you that the job can be both rewarding and challenging. One of the primary responsibilities of a service manager is to ensure that the service department is running smoothly. This means managing the workflow of technicians, scheduling appointments, ordering parts, and ensuring customer satisfaction.

Another critical responsibility of a service manager is to manage and train a team of technicians. This includes ensuring that they are up to date on the latest technology and techniques, providing feedback and coaching, and managing their performance. Additionally, service managers need to be comfortable with financial management, such as budgeting, forecasting, and managing expenses.

What Skills Are Required to Become a Service Manager?

To become a service manager at a car dealership, you need to have several skills. These include excellent communication skills, strong leadership abilities, and a customer-focused mindset. Additionally, you need to be comfortable with technology and have a solid understanding of automotive systems and repairs.

Another critical skill for a service manager is the ability to multitask and prioritize tasks effectively. Service managers need to handle several tasks simultaneously, including scheduling appointments, ordering parts, and managing technicians. Being able to prioritize these tasks effectively is critical for success in this position.

How Can You Become a Service Manager?

If you're interested in becoming a service manager at a car dealership, there are several steps you can take. First, you need to gain experience working in the automotive industry, preferably in a service-related position. This will give you a solid understanding of the industry and the skills required to succeed as a service manager.

You can also consider pursuing additional training or certifications to enhance your skills and knowledge. For example, you can pursue certification as an Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) Master Technician, which can demonstrate your expertise to potential employers.

What Are Some Challenges of Being a Service Manager?

As mentioned earlier, being a service manager at a car dealership can be a challenging position. One of the most significant challenges is managing customer expectations and ensuring their satisfaction. Customers can be demanding, and managing their needs and expectations can be stressful.

Another challenge is managing a team of technicians effectively. Technicians can have different personalities and work styles, and managing them can be challenging. Additionally, managing the financial performance of the department can be stressful, as you need to ensure that expenses are managed effectively and that revenue is generated.

Conclusion of What Does a Service Manager Make at a Car Dealership

In conclusion, a service manager at a car dealership plays a vital role in ensuring that the service department runs smoothly and that customers are satisfied.
