How Much Do Airplane Mechanics Make A Year

Written by Daniel Jun 04, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an airplane mechanic, but wondering how much you can expect to make? If so, you're not alone. The salary of an airplane mechanic is an important consideration when deciding whether to pursue this career path. In this post, we'll explore the average salary of an airplane mechanic, as well as factors that can affect how much you can earn in this field.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career in airplane mechanics is the fear of not earning a good income. It's important to know that the salary of an airplane mechanic can vary depending on a number of factors, such as geographical location, level of experience, and type of employer. However, with some training and experience, it is possible to earn a good income in this field.

The average salary of an airplane mechanic in the United States is around $62,000 per year. However, this can vary widely depending on the factors mentioned above. For example, airplane mechanics who work for major airlines tend to earn more than those who work for smaller, regional airlines or private companies. Additionally, airplane mechanics who work in large cities or areas with a high cost of living may earn more than those who work in rural areas.

In summary, the salary of an airplane mechanic can vary widely depending on a number of factors. However, with some training and experience, it is possible to earn a good income in this field. Let's take a closer look at some of the factors that can affect how much you can expect to make as an airplane mechanic.

Experience and Education

As with many careers, experience and education can play a big role in determining an airplane mechanic's salary. Generally speaking, those who have more experience and education in the field will earn more. This is because these individuals have a greater level of knowledge and expertise, which makes them more valuable to employers.

For example, an airplane mechanic who has completed an FAA-approved training program and has several years of experience may earn more than someone who is just starting out in the field. Additionally, those who have specialized in a particular area, such as avionics or engine repair, may earn more than those who work in more general maintenance roles.

Geographical Location

As mentioned earlier, the location of an airplane mechanic's job can have a big impact on their salary. Those who work in large cities or areas with a high cost of living may earn more than those who work in rural areas. This is because employers in these areas may need to pay more in order to attract and retain skilled workers.

Additionally, airplane mechanics who work in areas with a high demand for their services may be able to command a higher salary. For example, those who work in areas with a large number of airports or airlines may have more job opportunities and may be able to negotiate a higher salary as a result.

Type of Employer

The type of employer an airplane mechanic works for can also affect their salary. Those who work for major airlines tend to earn more than those who work for smaller, regional airlines or private companies. This is because major airlines have more resources and may be able to offer better benefits and higher salaries to their employees.

Additionally, those who work for the government may earn more than those who work in the private sector. This is because government jobs often come with better benefits and job security.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for airplane mechanics is generally good. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations. This growth is due to an increase in air travel and the need to maintain and repair aircraft.

Overall, airplane mechanics can expect to earn a good income with some training and experience. Factors such as geographical location, level of experience, and type of employer can all affect how much an airplane mechanic can expect to make. However, with a good work ethic and dedication to their craft, airplane mechanics can build a successful and rewarding career in this field.

Question and Answer

A: The average salary of an airplane mechanic in the United States is around $62,000 per year.

Q: Can airplane mechanics earn a good income?

A: Yes, with some training and experience, it is possible for airplane mechanics to earn a good income.

Conclusion of How Much Do Airplane Mechanics Make a Year

In conclusion, the salary of an airplane mechanic can vary widely depending on a number of factors. However, with some training and experience, it is possible to earn a good income in this field. Factors such as geographical location, level of experience, and type of employer can all affect how much an airplane mechanic can expect to make. Overall, airplane mechanics can build a successful and rewarding career in this field with dedication and hard work.
