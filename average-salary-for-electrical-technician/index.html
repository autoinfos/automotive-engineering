Average Salary For Electrical Technician Everything You Need To Know

Written by Daniel Oct 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as an electrical technician? One of the most important factors to consider when choosing a career is the earning potential. In this blog post, we will discuss the average salary for electrical technicians and everything you need to know about this career path.

As with any job, there are both pros and cons to being an electrical technician. One of the most common pain points is the physical demands of the job, which can be quite strenuous. Additionally, working with electrical wiring can be dangerous if proper safety protocols are not followed. However, for those who enjoy problem-solving and working with their hands, being an electrical technician can be a rewarding career.

According to the United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for electrical technicians was $62,190 as of May 2020. This means that half of all electrical technicians earned more than this amount, while half earned less. The lowest 10 percent of earners in this field made less than $38,320, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $96,580.

In summary, the average salary for electrical technicians is around $62,190 per year. However, this amount can vary based on factors such as location, level of experience, and education.

What Affects the Average Salary for Electrical Technicians?

Location is one of the biggest factors that can affect an electrical technician's salary. In areas where the cost of living is higher, such as major cities or coastal regions, electrical technicians may earn a higher salary to compensate for the increased expenses. Additionally, areas with a higher demand for skilled electrical technicians may offer higher salaries to attract top talent.

Another factor that can impact an electrical technician's salary is their level of experience. Those who have been working in the field for several years and have developed a high level of expertise may be eligible for higher pay. Additionally, those who pursue further education, such as earning a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering, may also be able to command a higher salary.

My Personal Experience as an Electrical Technician

As an electrical technician with over 10 years of experience, I can attest to the various factors that can affect an individual's salary in this field. One of the most important aspects is continuing education and staying up-to-date with the latest advancements in technology and safety protocols. In addition to increasing earning potential, this can also lead to more challenging and fulfilling job opportunities.

Is the Average Salary for Electrical Technicians Competitive?

Compared to other skilled trades, such as plumbing or carpentry, the average salary for electrical technicians is quite competitive. Additionally, the demand for skilled electrical technicians is expected to increase in the coming years, which means that there will likely be ample job opportunities available in this field.

How Can You Increase Your Earning Potential as an Electrical Technician?

One of the best ways to increase your earning potential as an electrical technician is to pursue further education and training. This can include earning a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering, obtaining certifications in specialized areas such as renewable energy or industrial automation, or simply staying up-to-date with the latest advancements in the field.

Conclusion of Average Salary for Electrical Technician

Overall, the average salary for electrical technicians is around $62,190 per year. While this amount can vary based on factors such as location, level of experience, and education, it is a competitive salary for a skilled trade. With the expected increase in demand for skilled electrical technicians in the coming years, pursuing a career in this field can be a smart choice for those interested in a challenging and rewarding career.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the job outlook for electrical technicians?

A: The job outlook for electrical technicians is positive, with a projected growth rate of 8 percent from 2019 to 2029.

Q: What are some of the benefits of being an electrical technician?

A: Benefits of being an electrical technician include competitive salaries, opportunities for advancement, and the ability to work with your hands and solve complex problems.

Q: What education is required to become an electrical technician?

A: While some electrical technicians may only need a high school diploma and on-the-job training, others may choose to pursue further education such as an associate's or bachelor's degree in electrical engineering.

Q: What are some of the potential drawbacks of being an electrical technician?

A: Some potential drawbacks of being an electrical technician include the physical demands of the job, the potential danger of working with electrical wiring, and the need to stay up-to-date with constantly evolving safety protocols and technology.
