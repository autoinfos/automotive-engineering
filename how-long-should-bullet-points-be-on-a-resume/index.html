How Long Should Bullet Points Be On A Resume

Written by William Jul 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you struggling to figure out how long your bullet points should be on your resume? You're not alone. Many job seekers have trouble deciding on the right length for their bullet points.

One of the biggest pain points related to how long bullet points should be on a resume is the fear of either not including enough information or including too much and overwhelming the reader. Finding the right balance can be tricky, but it's crucial to landing an interview.

So, how long should bullet points be on a resume? The general rule of thumb is to keep them between one and two lines in length. This keeps your resume clean, easy to read, and highlights your most important accomplishments.

In summary, it's important to keep your bullet points concise and to the point. Use keywords and phrases relevant to the job description and keep them between one to two lines in length. This will make your resume stand out and keep the recruiter interested.

How Long Should Bullet Points Be on a Resume and Why?

When I was applying for a job a few years ago, I had trouble deciding how long my bullet points should be on my resume. I thought that the more information I included, the better my chances of getting an interview. However, after doing some research, I realized that this was not the case.

Recruiters and hiring managers receive hundreds of resumes for each job posting, and they don't have time to read through lengthy bullet points. Keeping your bullet points concise and relevant to the job posting is crucial to getting your resume noticed.

The Dos and Don'ts of Bullet Points on a Resume

Do include keywords and phrases relevant to the job description. This will help your resume get past the ATS and into the hands of the recruiter.

Don't use long paragraphs or sentences. This makes your resume difficult to read and can be overwhelming for the recruiter.

Do highlight your accomplishments and achievements. This is your chance to show the recruiter what you can bring to the table.

Don't include irrelevant information. Stick to what's important and relevant to the job posting.

Tips for Writing Effective Bullet Points

Use action verbs to start each bullet point. This makes your accomplishments stand out and shows that you are proactive.

Quantify your achievements. This gives the recruiter an idea of what you can bring to the table and how you can contribute to the company.

Be specific about your accomplishments. Instead of saying "increased sales," say "increased sales by 20% in the first quarter."

How to Edit Your Bullet Points

After you've written your bullet points, it's important to edit them to make sure they are concise and relevant. One way to do this is to read them out loud. This will help you identify any awkward phrasing or unnecessary information.

Another way to edit your bullet points is to have someone else read them. This can give you a fresh perspective and help you identify any areas that need improvement.

Final Thoughts on How Long Should Bullet Points Be on a Resume

When writing your resume, it's important to remember that the recruiter is looking for relevant information that highlights your accomplishments and achievements. Keeping your bullet points concise and to the point is crucial to getting your resume noticed and landing an interview.

Question and Answer

Q: Can bullet points be longer than two lines?

A: It's best to keep your bullet points between one to two lines in length. This makes your resume easy to read and highlights your most important accomplishments.

Q: Should I include bullet points for every job on my resume?

A: It's best to include bullet points for your most recent and relevant job experiences. This shows the recruiter what you can bring to the table and how you can contribute to the company.

Q: What's the best way to format bullet points on a resume?

A: Use bullet points to highlight your accomplishments and achievements. Start each bullet point with an action verb and keep them between one to two lines in length.

Q: Can I use bullet points for my education section?

A: It's best to use bullet points for your work experience section. For your education section, you can list your degrees and any relevant coursework.

Conclusion of How Long Should Bullet Points Be on a Resume

When it comes to writing your resume, the length of your bullet points is crucial to getting noticed by the recruiter. Keeping them between one to two lines in length and highlighting your most important accomplishments is the key to landing an interview. Remember to keep your bullet points concise, relevant, and easy to read.
