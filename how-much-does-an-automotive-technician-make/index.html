How Much Does An Automotive Technician Make

Written by Aprilia Aug 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an automotive technician? If so, one of the most important questions you may have is: how much does an automotive technician make? The answer to this question can vary depending on a variety of factors, but we've put together this post to give you a better idea of what to expect.

Working as an automotive technician can be a rewarding career, but it can also come with its own set of challenges. One of the biggest pain points for many technicians is the issue of pay. It's not always easy to know what to expect in terms of salary or how to negotiate for a fair wage.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this can vary depending on factors such as level of experience, location, and the specific industry you work in.

In this post, we'll take a closer look at some of the factors that can impact how much an automotive technician makes, as well as some tips for negotiating a fair salary.

Understanding the Factors That Affect Pay

One of the most important things to understand about automotive technician pay is that there are a variety of factors that can impact how much you make. Here are some of the most important:

Experience

One of the biggest factors that can impact your pay as an automotive technician is your level of experience. Generally speaking, the more experience you have, the more you can expect to make. This is because experienced technicians are often more skilled and can complete jobs more quickly and efficiently.

Location

Another important factor that can impact pay is your location. In some areas of the country, automotive technicians are in high demand and may be able to command higher salaries. On the other hand, in areas where there is less demand for technicians, salaries may be lower.

Personal Experience

As someone who has worked as an automotive technician for several years, I can tell you that pay can vary widely depending on a variety of factors. When I was starting out, I took a job at a dealership and was paid a fairly low hourly rate. However, I was able to gain a lot of experience and skill while working there, which helped me negotiate a higher salary when I moved on to another job.

One thing I've learned over the years is that it's important to be proactive when it comes to negotiating your salary. Don't be afraid to ask for a higher rate if you feel that you deserve it. It's also a good idea to keep an eye on job postings in your area to get a sense of what other employers are paying for similar positions.

How to Negotiate Your Salary

If you're just starting out as an automotive technician, it can be tricky to know how to negotiate your salary. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Do Your Research

Before you go into a salary negotiation, it's important to do your research. Look up the average salaries for automotive technicians in your area, and be prepared to make a case for why you deserve a higher rate.

Be Confident

When negotiating your salary, it's important to be confident and assertive. Don't be afraid to ask for what you want, but also be prepared to explain why you feel you deserve it.

Question and Answer

Q: How Much Does an Entry-Level Automotive Technician Make?

A: Entry-level automotive technicians can expect to make around $30,000 to $40,000 per year.

Q: How Much Does an Experienced Automotive Technician Make?

A: Experienced automotive technicians can make upwards of $60,000 per year or more.

Q: What Industries Pay Automotive Technicians the Most?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, automotive technicians working in the aerospace product and parts manufacturing industry tend to make the most money.

Q: How Can I Increase My Salary as an Automotive Technician?

A: One of the best ways to increase your salary as an automotive technician is to gain experience and develop new skills. You may also want to consider specializing in a particular area, such as hybrid or electric vehicles.

Conclusion

As you can see, there are a variety of factors that can impact how much an automotive technician makes. However, with the right experience, skills, and negotiation tactics, it's possible to earn a fair and competitive salary in this field.
