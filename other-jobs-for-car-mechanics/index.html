Other Jobs For Car Mechanics Exploring Alternative Career Paths

Written by Luffy Aug 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Car mechanics are often perceived as solely working in repair shops or dealerships, but did you know that there are other career paths that a mechanic can pursue? In this blog post, we will explore other jobs for car mechanics and shed light on their opportunities and challenges.

Being a car mechanic is a physically demanding job that requires long hours and the ability to work under pressure. While it can be a rewarding career, it's not for everyone, and some mechanics may be looking for a change. Moreover, the automotive industry is constantly evolving, and new jobs are being created that require different sets of skills.

Automotive Engineer

Automotive engineers design and develop new vehicles or improve existing ones. They work on different aspects of a car, such as its engine, transmission, suspension, and electronics. They use computer-aided design (CAD) software to create models and simulations and collaborate with other engineers and designers to bring a car from concept to reality.

To become an automotive engineer, one typically needs a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering or a related field. The job outlook for automotive engineers is positive, with a projected growth rate of 4% from 2019 to 2029, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS).

Technical Trainer

Technical trainers teach mechanics and other automotive professionals how to repair and maintain vehicles. They design and deliver training programs that cover different topics, such as electrical systems, brakes, and engines. They may work for car manufacturers, dealerships, or training centers.

To become a technical trainer, one needs extensive experience as a car mechanic and excellent communication and teaching skills. The job outlook for technical trainers is also positive, with a projected growth rate of 6% from 2019 to 2029, according to the BLS.

Automotive Sales Representative

Automotive sales representatives sell cars, trucks, and other vehicles. They work for dealerships or car manufacturers and assist customers in choosing the right vehicle for their needs and budget. They also negotiate prices and financing options and explain the features and benefits of different models.

To become an automotive sales representative, one typically needs a high school diploma or equivalent and excellent communication and customer service skills. The job outlook for automotive sales representatives is average, with a projected growth rate of 2% from 2019 to 2029, according to the BLS.

Automotive Journalist

Automotive journalists write about cars and the automotive industry for magazines, newspapers, websites, and other media outlets. They test drive new vehicles, attend car shows and events, and interview car designers, engineers, and executives. They also write reviews, news articles, and feature stories that inform and entertain their readers.

To become an automotive journalist, one needs strong writing and research skills and a passion for cars and the automotive industry. The job outlook for automotive journalists is challenging, as the media industry is undergoing significant changes, but there are still opportunities for talented writers and reporters.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I become an automotive engineer without a degree in mechanical engineering?

A: While a degree in mechanical engineering or a related field is typically required for automotive engineering jobs, some employers may consider candidates with a degree in a different field if they have relevant experience and skills.

Q: How much do technical trainers make?

A: The median annual wage for technical trainers was $52,270 in May 2019, according to the BLS.

Q: Is automotive sales a high-pressure job?

A: Automotive sales can be a high-pressure job, as sales representatives need to meet sales quotas and compete with other dealerships. However, it can also be a rewarding job for those who enjoy working with customers and have good sales skills.

Q: How do I become an automotive journalist?

A: To become an automotive journalist, one typically needs to have a degree in journalism, communications, or a related field, and strong writing and research skills. One can also gain experience by writing for school newspapers, blogs, or other media outlets.

Conclusion of Other Jobs for Car Mechanics

Car mechanics have various options when it comes to alternative career paths. From designing cars to teaching others how to repair them, there are many jobs that require a mechanic's skills and expertise. By exploring other jobs for car mechanics, one can find new challenges and opportunities that can lead to a more fulfilling career.
