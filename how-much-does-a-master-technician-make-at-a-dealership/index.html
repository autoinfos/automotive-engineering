How Much Does A Master Technician Make At A Dealership

Written by William Jul 15, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a master technician at a dealership, but wondering how much you can expect to make? The salary of a master technician can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and the dealership itself. But if you're looking for a career that offers a competitive salary and opportunities for growth, becoming a master technician at a dealership might be the right choice for you.

Many people who are interested in becoming a master technician at a dealership may be concerned about the salary. While it's true that the salary can vary, the average salary for a master technician at a dealership is around $60,000 to $80,000 per year. This can vary depending on the location of the dealership, the experience and certifications of the technician, and the dealership's pay structure.

So how do you become a master technician at a dealership? The first step is to complete a formal automotive training program and obtain certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). Once you have obtained certification, you can begin working as a technician at a dealership and work your way up to become a master technician. Becoming a master technician typically requires additional training and certifications, as well as several years of experience as a technician.

In summary, becoming a master technician at a dealership can offer a competitive salary and opportunities for growth. The average salary for a master technician at a dealership is around $60,000 to $80,000 per year, but can vary depending on several factors. To become a master technician, you will need to complete a formal automotive training program, obtain ASE certification, and gain several years of experience as a technician.

What Does It Take to Become a Master Technician at a Dealership?

Becoming a master technician at a dealership requires dedication and hard work. The first step is to complete a formal automotive training program and obtain ASE certification. To become a master technician, you will need to obtain additional certifications and training, as well as several years of experience as a technician.

Location Matters

The location of the dealership can greatly impact the salary of a master technician. For example, a dealership located in a high-cost-of-living area may offer a higher salary than a dealership located in a lower-cost-of-living area. It's important to research the average salary for master technicians in your area before accepting a job offer.

How to Negotiate Your Salary

If you're considering a job as a master technician at a dealership, it's important to know how to negotiate your salary. Before accepting a job offer, research the average salary for master technicians in your area and use that information to negotiate a fair salary. Additionally, highlight your experience, certifications, and skills to show the employer why you're worth the salary you're requesting.

From my personal experience, being a master technician at a dealership can be a rewarding career choice. The salary is competitive, and there are opportunities for growth and advancement.
