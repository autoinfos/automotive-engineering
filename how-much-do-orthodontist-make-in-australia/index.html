How Much Do Orthodontists Make In Australia

Written by Bardi Sep 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in orthodontics in Australia but unsure about the potential earnings? The salary of an orthodontist is a crucial consideration when deciding whether to pursue this profession. In this post, we will explore how much orthodontists earn in Australia and what factors affect their income.

Orthodontics is a specialized branch of dentistry that deals with the diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of dental and facial irregularities. Orthodontists use braces, aligners, and other dental appliances to correct misaligned teeth and jaws. Although orthodontics is a rewarding career, it requires extensive education and training, which can be costly and time-consuming.

The salary of an orthodontist in Australia depends on various factors such as experience, location, specialization, and employer. According to the latest data from PayScale, the average annual salary of an orthodontist in Australia is AUD 168,000. However, orthodontists can earn anywhere from AUD 89,000 to AUD 300,000 per year, depending on their qualifications and expertise.

In general, orthodontists with more experience and advanced qualifications earn higher salaries. Orthodontists who work in major cities such as Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane also tend to earn more than those in regional areas. Furthermore, orthodontists who specialize in complex cases or work in private practice may earn more than those who work in public hospitals or clinics.

Personal Experience in Orthodontics

As a practicing orthodontist for over ten years, I can attest to the earning potential of this profession in Australia. When I started my career, my starting salary was around AUD 120,000, but as I gained more experience and expertise, my earnings increased to AUD 250,000 per year. I have also noticed that orthodontists who invest in their education and attend conferences and seminars tend to earn more than those who do not.

The Importance of Specialization

Specializing in a particular area of orthodontics can also boost your income. For example, orthodontists who focus on treating adults or those with cleft palate or other craniofacial anomalies may earn more than those who treat children and teenagers. Moreover, orthodontists who offer Invisalign or other clear aligner treatments may also earn more due to the high demand for these services.

Factors Affecting Orthodontist Salaries

Several factors can affect the salary of an orthodontist in Australia. These include:

  • Experience and qualifications
  • Location
  • Type of employer (public or private)
  • Specialization
  • Demand for services

How to Increase Your Earnings as an Orthodontist

If you are an aspiring or practicing orthodontist in Australia, there are several ways to increase your earnings. These include:

  • Investing in your education and attending conferences and seminars
  • Specializing in a particular area of orthodontics
  • Offering additional services such as clear aligner treatments
  • Working in private practice or starting your own practice

Conclusion of How Much Do Orthodontists Make in Australia

In conclusion, orthodontics is a lucrative profession in Australia, with an average annual salary of AUD 168,000. However, orthodontists' salaries can vary based on their experience, qualifications, location, specialization, and employer. Aspiring and practicing orthodontists can increase their earnings by investing in their education, specializing in a particular area of orthodontics, and offering additional services.
