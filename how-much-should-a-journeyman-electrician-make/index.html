How Much Should A Journeyman Electrician Make

Written by Robby Nov 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a journeyman electrician? Perhaps you're already working in the field and wondering if you're being paid what you're worth. Either way, you're likely wondering, "How much should a journeyman electrician make?"

Electricians are highly skilled professionals who play a crucial role in keeping our world functioning. They install, maintain, and repair electrical systems in homes, businesses, and industrial settings. However, despite their importance, electricians often struggle to earn a fair wage for the work they do.

So, how much should a journeyman electrician make? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for electricians was $56,180 as of May 2020. However, this figure can vary widely depending on a number of factors, such as location, experience, and industry.

In addition to the median wage, it's important to consider other factors that can impact an electrician's earnings. For example, unionized electricians tend to earn higher wages than non-unionized electricians. Similarly, electricians who work in niche industries such as aerospace or telecommunications may earn more than those who work in more traditional settings.

My Personal Experience

As a journeyman electrician with over a decade of experience, I can tell you that the amount you earn can vary widely depending on where you work and who you work for. When I first started out, I was making around $20 an hour, which was barely enough to cover my bills. However, as I gained experience and moved up the ranks, my earnings increased significantly. Today, I make well over the median wage for electricians, and I feel that I'm being fairly compensated for the work I do.

Factors That Impact Electrician Wages

As mentioned earlier, a number of factors can impact how much a journeyman electrician makes. Here are a few key factors to keep in mind:

Location

Electricians who work in high-cost-of-living areas such as New York City or San Francisco tend to earn more than those who work in rural areas with a lower cost of living.

Experience

The more experience you have as an electrician, the more you can expect to earn. As you gain experience, you'll become more valuable to employers, which can lead to higher wages.

The Importance of Fair Wages for Electricians

Electricians play an incredibly important role in our society, and they deserve to be compensated fairly for the work they do. Not only is this important for individual electricians who need to support themselves and their families, but it's also important for the industry as a whole. When electricians are paid fairly, it helps to attract new talent to the field and ensures that we have a strong and skilled workforce for years to come.

Question and Answer

Q: Do all electricians earn the same wage?

A: No, electrician wages can vary widely depending on a number of factors, such as location, experience, and industry.

Q: Are unionized electricians paid more than non-unionized electricians?

A: Yes, unionized electricians tend to earn higher wages than non-unionized electricians.

Q: How can electricians increase their earnings?

A: Electricians can increase their earnings by gaining more experience, seeking out higher-paying job opportunities, and working in niche industries such as aerospace or telecommunications.

Q: Why is fair pay important for electricians?

A: Fair pay is important for electricians because it helps to attract new talent to the field and ensures that we have a strong and skilled workforce for years to come.

Conclusion of How Much Should a Journeyman Electrician Make'

In conclusion, journeyman electricians play a crucial role in keeping our world running, and they deserve to be compensated fairly for the work they do. While the median wage for electricians is around $56,180, this figure can vary widely depending on a number of factors. By understanding these factors and advocating for fair pay, we can help to ensure that electricians are paid what they're worth.
