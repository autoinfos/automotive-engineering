What Does A Service Advisor Do At A Car Dealership

Written by Bardi Jun 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When you drop off your car at a dealership for servicing, you might be greeted by a service advisor. But what exactly does a service advisor do? And why is their role so important?

For many car owners, dealing with car repairs and maintenance can be a frustrating experience. From the cost to the time it takes to get your car back, there are a lot of pain points associated with car servicing. That's where a service advisor comes in.

A service advisor is the person who acts as the liaison between the car owner and the mechanics who will be working on the car. They are responsible for managing the entire process of getting your car serviced, from scheduling appointments to providing estimates and delivering the finished car back to you.

So, what exactly does a service advisor do? Here are the main responsibilities:

Scheduling Appointments

One of the primary responsibilities of a service advisor is to schedule appointments for car owners who want to get their cars serviced. They work with the mechanics to determine the availability of the service department and then schedule appointments accordingly.

Providing Estimates

Once the car has been inspected, the service advisor will provide the car owner with an estimate of the cost of the repairs or maintenance that needs to be done. They will also explain the necessary repairs and why they are needed.

Ordering Parts

If the car needs new parts, the service advisor is responsible for ordering them and making sure they arrive in time for the repairs to be completed. They will also keep the car owner informed about the status of the parts.

Delivering the Finished Car

Once the repairs or maintenance have been completed, the service advisor will deliver the finished car back to the owner. They will also explain any warranties or guarantees that come with the work done on the car.

Why a Service Advisor is Important

A service advisor is important because they are the main point of contact between the car owner and the mechanics who will be working on the car. They help to make the process of getting your car serviced as easy and stress-free as possible. They also ensure that the car is repaired or maintained properly, which can help to prevent future problems and save the car owner money in the long run.

Personal Experience

As a car owner, I have had to deal with many frustrating experiences when it comes to car repairs and maintenance. However, having a good service advisor can make all the difference. I recently had to get my car serviced, and my service advisor was incredibly helpful. She explained everything that needed to be done, provided me with an estimate, and kept me informed about the progress of the repairs. Thanks to her, my car was fixed quickly and I was back on the road in no time.

Question and Answer

Q: How do service advisors communicate with car owners?

A: Service advisors communicate with car owners in a variety of ways, including phone calls, emails, and in-person meetings.

Q: What qualifications do service advisors need?

A: Service advisors typically need a high school diploma or equivalent, as well as experience in customer service or the automotive industry.

Q: Can service advisors help with warranty claims?

A: Yes, service advisors can help car owners with warranty claims by providing the necessary documentation and working with the manufacturer or warranty provider.

Q: Do service advisors work on commission?

A: Some service advisors may work on commission, but it varies from dealership to dealership.

Conclusion

A service advisor plays an important role in the car servicing process by managing appointments, providing estimates, ordering parts, and delivering the finished car back to the owner. By having a good service advisor, car owners can have a stress-free experience and ensure that their car is repaired or maintained properly.
