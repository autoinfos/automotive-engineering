What Do Auto Body Technicians Make

Written by Eveline Sep 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you love working with cars and have a passion for fixing them when they're damaged? Becoming an auto body technician might be the perfect career for you! However, before pursuing this path, it's important to ask yourself, "what do auto body technicians make?"

Auto body technicians are responsible for repairing and restoring damaged vehicles. They work in various settings, including car dealerships, repair shops, and even manufacturing plants. While it can be a fulfilling career, it's important to consider the financial aspects, including salary and job stability.

The average salary for an auto body technician in the United States is around $45,000 per year, with some technicians earning as much as $70,000 per year. However, this can vary based on factors such as location, experience, and the specific company or industry you work for.

In summary, becoming an auto body technician can be a rewarding career, but it's important to consider the financial aspects, including the average salary and job stability.

What Determines an Auto Body Technician's Salary?

When it comes to determining an auto body technician's salary, there are several factors to consider. One of the main factors is experience. The more experience you have, the more valuable you are to potential employers, which can lead to higher salaries. Additionally, the location in which you work can also have an impact on your salary. In areas with a higher cost of living, salaries may be higher to compensate for the increased living expenses.

Personally, when I started out as an auto body technician, I earned around $35,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved to a larger company, my salary increased to around $50,000 per year.

Is Job Stability a Concern for Auto Body Technicians?

Job stability is a valid concern for anyone considering a career as an auto body technician. However, the good news is that there is a high demand for skilled technicians in this field. As long as people continue to drive cars and accidents happen, there will always be a need for auto body technicians. Additionally, as technology continues to advance, there may be even more opportunities for technicians who are skilled in repairing new types of vehicles and equipment.

Additional Factors That Can Impact an Auto Body Technician's Salary

In addition to experience and location, there are several other factors that can impact an auto body technician's salary. These include the specific industry you work in, the size of the company you work for, and any additional certifications or skills you have. For example, technicians who are skilled in repairing luxury vehicles may earn higher salaries than those who work on standard vehicles.

How to Increase Your Auto Body Technician Salary

If you're looking to increase your auto body technician salary, there are several steps you can take. One option is to gain additional certifications or training in specialized areas, such as hybrid vehicle repair. Additionally, building a strong reputation and network within the industry can lead to more job opportunities and potentially higher salaries.

Conclusion of What Do Auto Body Technicians Make?

Overall, becoming an auto body technician can be a fulfilling and lucrative career path. While salaries can vary based on several factors, including experience and location, there is a high demand for skilled technicians in this field. By gaining experience, building a strong network, and potentially pursuing additional certifications or training, you can increase your salary and advance in your career as an auto body technician.
