Question and Answer Section

Q: How do I know when my brakes need to be replaced?
A: You'll know it's time to replace your brakes when you hear a grinding noise when you apply the brakes. Other signs that your brakes need to be replaced include reduced braking power, a soft or spongy brake pedal, and vibrations or shuddering when you brake.

Q: Can I change my own oil?
A: Yes, you can change your own oil. However, it's important to follow the manufacturer's recommendations for oil type and change intervals. Additionally, make sure you have the appropriate tools and safety equipment before attempting to change your own oil.

Q: What should I do if my engine won't start?
A: If your engine won't start, start by checking the battery connections to make sure they're tight and free from corrosion. If the battery connections are fine, check the starter motor and ignition system for problems. If you're unable to diagnose and fix the issue on your own, consider taking your car to a mechanic for further assistance.

Q: How often should I rotate my tires?
A: You should rotate your tires every 5,000 to 7,500 miles. This will help ensure that your tires wear evenly and last as long as possible.
