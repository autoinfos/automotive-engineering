How Much Does A Collision Repair Technician Make A Year

Written by Oliver Oct 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in collision repair but wondering if it's worth it financially? Many people underestimate the earning potential of collision repair technicians, but it can actually be a lucrative career path. In this blog post, we'll explore the factors that impact a collision repair technician's salary and give you a better understanding of how much you can expect to make in this field.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career in collision repair is the misconception that it's a low-paying job. While it's true that some entry-level positions may not pay as much as other trades, there are opportunities for growth and advancement that can lead to a higher salary. Additionally, the location, type of employer, and level of experience can all have a significant impact on a collision repair technician's earnings.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive body and glass repairers, which includes collision repair technicians, was $44,550 as of May 2020. However, this number can vary widely based on several factors. For example, those working in the top 10% of the industry can earn over $70,000 per year, while those in the bottom 10% may earn less than $27,000 per year.

In summary, the amount a collision repair technician makes per year can vary significantly based on a variety of factors. However, with the right level of experience, qualifications, and employer, it's possible to earn a lucrative salary in this field.

What Factors Impact a Collision Repair Technician's Salary?

Experience is one of the most important factors that can impact a collision repair technician's salary. Those with more experience and skill are often able to command higher wages, as they are able to complete repairs more efficiently and accurately. Additionally, those with certifications or specialized training in certain areas of collision repair may be able to earn higher salaries than those without these qualifications.

The type of employer can also play a role in determining a collision repair technician's earnings. Those working for dealerships or large collision repair chains may have access to more resources and higher salaries than those working for smaller independent shops. Location can also impact earnings, as those working in areas with higher costs of living may be able to command higher wages.

My Experience as a Collision Repair Technician

As someone who has worked in the collision repair industry for over a decade, I can attest to the earning potential of this field. While my starting salary was modest, I was able to quickly advance and earn more as I gained experience and demonstrated my skills. Over time, I was able to earn a comfortable living and provide for my family.

Tips for Maximizing Your Earnings as a Collision Repair Technician

If you're interested in pursuing a career in collision repair and want to maximize your earnings potential, there are a few things you can do. First, focus on gaining experience and building your skill set by taking advantage of training opportunities and seeking out mentorship from experienced colleagues. Consider pursuing certifications in specialized areas, such as aluminum repair or structural analysis, to make yourself a more valuable asset to employers. Finally, consider working for dealerships or large collision repair chains, as these employers often offer higher salaries and better benefits packages.

FAQs About Collision Repair Technician Salaries

Q: How much does a collision repair technician make per hour?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive body and glass repairers was $21.42 as of May 2020.

Q: Is collision repair a good career choice?

A: Yes, collision repair can be a rewarding and lucrative career choice for those with the right skills and training.

Q: What is the job outlook for collision repair technicians?

A: The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects that employment of automotive body and glass repairers will grow 4% from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: Do collision repair technicians get benefits?

A: Yes, many collision repair technicians receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. However, the availability and extent of these benefits can vary widely based on the employer and the individual's level of experience.

Conclusion

While the amount a collision repair technician makes per year can vary widely based on a variety of factors, this can be a lucrative and rewarding career path for those with the right skills and qualifications. By focusing on gaining experience, building your skill set, and seeking out opportunities with the right employers, you can maximize your earnings potential in this field.
