How Much Do Machinist Make Per Hour A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby May 28, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career as a machinist? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make per hour. The answer may surprise you!

Machinists are skilled professionals who create precision metal parts using machine tools such as lathes, milling machines, and grinders. While this is an in-demand profession, many people are not aware of the earning potential that comes with it.

The average machinist in the United States makes around $20 per hour. However, this can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and industry. Some machinists make as little as $12 per hour, while others can earn upwards of $30 per hour or more.

So, how can you ensure that you are earning the highest possible wage as a machinist? Here are some tips:

1. Gain Experience

As with any profession, the more experience you have, the more money you can expect to make. Consider taking on an apprenticeship or intern position to gain valuable experience in the field. This can also help you build connections and network with other professionals in the industry.

2. Specialize in a Niche

If you specialize in a certain type of machining or have expertise in a specific industry, you may be able to command a higher wage. For example, a machinist who specializes in aerospace parts may earn more than one who works in a general machine shop.

3. Stay Current with Technology

The machining industry is constantly evolving, with new technology and techniques emerging all the time. By staying up-to-date with the latest trends and innovations, you can position yourself as a valuable asset to employers and potentially earn a higher wage.

4. Negotiate Your Salary

If you feel that you are not being compensated fairly for your work, don't be afraid to negotiate your salary with your employer. Be prepared to make a case for why you deserve a higher wage, and provide evidence to support your claims.

The Bottom Line

Overall, machinists can expect to make a decent living wage, with opportunities for growth and advancement over time. By gaining experience, specializing in a niche, staying current with technology, and negotiating your salary when appropriate, you can maximize your earning potential as a machinist.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the job outlook for machinists?

A: The job outlook for machinists is positive, with steady demand for skilled professionals in many industries. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of machinists is projected to grow 3 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: How do machinist wages compare to other skilled trades?

A: Machinist wages are generally on par with other skilled trades such as electricians, plumbers, and carpenters. However, the potential for growth and advancement in the machining industry may be higher than in some other trades.

Q: What is the highest-paying industry for machinists?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the highest-paying industry for machinists is aerospace product and parts manufacturing, with an average hourly wage of $28.58.

Q: Is it necessary to have a college degree to become a machinist?

A: While a college degree is not required, many machinists have completed vocational or technical training programs to gain the necessary skills and knowledge. Some employers may require a high school diploma or equivalent, as well as on-the-job training or an apprenticeship.

Conclusion of how much do machinist make per hour

Overall, a career as a machinist can be a rewarding and lucrative choice for those who enjoy working with their hands and have an eye for detail. By following the tips outlined in this guide, you can maximize your earning potential and build a successful career in this exciting field.
