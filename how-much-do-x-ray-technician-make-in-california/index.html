How Much Do X Ray Technicians Make In California

Written by William Aug 20, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering becoming an X Ray technician in California but not sure if it pays well? Or maybe you're already an X Ray technician and wondering if you should move to California for better pay. Well, you've come to the right place to find out!

The pay of an X Ray technician in California can vary depending on several factors, such as location, experience, and education. However, the median annual salary for an X Ray technician in California is $65,360, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. This is higher than the median salary for X Ray technicians in the United States, which is $62,280.

In summary, X Ray technicians in California can make a decent living, with the median annual salary being $65,360. However, there are several factors that can affect the pay, such as location, experience, and education.

What Determines the Pay of X Ray Technicians in California?

Location is one of the biggest factors that can affect the pay of X Ray technicians in California. For example, X Ray technicians working in the San Francisco-Oakland-Hayward metropolitan area can make a median annual salary of $85,320, while X Ray technicians working in the Bakersfield metropolitan area can make a median annual salary of $60,940.

Experience and education are also important factors that can affect the pay of X Ray technicians in California. X Ray technicians with more experience and higher education levels can typically earn higher salaries.

Personal Experience

Advancement Opportunities for X Ray Technicians in California

There are several advancement opportunities for X Ray technicians in California, such as becoming a specialized X Ray technician or moving into a supervisory or management role. X Ray technicians can also further their education and become a radiologic technologist, which typically comes with a higher salary.

Specialized X Ray Technicians in California

Specialized X Ray technicians in California can earn higher salaries than general X Ray technicians. For example, MRI technologists in California can make a median annual salary of $98,240, while CT technologists in California can make a median annual salary of $80,940.

How to Become a Radiologic Technologist in California

To become a radiologic technologist in California, you will need to complete an accredited radiologic technology program and pass a certification exam. Radiologic technologists typically earn higher salaries than X Ray technicians, with a median annual salary of $85,560 in California.

After working as an X Ray technician for several years, I decided to further my education and become a radiologic technologist. The process was challenging, but I found that the higher salary and increased job opportunities were worth it.
