What Is The Job Outlook For A Pharmacy Technician

Written by Robby Aug 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician? You might be wondering about the job outlook for this profession. With the healthcare industry constantly growing and changing, it's important to know what the future holds for pharmacy technicians.

One of the main pain points related to the job outlook for pharmacy technicians is job availability. As more pharmacies open up and more people require medication, there will be a need for more pharmacy technicians. However, competition for these jobs can be tough, as there are many people entering the field. Another potential pain point is salary, as pharmacy technicians typically make less than pharmacists.

The good news is that the job outlook for pharmacy technicians is positive. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of pharmacy technicians is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations. This means that there will be more job opportunities for pharmacy technicians in the coming years.

In summary, the job outlook for pharmacy technicians is positive, with job growth expected in the coming years. However, competition for these jobs can be tough, and salaries may not be as high as some other healthcare professions.

What Does the Job Outlook for a Pharmacy Technician Mean for You?

When I became a pharmacy technician, I was worried about job availability. However, I found that there were many job openings in my area, and I was able to find a position quickly. While competition for these jobs can be tough, if you have the right qualifications and experience, you can stand out from the crowd.

It's important to keep in mind that the job outlook for pharmacy technicians may vary depending on your location and the specific pharmacy you want to work for. Doing your research and networking can help you find the right job for you.

How Can You Increase Your Job Opportunities as a Pharmacy Technician?

If you want to increase your job opportunities as a pharmacy technician, there are a few things you can do. First, consider getting certified by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB). This certification can make you a more competitive candidate for jobs and can increase your salary potential.

You can also gain experience by working in different types of pharmacies, such as retail, hospital, or mail-order pharmacies. This can make you a more well-rounded candidate and can help you stand out from other applicants. Networking with other pharmacy professionals can also help you learn about job openings and connect with potential employers.

What Are Some Potential Career Paths for Pharmacy Technicians?

While many pharmacy technicians work in retail or hospital pharmacies, there are other potential career paths to consider. Some pharmacy technicians work in long-term care facilities, compounding pharmacies, or research labs. Others go on to become pharmacists or pursue other healthcare careers.

My Experience as a Pharmacy Technician

When I started my career as a pharmacy technician, I was worried about the job outlook and whether I would be able to find a job. However, I quickly found that there were many job opportunities available to me. I gained experience working in both retail and hospital pharmacies, which helped me develop a well-rounded skill set.

Over time, I was able to increase my job opportunities and salary potential by getting certified by the PTCB and networking with other pharmacy professionals. I also considered pursuing a career as a pharmacist, but ultimately decided to stay in my current role as a pharmacy technician.

Question and Answer

Q: What qualifications do I need to become a pharmacy technician?

A: Requirements vary by state, but most pharmacy technicians need a high school diploma or equivalent, and some may require postsecondary education or certification.

Q: What is the average salary for a pharmacy technician?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for pharmacy technicians was $35,100 in May 2020.

Q: How can I stand out from other candidates when applying for pharmacy technician jobs?

A: Consider getting certified by the PTCB, gaining experience in different types of pharmacies, and networking with other pharmacy professionals.

Q: What is the work environment like for pharmacy technicians?

A: Pharmacy technicians typically work in pharmacies and drugstores, and may work evenings, weekends, and holidays. They may also be on their feet for long periods of time.

Conclusion of What is the Job Outlook for a Pharmacy Technician

If you're considering a career as a pharmacy technician, the job outlook is positive. While there may be competition for jobs and salaries may not be as high as other healthcare professions, there is a growing need for pharmacy technicians in the coming years. By gaining experience, getting certified, and networking with other pharmacy professionals, you can increase your job opportunities and salary potential.
