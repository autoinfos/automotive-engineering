General Job Interview Questions What To Expect And How To Answer Them

Written by Aprilia Jul 03, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Job interviews can be nerve-wracking, especially when you don't know what to expect. However, being prepared for some of the most common interview questions can help ease your nerves and increase your chances of landing the job you want.

Many candidates struggle with general job interview questions because they are often open-ended and require thoughtful responses. It can be challenging to strike a balance between providing enough information without rambling or oversharing.

One of the most common general job interview questions is "Tell me about yourself." The interviewer wants to learn more about you as a person and a professional. Instead of reciting your entire resume, focus on highlighting your relevant skills and experiences that make you a good fit for the job.

In this post, we will explore some of the most common general job interview questions and provide tips on how to answer them effectively.

Question 1: "What are your strengths and weaknesses?"

This question can be tricky because it requires you to be honest about your weaknesses while still emphasizing your strengths. One way to approach this question is to mention a weakness that you have worked to improve and how you have overcome it. For example, "My weakness used to be public speaking, but I have taken courses and practiced presenting to become more confident." Then, highlight a strength that is relevant to the job, such as "I am skilled in project management and have successfully led teams to complete complex projects."

Question 2: "Why do you want to work for this company?"

This question demonstrates your interest and enthusiasm for the company. Do your research and mention specific aspects of the company that you admire, such as their mission statement or recent accomplishments. Additionally, you can explain how your skills align with the company's goals and how you can contribute to their success.

Question 3: "What are your salary expectations?"

It's important to do your research and have a realistic salary expectation based on the industry, location, and level of experience. You can also ask the interviewer about the salary range for the position or mention that you are open to negotiation based on the entire compensation package.

Question 4: "Can you tell me about a time when you faced a difficult situation and how you handled it?"

This behavioral question assesses your problem-solving and decision-making skills. Provide a specific example and explain the steps you took to resolve the situation. Be sure to highlight the positive outcome and what you learned from the experience.

Conclusion of General Job Interview Questions

General job interview questions can be daunting, but with preparation and practice, you can effectively answer them and impress the interviewer. Remember to focus on relevant skills and experiences, do your research on the company, and provide specific examples to demonstrate your abilities. Good luck!
