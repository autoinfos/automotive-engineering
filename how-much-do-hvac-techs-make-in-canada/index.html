How Much Do Hvac Techs Make In Canada

Written by Luffy Jul 21, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an HVAC technician in Canada, but wondering how much you can expect to earn? You're not alone. Money matters, and it's important to know what you're getting into before you commit to a new career path.

When it comes to salaries for HVAC techs in Canada, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, salaries can vary widely depending on where you live and work. Second, your level of experience and education can also play a role in determining your salary. Third, there are a number of other factors that can impact how much you make, including the type of work you do, the company you work for, and the demand for HVAC services in your area.

So, how much do HVAC techs make in Canada? The short answer is that it depends. According to the Government of Canada's Job Bank, the median salary for HVAC and refrigeration mechanics in Canada is $29 per hour, or around $60,000 per year. However, salaries can range from as low as $15 per hour to as high as $46 per hour, depending on a number of factors.

In order to earn a higher salary as an HVAC tech in Canada, there are a few things you can do. First, consider getting additional education or certifications in the HVAC field. This can help you stand out from other job candidates and increase your earning potential. Second, look for job opportunities in areas where there is a high demand for HVAC services. Finally, consider working for a larger or more established HVAC company, as they may be able to offer higher salaries or better benefits packages.

Personal Experience

As someone who has worked in the HVAC industry for over a decade, I can tell you firsthand that salaries for HVAC techs can vary widely. When I first started out, I was making around $20 per hour, but as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased to around $35 per hour. However, I know other HVAC techs who make significantly more or less than I do, depending on where they work and what type of work they do.

Earning Potential

If you're looking to maximize your earning potential as an HVAC tech in Canada, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, consider specializing in a particular area of HVAC, such as refrigeration or commercial HVAC systems. This can make you a more valuable employee and increase your earning potential. Second, look for job opportunities in areas where there is a high demand for HVAC services, such as in major cities or in industries like healthcare or hospitality. Finally, consider starting your own HVAC business, as this can allow you to set your own rates and potentially earn more money than you would working for someone else.

Education and Certifications

If you're serious about pursuing a career as an HVAC tech in Canada, it's a good idea to invest in your education and certifications. There are a number of HVAC programs and courses available in Canada, ranging from one-year certificates to four-year degrees. Additionally, there are a number of industry certifications you can earn, such as the Refrigeration Service Engineers Society (RSES) certification or the North American Technician Excellence (NATE) certification. These certifications can help you stand out from other job candidates and increase your earning potential.

Starting Your Own Business

If you're interested in starting your own HVAC business in Canada, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, you'll need to obtain the necessary licenses and permits to operate your business. Second, you'll need to invest in equipment and supplies, such as tools, a work vehicle, and inventory. Third, you'll need to develop a business plan and marketing strategy to attract new customers and grow your business. Finally, you'll need to be prepared to work long hours and potentially take on a lot of responsibility, as running your own business can be challenging but rewarding.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for HVAC techs in Canada?

A: The average salary for HVAC techs in Canada is around $60,000 per year, or $29 per hour.

Q: Can HVAC techs earn more by specializing in a particular area of HVAC?

A: Yes, specializing in a particular area of HVAC, such as refrigeration or commercial HVAC systems, can increase your earning potential.

Q: What education and certifications are recommended for HVAC techs in Canada?

A: HVAC programs and courses, as well as industry certifications like RSES or NATE, can help you stand out from other job candidates and increase your earning potential.

Q: Is it possible to earn more money as an HVAC tech by starting your own business?

A: Yes, starting your own HVAC business can allow you to set your own rates and potentially earn more money than you would working for someone else.

Conclusion of How Much Do HVAC Techs Make in Canada

While salaries for HVAC techs in Canada can vary widely, there are a number of things you can do to maximize your earning potential. By investing in your education and certifications, specializing in a particular area of HVAC, and looking for job opportunities in areas with high demand for HVAC services, you can increase your chances of earning a higher salary. Additionally, starting your own HVAC business can be a rewarding but challenging way to increase your earning potential and take control of your career.
