How To Sell Interview Questions A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling to sell your interview questions? Do you find it difficult to communicate their value to potential buyers? Look no further. In this article, we will provide a comprehensive guide on how to sell interview questions in a relaxed and effective manner.

Many interviewers and hiring managers struggle to come up with effective interview questions. They often resort to asking generic questions that do not provide any valuable insight into the candidate's skills and abilities. This is where your interview questions can make a difference. However, it can be challenging to convince potential buyers of their worth.

The key to selling interview questions is to communicate their value in a clear and concise manner. You need to explain how your questions can help the buyer gain a better understanding of the candidate's skills and abilities. You should also highlight how your questions are different from the generic ones commonly used in interviews.

In summary, to sell interview questions effectively, you need to:

Understand Your Target Audience

Before you start selling your interview questions, you need to understand your target audience. Who are your potential buyers? What are their pain points, and how can your questions help alleviate them? Understanding your target audience will help you tailor your pitch to their specific needs.

For example, if you are targeting hiring managers, you should highlight how your questions can help them identify the best candidate for the job. You can also emphasize how your questions can help them save time by eliminating unqualified candidates early in the hiring process.

Share Your Personal Experience

Sharing your personal experience can help establish credibility and build trust with potential buyers. It shows that you have experience in the field and that your questions are based on real-world scenarios. You can share anecdotes about how your questions have helped you identify top-performing candidates in the past.

For example, you can share a story about how one of your questions helped you identify a candidate who had a unique approach to problem-solving, which ultimately led to their success in the role.

Use the Right Keywords

Using the right keywords can help your interview questions stand out in search results. You need to use keywords that are relevant to your target audience and that accurately describe your questions. Some examples of relevant keywords include "interview questions for hiring managers," "behavioral interview questions," and "interview questions for customer service roles."

Provide Samples

Providing samples of your interview questions can help potential buyers understand their value and quality. You can create a sample document or presentation that showcases your questions and how they can be used in the hiring process. You can also provide testimonials from satisfied buyers who have used your questions in the past.

Question and Answer Section

Q: How do I know if my interview questions are effective?

A: Effective interview questions should be relevant to the role and provide insight into the candidate's skills and abilities. They should also be open-ended and encourage the candidate to provide detailed responses.

Q: How do I price my interview questions?

A: The price of your interview questions will depend on factors such as their quality, uniqueness, and effectiveness. You can research similar products and services to get an idea of what the market is willing to pay. You can also offer different pricing tiers based on the number of questions or the level of customization.

Q: How do I market my interview questions?

A: You can market your interview questions using various channels, such as social media, email marketing, and content marketing. You can also attend industry conferences and trade shows to network with potential buyers.

Q: Can I customize my interview questions for specific roles or industries?

A: Yes, you can customize your interview questions for specific roles or industries. This can help make your questions more relevant and valuable to potential buyers.

Conclusion of How to Sell Interview Questions

Selling interview questions can be challenging, but it is not impossible. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can effectively communicate the value of your questions and persuade potential buyers to invest in them. Remember to understand your target audience, share your personal experience, use the right keywords, and provide samples to showcase your questions' quality and effectiveness.
