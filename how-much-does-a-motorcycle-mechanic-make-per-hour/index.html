How Much Does A Motorcycle Mechanic Make Per Hour

Written by Christine Oct 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a motorcycle mechanic and wondering how much you can expect to make per hour? Or maybe you're a motorcycle enthusiast curious about the earning potential of mechanics. Either way, you've come to the right place. In this post, we'll explore the topic of how much does a motorcycle mechanic make per hour and provide you with all the information you need to know.

Being a motorcycle mechanic is a physically demanding job that requires technical expertise, attention to detail, and problem-solving skills. However, despite the skill and effort required, motorcycle mechanics are often paid less than other skilled trades. This can be a pain point for those who are passionate about motorcycles and want to make a living working on them.

So, how much does a motorcycle mechanic make per hour? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for motorcycle mechanics in the United States is $18.99. However, this can vary depending on a variety of factors such as experience level, location, and type of employer.

To summarize, while being a motorcycle mechanic can be a fulfilling career for those passionate about motorcycles, it may not be the most lucrative option. The median hourly wage for motorcycle mechanics is below average compared to other skilled trades.

Why Do Motorcycle Mechanics Make Less Than Other Skilled Trades?

One reason motorcycle mechanics make less than other skilled trades is the comparatively low demand for their services. While motorcycles are popular, they are not as widely used as cars and trucks, which means there are fewer opportunities for mechanics to work on them.

In addition, motorcycle mechanics may require specialized training and tools, which can be costly for employers. This can result in lower wages for mechanics as employers try to offset their expenses.

Personal Experience of a Motorcycle Mechanic

As a motorcycle mechanic with 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that the hourly wage for this profession is not as high as it should be. Despite the hard work and technical expertise required, I have struggled to make ends meet at times.

However, I also love working on motorcycles and find the job to be incredibly rewarding. For those who are passionate about motorcycles and want to make a living working on them, I would still recommend pursuing this career path.

Factors That Affect How Much a Motorcycle Mechanic Makes

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect how much a motorcycle mechanic makes per hour. These include:

  • Experience level
  • Location
  • Type of employer (e.g. dealership, independent shop)
  • Type of motorcycles worked on (e.g. vintage, high-end)

Generally, mechanics with more experience and those who work on high-end or vintage motorcycles can command higher wages. Additionally, those who work for dealerships may earn more than those who work for independent shops.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as a Motorcycle Mechanic

If you're a motorcycle mechanic looking to increase your earning potential, there are several steps you can take. These include:

  • Continuing your education and training to stay up-to-date on the latest motorcycle technology and repair techniques
  • Specializing in a particular area such as vintage motorcycles or high-performance bikes
  • Building a loyal customer base and establishing a reputation for quality work
  • Considering working for a dealership or starting your own business

Personal Experience of a Motorcycle Enthusiast

As a motorcycle enthusiast, I have a lot of respect for motorcycle mechanics and the work they do. While I understand that the hourly wage for this profession may not be as high as some other skilled trades, I believe that the passion and dedication of motorcycle mechanics make up for it.

Motorcycle mechanics are essential to keeping our beloved machines running smoothly and safely. Without them, we wouldn't be able to enjoy the freedom and exhilaration that comes with riding motorcycles.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a beginner motorcycle mechanic make per hour?

A: A beginner motorcycle mechanic can expect to make around $12-$14 per hour, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Q: What is the highest paying state for motorcycle mechanics?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, motorcycle mechanics in Alaska earn the highest median hourly wage at $26.20.

Q: Do motorcycle mechanics get benefits?

A: Whether or not motorcycle mechanics receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off varies by employer.

Q: Is being a motorcycle mechanic a good career choice?

A: Being a motorcycle mechanic can be a good career choice for those who are passionate about motorcycles and enjoy working with their hands. However, it may not be the most lucrative option.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Motorcycle Mechanic Make Per Hour

In conclusion, the median hourly wage for motorcycle mechanics in the United States is $18.99. While this may not be as high as some other skilled trades, being a motorcycle mechanic can still be a fulfilling career choice for those passionate about motorcycles. By continuing their education and training, specializing in a particular area, and building a loyal customer base, motorcycle mechanics can increase their earning potential and establish a successful career in the industry.
