How Much Do Civil Engineers Make An Hour In California

Written by Daniel Nov 20, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career in civil engineering in California, but wondering how much you could earn? Or maybe you're already a civil engineer in California and looking to negotiate a higher salary. Either way, understanding the average hourly wage for civil engineers in California is an important first step.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the mean hourly wage for civil engineers in California is $49.60, as of May 2020. This is higher than the national average for civil engineers, which is $44.65 per hour. However, it's important to note that actual wages may vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and industry.

In general, civil engineers in California can expect to earn a salary range of $67,000 to $144,000 per year. Entry-level civil engineers can start at around $67,000 per year, while those with more experience or in higher-level positions can earn up to $144,000 per year or more.

In summary, civil engineering is a lucrative career in California, with a mean hourly wage of $49.60 and a salary range of $67,000 to $144,000 per year. However, actual wages may vary depending on individual factors.

What Are the Factors That Affect Civil Engineer Hourly Wages in California?

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect civil engineer hourly wages in California. These include:

Experience:

Civil engineers with several years of experience in the field are likely to earn higher wages than those who are just starting.

Location:

Civil engineers working in metropolitan areas or larger cities in California may earn higher wages than those working in rural or less populated areas.

Industry:

Civil engineers working in certain industries, such as oil and gas extraction or architectural and engineering services, may earn higher wages than those working in other industries.

Education:

Civil engineers with advanced degrees or certifications may earn higher wages than those with only a bachelor's degree.

My Experience as a Civil Engineer in California

As a civil engineer in California, I've had a great experience in terms of salary and career growth. I started as an entry-level civil engineer earning around $67,000 per year, but with hard work and dedication, I was able to climb the corporate ladder and now earn around $120,000 per year.

How Can I Negotiate a Higher Hourly Wage as a Civil Engineer in California?

If you're looking to negotiate a higher hourly wage as a civil engineer in California, here are some tips:

Highlight Your Accomplishments:

Show your employer how you've contributed to the company's success and why you deserve a higher wage.

Do Your Research:

Research what other civil engineers in California are earning and use that information to negotiate a higher wage.

Be Willing to Negotiate:

Be open to compromise and find a solution that works for both you and your employer.
