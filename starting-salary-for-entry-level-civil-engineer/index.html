Starting Salary For Entry Level Civil Engineer What To Expect

Written by Luffy Jul 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an entry level civil engineer? You may be wondering what kind of salary you can expect to earn when you start out in this field. As someone who has been through it, I can tell you that it's not always easy to know what to expect. But don't worry, I've got you covered. In this post, I'll share some insights into starting salaries for entry level civil engineers and what you need to know.

If you're just starting out in your career, you may be feeling a bit overwhelmed by the prospect of negotiating your starting salary. After all, you don't have much experience, and you may not be sure what you're worth. This can make it difficult to know how much to ask for or what to expect. However, there are some general guidelines you can follow to help you get a sense of what to expect.

So, what can you expect in terms of starting salary for an entry level civil engineer? The short answer is that it varies depending on a number of factors, including your location, industry, and level of education. However, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for civil engineers was $87,060 as of May 2019. This breaks down to an hourly wage of $41.85.

My Personal Experience

When I graduated from college with a degree in civil engineering, I was excited to start my career. However, I was also a bit nervous about what kind of salary I could expect. I was applying for jobs in a variety of industries and locations, which made it challenging to know what to ask for. Ultimately, I ended up accepting a position with an engineering firm that offered me a starting salary of $60,000 per year. While this was lower than some of the other offers I received, I felt that the company was a good fit for me and offered opportunities for growth and advancement.

Factors That Affect Starting Salary

As I mentioned earlier, there are a number of factors that can influence your starting salary as an entry level civil engineer. Some of the most important factors include:

Location

The cost of living varies widely across different regions of the country, and this can have a big impact on your starting salary. For example, if you're working in a major city like New York or San Francisco, you can expect to earn a higher salary than if you're working in a smaller town in the Midwest.

Industry

The industry you work in can also have an impact on your starting salary. For example, if you're working in the private sector, you may be able to earn a higher salary than if you're working in the public sector.

Education and Experience

Your level of education and experience can also influence your starting salary. If you have a master's degree or several years of experience in the field, you may be able to command a higher salary than someone who is just starting out with a bachelor's degree.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it possible to negotiate my starting salary as an entry level civil engineer?

A: Yes, it's always possible to negotiate your starting salary. However, it's important to be realistic about what you can expect, especially if you don't have much experience. Do your research ahead of time so you have a good sense of what the industry standard is for your position and location.

Q: What are some tips for negotiating my starting salary?

A: Some tips for negotiating your starting salary include doing your research ahead of time, being clear about your expectations and needs, and being willing to compromise. Remember that your starting salary sets the foundation for your entire career, so it's important to make sure you're starting on the right foot.

Q: What kind of benefits can I expect as an entry level civil engineer?

A: Benefits can vary widely depending on the company you work for. Some common benefits include health insurance, retirement plans, paid time off, and professional development opportunities.

Q: What kind of growth opportunities can I expect as an entry level civil engineer?

A: Civil engineering is a rapidly growing field with many opportunities for advancement. With experience and additional education, you may be able to move into higher-level positions, such as project manager or senior engineer.

Conclusion of Starting Salary for Entry Level Civil Engineer

As you can see, there are many factors that can influence your starting salary as an entry level civil engineer. However, with the right education, experience, and negotiation skills, you can ensure that you're starting out on the right foot. Remember to do your research, be realistic about your expectations, and be willing to compromise when necessary. Good luck!
