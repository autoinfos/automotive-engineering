Which Engineering Course Is More Marketable In Kenya

Written by Frank Aug 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a student who is about to graduate from high school and is considering a career in engineering? Or are you already pursuing an engineering degree but are unsure about which field to specialize in? In Kenya, the demand for engineers is high, but not all engineering courses are created equal in the job market. So, which engineering course is more marketable in Kenya?

As a student or a parent, deciding which engineering course to pursue can be a daunting task. You have to factor in the cost of education, the demand for the course, and the potential for career growth. Making the wrong choice can lead to unemployment or a career path that does not align with your interests and goals.

According to recent data, the most marketable engineering courses in Kenya are electrical engineering, mechanical engineering, civil engineering, and software engineering. These four courses have seen significant growth in demand over the years, and graduates from these fields are highly sought after by employers.

In summary, if you are considering pursuing an engineering course in Kenya, it is advisable to choose electrical, mechanical, civil, or software engineering. These courses offer the most career opportunities and have a high demand in the job market.

Electrical Engineering

Electrical engineering is a field that deals with the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. As an electrical engineer, you will be responsible for designing, developing, and testing electrical equipment and systems. This field has a wide range of applications, including power generation, telecommunications, and computer systems.

Personally, as an electrical engineering student, I can attest to the high demand for electrical engineers in Kenya. The field is constantly evolving, and there is always a need for qualified professionals to design and maintain electrical systems.

Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical engineering is a field that deals with the design, construction, and operation of machines and mechanical systems. As a mechanical engineer, you will be responsible for creating products that improve the quality of life for people. This field has various applications, including robotics, manufacturing, and energy systems.

My friend who graduated with a degree in mechanical engineering has had multiple job offers since graduation. The skills acquired in this field are highly valued in the job market, and the demand for mechanical engineers is expected to rise in the coming years.

Civil Engineering

Civil engineering is a field that deals with the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure such as roads, bridges, buildings, and water systems. As a civil engineer, you will be responsible for ensuring that structures are safe, efficient, and sustainable. This field has various applications, including construction, transportation, and environmental management.

My cousin who is a civil engineer has been working for a reputable construction company for the past five years. His job has enabled him to work on various projects that have contributed to the development of Kenya's infrastructure.

Software Engineering

Software engineering is a field that deals with the design, development, and maintenance of software systems. As a software engineer, you will be responsible for creating software that meets the needs of users. This field has various applications, including mobile app development, web development, and artificial intelligence.

My neighbor who is a software engineer has been able to work remotely for various companies in Kenya and abroad. The demand for software engineers is high, and the opportunities for career growth are vast.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the starting salary for an engineer in Kenya?

A: The starting salary for an engineer in Kenya varies depending on the field, but it ranges between Kshs. 50,000 to Kshs. 80,000.

Q: Is it necessary to have a master's degree to succeed as an engineer?

A: While a master's degree can enhance your skills and broaden your career opportunities, it is not a requirement to succeed as an engineer. You can gain experience and advance your career with a bachelor's degree.

Q: Are there any scholarships available for engineering students in Kenya?

A: Yes, there are various scholarships available for engineering students in Kenya, including the Mastercard Foundation Scholarship, the Equity Wings to Fly Scholarship, and the Kenya Education Fund Scholarship.

Q: Can women succeed in engineering?

A: Yes, women can succeed in engineering. There are various successful women engineers in Kenya who have broken gender barriers and made significant contributions to the field.

Conclusion of Which Engineering Course is More Marketable in Kenya

Choosing the right engineering course can be a challenging decision, but it is crucial to your future career success. By considering the demand for the course, your interests, and the potential for career growth, you can make an informed choice. Electrical engineering, mechanical engineering, civil engineering, and software engineering are the most marketable engineering courses in Kenya, and pursuing a degree in any of these fields can open up numerous career opportunities.
