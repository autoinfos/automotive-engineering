How To Become A Vehicle Technician A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Nov 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you passionate about cars and want to turn your hobby into a career? Do you enjoy fixing things and have a knack for problem-solving? If you answered yes to these questions, then becoming a vehicle technician could be the perfect career choice for you!

Many people are interested in pursuing a career as a vehicle technician, but they may not know where to start or what steps to take. Some may even find the process daunting, especially if they have no prior experience in the automotive industry.

Don't worry! In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to become a vehicle technician and give you helpful tips along the way.

My Journey to Becoming a Vehicle Technician

Before we dive into the steps, let me share with you my personal journey to becoming a vehicle technician. I have always been interested in cars, and I spent most of my free time tinkering with engines and fixing up cars in my garage. However, I never thought of turning my hobby into a career until a friend suggested it to me.

At first, I was hesitant because I had no prior experience in the automotive industry. But I decided to give it a try and enrolled in a vocational school that offered a program in automotive technology. It was a challenging but rewarding experience, and it gave me the skills and knowledge I needed to start my career as a vehicle technician.

Step-by-Step Guide on How to Become a Vehicle Technician

Now, let's take a look at the steps you need to take to become a vehicle technician:

Step 1: Research

The first step in becoming a vehicle technician is to research the career and the automotive industry. This will help you determine if this career is the right fit for you and give you an idea of what to expect.

You can start by reading articles and blogs about becoming a vehicle technician, talking to professionals in the field, and exploring job listings to see what qualifications and skills are required.

Step 2: Get a High School Diploma or Equivalent

Most employers require that you have a high school diploma or equivalent before you can become a vehicle technician. This is because the job requires a basic understanding of math, science, and technology.

If you haven't completed high school, you can earn a General Education Development (GED) certificate instead.

Step 3: Enroll in a Vocational School or Community College

Enrolling in a vocational school or community college that offers a program in automotive technology is the next step in becoming a vehicle technician. These programs typically last two years and cover topics such as engine repair, brakes, suspension, and electrical systems.

You can also choose to specialize in a specific area of automotive technology, such as diesel engines or hybrid vehicles.

Step 4: Gain Hands-On Experience

While attending vocational school or community college, it's important to gain hands-on experience by working on cars and other vehicles. This will help you develop the practical skills needed to become a successful vehicle technician.

You can gain hands-on experience by working part-time at a repair shop, participating in an apprenticeship program, or volunteering at a local garage.

Step 5: Obtain Certification

Once you have completed your education and gained hands-on experience, the next step is to obtain certification. Although certification is not required, it can improve your job prospects and earning potential.

The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) offers certification in various areas of automotive technology, such as brakes, engine repair, and electrical systems. To become certified, you must pass an exam and have at least two years of hands-on experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does it take to become a vehicle technician?

A: It typically takes two years to complete a vocational school or community college program in automotive technology.

Q: Is certification required to become a vehicle technician?

A: Certification is not required, but it can improve your job prospects and earning potential.

Q: What skills do I need to become a vehicle technician?

A: You need basic math, science, and technology skills, as well as problem-solving and communication skills.

Q: How much does a vehicle technician earn?

A: The median annual salary for a vehicle technician is $42,090, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Conclusion

Becoming a vehicle technician is a rewarding career choice for those who love cars and enjoy problem-solving. By following these steps and gaining hands-on experience, you can turn your passion into a successful career.
